# AMC12 2000 Q17

![[assets/problems/2000/Q17.png]]

## Problem

A circle centered at $O$ has radius $1$ and contains the point $A.$ Segment $AB$ is tangent to the circle at $A$ and $\angle AOB = \theta.$ If point $C$ lies on $\overline{OA}$ and $BC$ bisects $\angle ABO,$ then what is $OC?$

(A) $\sec^2\theta - \tan\theta$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{2}$
(C) $\dfrac{\cos^2\theta}{1 + \sin\theta}$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{1 + \sin\theta}$
(E) $\dfrac{\sin\theta}{\cos^2\theta}$

## Answer

**D.** $\dfrac{1}{1 + \sin\theta}$

## Solution

Because $OA = 1$ and $AB$ is tangent at $A,$ angle $OAB$ is right, so
$$
 BA = \tan\theta, \qquad OB = \sec\theta.
$$

Since $BC$ bisects $\angle ABO,$ the angle bisector theorem gives $\dfrac{OC}{CA} = \dfrac{OB}{BA}.$ Using $OC + CA = OA = 1,$
$$
 \begin{aligned} OC &= \frac{OB}{OB + BA} \\ &= \frac{\sec\theta}{\sec\theta + \tan\theta}. \end{aligned}
$$

Multiplying numerator and denominator by $\cos\theta$ gives $OC = \dfrac{1}{1 + \sin\theta}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- Since $OA = 1$ and $\angle OAB = 90^\circ,$ we have $BA = \tan\theta$ and $OB = \sec\theta$
- The bisector gives $\dfrac{OC}{CA} = \dfrac{OB}{BA},$ and $OC + CA = OA = 1$
