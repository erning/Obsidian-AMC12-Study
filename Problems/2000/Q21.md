# AMC12 2000 Q21

![[assets/problems/2000/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Through a point on the hypotenuse of a right triangle, lines are drawn parallel to the legs of the triangle so that the triangle is divided into a square and two smaller right triangles. The area of one of the two small right triangles is $m$ times the area of the square. What is the ratio of the area of the other small right triangle to the area of the square?

(A) $\dfrac{1}{2m + 1}$
(B) $m$
(C) $1 - m$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{4m}$
(E) $\dfrac{1}{8m^2}$

## Answer

**D.** $\dfrac{1}{4m}$

## Solution

Let the square have side $1.$ The small triangle sharing one side of the square has a perpendicular leg $r,$ so its area is $\tfrac12 \cdot 1 \cdot r = m,$ giving $r = 2m.$

The two small triangles are similar, so the other triangle's leg along the square is $\dfrac1r,$ and its area is
$$
 \frac12 \cdot 1 \cdot \frac1r = \frac{1}{2r} = \frac{1}{4m}.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- Let the square have side $1$; one small triangle has a leg $r$ along the square, so its area is $\dfrac{r}{2} = m$
- The two small triangles are similar, so the other triangle's matching leg is $\dfrac{1}{r}$
