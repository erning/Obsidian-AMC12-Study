# AMC12 2000 Q13

![[assets/problems/2000/Q13.png]]

## Problem

One morning each member of Angela's family drank an $8$-ounce mixture of coffee with milk. The amounts of coffee and milk varied from cup to cup, but were never zero. Angela drank a quarter of the total amount of milk and a sixth of the total amount of coffee. How many people are in the family?

(A) $3$
(B) $4$
(C) $5$
(D) $6$
(E) $7$

## Answer

**C.** $5$

## Solution

Measure amounts in $8$-ounce cups, so Angela's cup holds $c$ coffee and $m$ milk with $c + m = 1.$

Since Angela drank a sixth of the coffee, the total coffee is $6c$; since she drank a quarter of the milk, the total milk is $4m.$ The number of people equals the total number of cups,
$$
 \begin{aligned} 6c + 4m &= 6c + 4(1 - c) \\ &= 4 + 2c. \end{aligned}
$$

This is an integer only when $2c$ is an integer, and since $0 \lt c \lt 1$ this forces $c = \tfrac12,$ giving $4 + 1 = 5$ people.

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- Suppose Angela's own cup contains $c$ units of coffee and $m$ units of milk with $c + m = 1$
- Then the family total is $6c$ coffee and $4m$ milk, so the number of people is $6c + 4m$
