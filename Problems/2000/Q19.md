# AMC12 2000 Q19

![[assets/problems/2000/Q19.png]]

## Problem

In triangle $ABC,$ $AB = 13,$ $BC = 14,$ and $AC = 15.$ Let $D$ denote the midpoint of $\overline{BC}$ and let $E$ denote the intersection of $\overline{BC}$ with the bisector of angle $BAC.$ Which of the following is closest to the area of triangle $ADE?$

(A) $2$
(B) $2.5$
(C) $3$
(D) $3.5$
(E) $4$

## Answer

**C.** $3$

## Solution

By Heron's formula, the area of $\triangle ABC$ is $\sqrt{21 \cdot 8 \cdot 7 \cdot 6} = 84,$ so the altitude from $A$ to $BC$ is $\dfrac{2 \cdot 84}{14} = 12.$

The midpoint $D$ is $7$ from $B.$ The bisector from $A$ meets $BC$ at $E$ with $BE : EC = AB : AC = 13 : 15,$ so $BE = 14 \cdot \dfrac{13}{28} = 6.5.$

Both $D$ and $E$ lie on $BC,$ so $\triangle ADE$ has base $DE = 7 - 6.5 = 0.5$ and altitude $12,$ giving area
$$
 \tfrac12 \cdot 0.5 \cdot 12 = 3.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- By Heron's formula the area of $\triangle ABC$ is $84,$ so the altitude from $A$ to $BC$ is $12$
- $D$ is at distance $7$ from $B$; the bisector foot $E$ satisfies $BE : EC = 13 : 15$
