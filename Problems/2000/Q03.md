# AMC12 2000 Q03

![[assets/problems/2000/Q03.png]]

## Problem

Each day, Jenny ate $20\%$ of the jellybeans that were in her jar at the beginning of that day. At the end of the second day, $32$ remained. How many jellybeans were in the jar originally?

(A) $40$
(B) $50$
(C) $55$
(D) $60$
(E) $75$

## Answer

**B.** $50$

## Solution

Since $20\%$ is eaten each day, $80\%$ remains at the end of each day. If $x$ is the original number, then
$$
 (0.8)^2 x = 32.
$$

Solving gives $0.64x = 32,$ so $x = 50.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Key idea

- Eating $20\%$ leaves $80\%$ at the end of each day
- After two days, $(0.8)^2$ of the original amount remains
