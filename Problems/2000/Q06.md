# AMC12 2000 Q06

![[assets/problems/2000/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Two different prime numbers between $4$ and $18$ are chosen. When their sum is subtracted from their product, which of the following numbers could be obtained?

(A) $21$
(B) $60$
(C) $119$
(D) $180$
(E) $231$

## Answer

**C.** $119$

## Solution

The primes between $4$ and $18$ are $5, 7, 11, 13,$ and $17.$

For two such primes, $xy - (x + y)$ $= (x - 1)(y - 1) - 1$ is a product of two even numbers minus $1,$ hence odd. Among the choices, only $119$ is odd.

Indeed,
$$
 \begin{aligned} 11 \cdot 13 - (11 + 13) &= 143 - 24 \\ &= 119. \end{aligned}
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- The primes are $5, 7, 11, 13,$ and $17$; note that $xy - (x + y)$ $= (x - 1)(y - 1) - 1$
- Both primes are odd, so $(x-1)(y-1)-1$ is odd; only one option is odd
