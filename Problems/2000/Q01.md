# AMC12 2000 Q01

![[assets/problems/2000/Q01.png]]

## Problem

In the year 2001, the United States will host the International Mathematical Olympiad. Let $I,$ $M,$ and $O$ be distinct positive integers such that the product $I \cdot M \cdot O = 2001.$ What is the largest possible value of the sum $I + M + O?$

(A) $23$
(B) $55$
(C) $99$
(D) $111$
(E) $671$

## Answer

**E.** $671$

## Solution

Factoring gives $2001 = 3 \cdot 23 \cdot 29.$

To maximize the sum of three distinct positive integers with this product, take $I = 1,$ $M = 3,$ and $O = 23 \cdot 29 = 667.$

The largest sum is $1 + 3 + 667 = 671.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Key idea

- Factor $2001$ into primes
- To maximize the sum, make one factor as large as possible and the others small
