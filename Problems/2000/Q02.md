## Problem

What is $2000(2000^{2000})?$

(A) $2000^{2001}$
(B) $4000^{2000}$
(C) $2000^{4000}$
(D) $4{,}000{,}000^{2000}$
(E) $2000^{4{,}000{,}000}$

## Answer

**A.** $2000^{2001}$

## Solution

Writing $2000 = 2000^1,$ we get
$$
 \begin{gathered} 2000^1 \cdot 2000^{2000} \\ = 2000^{1 + 2000} \\ = 2000^{2001}. \end{gathered}
$$

All of the other options are larger than this.

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Key idea

- Write the leading factor as $2000^1$
- Multiplying powers of the same base adds the exponents
