# AMC12 2008-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2008-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

For each positive integer $n,$ the mean of the first $n$ terms of a sequence is $n.$ What is the $2008$th term of the sequence?

(A) $2008$
(B) $4015$
(C) $4016$
(D) $4{,}030{,}056$
(E) $4{,}032{,}064$

## Answer

**B.** $4015$

## Solution

Since the mean of the first $n$ terms is $n,$ their sum is $n \cdot n = n^2.$

The $n$th term is the difference of consecutive sums, $n^2 - (n-1)^2 = 2n - 1.$

For $n = 2008,$ the term is $2 \cdot 2008 - 1 = 4015.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Mean
- Summation

## Key idea

Convert the statement about means into an equation for consecutive partial sums.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of the required units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
