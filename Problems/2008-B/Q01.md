# AMC12 2008-B Q01

![[assets/problems/2008-B/Q01.png]]

## Problem

A basketball player made $5$ baskets during a game. Each basket was worth either $2$ or $3$ points. How many different numbers could represent the total points scored by the player?

(A) $2$
(B) $3$
(C) $4$
(D) $5$
(E) $6$

## Answer

**E.** $6$

## Solution

The total ranges from $5 \cdot 2 = 10$ (all two-pointers) to $5 \cdot 3 = 15$ (all three-pointers). Swapping one two-pointer for a three-pointer raises the total by exactly $1,$ so every integer in between occurs.

The possible totals are $10, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15,$ which is $6$ values.

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Counting integers in a range

## Key idea

Count the attainable totals by identifying the minimum, maximum, and step size.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of the required units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
