# AMC12 2008-B Q18

![[assets/problems/2008-B/Q18.png]]

## Problem

A pyramid has a square base $ABCD$ and vertex $E.$ The area of square $ABCD$ is $196,$ and the areas of $\triangle ABE$ and $\triangle CDE$ are $105$ and $91,$ respectively. What is the volume of the pyramid?

(A) $392$
(B) $196\sqrt{6}$
(C) $392\sqrt{2}$
(D) $392\sqrt{3}$
(E) $784$

## Answer

**E.** $784$

## Solution

The square has side $\sqrt{196} = 14.$ Let $F$ and $G$ be the feet of the perpendiculars from $E$ to $AB$ and $CD.$ Then $FG = 14,$ $EF = \tfrac{2 \cdot 105}{14} = 15,$ and $EG = \tfrac{2 \cdot 91}{14} = 13.$

Triangle $EFG$ lies in a plane perpendicular to the base, so its altitude to $FG$ is the pyramid's height. By Heron's formula with $s = 21,$ its area is $\sqrt{21 \cdot 6 \cdot 8 \cdot 7} = 84,$ so the altitude to $FG$ is $\tfrac{2 \cdot 84}{14} = 12.$

The volume is $\tfrac13 \cdot 196 \cdot 12 = 784.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.
