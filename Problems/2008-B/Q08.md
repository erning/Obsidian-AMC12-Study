# AMC12 2008-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2008-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

Points $B$ and $C$ lie on $\overline{AD}.$ The length of $\overline{AB}$ is $4$ times the length of $\overline{BD},$ and the length of $\overline{AC}$ is $9$ times the length of $\overline{CD}.$ The length of $\overline{BC}$ is what fraction of the length of $\overline{AD}?$

(A) $\dfrac{1}{36}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{13}$
(C) $\dfrac{1}{10}$
(D) $\dfrac{5}{36}$
(E) $\dfrac{1}{5}$

## Answer

**C.** $\dfrac{1}{10}$

## Solution

Since $AB = 4\,BD$ and $AB + BD = AD,$ we have $5\,BD = AD,$ so $BD = \tfrac{1}{5}AD.$

Likewise $AC = 9\,CD$ with $AC + CD = AD$ gives $CD = \tfrac{1}{10}AD.$

Because $B$ and $C$ both measure from $A,$ $\;BC = BD - CD$ $= \tfrac{1}{5}AD - \tfrac{1}{10}AD$ $= \tfrac{1}{10}AD.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Ratio and proportion

## Key idea

Express all lengths or counts in one consistent ratio and cancel the common scale.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of the required units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
