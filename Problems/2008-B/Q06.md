# AMC12 2008-B Q06

![[assets/problems/2008-B/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Postman Pete has a pedometer to count his steps. The pedometer records up to $99999$ steps, then flips over to $00000$ on the next step. Pete plans to determine his mileage for a year. On January $1$ Pete sets the pedometer to $00000.$ During the year, the pedometer flips from $99999$ to $00000$ forty-four times. On December $31$ the pedometer reads $50000.$ Pete takes $1800$ steps per mile. Which of the following is closest to the number of miles Pete walked during the year?

(A) $2500$
(B) $3000$
(C) $3500$
(D) $4000$
(E) $4500$

## Answer

**A.** $2500$

## Solution

Each flip counts $100000$ steps, so the year's steps total
$$
 \begin{aligned} &44 \cdot 100000 \\ &\quad {}+ 50000 = 4{,}450{,}000. \end{aligned}
$$

At $1800$ steps per mile, the mileage is $\dfrac{4{,}450{,}000}{1800} \approx 2472,$ which is closest to $2500.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Place value
- Estimation
- Unit conversion

## Key idea

Separate the place-value rollover from the final displayed reading before converting to miles.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of the required units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
