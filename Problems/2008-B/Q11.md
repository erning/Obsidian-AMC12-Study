# AMC12 2008-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2008-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

A cone-shaped mountain has its base on the ocean floor and has a height of $8000$ feet. The top $\tfrac{1}{8}$ of the volume of the mountain is above water. What is the depth of the ocean at the base of the mountain, in feet?

(A) $4000$
(B) $2000(4 - \sqrt{2})$
(C) $6000$
(D) $6400$
(E) $7000$

## Answer

**A.** $4000$

## Solution

The part above the water is a cone similar to the whole mountain, with volume $\tfrac{1}{8}$ of the total. Since volume scales as the cube of length, the above-water cone's height is $\sqrt[3]{\tfrac{1}{8}} = \tfrac{1}{2}$ of the full height.

So the above-water height is $8000 \cdot \tfrac{1}{2} = 4000$ feet.

The ocean depth at the base is the submerged height, $8000 - 4000 = 4000$ feet.

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Cone
- Similarity
- Power scaling of length, area, and volume

## Key idea

Use similarity: volume scales as the cube of the linear scale factor.
