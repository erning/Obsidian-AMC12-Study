# AMC12 2008-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2008-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

On circle $O,$ points $C$ and $D$ are on the same side of diameter $\overline{AB},$ $\angle AOC = 30^\circ,$ and $\angle DOB = 45^\circ.$ What is the ratio of the area of the smaller sector $COD$ to the area of the circle?

(A) $\dfrac{2}{9}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{4}$
(C) $\dfrac{5}{18}$
(D) $\dfrac{7}{24}$
(E) $\dfrac{3}{10}$

## Answer

**D.** $\dfrac{7}{24}$

## Solution

Since $\angle AOC,$ $\angle COD,$ and $\angle DOB$ fill the straight angle over diameter $\overline{AB},$
$$
 \begin{aligned} \angle COD &= 180^\circ - 30^\circ - 45^\circ \\ &= 105^\circ. \end{aligned}
$$

The sector's share of the circle is $\dfrac{105^\circ}{360^\circ} = \dfrac{7}{24}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Sector
- Angle sum

## Key idea

Translate the central angles into a fraction of a full circle.
