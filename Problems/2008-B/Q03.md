# AMC12 2008-B Q03

![[assets/problems/2008-B/Q03.png]]

## Problem

A semipro baseball league has teams with $21$ players each. League rules state that a player must be paid at least $\$15{,}000,$ and that the total of all players' salaries for each team cannot exceed $\$700{,}000.$ What is the maximum possible salary, in dollars, for a single player?

(A) $270{,}000$
(B) $385{,}000$
(C) $400{,}000$
(D) $430{,}000$
(E) $700{,}000$

## Answer

**C.** $400{,}000$

## Solution

One player earns the most when the other $20$ players each receive the minimum salary of $\$15{,}000.$

Thus the maximum salary is $\$700{,}000$ $- 20 \cdot \$15{,}000$ $= \$700{,}000$ $- \$300{,}000$ $= \$400{,}000.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Optimization
- Extremal argument

## Key idea

Push the constrained quantities to the boundary that maximizes the requested value.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of the required units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
