# AMC12 2008-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2008-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

Points $A$ and $B$ are on a circle of radius $5$ and $AB = 6.$ Point $C$ is the midpoint of the minor arc $AB.$ What is the length of the line segment $AC?$

(A) $\sqrt{10}$
(B) $\dfrac{7}{2}$
(C) $\sqrt{14}$
(D) $\sqrt{15}$
(E) $4$

## Answer

**A.** $\sqrt{10}$

## Solution

Let $O$ be the center and $D$ the point where $\overline{OC}$ meets $\overline{AB}.$ Since $C$ is the midpoint of arc $AB,$ $\overline{OC}$ is the perpendicular bisector of the chord, so $AD = 3.$

In right triangle $ADO,$ $OD = \sqrt{5^2 - 3^2} = 4,$ so $DC = OC - OD = 5 - 4 = 1.$

Then in right triangle $ADC,$ $AC = \sqrt{AD^2 + DC^2}$ $= \sqrt{3^2 + 1^2}$ $= \sqrt{10}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Chord
- Perpendicular bisector
- Pythagorean theorem

## Key idea

Use the perpendicular bisector of a chord to create a right triangle.
