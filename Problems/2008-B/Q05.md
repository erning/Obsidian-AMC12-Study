# AMC12 2008-B Q05

![[assets/problems/2008-B/Q05.png]]

## Problem

A class collects $\$50$ to buy flowers for a classmate who is in the hospital. Roses cost $\$3$ each, and carnations cost $\$2$ each. No other flowers are to be used. How many different bouquets could be purchased for exactly $\$50?$

(A) $1$
(B) $7$
(C) $9$
(D) $16$
(E) $17$

## Answer

**C.** $9$

## Solution

Let $r$ be the number of roses and $c$ the number of carnations, so $3r + 2c = 50$ with $r, c \ge 0.$

Because $2c$ and $50$ are even, $3r$ must be even, forcing $r$ to be even. The largest possible $r$ is $16$ (since $3 \cdot 17 \gt 50$), so $r \in \{0, 2, 4, \ldots, 16\}.$

That gives $9$ values of $r,$ each determining a bouquet.

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Diophantine equation
- Parity

## Key idea

Use the integer equation together with parity or divisibility restrictions.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of the required units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
