# AMC12 2004-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2004-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

If
$$
\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \cos^{2n} \theta = 5,
$$
 what is the value of $\cos 2\theta?$

(A) $\dfrac{1}{5}$
(B) $\dfrac{2}{5}$
(C) $\dfrac{\sqrt{5}}{5}$
(D) $\dfrac{3}{5}$
(E) $\dfrac{4}{5}$

## Answer

**D.** $\dfrac{3}{5}$

## Solution

The series is geometric with first term $1$ and ratio $\cos^2 \theta,$ so its sum is $\dfrac{1}{1 - \cos^2 \theta} = \dfrac{1}{\sin^2 \theta} = 5.$

Thus $\sin^2 \theta = \tfrac15,$ and
$$
 \begin{aligned} \cos 2\theta &= 1 - 2\sin^2 \theta \\ &= 1 - \tfrac25 = \tfrac35. \end{aligned}
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Geometric sequence
- Trigonometric identity

## Key idea

Use the common ratio and sum formula.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
