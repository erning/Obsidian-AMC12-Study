# AMC12 2004-A Q17

![[assets/problems/2004-A/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Let $f$ be a function with the following properties:

(i) $f(1) = 1,$ and

(ii) $f(2n) = n \cdot f(n)$ for any positive integer $n.$

What is the value of $f(2^{100})?$

(A) $1$
(B) $2^{99}$
(C) $2^{100}$
(D) $2^{4950}$
(E) $2^{9999}$

## Answer

**D.** $2^{4950}$

## Solution

Applying $f(2n) = n \cdot f(n)$ with $n = 2^{k},$ we get $f(2^{k+1}) = 2^{k} \cdot f(2^{k}).$

Unwinding from $f(2^1) = f(2) = 1 \cdot f(1) = 2^0,$ the exponents accumulate:
$$
 \begin{aligned} f(2^n) &= 2^{0 + 1 + 2 + \cdots + (n-1)} \\ &= 2^{n(n-1)/2}. \end{aligned}
$$

Therefore $f(2^{100}) = 2^{100 \cdot 99 / 2} = 2^{4950}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.
