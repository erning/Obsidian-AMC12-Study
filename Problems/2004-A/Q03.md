# AMC12 2004-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2004-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

For how many ordered pairs of positive integers $(x, y)$ is $x + 2y = 100?$

(A) $33$
(B) $49$
(C) $50$
(D) $99$
(E) $100$

## Answer

**B.** $49$

## Solution

Writing $x = 100 - 2y,$ the value of $x$ is a positive integer precisely when $y$ is a positive integer with $1 \le y \le 49.$

This gives $49$ valid ordered pairs.

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Diophantine equation
- Counting integers in a range

## Key idea

Use integer constraints together with divisibility and bounds.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
