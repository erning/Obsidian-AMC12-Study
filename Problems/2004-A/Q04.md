# AMC12 2004-A Q04

![[assets/problems/2004-A/Q04.png]]

## Problem

Bertha has $6$ daughters and no sons. Some of her daughters have $6$ daughters, and the rest have none. Bertha has a total of $30$ daughters and granddaughters, and no great-granddaughters. How many of Bertha's daughters and granddaughters have no daughters?

(A) $22$
(B) $23$
(C) $24$
(D) $25$
(E) $26$

## Answer

**E.** $26$

## Solution

Bertha has $30 - 6 = 24$ granddaughters, none of whom have daughters.

These granddaughters are the children of $24 / 6 = 4$ of Bertha's daughters, so exactly $4$ women have daughters.

Therefore the number of women with no daughters is $30 - 4 = 26.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Basic counting

## Key idea

Count the possibilities directly, keeping cases disjoint.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
