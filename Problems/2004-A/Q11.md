# AMC12 2004-A Q11

![[assets/problems/2004-A/Q11.png]]

## Problem

The average value of all the pennies, nickels, dimes, and quarters in Paula's purse is $20$ cents. If she had one more quarter, the average value would be $21$ cents. How many dimes does she have in her purse?

(A) $0$
(B) $1$
(C) $2$
(D) $3$
(E) $4$

## Answer

**A.** $0$

## Solution

If Paula has $n$ coins, their total value is $20n$ cents. Adding a quarter gives $n + 1$ coins worth $20n + 25$ cents, which must also equal $21(n + 1)$ cents.

So $20n + 25 = 21(n + 1),$ giving $n = 4.$

Four coins totalling $80$ cents must be three quarters and one nickel, so the number of dimes is $0.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Mean
- Linear equation

## Key idea

Translate the mean into a total sum.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
