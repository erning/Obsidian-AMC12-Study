# AMC12 2004-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2004-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Let $U = 2 \cdot 2004^{2005},$ $V = 2004^{2005},$ $W = 2003 \cdot 2004^{2004},$ $X = 2 \cdot 2004^{2004},$ $Y = 2004^{2004}$ and $Z = 2004^{2003}.$ Which of the following is largest?

(A) $U - V$
(B) $V - W$
(C) $W - X$
(D) $X - Y$
(E) $Y - Z$

## Answer

**A.** $U - V$

## Solution

Compute each difference by factoring:

$U - V = 2004^{2005},$ $V - W = 2004^{2004},$ $W - X = 2001 \cdot 2004^{2004},$ $X - Y = 2004^{2004},$ and $Y - Z = 2003 \cdot 2004^{2003}.$

Since $2004^{2005} = 2004 \cdot 2004^{2004}$ exceeds each of the others, none of which reaches $2004^{2005},$ the difference $U - V$ is the largest.

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.
