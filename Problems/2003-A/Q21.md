# AMC12 2003-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2003-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

The graph of the polynomial
$$
 \begin{aligned} &P(x) = x^5 + ax^4 + bx^3 \\ &\quad {}+ cx^2 + dx + e \end{aligned}
$$

has five distinct $x$-intercepts, one of which is at $(0, 0).$ Which of the following coefficients cannot be zero?

(A) $a$
(B) $b$
(C) $c$
(D) $d$
(E) $e$

## Answer

**D.** $d$

## Solution

Since $(0,0)$ is an intercept, $P(0)=e=0,$ so $P(x)$ $=x\left(x^4+ax^3+bx^2+cx+d\right).$

The four remaining intercepts are nonzero and distinct, and $d$ equals their product, which is therefore nonzero.

Any of $a,b,c$ can be zero for suitable choices of those roots, but $d\neq0.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- $P(0)=0$ forces $e=0$
- Factor out $x;$ the constant term of the remaining quartic is the product of the four nonzero roots
