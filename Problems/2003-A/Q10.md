# AMC12 2003-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2003-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

Al, Bert, and Carl are the winners of a school drawing for a pile of Halloween candy, which they are to divide in a ratio of $3 : 2 : 1,$ respectively. Due to some confusion they come at different times to claim their prizes, and each assumes he is the first to arrive. If each takes what he believes to be his correct share of candy, what fraction of the candy goes unclaimed?

(A) $\dfrac{1}{18}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{6}$
(C) $\dfrac{2}{9}$
(D) $\dfrac{5}{18}$
(E) $\dfrac{5}{12}$

## Answer

**D.** $\dfrac{5}{18}$

## Solution

The shares are $\dfrac12,\dfrac13,\dfrac16$ of the pile.

Each person assumes he is first, so Al leaves $\dfrac12,$ Bert leaves $\dfrac23,$ and Carl leaves $\dfrac56$ of the candy present when he arrives.

The unclaimed fraction is $\dfrac12\cdot\dfrac23\cdot\dfrac56=\dfrac{5}{18},$ regardless of the order.

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- The three shares are $\dfrac12,\dfrac13,\dfrac16$ of the whole
- Each person leaves a fixed fraction of whatever candy is present, so the order does not matter
