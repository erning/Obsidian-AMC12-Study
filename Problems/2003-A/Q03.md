# AMC12 2003-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2003-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

A solid box is $15$ cm by $10$ cm by $8$ cm. A new solid is formed by removing a cube $3$ cm on a side from each corner of this box. What percent of the original volume is removed?

(A) $4.5$
(B) $9$
(C) $12$
(D) $18$
(E) $24$

## Answer

**D.** $18$

## Solution

The original volume is $15 \cdot 10 \cdot 8 = 1200.$

Eight corner cubes are removed, each of volume $3^3 = 27,$ totaling $8 \cdot 27 = 216.$

The fraction removed is $\dfrac{216}{1200} = 0.18,$ which is $18\%.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- The box has $8$ corners
- Each removed cube has volume $3^3$
