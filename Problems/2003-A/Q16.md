# AMC12 2003-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2003-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

A point $P$ is chosen at random in the interior of equilateral triangle $ABC.$ What is the probability that $\triangle ABP$ has a greater area than each of $\triangle ACP$ and $\triangle BCP?$

(A) $\dfrac{1}{6}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{4}$
(C) $\dfrac{1}{3}$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{2}$
(E) $\dfrac{2}{3}$

## Answer

**C.** $\dfrac{1}{3}$

## Solution

The triangles $\triangle ABP,$ $\triangle ACP,$ and $\triangle BCP$ have equal bases (the sides of the equilateral triangle), so their areas are proportional to the distances from $P$ to those sides.

By the threefold symmetry of the equilateral triangle, $\triangle ABP$ is the largest with the same probability as each of the other two, so that probability is $\dfrac13.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- The three triangles share equal bases, so their areas depend only on $P$'s distances to the sides
- By the threefold symmetry each side yields the largest triangle equally often
