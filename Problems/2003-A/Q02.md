# AMC12 2003-A Q02

![[assets/problems/2003-A/Q02.png]]

## Problem

Members of the Rockham Soccer League buy socks and T-shirts. Socks cost $\$4$ per pair and each T-shirt costs $\$5$ more than a pair of socks. Each member needs one pair of socks and a shirt for home games and another pair of socks and a shirt for away games. If the total cost is $\$2366,$ how many members are in the League?

(A) $77$
(B) $91$
(C) $143$
(D) $182$
(E) $286$

## Answer

**B.** $91$

## Solution

A T-shirt costs $4+5=9$ dollars.

Each member needs two pairs of socks and two shirts, costing $2(4)+2(9)=26$ dollars.

The number of members is $2366 \div 26 = 91.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Key idea

- A T-shirt costs $\$4+\$5=\$9$
- Each member buys two pairs of socks and two shirts
