# AMC12 2003-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2003-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Points $K,$ $L,$ $M,$ and $N$ lie in the plane of the square $ABCD$ so that $AKB,$ $BLC,$ $CMD,$ and $DNA$ are equilateral triangles. If $ABCD$ has an area of $16,$ find the area of $KLMN.$

(A) $32$
(B) $16 + 16\sqrt{3}$
(C) $48$
(D) $32 + 16\sqrt{3}$
(E) $64$

## Answer

**D.** $32 + 16\sqrt{3}$

## Solution

The square $ABCD$ has side $4.$ By the $90^\circ$ rotational symmetry, $KLMN$ is also a square.

Each equilateral triangle on a side of length $4$ has height $2\sqrt3,$ so the diagonal $KM=4+2(2\sqrt3)=4+4\sqrt3.$

A square with diagonal $d$ has area $\dfrac12 d^2,$ so $[KLMN]=\dfrac12(4+4\sqrt3)^2$ $=32+16\sqrt3.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- $KLMN$ is a square by the rotational symmetry of the figure
- Its diagonal $KM$ equals the square's side plus twice the triangle height $2\sqrt3$
