# AMC12 2003-A Q18

![[assets/problems/2003-A/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Let $n$ be a $5$-digit number, and let $q$ and $r$ be the quotient and remainder, respectively, when $n$ is divided by $100.$ For how many values of $n$ is $q + r$ divisible by $11?$

(A) $8180$
(B) $8181$
(C) $8182$
(D) $9000$
(E) $9090$

## Answer

**B.** $8181$

## Solution

Since $n=100q+r=(q+r)+99q$ and $99$ is divisible by $11,$ we have $q+r\equiv n\pmod{11}.$

So $11\mid(q+r)$ exactly when $11\mid n.$

Among the $5$-digit numbers, the count of multiples of $11$ is $\left\lfloor\dfrac{99999}{11}\right\rfloor$ $-\left\lfloor\dfrac{9999}{11}\right\rfloor$ $=9090-909=8181.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Key idea

- $n=100q+r=(q+r)+99q$
- Since $99$ is a multiple of $11,$ $q+r\equiv n\pmod{11}$
