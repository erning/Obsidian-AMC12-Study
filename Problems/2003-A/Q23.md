# AMC12 2003-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2003-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

How many perfect squares are divisors of the product $1! \cdot 2! \cdot 3! \cdots 9!\,?$

(A) $504$
(B) $672$
(C) $864$
(D) $936$
(E) $1008$

## Answer

**B.** $672$

## Solution

The product is
$$
1! \cdot 2! \cdots 9! = 2^{30}\cdot3^{13}\cdot5^{5}\cdot7^{3}.
$$

A perfect-square divisor has the form $2^{2a}3^{2b}5^{2c}7^{2d}$ with $0\le a\le15,$ $0\le b\le6,$ $0\le c\le2,$ and $0\le d\le1.$

The number of choices is $16\cdot7\cdot3\cdot2=672.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Key idea

- Combine the factorials into $2^{30}\cdot3^{13}\cdot5^{5}\cdot7^{3}$
- A square divisor uses an even exponent of each prime
