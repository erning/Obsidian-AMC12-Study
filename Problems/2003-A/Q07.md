# AMC12 2003-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2003-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

How many non-congruent triangles with perimeter $7$ have integer side lengths?

(A) $1$
(B) $2$
(C) $3$
(D) $4$
(E) $5$

## Answer

**B.** $2$

## Solution

Let the sides be $a\le b\le c$ with $a+b+c=7.$ The triangle inequality requires $c\lt a+b,$ so $c\lt3.5,$ forcing $c=3.$

Then $a+b=4$ with $a\le b\le3,$ giving the triangles $1\text{-}3\text{-}3$ and $2\text{-}2\text{-}3.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Key idea

- The longest side must be less than half the perimeter
- The longest side is at most $3$
