# AMC12 2003-A Q15

![[assets/problems/2003-A/Q15.png]]

## Problem

A semicircle of diameter $1$ sits at the top of a semicircle of diameter $2,$ as shown. The shaded area inside the smaller semicircle and outside the larger semicircle is called a lune. Determine the area of this lune.

(A) $\dfrac{1}{6}\pi - \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4}$
(B) $\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4} - \dfrac{1}{12}\pi$
(C) $\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4} - \dfrac{1}{24}\pi$
(D) $\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4} + \dfrac{1}{24}\pi$
(E) $\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4} + \dfrac{1}{12}\pi$

## Answer

**C.** $\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{4} - \dfrac{1}{24}\pi$

## Solution

The small semicircle's diameter is a chord of length $1$ in the large circle of radius $1,$ so it subtends a $60^\circ$ angle at the center.

The region bounded by that chord and the small arc is an equilateral triangle of area $\dfrac{\sqrt3}{4}$ topped by the small semicircle of area $\dfrac12\pi\left(\dfrac12\right)^2=\dfrac{\pi}{8}.$

Subtracting the $60^\circ$ sector of the large circle, $\dfrac16\pi(1)^2=\dfrac{\pi}{6},$ gives the lune area
$$
\dfrac{\sqrt3}{4}+\dfrac{\pi}{8}-\dfrac{\pi}{6}=\dfrac{\sqrt3}{4}-\dfrac{\pi}{24}.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- The small diameter is a chord of length $1$ in the large radius-$1$ circle, spanning a $60^\circ$ arc
- Lune $=$ (equilateral triangle $+$ small semicircle) $-$ the $60^\circ$ sector of the large circle
