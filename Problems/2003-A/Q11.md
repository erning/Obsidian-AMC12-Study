# AMC12 2003-A Q11

![[assets/problems/2003-A/Q11.png]]

## Problem

A square and an equilateral triangle have the same perimeter. Let $A$ be the area of the circle circumscribed about the square and $B$ be the area of the circle circumscribed about the triangle. Find $A/B.$

(A) $\dfrac{9}{16}$
(B) $\dfrac{3}{4}$
(C) $\dfrac{27}{32}$
(D) $\dfrac{3\sqrt{6}}{8}$
(E) $1$

## Answer

**C.** $\dfrac{27}{32}$

## Solution

Let the common perimeter be $12,$ so the square has side $3$ and the triangle has side $4.$

The square's circumradius is $\dfrac{3\sqrt2}{2},$ so $A=\pi\left(\dfrac{3\sqrt2}{2}\right)^2=\dfrac{9\pi}{2}.$

The triangle's circumradius is $\dfrac{4}{\sqrt3},$ so $B=\pi\left(\dfrac{4}{\sqrt3}\right)^2=\dfrac{16\pi}{3}.$

Then $\dfrac{A}{B}=\dfrac{9/2}{16/3}=\dfrac{27}{32}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- Choose a convenient common perimeter, such as $12$
- A square's circumradius is half its diagonal; an equilateral triangle's is $\dfrac{\text{side}}{\sqrt3}$
