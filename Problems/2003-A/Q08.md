# AMC12 2003-A Q08

![[assets/problems/2003-A/Q08.png]]

## Problem

What is the probability that a randomly drawn positive factor of $60$ is less than $7?$

(A) $\dfrac{1}{10}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{6}$
(C) $\dfrac{1}{4}$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{3}$
(E) $\dfrac{1}{2}$

## Answer

**E.** $\dfrac{1}{2}$

## Solution

The number $60$ has $12$ positive factors: $1,2,3,4,5,6,$ $10,12,15,20,30,60.$

Six of them are less than $7,$ namely $1,2,3,4,5,6,$ so the probability is $\dfrac{6}{12}=\dfrac12.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Key idea

- $60=2^2\cdot3\cdot5$ has $(2{+}1)(1{+}1)(1{+}1)=12$ factors
- Count the factors from $1$ through $6$
