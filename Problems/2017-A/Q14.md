# AMC12 2017-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2017-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Alice refuses to sit next to Bob or Carla, and Derek refuses to sit next to Eric. How many arrangements of the five people in a row satisfy these conditions?

## Answer

**C.** $28$

## Solution

Let $X,Y,Z$ be the forbidden adjacency events $AB,AC,DE$. Each has $2\cdot4!=48$ arrangements. The intersections have sizes

$$|X\cap Y|=2\cdot3!=12,\qquad |X\cap Z|=|Y\cap Z|=2\cdot2\cdot3!=24,$$

and $|X\cap Y\cap Z|=2\cdot2\cdot2!=8$. Inclusion-exclusion gives forbidden arrangements

$$144-(12+24+24)+8=92.$$

Therefore the valid count is $5!-92=28$.

## Knowledge points

- Inclusion-exclusion
- Block arrangements

## Key idea

Treat each forbidden adjacent pair as a block and use inclusion-exclusion.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting the two orientations of a block.
