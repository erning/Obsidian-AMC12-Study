# AMC12 2017-A Q04

![[assets/problems/2017-A/Q04.png]]

## Problem

Jerry walks along two sides of a square field while Silvia walks along its diagonal. How much shorter is Silvia’s trip, as a percentage of Jerry’s trip?

## Answer

**A.** About $30\%$

## Solution

If the side is $s$, Jerry travels $2s$ while Silvia travels $s\sqrt2$. The fraction saved is

$$\frac{2s-s\sqrt2}{2s}=1-\frac{\sqrt2}{2}\approx0.293.$$

This is closest to 30%.

## Knowledge points

- Pythagorean theorem
- Percentage decrease

## Key idea

Compare the two lengths relative to Jerry’s length.

## Common mistakes

- Dividing by Silvia’s distance instead of Jerry’s.
