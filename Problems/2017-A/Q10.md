# AMC12 2017-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2017-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

Chloé chooses uniformly from $[0,2017]$, while Laurent independently chooses uniformly from $[0,4034]$. What is the probability Laurent’s number is greater?

## Answer

**C.** $\frac34$

## Solution

Laurent lands in $[2017,4034]$ with probability $1/2$, and then certainly exceeds Chloé. In the lower half $[0,2017]$, the two choices are identically distributed, so Laurent is larger with probability $1/2$. Hence

$$\frac12\cdot1+\frac12\cdot\frac12=\frac34.$$

## Knowledge points

- Continuous probability
- Conditioning

## Key idea

Split Laurent’s interval at the endpoint of Chloé’s interval.

## Common mistakes

- Treating both intervals as equal from the start.
