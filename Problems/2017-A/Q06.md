# AMC12 2017-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2017-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Rods have every integer length from 1 to 30 cm. Rods of lengths 3, 7, and 15 are already chosen. How many remaining rods can form a positive-area quadrilateral with them?

## Answer

**B.** $17$

## Solution

Four lengths form a nondegenerate quadrilateral exactly when the longest is less than the sum of the other three. If the fourth length is $n$, this requires

$$15<3+7+n,\qquad n<3+7+15,$$

so $5<n<25$. There are 19 integers from 6 through 24, but 7 and 15 are already used. Thus $19-2=17$ choices remain.

## Knowledge points

- Polygon inequality
- Integer intervals

## Key idea

Apply the generalized triangle inequality to the four side lengths.

## Common mistakes

- Including the degenerate endpoints 5 and 25.
