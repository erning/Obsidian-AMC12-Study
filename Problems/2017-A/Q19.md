# AMC12 2017-A Q19

![[assets/problems/2017-A/Q19.png]]

## Problem

A square of side $x$ is inscribed at the right-angle vertex of a $3$-$4$-$5$ triangle. A square of side $y$ has one side on the hypotenuse of another such triangle. Find $x/y$.

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{37}{35}$

## Solution

For the first square, similar triangles give

$$\frac{x}{3-x}=\frac43,$$

so $x=12/7$. For the second square, use the hypotenuse as base $b=5$ and altitude $h=3\cdot4/5=12/5$. A square resting on a base with altitude $h$ has side

$$y=\frac{bh}{b+h}=\frac{5(12/5)}{5+12/5}=\frac{60}{37}.$$

Therefore

$$\frac xy=\frac{12}{7}\cdot\frac{37}{60}=\frac{37}{35}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Similar triangles
- Inscribed squares

## Key idea

Use the general base-height relation for a square fitted against a triangle.

## Common mistakes

- Using 3 or 4 as the hypotenuse altitude.
