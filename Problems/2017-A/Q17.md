# AMC12 2017-A Q17

![[assets/problems/2017-A/Q17.png]]

## Problem

There are 24 complex numbers satisfying $z^{24}=1$. For how many is $z^6$ real?

## Answer

**D.** $12$

## Solution

The roots are $z=e^{\pi ik/12}$ for $k=0,1,\ldots,23$. Then

$$z^6=e^{\pi ik/2}.$$

This is real exactly when $k$ is even, and there are 12 even values in the range.

## Knowledge points

- Roots of unity
- Complex arguments

## Key idea

Raise the polar form to the sixth power and test when the argument is a multiple of $\pi$.

## Common mistakes

- Counting only the real roots of $z^{24}=1$.
