# AMC12 2017-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2017-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

For real $a,b,c$, the polynomial

$$g(x)=x^3+ax^2+x+10$$

has three distinct roots, all also roots of

$$f(x)=x^4+x^3+bx^2+100x+c.$$

What is $f(1)$?

## Answer

**C.** $-7007$

## Solution

Since the quartic contains the three distinct roots of $g$, write $f(x)=(x-q)g(x)$. Expanding gives the $x$ coefficient $10-q$, which must equal 100, so $q=-90$. The $x^3$ coefficient is $a-q=1$, so $a=-89$. Therefore

$$g(1)=1-89+1+10=-77,$$

and

$$f(1)=(1-q)g(1)=91(-77)=-7007.$$

## Knowledge points

- Polynomial factorization
- Coefficient comparison

## Key idea

Factor the quartic by the missing fourth root.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming $f=g$ because the roots overlap.
