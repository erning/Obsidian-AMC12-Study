# AMC12 2017-A Q22

![[assets/problems/2017-A/Q22.png]]

## Problem

A particle starts at the origin and each second moves uniformly to one of the eight neighboring lattice points. It eventually first hits the square with vertices $(\pm2,\pm2)$. What is the probability it hits a corner rather than an interior side point?

## Answer

**E.** $\frac{4}{35}$

## Solution

By symmetry, classify interior starting points as the center type $C$, axis type $A$, and diagonal type $I$. Let their corner-hitting probabilities be $c,a,i$. Reading the eight possible moves gives

$$a=\frac28a+\frac18c+\frac28i,$$

$$c=\frac48a+\frac48i,$$

$$i=\frac28a+\frac18c+\frac18.$$

Solving yields $c=4/35$. Since the particle starts at the center, the required probability is $4/35$.

## Knowledge points

- Symmetry reduction
- Systems of probability equations

## Key idea

Replace many lattice points by three symmetry classes.

## Common mistakes

- Counting only paths that hit a corner immediately.
