# AMC12 2017-A Q18

![[assets/problems/2017-A/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Let $S(n)$ be the digit sum. If $S(n)=1274$, which value could be $S(n+1)$?

## Answer

**D.** $1239$

## Solution

If $n$ ends in exactly $k$ nines, adding 1 increases the first preceding digit by 1 and changes each trailing 9 to 0. Therefore

$$S(n+1)=1274+1-9k=1275-9k.$$

For $1239$, we have $1275-1239=36=9\cdot4$, so it is possible for $n$ to end in four nines. The other choices do not have the required congruence.

## Knowledge points

- Decimal digit sums
- Carrying in base 10

## Key idea

Each trailing 9 reduces the digit sum by 9 after the carry.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming adding 1 always increases the digit sum by 1.
