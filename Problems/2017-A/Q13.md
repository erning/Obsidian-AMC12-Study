# AMC12 2017-A Q13

![[assets/problems/2017-A/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Sharon usually drives the trip in 180 minutes. After one third of the distance, she reduces her speed by 20 mph, making the total time 276 minutes. What is the distance?

## Answer

**B.** $135$ miles

## Solution

Let distance be $d$ and usual speed $r$. Since 180 minutes is 3 hours, $d=3r$. The first third takes 60 minutes, leaving 216 minutes, or $18/5$ hours, for the remaining $2d/3=2r$ miles at speed $r-20$. Thus

$$2r=(r-20)\frac{18}{5}.$$

Solving gives $r=45$, so $d=3r=135$.

## Knowledge points

- Rates and time
- Fractional distance

## Key idea

Express each segment’s distance and time in terms of the usual speed.

## Common mistakes

- Subtracting 20 from the total distance instead of the speed.
