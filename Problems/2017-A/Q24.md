# AMC12 2017-A Q24

![[assets/problems/2017-A/Q24.png]]

## Problem

In cyclic quadrilateral $ABCD$, the side lengths are $AB=3$, $BC=2$, $CD=6$, and $DA=8$. Points $X,Y$ lie on $BD$ with $DX/BD=1/4$ and $BY/BD=11/36$. Construct $E,F,G$ as shown. Find $XF\cdot XG$.

## Answer

**A.** $17$

## Solution

The parallel lines give similar triangles, yielding

$$\frac{XF}{XD}=\frac{XC}{XY}.$$

Power of a point at $X$ gives

$$XC\cdot XG=XD\cdot XB.$$

Combining these relations gives $XF\cdot XG=XB\cdot XY$. If $BD=d$, then

$$XB=\frac34d,\qquad XY=d-\frac14d-\frac{11}{36}d=\frac49d,$$

so the product is $d^2/3$. The cyclic condition and the Law of Cosines in the two triangles give $d^2=51$. Therefore

$$XF\cdot XG=\frac{51}{3}=17.$$

## Knowledge points

- Similar triangles
- Power of a point
- Cyclic quadrilaterals

## Key idea

Turn the constructed-length product into a chord product at $X$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $DX$ or $BY$ as a full fraction of the wrong orientation.
