# AMC12 2017-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2017-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

Two semicircles of radii 2 and 1 lie inside and are tangent to a larger semicircle. A circle centered at $P$ is tangent to both small semicircles and internally tangent to the large one. What is its radius?

## Answer

**B.** $\frac67$

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2017-A/Q16-draft.svg]]

Place the diameter on the $x$-axis, with small-circle centers at 2 and 5 and large-circle center at 3. Let the desired radius be $r$ and let $P$ have horizontal coordinate $3+x$ and height $h$. Tangency gives

$$h^2=(2+r)^2-(1+x)^2=(1+r)^2-(2-x)^2=(3-r)^2-x^2.$$

Subtracting these equations produces two linear equations in $r,x$, whose solution is $r=6/7$.

## Knowledge points

- Circle tangency
- Coordinate geometry

## Key idea

Use equal-distance tangency equations and eliminate the height.

## Common mistakes

- Using external tangency to the large semicircle instead of internal tangency.
