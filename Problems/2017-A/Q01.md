# AMC12 2017-A Q01

![[assets/problems/2017-A/Q01.png]]

## Problem

Single popsicles cost $1$, boxes of 3 cost $2$, and boxes of 5 cost $3$. What is the greatest number of popsicles Pablo can buy with $8$?

## Answer

**D.** $13$

## Solution

Five-popsicle boxes are cheapest per popsicle. Even at that rate, 14 popsicles would cost $14(3/5)=8.4$, so at most 13 are possible. Two 5-boxes and one 3-box cost $6+2=8$ and contain $13$ popsicles, so the bound is attained.

## Knowledge points

- Integer optimization
- Unit price comparison

## Key idea

Find an upper bound from the cheapest possible unit price, then construct it.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming only 5-boxes can be used.
