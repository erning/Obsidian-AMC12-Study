# AMC12 2001 Q02

![[assets/problems/2001/Q02.png]]

## Problem

Let $P(n)$ and $S(n)$ denote the product and the sum, respectively, of the digits of the integer $n.$ For example, $P(23) = 6$ and $S(23) = 5.$ Suppose $N$ is a two-digit number such that $N = P(N) + S(N).$ What is the units digit of $N?$

(A) $2$
(B) $3$
(C) $6$
(D) $8$
(E) $9$

## Answer

**E.** $9$

## Solution

Write $N = 10a + b.$ Then $P(N) = ab$ and $S(N) = a + b,$ so
$$
 10a + b = ab + a + b.
$$
 This reduces to $9a = ab.$ Since $a \neq 0,$ we can divide by $a$ to get $b = 9.$

The units digit of $N$ is $9.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Key idea

- Write $N = 10a + b$ where $a$ is the tens digit and $b$ is the units digit
- The equation $10a + b = ab + a + b$ simplifies to $9a = ab$
