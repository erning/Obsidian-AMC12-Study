# AMC12 2001 Q05

![[assets/problems/2001/Q05.png]]

## Problem

What is the product of all positive odd integers less than $10{,}000?$

(A) $\dfrac{10000!}{(5000!)^2}$
(B) $\dfrac{10000!}{2^{5000}}$
(C) $\dfrac{9999!}{2^{5000}}$
(D) $\dfrac{10000!}{2^{5000} \cdot 5000!}$
(E) $\dfrac{5000!}{2^{5000}}$

## Answer

**D.** $\dfrac{10000!}{2^{5000} \cdot 5000!}$

## Solution

The product of every integer from $1$ to $10000$ is $10000!,$ so the product of the odd ones is $10000!$ divided by the product of the even ones.

The even numbers factor as
$$
 \begin{gathered} 2 \cdot 4 \cdots 10000 \\ = 2^{5000}(1 \cdot 2 \cdots 5000) \\ = 2^{5000} \cdot 5000!. \end{gathered}
$$

Therefore the product of the odd integers is
$$
 \dfrac{10000!}{2^{5000} \cdot 5000!}.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- The product of all odd numbers equals $10000!$ divided by the product of all even numbers up to $10000$
- The even numbers are $2 \cdot 4 \cdots 10000$ $= 2^{5000}(1 \cdot 2 \cdots 5000)$
