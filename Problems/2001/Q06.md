# AMC12 2001 Q06

![[assets/problems/2001/Q06.png]]

## Problem

A telephone number has the form $ABC - DEF - GHIJ,$ where each letter represents a different digit. The digits in each part of the number are in decreasing order; that is, $A \gt B \gt C,$ $D \gt E \gt F,$ and $G \gt H \gt I \gt J.$ Furthermore, $D,$ $E,$ and $F$ are consecutive even digits; $G,$ $H,$ $I,$ and $J$ are consecutive odd digits; and $A + B + C = 9.$ Find $A.$

(A) $4$
(B) $5$
(C) $6$
(D) $7$
(E) $8$

## Answer

**E.** $8$

## Solution

The four consecutive decreasing odd digits $GHIJ$ are either $9753$ or $7531,$ leaving one odd digit ($1$ or $9$) for $ABC.$

Since $A + B + C = 9$ and the other two digits of $ABC$ are even, the odd digit must be $1$ (a $9$ would force the two even digits to sum to $0$). So the two even digits sum to $8.$

The three consecutive decreasing even digits $DEF$ are $864,$ $642,$ or $420,$ leaving the even pairs $\{2, 0\},$ $\{8, 0\},$ or $\{8, 6\}$ for $ABC.$ Only $\{8, 0\}$ sums to $8,$ so $ABC = 810$ and $A = 8.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Key idea

- Four consecutive decreasing odd digits must be $9753$ or $7531$
- The leftover odd digit sits in $ABC,$ and with two even digits summing to $9$ it must be $1$
