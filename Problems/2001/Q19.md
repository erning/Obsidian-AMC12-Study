# AMC12 2001 Q19

![[assets/problems/2001/Q19.png]]

## Problem

The polynomial $P(x) = x^3 + ax^2 + bx + c$ has the property that the mean of its zeros, the product of its zeros, and the sum of its coefficients are all equal. If the $y$-intercept of the graph of $y = P(x)$ is $2,$ what is $b?$

(A) $-11$
(B) $-10$
(C) $-9$
(D) $1$
(E) $5$

## Answer

**A.** $-11$

## Solution

The $y$-intercept is $P(0) = c = 2.$ By Vieta's formulas the product of the zeros is $-c = -2,$ the mean of the zeros is $-\dfrac{a}{3},$ and the sum of the coefficients is $P(1) = 1 + a + b + c.$

All three are equal to $-2.$ From $-\dfrac{a}{3} = -2$ we get $a = 6.$

Then $1 + a + b + c = -2$ becomes $1 + 6 + b + 2 = -2,$ so $b = -11.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Key idea

- By Vieta, the sum of the zeros is $-a$ and the product is $-c;$ the sum of coefficients is $P(1)$
- The $y$-intercept gives $c = 2,$ so the common value is the product of the zeros
