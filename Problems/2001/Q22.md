# AMC12 2001 Q22

![[assets/problems/2001/Q22.png]]

## Problem

In rectangle $ABCD,$ points $F$ and $G$ lie on $\overline{AB}$ so that $AF = FG = GB$ and $E$ is the midpoint of $\overline{DC}.$ Also, $\overline{AC}$ intersects $\overline{EF}$ at $H$ and $\overline{EG}$ at $J.$ The area of rectangle $ABCD$ is $70.$ Find the area of triangle $EHJ.$

(A) $\dfrac{5}{2}$
(B) $\dfrac{35}{12}$
(C) $3$
(D) $\dfrac{7}{2}$
(E) $\dfrac{35}{8}$

## Answer

**C.** $3$

## Solution

Triangle $EFG$ has base $FG = \dfrac{1}{3}AB$ and height equal to the rectangle's height, so its area is $\dfrac{1}{6}(70) = \dfrac{35}{3}.$

Because $EC \parallel AF,$ triangles $AFH$ and $CEH$ are similar with ratio $\dfrac{EC}{AF} = \dfrac{3}{2},$ so $\dfrac{EH}{EF} = \dfrac{3}{5}.$ Likewise $\dfrac{EJ}{EG} = \dfrac{3}{7}.$

Then $\dfrac{[EHJ]}{[EFG]}$ $= \dfrac{EH}{EF}\cdot\dfrac{EJ}{EG}$ $= \dfrac{3}{5}\cdot\dfrac{3}{7}$ $= \dfrac{9}{35},$ giving
$$
 [EHJ] = \dfrac{9}{35}\cdot\dfrac{35}{3} = 3.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- Triangle $EFG$ has base $\dfrac{1}{3}AB$ and the full height, so its area is $\dfrac{1}{6}$ of the rectangle
- Use $EC \parallel AB$ to get $\dfrac{EH}{EF} = \dfrac{3}{5}$ and $\dfrac{EJ}{EG} = \dfrac{3}{7}$ from similar triangles
