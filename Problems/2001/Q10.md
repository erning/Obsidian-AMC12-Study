# AMC12 2001 Q10

![[assets/problems/2001/Q10.png]]

## Problem

The plane is tiled by congruent squares and congruent pentagons as indicated. The percent of the plane that is enclosed by the pentagons is closest to

(A) $50$
(B) $52$
(C) $54$
(D) $56$
(E) $58$

## Answer

**D.** $56$

## Solution

The pattern repeats over a $3 \times 3$ block of nine unit squares. Four of these nine squares are not covered by pentagons; the rest of the area belongs to the pentagons.

So the pentagons enclose
$$
 1 - \dfrac{4}{9} = \dfrac{5}{9} = 55.5\overline{5}\%,
$$
 which is closest to $56.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- The pattern repeats in blocks of $3 \times 3$ unit squares
- In each block, the four squares that stay square make up $\dfrac{4}{9}$ of the area
