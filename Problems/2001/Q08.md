# AMC12 2001 Q08

![[assets/problems/2001/Q08.png]]

## Problem

Which of the cones below can be formed from a $252^\circ$ sector of a circle of radius $10$ by aligning the two straight sides?

(A) ![[assets/problems/2001/Q08-A.svg]]
(B) ![[assets/problems/2001/Q08-B.svg]]
(C) ![[assets/problems/2001/Q08-C.svg]]
(D) ![[assets/problems/2001/Q08-D.svg]]
(E) ![[assets/problems/2001/Q08-E.svg]]

## Answer

**C.**

## Solution

When the sector is rolled into a cone, its radius $10$ becomes the slant height, and its arc becomes the base circle.

The arc length is
$$
 \dfrac{252}{360}\cdot 2\pi(10) = \dfrac{7}{10}\cdot 20\pi = 14\pi,
$$
 so the base circumference is $14\pi$ and the base radius is $7.$

The cone therefore has base radius $7$ and slant height $10,$ which is choice C.

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- The radius of the sector becomes the slant height of the cone, so the slant height is $10$
- The arc length of the sector becomes the base circumference: $\dfrac{252}{360}\cdot 2\pi(10)$
