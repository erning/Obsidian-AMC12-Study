# AMC12 2001 Q21

![[assets/problems/2001/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Four positive integers $a,$ $b,$ $c,$ and $d$ have a product of $8!$ and satisfy

$$
ab + a + b = 524,
$$

$$
bc + b + c = 146,
$$

$$
cd + c + d = 104.
$$

What is $a - d?$

(A) $4$
(B) $6$
(C) $8$
(D) $10$
(E) $12$

## Answer

**D.** $10$

## Solution

Adding $1$ to each equation factors the left sides:
$$
 \begin{aligned} (a + 1)(b + 1) &= 525 = 3 \cdot 5^2 \cdot 7, \\ (b + 1)(c + 1) &= 147 = 3 \cdot 7^2, \\ (c + 1)(d + 1) &= 105 = 3 \cdot 5 \cdot 7. \end{aligned}
$$

Since $525$ has a factor of $25$ while $147$ is not divisible by $5,$ the factor $a + 1$ must carry the $25.$ Among divisors of $525,$ only $a + 1 = 25$ makes $a = 24$ divide $8! = 40320.$

Then $b + 1 = 21,$ $c + 1 = 7,$ and $d + 1 = 15,$ giving $b = 20,$ $c = 6,$ $d = 14.$ (Indeed $24 \cdot 20 \cdot 6 \cdot 14 = 40320 = 8!.$)

So $a - d = 24 - 14 = 10.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- Add $1$ to each equation so the left side factors: $(a + 1)(b + 1) = 525,$ and so on
- $525 = 3 \cdot 5^2 \cdot 7$ has a factor of $25$ but $(b+1)(c+1) = 147$ does not, so $a + 1$ is a multiple of $25$

- Simon’s favorite factoring trick
- Divisibility
- System of equations

## Common mistakes

- Translate the wording into the correct equation, diagram, or counting model before calculating.
- Check units, endpoint conditions, and whether the requested quantity is a count, length, area, or probability.
