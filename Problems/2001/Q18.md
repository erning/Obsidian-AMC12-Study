# AMC12 2001 Q18

![[assets/problems/2001/Q18.png]]

## Problem

A circle centered at $A$ with a radius of $1$ and a circle centered at $B$ with a radius of $4$ are externally tangent. A third circle is tangent to the first two and to one of their common external tangents as shown. The radius of the third circle is

(A) $\dfrac{1}{3}$
(B) $\dfrac{2}{5}$
(C) $\dfrac{5}{12}$
(D) $\dfrac{4}{9}$
(E) $\dfrac{1}{2}$

## Answer

**D.** $\dfrac{4}{9}$

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2001/Q18-draft.svg]]

When two mutually tangent circles of radii $r$ and $s$ both rest on a line, the distance between their points of tangency is $2\sqrt{rs}.$

The big circles' contact points are $2\sqrt{1 \cdot 4} = 4$ apart. Placing the small circle of radius $x$ between them, its two tangent distances add up:
$$
 2\sqrt{1 \cdot x} + 2\sqrt{4 \cdot x} = 4.
$$

Then $6\sqrt{x} = 4,$ so $\sqrt{x} = \dfrac{2}{3}$ and $x = \dfrac{4}{9}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- For two circles of radii $r$ and $s$ both tangent to a line and to each other, their contact points on the line are $2\sqrt{rs}$ apart
- The contact point of the small circle splits the segment: $2\sqrt{x} + 2\sqrt{4x} = 2\sqrt{4}$
