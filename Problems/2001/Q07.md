# AMC12 2001 Q07

![[assets/problems/2001/Q07.png]]

## Problem

A charity sells $140$ benefit tickets for a total of $\$2001.$ Some tickets sell for full price (a whole dollar amount), and the rest sell for half price. How much money is raised by the full-price tickets?

(A) $\$782$
(B) $\$986$
(C) $\$1158$
(D) $\$1219$
(E) $\$1449$

## Answer

**A.** $\$782$

## Solution

Let $n$ tickets sell at full price $p$ dollars. Then
$$
 np + (140 - n)\dfrac{p}{2} = 2001,
$$
 so $p(n + 140)$ $= 4002$ $= 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 23 \cdot 29.$

Since $0 \le n \le 140,$ we need a factor of $4002$ with $140 \le n + 140 \le 280.$ The only such factor is $174 = 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 29,$ giving $n = 34$ and $p = 23.$

The full-price tickets raise $34 \cdot 23 = 782$ dollars.

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Key idea

- With $n$ full-price tickets at price $p,$ the total is $np + (140 - n)\dfrac{p}{2} = 2001$
- This becomes $p(n + 140)$ $= 4002$ $= 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 23 \cdot 29,$ and $n + 140$ lies between $140$ and $280$
