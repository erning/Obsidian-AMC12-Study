# AMC12 2001 Q01

![[assets/problems/2001/Q01.png]]

## Problem

The sum of two numbers is $S.$ Suppose $3$ is added to each number and then each of the resulting numbers is doubled. What is the sum of the final two numbers?

(A) $2S + 3$
(B) $3S + 2$
(C) $3S + 6$
(D) $2S + 6$
(E) $2S + 12$

## Answer

**E.** $2S + 12$

## Solution

Adding $3$ to each number raises the sum from $S$ to $S + 6.$ Doubling each number doubles the sum, giving
$$
 2(S + 6) = 2S + 12.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Key idea

- Adding $3$ to each of the two numbers increases their sum by $6$
- Doubling each number doubles the sum

## Knowledge points

- Algebraic manipulation
- Distributive property

## Common mistakes

- Translate the wording into the correct equation, diagram, or counting model before calculating.
- Check units, endpoint conditions, and whether the requested quantity is a count, length, area, or probability.
