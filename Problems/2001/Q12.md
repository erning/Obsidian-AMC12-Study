# AMC12 2001 Q12

![[assets/problems/2001/Q12.png]]

## Problem

How many positive integers not exceeding $2001$ are multiples of $3$ or $4$ but not $5?$

(A) $768$
(B) $801$
(C) $934$
(D) $1067$
(E) $1167$

## Answer

**B.** $801$

## Solution

Multiples of $3$ or $4$ up to $2001$ number
$$
 667 + 500 - 166 = 1001,
$$
 using $\lfloor 2001/3 \rfloor = 667,$ $\lfloor 2001/4 \rfloor = 500,$ and $\lfloor 2001/12 \rfloor = 166.$

Among these, the ones divisible by $5$ are multiples of $15$ or $20$:
$$
 133 + 100 - 33 = 200,
$$
 using $\lfloor 2001/15 \rfloor = 133,$ $\lfloor 2001/20 \rfloor = 100,$ and $\lfloor 2001/60 \rfloor = 33.$

The count is $1001 - 200 = 801.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Key idea

- First count multiples of $3$ or $4$ using $\lfloor 2001/3 \rfloor$ $+ \lfloor 2001/4 \rfloor$ $- \lfloor 2001/12 \rfloor$
- Then subtract those also divisible by $5,$ i.e. the multiples of $15$ or $20$
