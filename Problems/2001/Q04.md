# AMC12 2001 Q04

![[assets/problems/2001/Q04.png]]

## Problem

The mean of three numbers is $10$ more than the least of the numbers and $15$ less than the greatest. The median of the three numbers is $5.$ What is their sum?

(A) $5$
(B) $20$
(C) $25$
(D) $30$
(E) $36$

## Answer

**D.** $30$

## Solution

Let $m$ be the mean. The least number is $m - 10,$ the greatest is $m + 15,$ and the middle number is the median $5.$ Their sum is $3m,$ so
$$
 (m - 10) + 5 + (m + 15) = 3m.
$$

This gives $m = 10,$ so the sum of the three numbers is $3m = 30.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- Let $m$ be the mean; the least number is $m - 10$ and the greatest is $m + 15$
- The median is the middle number $5,$ so $(m - 10) + 5 + (m + 15) = 3m$
