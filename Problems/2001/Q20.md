# AMC12 2001 Q20

![[assets/problems/2001/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Points $A = (3, 9),$ $B = (1, 1),$ $C = (5, 3),$ and $D = (a, b)$ lie in the first quadrant and are the vertices of quadrilateral $ABCD.$ The quadrilateral formed by joining the midpoints of $\overline{AB},$ $\overline{BC},$ $\overline{CD},$ and $\overline{DA}$ is a square. What is the sum of the coordinates of point $D?$

(A) $7$
(B) $9$
(C) $10$
(D) $12$
(E) $16$

## Answer

**C.** $10$

## Solution

The midpoints are $M = (2, 5)$ of $\overline{AB}$ and $N = (3, 2)$ of $\overline{BC}.$

For the midpoint quadrilateral to be a square, consecutive sides are perpendicular and equal. With $\overrightarrow{NM} = \langle -1, 3 \rangle,$ the side $\overrightarrow{MQ}$ to the midpoint $Q$ of $\overline{DA}$ must be $\langle 3, 1 \rangle,$ so $Q = (5, 6).$

Since $Q$ is the midpoint of $\overline{DA}$ and $A = (3, 9),$ we get $D = 2Q - A = (7, 3).$ The sum of its coordinates is $7 + 3 = 10.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- Let $M$ and $N$ be the midpoints of $\overline{AB}$ and $\overline{BC};$ find $M$ and $N$ first
- The next midpoint $Q$ satisfies $\overrightarrow{MQ} = \overrightarrow{NM}$ rotated $90^\circ$ (same length, perpendicular)
