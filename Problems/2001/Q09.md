## Problem

Let $f$ be a function satisfying $f(xy) = \dfrac{f(x)}{y}$ for all positive real numbers $x$ and $y.$ If $f(500) = 3,$ what is the value of $f(600)?$

(A) $1$
(B) $2$
(C) $\dfrac{5}{2}$
(D) $3$
(E) $\dfrac{18}{5}$

## Answer

**C.** $\dfrac{5}{2}$

## Solution

Choose $x = 500$ and $y = \dfrac{6}{5}$ so that $xy = 600.$ Then
$$
 f(600) = \dfrac{f(500)}{6/5} = \dfrac{3}{6/5} = \dfrac{5}{2}.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- Write $600$ as $500$ times a convenient factor
- $f(600) = f\!\left(500 \cdot \dfrac{6}{5}\right) = \dfrac{f(500)}{6/5}$
