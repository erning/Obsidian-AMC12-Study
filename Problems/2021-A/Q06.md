# AMC12 2021-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

A deck of cards has only red cards and black cards. The probability of choosing a red card is $\frac13$. When $4$ black cards are added, the probability of choosing red becomes $\frac14$. How many cards were in the deck originally?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $12$**

## Solution

Let $r$ be the number of red cards and $t$ the original total. From the first probability,
$$
\frac rt=\frac13,
\qquad t=3r.
$$
After adding four black cards,
$$
\frac r{t+4}=\frac14.
$$
Substituting $t=3r$ gives $4r=3r+4$, so $r=4$ and $t=12$.

## Knowledge points

- Basic probability
- Systems of equations

## Key idea

Use the unchanged red-card count in both probability equations.

## Common mistakes

- Adding four to the numerator as well as the denominator.
- Confusing the original total with the number of black cards.
