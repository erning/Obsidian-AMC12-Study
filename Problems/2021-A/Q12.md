# AMC12 2021-A Q12

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q12.png]]

## Problem

All roots of
$$
z^6-10z^5+Az^4+Bz^3+Cz^2+Dz+16
$$
are positive integers, possibly repeated. What is $B$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $-88$**

## Solution

By Vieta's formulas, the six positive integer roots have sum $10$ and product $16$. The only multiset of six positive integers satisfying both conditions is
$$
1,1,2,2,2,2.
$$
Therefore the polynomial is
$$
(z-1)^2(z-2)^4.
$$
Expanding,
$$
(z^2-2z+1)(z^4-8z^3+24z^2-32z+16)
=z^6-10z^5+41z^4-88z^3+104z^2-64z+16.
$$
Thus $B=-88$.

## Knowledge points

- Vieta's formulas
- Integer partitions
- Polynomial expansion

## Key idea

The sum and product of the positive integer roots nearly determine the root multiset.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting that roots may be repeated.
- Reading $B$ as the coefficient of $z^4$ rather than $z^3$.
