# AMC12 2021-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

When a student multiplied the number $66$ by the repeating decimal $1.\overline{ab}=1.ababab\ldots$, where $a$ and $b$ are digits, he did not notice the notation and instead multiplied $66$ by the terminating decimal $1.ab$. His answer was $0.5$ less than the correct answer. What is the two-digit integer $\overline{ab}$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $75$**

## Solution

Let $n=\overline{ab}$, so $n$ is the two-digit integer represented by the digits $a,b$. The repeating and terminating decimals are
$$
1.\overline{ab}=1+\frac{n}{99},
\qquad
1.ab=1+\frac{n}{100}.
$$
The difference between the correct and incorrect products is
$$
66\left(\frac{n}{99}-\frac{n}{100}\right)
=\frac{n}{150}.
$$
Setting this equal to $0.5$ gives $n=75$.

## Knowledge points

- Repeating decimals
- Place value
- Linear equations

## Key idea

The two-decimal repeating block has denominator $99$, while the terminating version has denominator $100$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $n/10$ or $n/1000$ for the repeating decimal.
- Reversing the sign of the difference.
