# AMC12 2021-A Q08

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q08.png]]

## Problem

A sequence is defined by $D_0=0$, $D_1=0$, $D_2=1$, and
$$
D_n=D_{n-1}+D_{n-3}
$$
for $n\ge3$. What are the parities of $(D_{2021},D_{2022},D_{2023})$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $(E,O,E)$**

## Solution

Work modulo $2$. Starting with $D_0,D_1,D_2$, the parity sequence is
$$
E,E,O,O,O,E,O,E,E,O,O,O,E,O,\ldots
$$
The triple $(E,E,O)$ repeats every $7$ terms. Since
$$
2021\equiv5,\qquad 2022\equiv6,\qquad 2023\equiv0\pmod7,
$$
the required parities match $D_5,D_6,D_0$, namely $(E,O,E)$.

## Knowledge points

- Recurrence relations
- Modular arithmetic
- Periodicity

## Key idea

Only parity matters, so reduce the recurrence modulo $2$ and find its period.

## Common mistakes

- Computing the enormous terms themselves.
- Using period $6$ or $8$ without checking the initial triple.
