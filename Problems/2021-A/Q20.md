# AMC12 2021-A Q20

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Suppose a parabola has vertex $V$ and focus $F$, and a point $A$ on the parabola satisfies $AF=20$ and $AV=21$. What is the sum of all possible values of $FV$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $\frac{40}{3}$**

## Solution

Set $V=(0,0)$ and $F=(0,f)$, where $f=FV>0$. The directrix is $y=-f$. If $A=(x,y)$ lies on the parabola, the focus-directrix definition gives
$$
x^2+y^2=(y+f)^2.
$$
Since $AF=20$, $y+f=20$, so $y=20-f$. Since $AV=21$,
$$
x^2+y^2=441.
$$
Substitute $y=20-f$ and use $x^2=4fy$ from the parabola equation:
$$
4f(20-f)+(20-f)^2=441.
$$
This simplifies to
$$
3f^2-40f+41=0.
$$
By Vieta's formulas, the sum of the two possible positive values of $f$ is $40/3$.

## Knowledge points

- Focus-directrix definition
- Coordinate geometry
- Vieta's formulas

## Key idea

Use the two given distances to obtain a quadratic for the focal length, then use the sum of roots.

## Common mistakes

- Using $AV=21$ as the focal length.
- Forgetting that both positive roots are possible.
