# AMC12 2021-A Q18

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Let $f$ be defined on the positive rational numbers by
$$
f(ab)=f(a)+f(b)
$$
for all positive rational $a,b$, and suppose $f(p)=p$ for every prime $p$. For which listed number $x$ is $f(x)<0$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $\frac{11}{25}$**

## Solution

The functional equation makes $f$ completely additive. If
$$
x=\prod_p p^{e_p},
$$
where exponents may be negative for denominator primes, then
$$
f(x)=\sum_p e_p f(p)=\sum_p e_p p.
$$
Evaluate the choices:
$$
f\left(\frac{32}{17}\right)=17-5\cdot2=7,
$$
$$
f\left(\frac{16}{11}\right)=11-4\cdot2=3,
\qquad
f\left(\frac97\right)=7-2\cdot3=1,
$$
$$
f\left(\frac67\right)=7-2-3=2,
\qquad
f\left(\frac{11}{25}\right)=2\cdot5-11=-1.
$$
Only the last value is negative.

> Note: the computation above has the sign reversed. Since $f(ab)=f(a)+f(b)$, we have $f(a/b)=f(a)-f(b)$, so $f(11/25)=f(11)-f(25)=11-2\cdot5=+1>0$ (not $-1$); likewise $f(32/17)=10-17=-7<0$. With the five fractions as written here, four satisfy $f<0$. For the answer to be uniquely (E), the original options must be their reciprocals ($17/32, 11/16, 7/9, 7/6, 25/11$), in which case only $25/11$ gives $f(25/11)=10-11=-1<0$. Please verify against the original figure.

## Knowledge points

## Key idea

Numerator primes contribute positively and denominator primes contribute negatively.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $f$ as multiplicative rather than additive over products.
- Forgetting that $f(1/p)=-f(p)$.
