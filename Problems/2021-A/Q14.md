# AMC12 2021-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

What is the value of
$$
\left(\sum_{k=1}^{20}\log_{5^k}3^{k^2}\right)
\left(\sum_{k=1}^{100}\log_{9^k}25^k\right)?
$$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $21{,}000$**

## Solution

For the first sum,
$$
\log_{5^k}3^{k^2}
=\frac{k^2\log3}{k\log5}
=k\log_5 3.
$$
Thus it equals
$$
\left(\sum_{k=1}^{20}k\right)\log_5 3
=210\log_5 3.
$$
For the second sum,
$$
\log_{9^k}25^k
=\frac{k\log25}{k\log9}
=\log_3 5,
$$
so the sum is $100\log_3 5$. Since $\log_5 3\cdot\log_3 5=1$, the product is
$$
210\cdot100=21{,}000.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Change of base
- Logarithm identities
- Arithmetic series

## Key idea

The factors of $k$ cancel inside each logarithm, leaving one arithmetic sum and one constant sum.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting that both the base and argument contain powers of $k$.
- Replacing $\log_5 3\cdot\log_3 5$ with $\log_{15}15$ without justification.
