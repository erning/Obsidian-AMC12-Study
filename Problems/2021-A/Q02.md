# AMC12 2021-A Q02

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q02.png]]

## Problem

Under what conditions is
$$
\sqrt{a^2+b^2}=a+b
$$
true, where $a$ and $b$ are real numbers?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $ab=0$ and $a+b\ge0$**

## Solution

The left side is nonnegative, so equality requires $a+b\ge0$. Under that condition, squaring is reversible:
$$
a^2+b^2=(a+b)^2=a^2+2ab+b^2.
$$
Thus $ab=0$. Conversely, if $ab=0$ and $a+b\ge0$, then
$$
\sqrt{a^2+b^2}=\sqrt{(a+b)^2}=|a+b|=a+b.
$$
Both conditions are therefore necessary and sufficient.

## Knowledge points

- Principal square root
- Squaring equations
- Necessary and sufficient conditions

## Key idea

Check the sign condition before squaring.

## Common mistakes

- Concluding only $ab=0$ and forgetting that the square root is nonnegative.
- Squaring without checking whether the converse remains valid.
