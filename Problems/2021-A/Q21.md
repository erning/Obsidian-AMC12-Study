# AMC12 2021-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

The five solutions of
$$
(z-1)(z^2+2z+4)^2(z^2+4z+6)=0
$$
may be written as $x_k+y_ki$ for $1\le k\le5$, with real $x_k,y_k$. Let $E$ be the unique ellipse through these five points. If its eccentricity is $\frac mn$, where $m,n$ are relatively prime positive integers, what is $m+n$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $7$**

## Solution

The roots are
$$
1,\qquad -1\pm\sqrt3\,i,\qquad -2\pm\sqrt2\,i.
$$
The points are symmetric about the $x$-axis, so the ellipse has no $xy$ term and can be written
$$
Ax^2+Cy^2+Dx+F=0.
$$
Substitution of the points gives, after scaling,
$$
5x^2+6y^2+9x-14=0.
$$
Complete the square:
$$
5\left(x+\frac9{10}\right)^2+6y^2=\frac{361}{20}.
$$
Thus the semiaxes satisfy
$$
a^2=\frac{361}{100},\qquad b^2=\frac{361}{120}.
$$
The eccentricity is
$$
e=\sqrt{1-\frac{b^2}{a^2}}
=\sqrt{1-\frac56}
=\frac1{\sqrt6}.
$$
Therefore $m=1,n=6$, and $m+n=7$.

- Complex roots
- Conic equations
- Ellipse eccentricity

## Key idea

Use conjugate-root symmetry to simplify the ellipse equation before substituting points.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the imaginary parts as $y$-coordinates without pairing conjugates.
- Choosing the smaller semiaxis as $a$ in the eccentricity formula.
