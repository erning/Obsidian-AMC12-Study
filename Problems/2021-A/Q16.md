# AMC12 2021-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

In the list
$$
1,2,2,3,3,3,4,4,4,4,\ldots,200,200,\ldots,200,
$$
the integer $n$ appears $n$ times for $1\le n\le200$. What is the median?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $142$**

## Solution

The list has
$$
1+2+\cdots+200=\frac{200\cdot201}{2}=20{,}100
$$
terms, so the median is the average of positions $10{,}050$ and $10{,}051$. The block of $n$'s ends at position
$$
\frac{n(n+1)}2.
$$
Now
$$
\frac{141\cdot142}{2}=10{,}011,
\qquad
\frac{142\cdot143}{2}=10{,}153.
$$
Both middle positions lie in the block of $142$'s, so the median is $142$.

## Knowledge points

- Median of an ordered list
- Triangular numbers

## Key idea

Locate the two middle positions using cumulative block lengths.

## Common mistakes

- Using position $10{,}050$ as the median without checking that the list length is even.
- Using $n(n+1)$ instead of $n(n+1)/2$.
