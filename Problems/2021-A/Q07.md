# AMC12 2021-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

What is the least possible value of
$$
(xy-1)^2+(x+y)^2
$$
for real numbers $x$ and $y$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $1$**

## Solution

Expand and factor:
$$
(xy-1)^2+(x+y)^2
=x^2y^2-2xy+1+x^2+2xy+y^2
=(x^2+1)(y^2+1).
$$
Each factor is at least $1$, so the product is at least $1$. Equality occurs at $x=y=0$, hence the least possible value is $1$.

## Knowledge points

- Algebraic identities
- Nonnegative expressions

## Key idea

The cross terms cancel and produce a product of two factors each at least $1$.

## Common mistakes

- Expanding $(x+y)^2$ incorrectly.
- Trying to minimize the two squares independently.
