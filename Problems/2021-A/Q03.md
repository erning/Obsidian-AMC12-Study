# AMC12 2021-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

The sum of two natural numbers is $17{,}402$. One of the two numbers is divisible by $10$. If the units digit of that number is erased, the other number is obtained. What is the difference of these two numbers?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $14{,}238$**

## Solution

The number divisible by $10$ ends in $0$. Erasing that digit divides it by $10$, so if the smaller number is $x$, the larger number is $10x$. Then
$$
x+10x=17{,}402,
\qquad x=1{,}582.
$$
The two numbers are $1{,}582$ and $15{,}820$, whose difference is
$$
15{,}820-1{,}582=14{,}238.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Place value
- Linear equations

## Key idea

Erasing a trailing zero divides the number by $10$.

## Common mistakes

- Adding or subtracting $10$ instead of multiplying by $10$.
- Reversing which number is larger.
