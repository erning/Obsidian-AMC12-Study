# AMC12 2021-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

Two right circular cones with vertices facing down contain the same amount of liquid. The radii of the tops of the liquid surfaces are $3$ cm and $6$ cm. A spherical marble of radius $1$ cm is dropped into each cone and is completely submerged without spilling. What is the ratio of the rise of the liquid level in the narrow cone to the rise in the wide cone?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $4:1$**

## Solution

The liquid in each cone forms a smaller cone similar to the container. Let their liquid heights be $h_1,h_2$ for radii $3$ and $6$. Equal volumes give
$$
\frac13\pi(3^2)h_1=\frac13\pi(6^2)h_2,
\qquad h_1=4h_2.
$$
The marble adds the same volume $\Delta V=\frac43\pi$ to each liquid. Since similar cone volumes scale as the cube of height, the fractional height increase is the same in both cones. Thus the absolute rises are proportional to the original heights, giving
$$
\text{rise}_1:\text{rise}_2=h_1:h_2=4:1.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Similarity
- Cone volume
- Scaling laws

## Key idea

Equal starting liquid volumes make the fractional height increase from the same added volume equal.

## Common mistakes

- Comparing cross-sectional areas instead of heights.
- Forgetting that cone volume scales as the cube of a linear scale.
