# AMC12 2021-A Q09

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q09.png]]

## Problem

Which of the following is equivalent to
$$
(2+3)(2^2+3^2)(2^4+3^4)(2^8+3^8)(2^{16}+3^{16})(2^{32}+3^{32})(2^{64}+3^{64})?
$$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $3^{128}-2^{128}$**

## Solution

Since $3-2=1$, multiply the product by $3-2$ without changing its value. The first factor becomes
$$
(3-2)(3+2)=3^2-2^2.
$$
Multiplying by the next factor gives $3^4-2^4$, and the same difference-of-squares step doubles the exponent at every stage. After all seven factors, the result is
$$
3^{128}-2^{128}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Difference of squares
- Telescoping products

## Key idea

Insert the factor $3-2=1$ so every factor becomes the next difference of powers.

## Common mistakes

- Using $3+2$ instead of $3-2$.
- Stopping after the first difference-of-squares step.
