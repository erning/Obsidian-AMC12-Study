# AMC12 2021-A Q17

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Trapezoid $ABCD$ has $AB\parallel CD$, $BC=CD=43$, and $AD\perp BD$. Let $O$ be the intersection of diagonals $AC$ and $BD$, and let $P$ be the midpoint of $BD$. Given $OP=11$, write $AD=m\sqrt n$ with $n$ squarefree. What is $m+n$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $194$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q17-draft.svg]]

Because $BCD$ is isosceles, the median $CP$ is perpendicular to $BD$. Since $AD\perp BD$, we have $CP\parallel AD$. Let $E=CP\cap AB$. Then $AECD$ is a parallelogram, so $AE=CD=43$. The symmetry of the isosceles triangle $BCD$ gives $EB=CB=43$, hence
$$
AB=AE+EB=86.
$$
The diagonal intersection in a trapezoid gives
$$
\frac{DO}{BO}=\frac{CD}{AB}=\frac12,
$$
so $DO=BD/3$. Since $DP=BD/2$,
$$
OP=DP-DO=\frac16BD=11,
\qquad BD=66.
$$
Triangle $ABD$ is right at $D$, so
$$
AD=\sqrt{AB^2-BD^2}
=\sqrt{86^2-66^2}
=\sqrt{20\cdot152}
=4\sqrt{190}.
$$
Thus $m+n=4+190=194$.

## Knowledge points

- Isosceles triangles
- Trapezoid diagonals
- Similar triangles
- Pythagorean theorem

## Key idea

Use the isosceles-triangle symmetry to determine $AB$, then use the diagonal ratio to determine $BD$.

## Common mistakes

- Reading $43$ as $4\sqrt3$; it is the integer $43$.
- Using $DO=BD/2$ instead of the trapezoid diagonal ratio.
