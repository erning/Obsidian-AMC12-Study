# AMC12 2021-A Q01

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q01.png]]

## Problem

What is the value of
$$
2^{1+2+3}-(2^1+2^2+2^3)?
$$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $50$**

## Solution

The exponent in the first term is $1+2+3=6$, so that term is $2^6=64$. The parenthesized sum is
$$
2+4+8=14.
$$
Therefore the value is $64-14=50$.

## Knowledge points

- Exponent rules
- Arithmetic order of operations

## Key idea

Evaluate the exponent and the sum separately before subtracting.

## Common mistakes

- Reading $2^{1+2+3}$ as $2^1+2^2+2^3$.
- Forgetting the parentheses around the second expression.
