# AMC12 2021-A Q22

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q22.png]]

## Problem

Suppose the roots of
$$
P(x)=x^3+ax^2+bx+c
$$
are $\cos\frac{2\pi}{7}$, $\cos\frac{4\pi}{7}$, and $\cos\frac{6\pi}{7}$. What is $abc$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $\frac1{32}$**

## Solution

The three cosine values are the roots of
$$
8x^3+4x^2-4x-1=0.
$$
Dividing by $8$ gives the monic polynomial
$$
x^3+\frac12x^2-\frac12x-\frac18.
$$
Hence
$$
a=\frac12,\qquad b=-\frac12,\qquad c=-\frac18.
$$
Therefore
$$
abc=\frac12\left(-\frac12\right)\left(-\frac18\right)=\frac1{32}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Trigonometric multiple-angle identities
- Polynomial roots
- Coefficient comparison

## Key idea

Use the known cubic satisfied by the three cosine values and make it monic.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting to divide every coefficient by $8$.
- Losing the two negative signs in the product $abc$.
