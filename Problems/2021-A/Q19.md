# AMC12 2021-A Q19

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q19.png]]

## Problem

How many solutions does
$$
\sin\left(\frac\pi2\cos x\right)
=\cos\left(\frac\pi2\sin x\right)
$$
have in the closed interval $[0,\pi]$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $2$**

## Solution

Use $\cos u=\sin(\frac\pi2-u)$ to rewrite the equation as
$$
\sin\left(\frac\pi2\cos x\right)
=\sin\left(\frac\pi2-\frac\pi2\sin x\right).
$$
For $\sin u=\sin v$, either $u=v+2k\pi$ or $u=\pi-v+2k\pi$.

The first case gives
$$
\cos x+\sin x=1+4k.
$$
Because $\cos x+\sin x\in[-\sqrt2,\sqrt2]$, only $k=0$ is possible. The equation $\cos x+\sin x=1$ has solutions $x=0$ and $x=\pi/2$ in $[0,\pi]$.

The second case gives
$$
\cos x-\sin x=1+4k.
$$
The range permits only $k=0$, and $\cos x-\sin x=1$ has only $x=0$ in the interval. This is already counted, so the distinct solutions are $0$ and $\pi/2$, for a total of $2$.

## Key idea

Convert cosine to sine, then use the two general solutions of $\sin u=\sin v$.

## Common mistakes

- Counting $x=0$ twice.
- Ignoring the range restriction on $\sin x+\cos x$.
