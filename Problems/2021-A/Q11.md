# AMC12 2021-A Q11

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q11.png]]

## Problem

A laser is placed at $(3,5)$. The beam hits and bounces off the $y$-axis, then the $x$-axis, and finally reaches $(7,5)$. What is the total distance traveled?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $10\sqrt2$**

## Solution

Reflect the path across each axis at a bounce so that the reflected path becomes a straight segment. Reflect the starting point across the $y$-axis:
$$
(3,5)\longmapsto(-3,5).
$$
Reflect the target across the $x$-axis:
$$
(7,5)\longmapsto(7,-5).
$$
The total reflected path length is
$$
\sqrt{(7-(-3))^2+(-5-5)^2}
=\sqrt{100+100}=10\sqrt2.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Reflection method
- Distance formula

## Key idea

Unfold reflections at the axes to replace a broken path with one line segment.

## Common mistakes

- Reflecting the wrong endpoint across the wrong axis.
- Adding horizontal and vertical distances instead of using the straight-line distance.
