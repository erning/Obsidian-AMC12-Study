# AMC12 2021-A Q13

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Of the listed complex numbers $z$, which one has the greatest real part of $z^5$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $-\sqrt3+i$**

## Solution

Every listed number has modulus $2$, so $z^5$ has modulus $32$ and real part $32\cos(5\theta)$, where $\theta$ is the argument of $z$. The arguments in choices A through E are respectively
$$
180^\circ,\ 150^\circ,\ 135^\circ,\ 120^\circ,\ 90^\circ.
$$
Multiplying by $5$ and reducing modulo $360^\circ$ gives
$$
180^\circ,\ 30^\circ,\ 315^\circ,\ 240^\circ,\ 90^\circ.
$$
The largest cosine is $\cos30^\circ=\sqrt3/2$, attained by choice B. Its real part is $32(\sqrt3/2)=16\sqrt3$.

## Knowledge points

- Polar form of complex numbers
- Arguments and powers

## Key idea

For equal moduli, compare only the arguments after multiplying by $5$.

## Common mistakes

- Comparing the real parts of $z$ instead of $z^5$.
- Failing to reduce angles modulo $360^\circ$.
