# AMC12 2021-A Q15

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q15.png]]

## Problem

A choir director selects a nonempty group from $6$ tenors and $8$ basses. The difference between the number of tenors and basses must be a multiple of $4$. If $N$ is the number of possible groups, what is the remainder when $N$ is divided by $100$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $95$**

## Solution

Let $t$ and $b$ be the selected numbers of tenors and basses. The roots-of-unity filter for $t-b\equiv0\pmod4$ gives
$$
N+1=\frac14\sum_{j=0}^3(1+i^j)^6(1+i^{-j})^8.
$$
The $j=0$ term is $2^6\cdot2^8=16{,}384$. The $j=2$ term is zero because $(1+i^2)^6=0$. The $j=1$ and $j=3$ terms are $-128i$ and $128i$, which cancel. Hence
$$
N+1=\frac{16{,}384}{4}=4096,
\qquad N=4095.
$$
Therefore $N\equiv95\pmod{100}$.

## Knowledge points

- Binomial counting
- Congruence conditions
- Roots-of-unity filter

## Key idea

Use fourth roots of unity to retain exactly the selections with $t-b$ divisible by $4$.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting to remove the empty group.
- Counting only groups with equal numbers of tenors and basses.
