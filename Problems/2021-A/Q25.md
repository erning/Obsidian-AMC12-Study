# AMC12 2021-A Q25

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q25.png]]

## Problem

Let $d(n)$ be the number of positive divisors of $n$, and define
$$
f(n)=\frac{d(n)}{\sqrt[3]{n}}.
$$
There is a unique positive integer $N$ such that $f(N)>f(n)$ for every positive integer $n\ne N$. What is the sum of the digits of $N$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $9$**

## Solution

If
$$
n=\prod p^e,
$$
then
$$
f(n)=\prod_p\frac{e+1}{p^{e/3}}.
$$
The choices of exponents can be optimized independently for each prime. For $p=2$, the factor is largest at $e=3$, with value $2$. For $p=3$, it is largest at $e=2$; for $p=5$ and $p=7$, at $e=1$. For every prime $p\ge11$, including it decreases the value, so its exponent is $0$.

Consequently,
$$
N=2^3\cdot3^2\cdot5\cdot7=2520.
$$
The digit sum is $2+5+2+0=9$.

## Knowledge points

- Divisor function
- Prime factorization
- Multiplicative optimization

## Key idea

Factor $f(n)$ into independent prime-power contributions and optimize each exponent.

## Common mistakes

- Using $e$ instead of $e+1$ in the divisor count.
- Maximizing $d(n)$ alone without accounting for the cube root of $n$.
