# AMC12 2021-A Q24

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q24.png]]

## Problem

Semicircle $\Gamma$ has diameter $AB$ of length $14$. Circle $\Omega$ is tangent to $AB$ at $P$ and intersects $\Gamma$ at $Q$ and $R$. If $QR=3\sqrt3$ and $\angle QPR=60^\circ$, the area of $\triangle PQR$ is $\frac{a\sqrt b}{c}$, where $a,c$ are relatively prime and $b$ is squarefree. What is $a+b+c$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $122$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q24-draft.svg]]

Let $O_1$ and $O_2$ be the centers of $\Gamma$ and $\Omega$, and let $X=QR\cap AB$. Let $M$ be the midpoint of $QR$. Since $\angle QPR=60^\circ$ is an inscribed angle of $\Omega$, the central angle $\angle QO_2R$ is $120^\circ$. Thus
$$
O_2Q=3,\qquad O_2M=\frac32.
$$
Also $O_1Q=7$ and $MQ=3\sqrt3/2$, so
$$
O_1M=\sqrt{7^2-\left(\frac{3\sqrt3}{2}\right)^2}
=\frac{13}{2}.
$$
The centers and $M$ are collinear, giving $O_1O_2=13/2-3/2=5$. Since $O_2P=3$,
$$
O_1P=\sqrt{5^2-3^2}=4.
$$
Similar triangles formed by the two intersecting circles give
$$
\sin(\angle PXQ)=\sin(\angle PO_2O_1)=\frac45.
$$
Therefore
$$
O_1X=\frac{O_1M}{\sin(\angle PXQ)}
=\frac{13}{2}\div\frac45=\frac{65}{8},
$$
and
$$
XP=O_1X-O_1P=\frac{65}{8}-4=\frac{33}{8}.
$$
Finally,
$$
[PQR]=\frac12\cdot XP\cdot QR\cdot\sin(\angle PXQ)
=\frac12\cdot\frac{33}{8}\cdot3\sqrt3\cdot\frac45
=\frac{99\sqrt3}{20}.
$$
Thus $a=99$, $b=3$, $c=20$, and the requested sum is $99+3+20=122$.

## Key idea

Use the inscribed-angle chord formula to find the small circle's radius, then use coordinates for the altitude.

## Common mistakes

- Using $QR=2r\sin30^\circ$ instead of the $60^\circ$ angle.
- Choosing the algebraic solution that places $P$ outside $AB$.
