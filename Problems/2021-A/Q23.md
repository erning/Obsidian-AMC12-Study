# AMC12 2021-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Frieda the frog starts at the center of a $3\times3$ grid. On each hop she chooses up, down, left, or right uniformly, wrapping around the grid when a hop leaves an edge. She stops if she lands on a corner. What is the probability that she reaches a corner within at most four hops?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $\frac{25}{32}$**

## Solution

Classify the squares as center $C$, edge-middle $E$, and corner. From an edge-middle square, two of the four moves land on corners, one on the center, and one on another edge-middle square. Let $a_n$ be the probability of reaching a corner within $n$ hops starting from an edge-middle square. The first hop gives
$$
a_1=\frac12.
$$
From the center, the first hop always reaches an edge-middle square, so a center with $n$ hops remaining has success probability $a_{n-1}$. For $n\ge2$, therefore
$$
a_n=\frac12+\frac14a_{n-1}+\frac14a_{n-2},
$$
with $a_0=0$. Hence
$$
a_2=\frac12+\frac14\cdot\frac12=\frac58,
$$
and
$$
a_3=\frac12+\frac14\cdot\frac58+\frac14\cdot\frac12
=\frac{25}{32}.
$$
Starting from the center with four hops is equivalent to starting from an edge-middle square with three hops, so the answer is $a_3=25/32$.

## Knowledge points

- State classification
- Conditional probability
- Recurrence relations

## Key idea

Collapse the $3\times3$ torus into three states and track the remaining hop count.

## Common mistakes

- Counting diagonal moves, which are not allowed.
- Treating a center-to-edge move as a possible immediate corner landing.
