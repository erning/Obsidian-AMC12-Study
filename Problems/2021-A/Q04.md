# AMC12 2021-A Q04

![[assets/problems/2021-A/Q04.png]]

## Problem

Tom has a collection of $13$ snakes, $4$ of which are purple and $5$ of which are happy. He observes that:

- all of his happy snakes can add;
- none of his purple snakes can subtract;
- all of his snakes that cannot subtract also cannot add.

Which conclusion can be drawn about Tom's snakes?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) Happy snakes are not purple**

## Solution

Every purple snake cannot subtract. By the third observation, every snake that cannot subtract also cannot add. Therefore every purple snake cannot add. On the other hand, every happy snake can add. A snake cannot both be purple and happy, so happy snakes are not purple.

## Knowledge points

- Implication chains
- Logical negation
- Set disjointness

## Key idea

Chain “purple” to “cannot subtract” to “cannot add,” then compare with “happy” to “can add.”

## Common mistakes

- Reversing an implication.
- Assuming the total numbers of purple and happy snakes determine the conclusion.
