# AMC12 2007-B Q23

![[assets/problems/2007-B/Q23.png]]

## Problem

How many non-congruent right triangles with positive integer leg lengths have areas that are numerically equal to $3$ times their perimeters?

(A) $6$
(B) $7$
(C) $8$
(D) $10$
(E) $12$

## Answer

**A.** $6$

## Solution

Let the legs be $a\le b.$ The condition is $\tfrac12 ab=3\left(a+b+\sqrt{a^2+b^2}\right),$ so
$$
 ab-6a-6b=6\sqrt{a^2+b^2}.
$$

Squaring and simplifying gives $ab(ab-12a-12b+72)=0,$ hence $(a-12)(b-12)=72.$ The positive integer solutions are $(a,b)=(3,4),$ $(13,84),$ $(14,48),$ $(15,36),$ $(16,30),$ $(18,24),$ $(20,21).$

The pair $(3,4)$ is extraneous (its area $6$ does not equal $3$ times its perimeter $12$), so exactly $6$ triangles work.

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.
