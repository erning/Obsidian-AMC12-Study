# AMC12 2007-B Q22

![[assets/problems/2007-B/Q22.png]]

## Problem

Two particles move along the edges of equilateral $\triangle ABC$ in the direction
$$
A\to B\to C\to A,
$$
 starting simultaneously and moving at the same speed. One starts at $A,$ and the other starts at the midpoint of $\overline{BC}.$ The midpoint of the line segment joining the two particles traces out a path that encloses a region $R.$ What is the ratio of the area of $R$ to the area of $\triangle ABC?$

(A) $\dfrac{1}{16}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{12}$
(C) $\dfrac{1}{9}$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{6}$
(E) $\dfrac{1}{4}$

## Answer

**A.** $\dfrac{1}{16}$

## Solution

Track a third point always halfway between the two particles. Between the moments when the particles are at vertices/midpoints, both particles move linearly, so the midpoint moves linearly too, tracing straight segments that form a small triangle $XYZ.$

By symmetry $XYZ$ shares its center with $\triangle ABC.$ If $O$ is that center and $F$ is the midpoint of a side, then
$$
 \begin{aligned} OZ&=OC-ZC \\ &=\dfrac23 CF-\dfrac12 CF \\ &=\dfrac16 CF, \end{aligned}
$$
 while $OC=\dfrac23 CF.$

So the ratio of circumradii is $\dfrac{OZ}{OC}=\dfrac14,$ and the area ratio is $\left(\dfrac14\right)^2=\dfrac{1}{16}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Key idea

Use corresponding side ratios and the induced area ratio.
