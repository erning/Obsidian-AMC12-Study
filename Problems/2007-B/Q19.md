# AMC12 2007-B Q19

![[assets/problems/2007-B/Q19.png]]

## Problem

Rhombus $ABCD,$ with side length $6,$ is rolled to form a cylinder of volume $6$ by taping $\overline{AB}$ to $\overline{DC}.$ What is $\sin(\angle ABC)?$

(A) $\dfrac{\pi}{9}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{2}$
(C) $\dfrac{\pi}{6}$
(D) $\dfrac{\pi}{4}$
(E) $\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$

## Answer

**A.** $\dfrac{\pi}{9}$

## Solution

Let $\theta=\angle ABC.$ The base circle has circumference $6,$ so its radius is $\dfrac{6}{2\pi}=\dfrac3\pi.$ The height of the cylinder is the rhombus altitude $6\sin\theta.$

The volume is
$$
 \pi\left(\dfrac3\pi\right)^2(6\sin\theta)=\dfrac{54}{\pi}\sin\theta=6,
$$
 so $\sin\theta=\dfrac{\pi}{9}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Key idea

Use the circumference as the side length of the square or rectangle in the net.
