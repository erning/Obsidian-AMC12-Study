# AMC12 2007-B Q01

![[assets/problems/2007-B/Q01.png]]

## Problem

Isabella's house has $3$ bedrooms. Each bedroom is $12$ feet long, $10$ feet wide, and $8$ feet high. Isabella must paint the walls of all the bedrooms. Doorways and windows, which will not be painted, occupy $60$ square feet in each bedroom. How many square feet of walls must be painted?

(A) $678$
(B) $768$
(C) $786$
(D) $867$
(E) $876$

## Answer

**E.** $876$

## Solution

The perimeter of each bedroom floor is $2(12+10)=44$ feet.

So the wall area in one bedroom is $44\cdot8-60=352-60=292$ square feet. Across all three bedrooms, Isabella paints $3\cdot292=876$ square feet.

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Perimeter
- Area

## Key idea

Write the perimeter and area in terms of the same scale variables before solving.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
