# AMC12 2007-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2007-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

Tom's age is $T$ years, which is also the sum of the ages of his three children. His age $N$ years ago was twice the sum of their ages then. What is $T/N?$

(A) $2$
(B) $3$
(C) $4$
(D) $5$
(E) $6$

## Answer

**D.** $5$

## Solution

Tom's age $N$ years ago was $T-N.$ His three children were each $N$ years younger, so their ages then totaled $T-3N.$

The condition gives
$$
 T-N=2(T-3N),
$$
 so $5N=T$ and $T/N=5.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Ages
- Linear equation

## Key idea

Define the present age variables, then translate the time shift into a linear equation.
