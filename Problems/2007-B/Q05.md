# AMC12 2007-B Q05

![[assets/problems/2007-B/Q05.png]]

## Problem

The 2007 AMC 12 contests will be scored by awarding $6$ points for each correct response, $0$ points for each incorrect response, and $1.5$ points for each problem left unanswered. After looking over the $25$ problems, Sarah has decided to attempt the first $22$ and leave the last $3$ unanswered. How many of the first $22$ problems must she solve correctly in order to score at least $100$ points?

(A) $13$
(B) $14$
(C) $15$
(D) $16$
(E) $17$

## Answer

**D.** $16$

## Solution

The three unanswered problems give $3\cdot1.5=4.5$ points.

So Sarah needs at least $100-4.5=95.5$ points from correct answers. Since
$$
 15\lt\dfrac{95.5}{6}\lt16,
$$
 she must solve at least $16$ problems correctly, which would give her $16\cdot6+4.5=100.5$ points.

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

Check the boundary cases after deriving the sharp bound.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
