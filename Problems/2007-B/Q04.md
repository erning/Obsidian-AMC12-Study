# AMC12 2007-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2007-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

At Frank's Fruit Market, $3$ bananas cost as much as $2$ apples, and $6$ apples cost as much as $4$ oranges. How many oranges cost as much as $18$ bananas?

(A) $6$
(B) $8$
(C) $9$
(D) $12$
(E) $18$

## Answer

**B.** $8$

## Solution

Since $3$ bananas cost as much as $2$ apples, $18$ bananas cost as much as $12$ apples.

Since $6$ apples cost as much as $4$ oranges, $12$ apples cost as much as $8$ oranges. Therefore $18$ bananas cost as much as $8$ oranges.

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Ratio and proportion
- Unit conversion

## Key idea

Introduce a scale factor so all corresponding quantities are represented consistently.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
