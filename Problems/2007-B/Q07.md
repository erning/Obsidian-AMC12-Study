# AMC12 2007-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2007-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

All sides of the convex pentagon $ABCDE$ are of equal length, and $\angle A=\angle B=90^\circ.$ What is the degree measure of $\angle E?$

(A) $90$
(B) $108$
(C) $120$
(D) $144$
(E) $150$

## Answer

**E.** $150$

## Solution

Because $AB=BC=EA$ and $\angle A=\angle B=90^\circ,$ quadrilateral $ABCE$ is a square, so $\angle AEC=90^\circ$ and $EC$ equals the common side length.

Then $CD=DE=EC,$ so $\triangle CDE$ is equilateral and $\angle CED=60^\circ.$ Therefore
$$
 \begin{aligned} \angle E&=\angle AEC+\angle CED \\ &=90^\circ+60^\circ \\ &=150^\circ. \end{aligned}
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Equilateral triangle
- Square geometry
- Angle sum

## Key idea

Use the fixed angles and equal sides of the equilateral triangle.
