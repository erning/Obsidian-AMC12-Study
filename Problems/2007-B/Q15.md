# AMC12 2007-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2007-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

The geometric series $a+ar+ar^2+\cdots$ has a sum of $7,$ and the terms involving odd powers of $r$ have a sum of $3.$ What is $a+r?$

(A) $\dfrac{4}{3}$
(B) $\dfrac{12}{7}$
(C) $\dfrac{3}{2}$
(D) $\dfrac{7}{3}$
(E) $\dfrac{5}{2}$

## Answer

**E.** $\dfrac{5}{2}$

## Solution

The odd-power terms are $ar+ar^3+\cdots$ $=r(a+ar^2+\cdots),$ that is, $r$ times the even-power terms. The even-power terms sum to $7-3=4.$

So $3=4r,$ giving $r=\tfrac34.$ Then $a=7(1-r)=\tfrac74,$ and
$$
 a+r=\dfrac74+\dfrac34=\dfrac52.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.
