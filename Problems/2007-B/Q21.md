# AMC12 2007-B Q21

![[assets/problems/2007-B/Q21.png]]

## Problem

The first $2007$ positive integers are each written in base $3.$ How many of these base-$3$ representations are palindromes? (A palindrome is a number that reads the same forward and backward.)

(A) $100$
(B) $101$
(C) $102$
(D) $103$
(E) $104$

## Answer

**A.** $100$

## Solution

A palindrome is fixed by its first half. Counting base-$3$ palindromes by length gives $2$ of length $1$ or $2,$ $6$ of length $3$ or $4,$ $18$ of length $5$ or $6,$ and $54$ of length $7.$

That totals $2+2+6+6+18+18$ $+54=106$ palindromes with at most $7$ digits. Since $2007=2202100_3,$ the $7$-digit palindromes larger than it are $2210122,$ $2211122,$ $2212122,$ $2220222,$ $2221222,$ and $2222222,$ which is $6$ of them.

Therefore the count is $106-6=100.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Key idea

Expand the digits in the given base before comparing values.
