# AMC12 2007-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2007-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Point $P$ is inside equilateral $\triangle ABC.$ Points $Q,$ $R,$ and $S$ are the feet of the perpendiculars from $P$ to $\overline{AB},$ $\overline{BC},$ and $\overline{CA},$ respectively. Given that $PQ=1,$ $PR=2,$ and $PS=3,$ what is $AB?$

(A) $4$
(B) $3\sqrt{3}$
(C) $6$
(D) $4\sqrt{3}$
(E) $9$

## Answer

**D.** $4\sqrt{3}$

## Solution

Let $s=AB.$ Joining $P$ to the vertices splits the triangle into $\triangle PAB,$ $\triangle PBC,$ and $\triangle PCA,$ with areas $\tfrac{s}{2},$ $s,$ and $\tfrac{3s}{2}.$

Their total is $3s,$ which must equal the area $\tfrac{\sqrt3}{4}s^2$ of the equilateral triangle. So
$$
 3s=\dfrac{\sqrt3}{4}s^2,
$$
 giving $s=\dfrac{12}{\sqrt3}=4\sqrt3.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

- Equilateral triangle
- Triangle area

## Key idea

Split the figure into simple pieces whose areas can be added or subtracted.
