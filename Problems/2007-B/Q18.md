# AMC12 2007-B Q18

![[assets/problems/2007-B/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Let $a,$ $b,$ and $c$ be digits with $a\ne0.$ The three-digit integer $\overline{abc}$ lies one third of the way from the square of a positive integer to the square of the next larger integer. The integer $\overline{acb}$ lies two thirds of the way between the same two squares. What is $a+b+c?$

(A) $10$
(B) $13$
(C) $16$
(D) $18$
(E) $21$

## Answer

**C.** $16$

## Solution

Let the smaller square be $N^2,$ so the larger is $(N+1)^2$ and the gap is $2N+1.$ Then
$$
 \overline{abc}=N^2+\dfrac{2N+1}{3},
$$

$$
 \overline{acb}=N^2+\dfrac{2(2N+1)}{3}.
$$

Subtracting, $\overline{acb}-\overline{abc}=9(c-b)$ $=\dfrac{2N+1}{3},$ so $27(c-b)=2N+1.$ If $c-b=0$ or $2,$ then $N$ is not an integer; if $c-b\ge3,$ then $N\ge40$ and the numbers are not three digits.

So $c-b=1,$ giving $N=13.$ The points one third and two thirds of the way from $13^2=169$ to $14^2=196$ are $178$ and $187,$ so $a+b+c=1+7+8=16.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.
