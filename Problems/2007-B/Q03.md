# AMC12 2007-B Q03

![[assets/problems/2007-B/Q03.png]]

## Problem

The point $O$ is the center of the circle circumscribed about $\triangle ABC,$ with $\angle BOC=120^\circ$ and $\angle AOB=140^\circ,$ as shown. What is the degree measure of $\angle ABC?$

(A) $35$
(B) $40$
(C) $45$
(D) $50$
(E) $60$

## Answer

**D.** $50$

## Solution

The angles around $O$ sum to $360^\circ,$ so
$$
 \begin{aligned} \angle AOC&=360^\circ-140^\circ-120^\circ \\ &=100^\circ. \end{aligned}
$$

By the inscribed angle theorem, $\angle ABC$ subtends the same arc $AC$ as the central angle $\angle AOC,$ so
$$
 \angle ABC=\tfrac12\angle AOC=50^\circ.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Inscribed angle
- Angle sum

## Key idea

Relate the inscribed angle to its intercepted arc, then use the angle sum.
