# AMC12 2014-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

Susie pays for $4$ muffins and $3$ bananas. Calvin spends twice as much paying for $2$ muffins and $16$ bananas. How many times as expensive is a muffin as a banana?

## Answer

**B.** $\frac53$

## Solution

Let muffin and banana prices be $m$ and $b$. Calvin's spending is twice Susie's:
$$2(4m+3b)=2m+16b.$$
Thus
$$8m+6b=2m+16b,\qquad6m=10b,$$
so $m/b=5/3$.

## Knowledge points

- Linear equations
- Ratios

## Key idea

Set up one equation from the spending comparison.

## Common mistakes

- Doubling the wrong purchase
- Reporting $b/m$ instead of $m/b$
