# AMC12 2014-B Q02

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q02.png]]

## Problem

Orvin has just enough money to buy $30$ balloons at the regular price. A sale lets him buy one balloon at the regular price and a second at $\frac13$ off the regular price. What is the greatest number of balloons he can buy?

## Answer

**C.** $36$

## Solution

A sale pair costs
$$1+\left(1-\frac13\right)=\frac53$$
regular balloon prices. His money is worth $30$ regular prices, so he can buy
$$30\div\frac53=18$$
pairs, or $36$ balloons.

## Knowledge points

- Fractions
- Unit-price comparisons

## Key idea

Convert the sale pair into regular-price units.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the second balloon as free
- Applying the discount to both balloons
