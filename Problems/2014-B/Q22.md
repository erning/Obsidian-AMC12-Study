# AMC12 2014-B Q22

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q22.png]]

## Problem

In a pond there are lily pads $0$ through $10$. A frog starts on pad $1$. From pad $N$, it jumps to $N-1$ with probability $\frac N{10}$ and to $N+1$ with probability $1-\frac N{10}$. Reaching pad $0$ is fatal and reaching pad $10$ is escape. What is the probability of escape?

## Answer

**C.** $\frac{63}{146}$

## Solution

Let $p_j$ be the escape probability starting from pad $j$. Then
$$p_0=0,\qquad p_{10}=1,$$
and for $1\le j\le9$,
$$p_j=\frac j{10}p_{j-1}+\left(1-\frac j{10}\right)p_{j+1}.$$
Solving the recurrence from the two boundary values gives
$$p_1=\frac{63}{146}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Absorbing Markov chains
- Recurrences
- Boundary conditions

## Key idea

Set up the probability recurrence for each interior pad.

## Common mistakes

- Reversing the jump probabilities
- Treating the process as a fair random walk
