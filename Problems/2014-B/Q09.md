# AMC12 2014-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

A convex quadrilateral $ABCD$ has $AB=3$, $BC=4$, $CD=13$, $AD=12$, and $\angle ABC=90^\circ$. What is its area?

## Answer

**B.** $36$

## Solution

In right triangle $ABC$,
$$AC=\sqrt{3^2+4^2}=5.$$
Since $5^2+12^2=13^2$, triangle $ACD$ is also right, with legs $5$ and $12$. Therefore
$$[ABC]=\frac12(3)(4)=6,\qquad[ACD]=\frac12(5)(12)=30,$$
and the quadrilateral area is $36$.

## Knowledge points

- Pythagorean theorem
- Decomposing quadrilaterals

## Key idea

Use the diagonal $AC$ to split the quadrilateral into two right triangles.

## Common mistakes

- Using $13$ as a diagonal
- Forgetting the second triangle's area
