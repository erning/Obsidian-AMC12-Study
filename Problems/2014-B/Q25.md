# AMC12 2014-B Q25

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q25.png]]

## Problem

What is the sum of all positive real solutions $x$ to
$$
2\cos(2x)\left(\cos(2x)-\cos\left(\frac{2014\pi^2}{x}\right)\right)
=\cos(4x)-1?
$$

## Answer

**D.** $1080\pi$

## Solution

Use $\cos(4x)-1=-2\sin^2(2x)$ and
$$2\cos^2(2x)=1+\cos(4x).$$
After simplification, the equation becomes
$$\cos(2x)\cos\left(\frac{2014\pi^2}{x}\right)=1.$$
Thus both cosines must be $1$ or both must be $-1$. Writing
$$x=\pi a,\qquad a\in\mathbb Z_{>0},$$
the two phases must have the same parity. Since
$$2014=2\cdot19\cdot53,$$
the relevant positive odd divisors are
$$a\in\{1,19,53,19\cdot53\}.$$
The corresponding solutions sum to
$$\pi(1+19+53+19\cdot53)=\pi(20)(54)=1080\pi.$$

## Knowledge points

- Trigonometric identities
- Integer phase conditions
- Divisor enumeration

## Key idea

Reduce the equation to a product of two cosines equal to $1$.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting both cosine sign possibilities
- Counting nonpositive or duplicate solutions
