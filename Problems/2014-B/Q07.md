# AMC12 2014-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

For how many positive integers $n$ is $\frac{30-n}{n}$ also a positive integer?

## Answer

**D.** $7$

## Solution

The expression equals
$$\frac{30}{n}-1.$$
It is a positive integer exactly when $n$ is a positive divisor of $30$ and
$$\frac{30}{n}\ge2,$$
so $n\le15$. The eligible divisors are
$$1,2,3,5,6,10,15,$$
giving $7$ values.

## Knowledge points

- Divisibility
- Positive-integer constraints

## Key idea

Rewrite the expression so the divisors of $30$ are visible.

## Common mistakes

- Counting $30$ itself
- Allowing a zero result
