# AMC12 2014-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

Danica drives a whole number of hours at $55$ miles per hour. The initial odometer reading is the three-digit number $\overline{abc}$, with $a\ge1$ and $a+b+c\le7$. The final reading is $\overline{cba}$. What is $a^2+2b^2+c^2$?

## Answer

**D.** $37$

## Solution

The odometer difference is
$$\overline{cba}-\overline{abc}=99(c-a).$$
The distance is a multiple of $55$, so it is also a multiple of $11$. Since it is at most three digits, it must be a multiple of $55$ and $99(c-a)$; the only viable difference is $495$, giving $c-a=5$. The digit-sum condition then forces
$$(a,b,c)=(1,0,6).$$
$$a^2+2b^2+c^2=1+0+36=37.$$

## Knowledge points

- Place value
- Digit reversal
- Divisibility

## Key idea

Convert the odometer difference into a multiple of $99$.

## Common mistakes

- Confusing the printed expression with the digit sum
- Ignoring the whole-hour speed condition
