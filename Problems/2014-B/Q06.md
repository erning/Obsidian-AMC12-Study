# AMC12 2014-B Q06

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Ed orders a regular lemonade. Ann's large lemonade is $50\%$ more than the regular. After both consume $\frac34$ of their drinks, Ann gives Ed one-third of what she has left and $2$ additional ounces. They then finish their drinks and discover that they drank equal amounts. How many ounces did they drink together?

## Answer

**D.** $40$

## Solution

Let a regular drink contain $a$ ounces. Ann's drink contains $\frac32a$. After consuming $\frac34$ of their drinks, Ann has
$$\frac14\cdot\frac32a=\frac38a$$
left. The amount she gives Ed is
$$\frac13\cdot\frac38a+2=\frac18a+2.$$
Ed ultimately drinks $a$ plus this gift, while Ann drinks $\frac32a$ minus it. Hence
$$a+\left(\frac18a+2\right)=\frac32a-\left(\frac18a+2\right),$$
which gives $a=16$. Ed drinks $16$ ounces and Ann drinks $24$, totaling $40$.

## Knowledge points

- Percentages
- Algebraic modeling
- Conservation of quantity

## Key idea

Track the final amount drunk by each person after the transfer.

## Common mistakes

- Comparing only the amounts before the transfer
- Forgetting that Ed finishes his own regular drink
