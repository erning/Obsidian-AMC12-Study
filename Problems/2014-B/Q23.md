# AMC12 2014-B Q23

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q23.png]]

## Problem

The prime $2017$ is given. Let
$$S=\sum_{k=0}^{62}\binom{2014}{k}.$$
What is the remainder when $S$ is divided by $2017$?

## Answer

**C.** $1024$

## Solution

Modulo $2017$, use
$$\binom{2014}{k}=\frac{2014!}{k!(2014-k)!}.$$
Since $2017$ is prime, the factorial expression reduces to
$$\binom{2014}{k}\equiv(-1)^k\binom{2014}{k+2}\pmod{2017}.$$
Therefore
$$
S\equiv1+\sum_{k=1}^{31}\left(\binom2{2k+2}-\binom2{2k+1}\right)
=1+(3+5+\cdots+63)=1024.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Modular arithmetic
- Binomial coefficients
- Prime moduli

## Key idea

Transform the binomial coefficient using factorials modulo the prime.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting the shift by $2$
- Treating the congruence as an equality over the integers
