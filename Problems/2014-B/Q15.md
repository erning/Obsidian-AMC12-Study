# AMC12 2014-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

Let
$$p=\sum_{k=1}^{6}k\ln k,$$
and suppose $e^p$ is an integer. What is the largest power of $2$ that divides $e^p$?

## Answer

**C.** $2^{16}$

## Solution

Using $e^{\ln k}=k$,
$$e^p=\prod_{k=1}^{6}k^k=1^1\cdot2^2\cdot3^3\cdot4^4\cdot5^5\cdot6^6.$$
The exponent of $2$ is
$$2+4\cdot2+6=16,$$
coming from $2^2$, $4^4$, and $6^6$. Therefore the largest power is $2^{16}$.

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm and exponential identities
- Prime exponents

## Key idea

Convert the sum of logarithms into a product and count factors of $2$.

## Common mistakes

- Counting $4^4$ as only four factors of $2$
- Including the factor $5$
