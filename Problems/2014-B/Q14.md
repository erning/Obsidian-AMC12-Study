# AMC12 2014-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

A rectangular box has surface area $94$ square inches. The sum of the lengths of all its edges is $48$ inches. What is the sum of the lengths of all its interior diagonals?

## Answer

**D.** $20\sqrt2$

## Solution

Let the edge lengths be $x,y,z$. Then
$$xy+yz+zx=47,\qquad x+y+z=12.$$
Therefore
$$x^2+y^2+z^2=(x+y+z)^2-2(xy+yz+zx)=144-94=50.$$
Each of the four space diagonals has length
$$\sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}=5\sqrt2.$$
The total is $4\cdot5\sqrt2=20\sqrt2$.

## Knowledge points

- Surface area
- Symmetric identities
- Space diagonals

## Key idea

Use the given sum and pairwise-product sum to obtain $x^2+y^2+z^2$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $94$ instead of $47$ for $xy+yz+zx$
- Counting twelve edges as diagonals
