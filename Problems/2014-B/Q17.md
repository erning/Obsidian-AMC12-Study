# AMC12 2014-B Q17

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Let $P$ be the parabola $y=x^2$ and $Q=(20,14)$. There are real numbers $r<s$ such that the line through $Q$ with slope $m$ does not intersect $P$ if and only if $r<m<s$. What is $r+s$?

## Answer

**E.** $80$

## Solution

The line through $Q$ is
$$y=m(x-20)+14.$$
Intersecting with $y=x^2$ gives
$$x^2-mx+(20m-14)=0.$$
There is no intersection when the discriminant is negative:
$$m^2-4(20m-14)<0.$$
The endpoints $r,s$ are the roots of
$$m^2-80m+56=0.$$
By Vieta's formulas,
$$r+s=80.$$

## Knowledge points

- Quadratic discriminants
- Vieta's formulas

## Key idea

The no-intersection slopes form the interval where the quadratic discriminant is negative.

## Common mistakes

- Checking the discriminant of the wrong quadratic
- Solving for $r$ and $s$ individually
