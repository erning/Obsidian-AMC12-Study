# AMC12 2014-B Q19

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q19.png]]

## Problem

A sphere is inscribed in a truncated right circular cone. The frustum volume is twice the sphere volume. What is the ratio of the bottom radius to the top radius?

## Answer

**E.** $\frac{3+\sqrt5}{2}$

## Solution

Let the top radius be $1$, the bottom radius be $r$, and the sphere radius be $a$. Because the sphere touches both bases, the frustum height is $2a$. In an axial cross-section, the horizontal radius change is $r-1$ and the vertical distance from the sphere center to the side is $a$, so the right-triangle relation gives
$$r=a^2.$$
The frustum volume is
$$\frac13\pi(r^2+r+1)(2a),$$
while twice the sphere volume is
$$2\cdot\frac43\pi a^3=\frac83\pi a^3.$$
Equating them and substituting $r=a^2$ gives
$$a^4-3a^2+1=0,$$
or
$$r^2-3r+1=0.$$
Since $r>1$,
$$r=\frac{3+\sqrt5}{2}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Frustum volume
- Tangency geometry
- Quadratic equations

## Key idea

Use the axial cross-section and then impose the volume condition.

## Common mistakes

- Confusing top radius with the ratio
- Using cone volume instead of frustum volume
