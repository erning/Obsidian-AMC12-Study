# AMC12 2014-B Q24

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q24.png]]

## Problem

Let $ABCDE$ be a cyclic pentagon with $AB=CD=3$, $BC=DE=10$, and $AE=14$. The sum of all diagonals is $\frac mn$ in lowest terms. What is $m+n$?

## Answer

**D.** $391$

## Solution

Equal arcs imply
$$AC=BD=CE=x,\qquad AD=y,\qquad BE=z.$$
Let the remaining diagonal be $z$. Ptolemy's theorem on three cyclic quadrilaterals gives
$$10y+9=x^2,\qquad100+3z=x^2,\qquad30+14x=yz.$$
Eliminating $y,z$ yields
$$x^3-109x-420=0=(x-12)(x+5)(x+7),$$
so $x=12$. Then
$$y=\frac{27}{2},\qquad z=\frac{44}{3}.$$
The diagonal sum is
$$
3x+y+z=36+\frac{27}{2}+\frac{44}{3}
=\frac{385}{6}.
$$
Thus $m+n=385+6=391$.

## Knowledge points

- Cyclic quadrilaterals
- Ptolemy's theorem
- Rational simplification

## Key idea

Use Ptolemy on carefully chosen four-point subsets of the pentagon.

## Common mistakes

- Assigning equal lengths to sides instead of equal arcs
- Omitting one of the five diagonals
