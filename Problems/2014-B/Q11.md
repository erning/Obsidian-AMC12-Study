# AMC12 2014-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2014-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

A list of $11$ positive integers has mean $10$, median $9$, and unique mode $8$. What is the largest possible integer in the list?

## Answer

**E.** $35$

## Solution

The total sum is $11\cdot10=110$. To maximize one entry, minimize the other ten while preserving median $9$ and unique mode $8$. The smallest suitable ten entries are
$$1,1,8,8,8,9,9,10,10,11,$$
whose sum is $75$. The remaining entry is
$$110-75=35.$$

## Knowledge points

- Mean and median
- Mode constraints
- Extremal construction

## Key idea

Minimize all entries except the one being maximized.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting that the mode must be unique
- Putting fewer than six entries at or below the median
