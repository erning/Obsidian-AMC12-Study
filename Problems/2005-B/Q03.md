# AMC12 2005-B Q03

## Problem

Brianna is using part of the money she earned on her weekend job to buy several equally-priced CDs. She used one fifth of her money to buy one third of the CDs. What fraction of her money will she have left after she buys all the CDs?

(A) $\dfrac{1}{5}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{3}$
(C) $\dfrac{2}{5}$
(D) $\dfrac{2}{3}$
(E) $\dfrac{4}{5}$

## Answer

**C.** $\dfrac{2}{5}$

## Solution

Buying all the CDs costs three times what one third of them cost, namely $3\cdot\dfrac15 = \dfrac35$ of her money.

She has $1 - \dfrac35 = \dfrac25$ of her money left.

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Fraction
- Ratio and proportion

## Key idea

Put the quantities over a common denominator before comparing them.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
