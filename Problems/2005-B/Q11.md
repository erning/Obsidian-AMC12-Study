# AMC12 2005-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2005-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

An envelope contains eight bills: $2$ ones, $2$ fives, $2$ tens, and $2$ twenties. Two bills are drawn at random without replacement. What is the probability that their sum is $\$20$ or more?

(A) $\dfrac{1}{4}$
(B) $\dfrac{2}{7}$
(C) $\dfrac{3}{7}$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{2}$
(E) $\dfrac{2}{3}$

## Answer

**D.** $\dfrac{1}{2}$

## Solution

There are $\binom{8}{2} = 28$ equally likely pairs of bills.

The sum is $\$20$ or more in these cases: both twenties ($1$ way), one twenty with one of the six smaller bills ($2 \cdot 6 = 12$ ways), or both tens ($1$ way).

That is $1 + 12 + 1 = 14$ favorable pairs, so the probability is $\dfrac{14}{28} = \dfrac12.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

Count equally likely outcomes and divide favorable by total.
