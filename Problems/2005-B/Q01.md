# AMC12 2005-B Q01

![[assets/problems/2005-B/Q01.png]]

## Problem

A scout troop buys $1000$ candy bars at a price of five for $\$2.$ They sell all the candy bars at a price of two for $\$1.$ What was their profit, in dollars?

(A) $100$
(B) $200$
(C) $300$
(D) $400$
(E) $500$

## Answer

**A.** $100$

## Solution

The troop buys $1000 \div 5 = 200$ groups of five bars, costing $200 \cdot 2 = 400$ dollars.

They sell $1000 \div 2 = 500$ pairs of bars, earning $500 \cdot 1 = 500$ dollars.

The profit is $500 - 400 = 100$ dollars.

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Ratio and proportion
- Money

## Key idea

Introduce a scale factor for the corresponding quantities.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
