# AMC12 2005-B Q17

![[assets/problems/2005-B/Q17.png]]

## Problem

How many distinct four-tuples $(a, b, c, d)$ of rational numbers are there with
$$
 \begin{aligned} &a\log_{10} 2 + b\log_{10} 3 \\ &\quad {}+ c\log_{10} 5 + d\log_{10} 7 = 2005? \end{aligned}
$$

(A) $0$
(B) $1$
(C) $17$
(D) $2004$
(E) infinitely many

## Answer

**B.** $1$

## Solution

The equation is equivalent to $\log_{10}\left(2^a 3^b 5^c 7^d\right) = 2005,$ so
$$
 2^a 3^b 5^c 7^d = 10^{2005} = 2^{2005} \cdot 5^{2005}.
$$

Clearing the denominators of $a, b, c, d$ with a common integer multiplier and using the uniqueness of prime factorization, the exponents must match: $a = 2005,$ $b = 0,$ $c = 2005,$ and $d = 0.$

So there is exactly $1$ such four-tuple.

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

- Prime factorization
