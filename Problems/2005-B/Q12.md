# AMC12 2005-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2005-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

The quadratic equation $x^2 + mx + n = 0$ has roots that are twice those of $x^2 + px + m = 0,$ and none of $m,$ $n,$ and $p$ is zero. What is the value of $\dfrac{n}{p}?$

(A) $1$
(B) $2$
(C) $4$
(D) $8$
(E) $16$

## Answer

**D.** $8$

## Solution

Let $r_1$ and $r_2$ be the roots of $x^2 + px + m = 0,$ so $m = r_1 r_2$ and $p = -(r_1 + r_2).$

The roots of $x^2 + mx + n = 0$ are $2r_1$ and $2r_2,$ so $n = 4r_1 r_2$ and $m = -2(r_1 + r_2).$

Then $n = 4m$ and $m = 2p,$ which gives $p = \dfrac{m}{2},$ so
$$
 \dfrac{n}{p} = \dfrac{4m}{\tfrac{m}{2}} = 8.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.
