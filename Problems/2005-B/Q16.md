# AMC12 2005-B Q16

![[assets/problems/2005-B/Q16.png]]

## Problem

Eight spheres of radius $1,$ one per octant, are each tangent to the coordinate planes. What is the radius of the smallest sphere, centered at the origin, that contains these eight spheres?

(A) $\sqrt{2}$
(B) $\sqrt{3}$
(C) $1 + \sqrt{2}$
(D) $1 + \sqrt{3}$
(E) $3$

## Answer

**D.** $1 + \sqrt{3}$

## Solution

A sphere of radius $1$ tangent to the three coordinate planes in one octant has its center at a point like $(1, 1, 1),$ at distance
$$
 \sqrt{1^2 + 1^2 + 1^2} = \sqrt3
$$
 from the origin.

The farthest point of that sphere from the origin is at distance $\sqrt3 + 1,$ so the containing sphere has radius $1 + \sqrt3.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.
