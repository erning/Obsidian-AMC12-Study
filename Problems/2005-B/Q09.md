# AMC12 2005-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2005-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

On a certain math exam, $10\%$ of the students got $70$ points, $25\%$ got $80$ points, $20\%$ got $85$ points, $15\%$ got $90$ points, and the rest got $95$ points. What is the difference between the mean and the median score on this exam?

(A) $0$
(B) $1$
(C) $2$
(D) $4$
(E) $5$

## Answer

**B.** $1$

## Solution

The percentage scoring $95$ is $100 - 10 - 25 - 20 - 15 = 30.$

The mean is
$$
 \begin{aligned} &0.10(70) + 0.25(80) \\ &\quad {}+ 0.20(85) + 0.15(90) \\ &\quad {}+ 0.30(95) = 86. \end{aligned}
$$

Cumulatively, $10\%$ are below $80,$ $35\%$ are at or below $80,$ and $55\%$ are at or below $85.$ The middle scores fall at $85,$ so the median is $85.$

The difference is $86 - 85 = 1.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.
