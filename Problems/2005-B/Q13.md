# AMC12 2005-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2005-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Suppose that $4^{x_1} = 5,$ $5^{x_2} = 6,$ $6^{x_3} = 7, \ldots, 127^{x_{124}} = 128.$ What is $x_1 x_2 \cdots x_{124}?$

(A) $2$
(B) $\dfrac{5}{2}$
(C) $3$
(D) $\dfrac{7}{2}$
(E) $4$

## Answer

**D.** $\dfrac{7}{2}$

## Solution

From $4^{x_1} = 5$ we get $x_1 = \log_4 5,$ and in general $x_k = \log_{k+3}(k+4).$

The product telescopes:
$$
 \begin{aligned} &x_1 x_2 \cdots x_{124} \\ &= \log_4 5 \cdot \log_5 6 \cdots \log_{127} 128 \\ &= \log_4 128. \end{aligned}
$$

Since $128 = 2^7$ and $4 = 2^2,$ this equals $\dfrac{7\log 2}{2\log 2} = \dfrac72.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm
- Telescoping

## Key idea

Apply logarithm rules and simplify the resulting expression.
