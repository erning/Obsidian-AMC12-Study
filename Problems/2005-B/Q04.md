# AMC12 2005-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2005-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

At the beginning of the school year, Lisa's goal was to earn an A on at least $80\%$ of her $50$ quizzes for the year. She earned an A on $22$ of the first $30$ quizzes. If she is to achieve her goal, on at most how many of the remaining quizzes can she earn a grade lower than an A?

(A) $1$
(B) $2$
(C) $3$
(D) $4$
(E) $5$

## Answer

**B.** $2$

## Solution

Lisa needs an A on at least $0.8 \cdot 50 = 40$ quizzes.

She has $22$ already, so she needs $40 - 22 = 18$ more of the remaining $20$ quizzes.

She can earn a lower grade on at most $20 - 18 = 2$ of them.

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Percentage

## Key idea

Represent the percentage change using the same original quantity.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
