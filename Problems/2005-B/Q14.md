# AMC12 2005-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2005-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

A circle having center $(0, k),$ with $k \gt 6,$ is tangent to the lines $y = x,$ $y = -x$ and $y = 6.$ What is the radius of this circle?

(A) $6\sqrt{2} - 6$
(B) $6$
(C) $6\sqrt{2}$
(D) $12$
(E) $6 + 6\sqrt{2}$

## Answer

**E.** $6 + 6\sqrt{2}$

## Solution

Since the circle is tangent to $y = 6$ and its center $(0, k)$ is above that line, the radius is $r = k - 6.$

The distance from $(0, k)$ to the line $x - y = 0$ is $\dfrac{|0 - k|}{\sqrt2} = \dfrac{k}{\sqrt2},$ and this must also equal $r.$

Setting $\dfrac{k}{\sqrt2} = k - 6$ gives $k = \dfrac{6\sqrt2}{\sqrt2 - 1}$ $= 6\sqrt2\,(\sqrt2+1)$ $= 12 + 6\sqrt2.$

Then $r = k - 6 = 6 + 6\sqrt2.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.
