# AMC12 2005-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2005-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

What is the area enclosed by the graph of $|3x| + |4y| = 12?$

(A) $6$
(B) $12$
(C) $16$
(D) $24$
(E) $25$

## Answer

**D.** $24$

## Solution

Setting $y = 0$ gives $|3x| = 12,$ so $x = \pm 4.$ Setting $x = 0$ gives $|4y| = 12,$ so $y = \pm 3.$

The graph is a rhombus with vertices $(\pm 4, 0)$ and $(0, \pm 3),$ so its diagonals have lengths $8$ and $6.$

Its area is $\dfrac12 \cdot 8 \cdot 6 = 24.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Absolute value
- Rhombus

## Key idea

Split the absolute-value equation into its possible linear cases.
