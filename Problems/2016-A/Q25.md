# AMC12 2016-A Q25

![[assets/problems/2016-A/Q25.png]]

## Problem

For even $k$, Bernardo writes consecutive squares with $k+1$ digits and Silvia erases the last $k$ digits. Find the digit sum of $f(2)+f(4)+\cdots+f(2016)$.

## Answer

**E.** $8064$

## Solution

Write $k=2j$. The first relevant square is $(10^j)^2$, and the displayed values are $\lfloor n^2/10^{2j}\rfloor$. The first jump of at least $2$ occurs after the last value contributing
$$f(2j)=\frac{10^{2j}}4+10^j.$$
Summing for $j=1,\ldots,1008$ creates a block of $1008$ copies of $25$ and a block of $1008$ ones, with no carries. The digit sum is
$$1008(2+5)+1008=1008\cdot8=8064.$$

## Knowledge points

- Perfect squares and floor functions
- Digit-pattern summation

## Key idea

Analyze the first jump in $\lfloor n^2/10^{2j}\rfloor$ and then sum the resulting decimal blocks.

## Common mistakes

- Summing the erased values instead of $f(k)$
- Ignoring carries in the decimal blocks
