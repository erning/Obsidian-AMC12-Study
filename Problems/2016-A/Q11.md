# AMC12 2016-A Q11

![[assets/problems/2016-A/Q11.png]]

## Problem

Among $100$ students, $42$ cannot sing, $65$ cannot dance, and $29$ cannot act. No student has all three talents. How many have exactly two talents?

## Answer

**E.** $64$

## Solution

The numbers who can sing, dance, and act are $58,35,71$, respectively. Their total is
$$58+35+71=164.$$
Students with one talent are counted once and those with two talents twice. Since all $100$ students are counted at least once, the excess $164-100=64$ is exactly the number with two talents.

## Knowledge points

- Inclusion-exclusion style counting
- Counting multiplicities

## Key idea

Compare the total number of talent incidences with the number of students.

## Common mistakes

- Counting students with at least two talents
- Subtracting from $100$ in the wrong direction
