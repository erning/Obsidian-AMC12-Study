# AMC12 2016-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2016-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

A quadrilateral is inscribed in a circle of radius $200\sqrt2$. Three sides have length $200$. Find the fourth side.

## Answer

**E.** $500$

## Solution

For a side of length $200$, the subtended central angle $\theta$ satisfies
$$200^2=2(200\sqrt2)^2(1-\cos\theta),$$
so $\cos\theta=3/4$. The fourth side subtends $3\theta$, and
$$\cos3\theta=4(3/4)^3-3(3/4)=-\frac9{16}.$$
Its square is
$$2R^2(1-\cos3\theta)=160000\cdot\frac{25}{16}=250000,$$
so the length is $500$.

## Knowledge points

- Chord length and central angles
- Triple-angle identity

## Key idea

Three equal sides subtend equal central angles; the fourth subtends $3\theta$.

## Common mistakes

- Using the inscribed angle instead of the central angle
- Taking the wrong square root
