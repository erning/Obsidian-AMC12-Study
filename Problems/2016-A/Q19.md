# AMC12 2016-A Q19

![[assets/problems/2016-A/Q19.png]]

## Problem

A fair coin is tossed eight times, moving $+1$ for heads and $-1$ for tails. What is the probability of reaching $4$ at some time?

## Answer

**B.** $151$

## Solution

With $0,1,$ or $2$ tails, the walk certainly reaches $4$, giving
$$\binom80+\binom81+\binom82=37$$
sequences. With $3$ tails, reaching $4$ requires at most one tail among the first five tosses, giving $8$ sequences. With $4$ tails, only $HHHHTTTT$ works, giving $1$. Thus there are $46$ favorable sequences:
$$\frac{46}{256}=\frac{23}{128},$$
and $23+128=151$.

## Knowledge points

- Random walks
- Counting paths by number of tails

## Key idea

Classify paths by total tails, then impose the first-hit condition.

## Common mistakes

- Counting only final position
- Assuming every sequence with four heads reaches $4$
