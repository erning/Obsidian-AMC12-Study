# AMC12 2016-A Q12

![[assets/problems/2016-A/Q12.png]]

## Problem

In $\triangle ABC$, $AB=6$, $BC=7$, $CA=8$. The angle bisectors from $A$ and $B$ meet at $F$. Find $AF:FD$, where $D$ is on $BC$.

## Answer

**C.** $2:1$

## Solution

By the Angle Bisector Theorem,
$$BD:DC=AB:AC=6:8,$$
so $BD=3$. In $\triangle ABD$, $BF$ bisects angle $ABD$, hence
$$AF:FD=AB:BD=6:3=2:1.$$

## Knowledge points

- Angle Bisector Theorem
- Applying a theorem in a subtriangle

## Key idea

Apply the theorem first in $\triangle ABC$, then again in $\triangle ABD$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $BC$ instead of $BD$
- Applying the first ratio to the wrong angle
