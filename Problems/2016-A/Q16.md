# AMC12 2016-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2016-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

How many points with positive $x$ lie on at least two of $y=\log_3x$, $y=\log_x3$, $y=\log_{1/3}x$, and $y=\log_x(1/3)$?

## Answer

**D.** $5$

## Solution

Let $u=\log_3x$. The four functions become
$$u,\quad\frac1u,\quad -u,\quad -\frac1u.$$
Intersections occur at $u=\pm1$ and $u=0$ (the latter gives the two logarithm graphs equal to $0$). Thus the distinct positive $x$ values are $x=1,3,1/3$, with the corresponding intersection points giving a total of $5$ points.

## Knowledge points

- Change of variables in logarithms
- Intersection counting

## Key idea

Express all four graphs using one variable $u=\log_3x$.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $x=1$ as undefined in the original graphs
- Counting the same point from multiple pairings
