# AMC12 2016-A Q15

![[assets/problems/2016-A/Q15.png]]

## Problem

Three tangent circles have radii $1,2,3$ and tangent points $P',Q',R'$ on a line, with $Q'$ between the other two. Circle $Q$ is tangent to both other circles. Find $[PQR]$.

## Answer

**D.** $\sqrt6-\sqrt2$

## Solution

The center heights above the tangent line are $1,2,3$. Since $PQ=1+2=3$, the horizontal separation is
$$P'Q'=\sqrt{3^2-(2-1)^2}=2\sqrt2.$$
Similarly $QR=5$, so
$$Q'R'=\sqrt{5^2-(3-2)^2}=2\sqrt6.$$
Using coordinates $P=(0,1)$, $Q=(2\sqrt2,2)$, $R=(2\sqrt2+2\sqrt6,3)$ and the shoelace formula gives
$$[PQR]=\sqrt6-\sqrt2.$$

## Knowledge points

- Tangent circles
- Coordinate area

## Key idea

Use right triangles formed by the radii and horizontal center separations.

## Common mistakes

- Using radius differences as center distances
- Forgetting the centers are one unit higher at each step
