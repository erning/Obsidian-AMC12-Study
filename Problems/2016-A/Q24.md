# AMC12 2016-A Q24

![[assets/problems/2016-A/Q24.png]]

## Problem

Find the unique $b$ for the smallest positive $a$ such that all roots of $x^3-ax^2+bx-a$ are real.

## Answer

**B.** $9$

## Solution

Let the positive roots be $r,s,t$. Vieta gives
$$r+s+t=a,\qquad rst=a.$$
Thus $r+s+t=rst$. AM-GM gives
$$27rst\le(r+s+t)^3=(rst)^3,$$
so $a=rst\ge27$, with equality at $r=s=t=3$. Then
$$b=rs+rt+st=3\cdot3^2=9.$$

## Knowledge points

- Vieta's formulas
- AM-GM inequality

## Key idea

The smallest $a$ occurs at equality in AM-GM.

## Common mistakes

- Allowing negative roots without checking the signs
- Confusing $b$ with the product of roots
