# AMC12 2016-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2016-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Three independent uniform numbers in $[0,1]$ are chosen. Find the probability they are side lengths of a nondegenerate triangle.

## Answer

**C.** $\frac12$

## Solution

The ordered triples form a unit cube. Failure occurs when one number is at least the sum of the other two. Each failure region is a tetrahedron of volume $1/6$, and the three regions have disjoint interiors. Thus the failure probability is $3/6=1/2$, so the desired probability is $1/2$.

## Knowledge points

- Geometric probability
- Triangle inequality

## Key idea

Interpret the three-dimensional sample space geometrically.

## Common mistakes

- Counting degenerate triangles as valid
- Overlapping the three failure regions
