# AMC12 2016-A Q18

![[assets/problems/2016-A/Q18.png]]

## Problem

For positive integer $n$, $110n^3$ has $110$ divisors. How many divisors does $81n^4$ have?

## Answer

**D.** $325$

## Solution

Since $110=2\cdot5\cdot11$, the three exponents in $110n^3$ must be $1,4,10$ in some order. Assigning these to $2,5,11$ gives
$$n^3=2^0\cdot5^3\cdot11^9,$$
so $n=5\cdot11^3$. Therefore
$$81n^4=3^4\cdot5^4\cdot11^{12},$$
and the divisor count is
$$(4+1)(4+1)(12+1)=325.$$

## Knowledge points

- Prime factorization
- Divisor-count formula

## Key idea

The exponents of the three primes must have product $(110)$ after adding one.

## Common mistakes

- Assigning exponents to the wrong prime factors
- Forgetting that $n^3$ changes exponents by multiples of $3$
