# AMC12 2016-A Q09

![[assets/problems/2016-A/Q09.png]]

## Problem

Five congruent shaded squares lie inside a unit square as shown. Their common side length is $(a-2)/b$. Find $a+b$.

## Answer

**E.** $11$

## Solution

Let the small-square side be $x$. A diagonal of the unit square has length $\sqrt2$ and consists of two small-square diagonals plus one side:
$$2\sqrt2x+x=\sqrt2.$$
Solving,
$$x=\frac{4-2\sqrt2}{7}=\frac{a-2}{b},$$
so $a=4$, $b=7$, and $a+b=11$.

## Knowledge points

- Similar lengths in a diagram
- Rationalizing denominators

## Key idea

Measure the unit-square diagonal along the displayed chain of small squares.

## Common mistakes

- Counting a small-square diagonal as a side
- Choosing an unsimplified equivalent fraction
