# AMC12 2016-A Q17

![[assets/problems/2016-A/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Equilateral triangles are erected externally on the four sides of a square. Their centers form square $EFGH$. Find the area ratio $[EFGH]/[ABCD]$.

## Answer

**B.** $\frac{2+\sqrt3}{3}$

## Solution

Let the original square side be $6$. Each equilateral triangle has height $3\sqrt3$, and its center is one-third of that height, namely $\sqrt3$, from the square side. The diagonal of $EFGH$ is therefore $6+2\sqrt3$, while the original diagonal is $6\sqrt2$. Hence
$$\frac{[EFGH]}{[ABCD]}=\left(\frac{6+2\sqrt3}{6\sqrt2}\right)^2=\frac{2+\sqrt3}{3}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Equilateral triangle centers
- Area ratios from diagonals

## Key idea

Compare the diagonals of the two squares.

## Common mistakes

- Using the triangle height instead of the center distance
- Forgetting to square the diagonal ratio
