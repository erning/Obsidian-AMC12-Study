# AMC12 2016-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2016-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Describe the graph of $x^2(x+y+1)=y^2(x+y+1)$.

## Answer

**D.** Three lines that do not all pass through a common point

## Solution

Move everything to one side:
$$(x^2-y^2)(x+y+1)=0,$$
so
$$(x-y)(x+y)(x+y+1)=0.$$
The graph is three lines: $x=y$, $x=-y$, and $x+y=-1$. The first two meet at the origin, while the third is parallel to $x+y=0$, so they do not all meet at one point.

## Knowledge points

- Factoring algebraic curves
- Intersections of lines

## Key idea

Factor before interpreting the graph.

## Common mistakes

- Canceling $x+y+1$
- Assuming all three lines are concurrent
