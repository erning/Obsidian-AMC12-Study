# AMC12 2016-A Q08

![[assets/problems/2016-A/Q08.png]]

## Problem

Find the shaded area in the given $8\times5$ rectangle.

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{13}{2}$

## Solution

The two shaded pieces along the diagonal can be split into four triangles. The two with horizontal base $1$ have total area
$$2\cdot\frac12\cdot1\cdot\frac52=\frac52.$$
The two with vertical base $1$ have total area
$$2\cdot\frac12\cdot1\cdot4=4.$$
Thus the shaded area is
$$\frac52+4=\frac{13}{2}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Triangle area
- Decomposing a composite region

## Key idea

Use the rectangle's center and the diagonal to find the triangle altitudes.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the shaded region as one triangle
- Using the full side as an altitude
