# AMC12 2014-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

Walking down Jane Street, Ralph passed four houses in a row, each painted a different color. He passed the orange house before the red house, and the blue house before the yellow house. The blue house was not next to the yellow house. How many orderings are possible?

## Answer

**B.** $3$

## Solution

If orange is first, the order constraints and the nonadjacency condition force
$$O,B,R,Y.$$
If blue is first, yellow cannot be second. The two possibilities are
$$B,O,Y,R\quad\text{and}\quad B,O,R,Y.$$
No other first color is possible: orange must precede red and blue must precede yellow. Hence there are $3$ orderings.

## Knowledge points

- Permutations
- Casework
- Adjacency restrictions

## Key idea

Split according to which of the two required-first colors appears first.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the blue-yellow pair as an unordered pair
- Allowing blue and yellow to be adjacent
