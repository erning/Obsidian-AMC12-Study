# AMC12 2014-A Q04

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q04.png]]

## Problem

Suppose that $a$ cows give $b$ gallons of milk in $c$ days. At this rate, how many gallons will $d$ cows give in $e$ days?

## Answer

**A.** $\frac{bde}{ac}$

## Solution

The rate per cow per day is
$$\frac{b}{ac}.$$
Multiplying by $d$ cows and $e$ days gives
$$\frac{b}{ac}\cdot de=\frac{bde}{ac}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Rates
- Proportional reasoning

## Key idea

Normalize the production to one cow for one day.

## Common mistakes

- Inverting the number of days
- Scaling by $a$ instead of $d$
