# AMC12 2014-A Q01

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q01.png]]

## Problem

What is
$$10\left(\frac12+\frac15+\frac1{10}\right)^{-1}?$$

## Answer

**C.** $\frac{25}{2}$

## Solution

The sum inside the parentheses is
$$\frac12+\frac15+\frac1{10}=\frac{5+2+1}{10}=\frac45.$$
Taking the reciprocal and multiplying by $10$ gives
$$10\left(\frac45\right)^{-1}=10\cdot\frac54=\frac{25}{2}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Fractions
- Reciprocals
- Order of operations

## Key idea

Combine the fractions before applying the exponent $-1$.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the reciprocal as a negative number
- Forgetting that $10^{-1}$ means $1/10$
