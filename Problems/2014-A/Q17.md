# AMC12 2014-A Q17

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q17.png]]

## Problem

A $4\times4\times h$ rectangular box contains a sphere of radius $2$ and eight smaller spheres of radius $1$. Each smaller sphere is tangent to three sides of the box, and the larger sphere is tangent to each smaller sphere. What is $h$?

## Answer

**A.** $2+2\sqrt7$

## Solution

Place the box with a corner at the origin. The centers of the four top
small spheres form a square of side $2$, whose center is horizontally
$\sqrt2$ from each corner center. The large sphere's center is on the axis,
at distance $2+1=3$ from each top small-sphere center. Thus their vertical
separation is
$$\sqrt{3^2-(\sqrt2)^2}=\sqrt7.$$
The large sphere's center is at height $h/2$, while the top small-sphere
centers are at height $h-1$. Therefore
$$
(h-1)-\frac h2=\sqrt7,
$$
so
$$h=2+2\sqrt7.$$

## Knowledge points

- Three-dimensional distance
- Sphere tangency
- Coordinate geometry

## Key idea

Use the center-to-center distance and the symmetry axis of the box.

## Common mistakes

- Using sphere radii as center distances
- Ignoring the horizontal offset between sphere centers
