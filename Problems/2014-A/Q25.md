# AMC12 2014-A Q25

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q25.png]]

## Problem

The parabola $P$ has focus $(0,0)$ and passes through $(4,3)$ and $(-4,-3)$. For how many integer-coordinate points $(x,y)\in P$ is
$$|4x+3y|\le1000?$$

## Answer

**B.** $40$

## Solution

The midpoint of $(4,3)$ and $(-4,-3)$ is the focus, so the segment joining them is the latus rectum. Its line is
$$4y-3x+25=0,$$
and the directrix is the parallel line at distance $5$ on the far side:
$$4y-3x+25=0.$$
Equating distance to the focus and distance to the directrix gives
$$
(4x+3y)^2=25\bigl(25+2(4y-3x)\bigr).
$$
Writing $4x+3y=5s$ forces $s$ to be an odd multiple of $5$. With
$s=5(2u+1)$, the integer points are parametrized by
$$
x=-6u^2+2u+4,\qquad y=8u^2+14u+3,
$$
where $u$ is an integer. Then
$$|4x+3y|=|50u+25|=25|2u+1|\le1000,$$
so
$$-20\le u\le19.$$
There are $40$ integer values of $u$.

## Knowledge points

- Parabolas and directrices
- Lattice-point parametrization
- Absolute values

## Key idea

Use the focus and the latus rectum to identify the directrix, then parametrize integer points.

## Common mistakes

- Using the segment through the focus as the directrix
- Counting the integer parameter values incorrectly
