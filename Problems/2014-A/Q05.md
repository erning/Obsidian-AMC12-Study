# AMC12 2014-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

On an algebra quiz, $10\%$ of the students scored $70$ points, $35\%$ scored $80$ points, $30\%$ scored $90$ points, and the rest scored $100$ points. What is the difference between the mean and the median?

## Answer

**C.** $3$

## Solution

The remaining percentage is
$$100\%-10\%-35\%-30\%=25\%.$$
At least $50\%$ of the scores are at most $90$, and fewer than $50\%$ are at most $80$, so the median is $90$.
The mean is
$$0.10(70)+0.35(80)+0.30(90)+0.25(100)=87.$$
Therefore the difference is
$$90-87=3.$$

## Knowledge points

- Weighted mean
- Median

## Key idea

Find the median from cumulative percentages before computing the weighted mean.

## Common mistakes

- Taking the average of the four listed scores
- Treating the median as the middle score value without frequencies
