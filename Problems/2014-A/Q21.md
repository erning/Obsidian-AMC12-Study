# AMC12 2014-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

For real $x$, let $\lfloor x\rfloor$ be the greatest integer not exceeding $x$, and let
$$f(x)=\lfloor x\rfloor^{2014^{\,x-\lfloor x\rfloor}}-1.$$
The set of all $x$ such that $1\le x<2014$ and $f(x)\le1$ is a union of disjoint intervals. What is the sum of their lengths?

## Answer

**A.** $1$

## Solution

Write $x=n+r$, where $n=\lfloor x\rfloor$, $1\le n\le2013$, and $0\le r<1$. The inequality becomes
$$n\left(2014^r-1\right)\le1,$$
or
$$2014^r\le1+\frac1n=\frac{n+1}{n}.$$
Thus the interval contributed by the integer part $n$ has length
$$\log_{2014}\left(\frac{n+1}{n}\right).$$
Summing for $n=1,\ldots,2013$ telescopes:
$$
\sum_{n=1}^{2013}\log_{2014}\left(\frac{n+1}{n}\right)
=\log_{2014}\left(\frac{2}{1}\cdot\frac{3}{2}\cdots
\frac{2014}{2013}\right)
=\log_{2014}(2014)=1.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Floor functions
- Logarithmic inequalities
- Telescoping products

## Key idea

Partition the domain into unit intervals according to $\lfloor x\rfloor$.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $\lfloor x\rfloor$ as $x$
- Forgetting that the fractional part lies in $[0,1)$
