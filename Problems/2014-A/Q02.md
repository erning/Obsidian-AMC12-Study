# AMC12 2014-A Q02

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q02.png]]

## Problem

At the theater children get in for half price. The price for $5$ adult tickets and $4$ child tickets is $\$24.50$. How much would $8$ adult tickets and $6$ child tickets cost?

## Answer

**B.** $\$38.50$

## Solution

One child ticket costs half an adult ticket. Thus $5$ adult and $4$ child tickets are equivalent to
$$5+\frac42=7$$
adult tickets, so one adult ticket costs $24.50/7=\$3.50$.
The second purchase is equivalent to
$$8+\frac62=11$$
adult tickets, costing
$$11\cdot\$3.50=\$38.50.$$

## Knowledge points

- Ratios
- Unit prices
- Linear scaling

## Key idea

Convert child tickets into equivalent adult tickets.

## Common mistakes

- Adding child tickets at full price
- Solving for the wrong ticket price
