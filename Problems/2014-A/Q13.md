# AMC12 2014-A Q13

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q13.png]]

## Problem

A fancy bed and breakfast inn has $5$ rooms, each with distinctive color-coded decor. Five friends arrive. They may room in any combination, with no more than $2$ friends per room. In how many ways can the innkeeper assign the guests?

## Answer

**B.** $2220$

## Solution

Count by the number of pairs.

- All singles: assign the five labeled friends to five rooms in $5!=120$ ways.
- One pair and three singles: choose the pair in $\binom52=10$ ways, then assign the four groups to rooms in $5P4=120$ ways, for $1200$.
- Two pairs and one single: choose the single in $5$ ways, partition the remaining four into two unlabeled pairs in $3$ ways, then assign the three groups to rooms in $5P3=60$ ways, for $900$.

The total is
$$120+1200+900=2220.$$

## Knowledge points

- Counting partitions
- Permutations of groups
- Casework

## Key idea

Separate the possible occupancy patterns: $1+1+1+1+1$, $2+1+1+1$, and $2+2+1$.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the two pairs as ordered
- Allowing three friends in one room
