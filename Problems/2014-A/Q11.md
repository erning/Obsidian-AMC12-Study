# AMC12 2014-A Q11

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q11.png]]

## Problem

David drives $35$ miles in the first hour. At that speed he will be $1$ hour late. He increases his speed by $15$ miles per hour and arrives $30$ minutes early. How many miles is the airport from his home?

## Answer

**C.** $210$

## Solution

Let $d$ be the remaining distance after the first hour and let $t$ be the remaining time until the scheduled arrival. At $35$ mph, being one hour late gives
$$d=35(t+1).$$
At $50$ mph, arriving half an hour early gives
$$d=50\left(t-\frac12\right).$$
Equating,
$$35t+35=50t-25,\qquad t=4.$$
Thus $d=35(5)=175$, and the total distance is
$$35+175=210.$$

## Knowledge points

- Rates and time
- Linear equations

## Key idea

Use the same remaining distance under the two speed scenarios.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the first hour as part of the remaining time
- Using $30$ minutes as $30$ hours
