# AMC12 2014-A Q08

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q08.png]]

## Problem

A customer has three coupons, only one of which may be used:

- Coupon 1: $10\%$ off the listed price if the price is at least $\$50$.
- Coupon 2: $\$20$ off if the price is at least $\$100$.
- Coupon 3: $18\%$ off the amount by which the price exceeds $\$100$.

For which listed price does coupon 1 give a greater reduction than both other coupons?

## Answer

**C.** $\$219.95$

## Solution

For a listed price $P>100$, coupon 1 saves $0.10P$, coupon 2 saves $20$, and coupon 3 saves
$$0.18(P-100).$$
Coupon 1 beats coupon 2 when
$$0.10P>20\quad\Longrightarrow\quad P>200.$$
It beats coupon 3 when
$$0.10P>0.18(P-100),$$
which simplifies to $P<225$. Thus the price must lie in $(200,225)$. Among the choices, only $\$219.95$ lies in this interval.

## Knowledge points

- Piecewise linear inequalities
- Percent discounts

## Key idea

Translate each coupon into a savings function and compare the functions.

## Common mistakes

- Applying coupon 3 to the whole price
- Forgetting coupon eligibility thresholds
