# AMC12 2014-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Let $a<b<c$ be three integers such that $a,b,c$ is an arithmetic progression and $a,c,b$ is a geometric progression. What is the smallest possible value of $c$?

## Answer

**C.** $2$

## Solution

Write the arithmetic progression as
$$a,\quad a+d,\quad a+2d,$$
where $d>0$. Thus $b=a+d$ and $c=a+2d$.
The geometric progression $a,c,b$ satisfies
$$c^2=ab.$$
Substitution gives
$$(a+2d)^2=a(a+d),$$
so
$$3ad+4d^2=0,\qquad 3a+4d=0.$$
Let $d=3k$. Then $a=-4k$, $b=-k$, and $c=2k$. The smallest positive $c$ occurs at $k=1$, giving $c=2$.

## Knowledge points

- Arithmetic progressions
- Geometric progressions
- Integer parametrization

## Key idea

Use the arithmetic progression to express all three variables in terms of $a$ and $d$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $b^2=ac$ for the wrong order of the geometric progression
- Assuming all three integers must be positive
