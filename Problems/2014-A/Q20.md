# AMC12 2014-A Q20

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q20.png]]

## Problem

In triangle $BAC$, $\angle BAC=40^\circ$, $AB=10$, and $AC=6$. Points $D$ and $E$ lie on $AB$ and $AC$, respectively. What is the minimum possible value of $BE+DE+CD$?

## Answer

**D.** $14$

## Solution

Reflect $B$ across line $AC$ to $B'$ and reflect $C$ across line $AB$ to $C'$. Reflection preserves the relevant lengths, so the broken path has the same length as
$$BB'+B'E+ED+DC'.$$
The variable path is minimized when the middle portion is straight, giving the distance $B'C'$. The angle between the reflected rays is
$$3\cdot40^\circ=120^\circ.$$
By the Law of Cosines,
$$B'C'^2=10^2+6^2-2(10)(6)\cos120^\circ=136+60=196,$$
so $B'C'=14$.

## Knowledge points

- Reflection and shortest paths
- Law of cosines

## Key idea

Unfold the broken path by reflecting across the sides of the angle.

## Common mistakes

- Reflecting across the wrong side
- Using $40^circ$ instead of the resulting $120^circ$ angle
