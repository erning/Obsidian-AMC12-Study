# AMC12 2014-A Q12

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q12.png]]

## Problem

Two circles intersect at $A$ and $B$. The minor arcs $AB$ measure $30^\circ$ on one circle and $60^\circ$ on the other. What is the ratio of the area of the larger circle to the smaller circle?

## Answer

**D.** $2+\sqrt3$

## Solution

If the radii are $R$ for the circle with the $30^\circ$ arc and $r$ for the circle with the $60^\circ$ arc, the common chord gives
$$2R\sin15^\circ=2r\sin30^\circ.$$
Thus
$$\frac Rr=\frac{\sin30^\circ}{\sin15^\circ}.$$
The $30^\circ$ arc belongs to the larger circle. Therefore the area ratio is
$$\left(\frac Rr\right)^2
=\frac{\sin^2 30^\circ}{\sin^2 15^\circ}
=\frac{1/4}{(2-\sqrt3)/4}
=2+\sqrt3.$$

## Knowledge points

- Chord length
- Central angles
- Trigonometric identities

## Key idea

The same chord relates each radius to the sine of its half-central angle.

## Common mistakes

- Comparing the arc angles directly instead of using chord length
- Choosing the smaller circle incorrectly
