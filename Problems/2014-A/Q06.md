# AMC12 2014-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

The difference between a two-digit number and the number obtained by reversing its digits is $5$ times the sum of the digits. What is the sum of the number and its reverse?

## Answer

**D.** $99$

## Solution

Let the number be $10a+b$, with $a>b$. Then
$$9(a-b)=5(a+b).$$
Thus
$$9a-9b=5a+5b,\qquad 2a=7b.$$
The only digit solution is $a=7$, $b=2$, so the numbers are $72$ and $27$. Their sum is
$$72+27=99.$$

## Knowledge points

- Place value
- Digit equations
- Integer constraints

## Key idea

Express both the difference and digit sum in terms of the tens and ones digits.

## Common mistakes

- Reversing the sign of the difference
- Allowing a leading zero in the reversed number
