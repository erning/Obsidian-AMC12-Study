# AMC12 2014-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2014-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

The first three terms of a geometric progression are $\sqrt3$, $\sqrt[3]3$, and $\sqrt[6]3$. What is the fourth term?

## Answer

**A.** $1$

## Solution

Write the terms as powers of $3$:
$$3^{1/2},\quad3^{1/3},\quad3^{1/6}.$$
The exponents form an arithmetic progression with common difference $-1/6$, so the common ratio is $3^{-1/6}$. The fourth exponent is
$$\frac16-\frac16=0,$$
and the fourth term is $3^0=1$.

## Knowledge points

- Geometric progressions
- Exponent arithmetic

## Key idea

Recognize the geometric ratio by subtracting the exponents.

## Common mistakes

- Multiplying the exponents instead of subtracting them
- Assuming the terms themselves have integer ratios
