# AMC12 2019-A Q25

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q25.png]]

## Problem

Let $\triangle A_0B_0C_0$ be a triangle with angles exactly $59.999^\circ$, $60^\circ$, and $60.001^\circ$. For each positive integer $n$, let $A_n$ be the foot of the altitude from $A_{n-1}$ to line $B_{n-1}C_{n-1}$, and define $B_n,C_n$ cyclically. What is the least positive integer $n$ for which $\triangle A_nB_nC_n$ is obtuse?

## Answer

**E.** $15$

## Solution

The triangle formed by the three altitude feet is the orthic triangle. If an acute triangle has an angle $\alpha$, the corresponding angle of its orthic triangle is

$$180^\circ-2\alpha.$$

Write an angle as $60^\circ+x$. Its new angle is

$$180^\circ-2(60^\circ+x)=60^\circ-2x.$$

Thus each deviation from $60^\circ$ changes sign and doubles in magnitude at every step. The initial deviations have magnitude $0.001^\circ$, so after $n$ steps the magnitude is $0.001\cdot2^n$ degrees.

The triangle first becomes obtuse when this exceeds $30^\circ$:

$$0.001\cdot2^n>30\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad2^n>30000.$$

Because $2^{14}=16384<30000<32768=2^{15}$, the least such $n$ is $15$.

## Knowledge points

- Orthic triangles
- Angle chasing
- Exponential growth

## Key idea

The altitude-foot operation multiplies each angle's deviation from $60^\circ$ by $-2$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $180^\circ-\alpha$ instead of $180^\circ-2\alpha$.
- Checking when the deviation reaches $30^\circ$ without requiring it to exceed $30^\circ$.
