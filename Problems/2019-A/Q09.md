# AMC12 2019-A Q09

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q09.png]]

## Problem

A sequence is defined recursively by $a_1=1$, $a_2=\frac37$, and

$$a_n=\frac{a_{n-2}a_{n-1}}{2a_{n-2}-a_{n-1}}\qquad(n\ge3).$$

Then $a_{2019}=\frac pq$, where $p$ and $q$ are relatively prime positive integers. What is $p+q$?

## Answer

**E.** $8078$

## Solution

Take reciprocals and simplify:

$$\frac1{a_n}=\frac{2a_{n-2}-a_{n-1}}{a_{n-2}a_{n-1}}
=\frac2{a_{n-1}}-\frac1{a_{n-2}}.$$

Let $b_n=1/a_n$. Then

$$b_n=2b_{n-1}-b_{n-2},$$

so $b_n$ is an arithmetic sequence. Since $b_1=1$ and $b_2=7/3$, its common difference is $4/3$. Therefore

$$b_{2019}=1+2018\cdot\frac43=\frac{8075}{3},$$

and hence $a_{2019}=3/8075$. The numerator and denominator are relatively prime, so

$$p+q=3+8075=8078.$$

## Knowledge points

- Reciprocal substitutions
- Linear recurrences
- Arithmetic sequences

## Key idea

The nonlinear recurrence becomes linear after taking reciprocals.

## Common mistakes

- Taking the reciprocal without distributing it over the fraction correctly.
- Using $2019$ instead of the number of steps, $2018$.
