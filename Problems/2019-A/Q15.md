# AMC12 2019-A Q15

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q15.png]]

## Problem

Positive real numbers $a$ and $b$ satisfy

$$\sqrt{\log a}+\sqrt{\log b}+\log\sqrt a+\log\sqrt b=100,$$

where $\log$ denotes the base-$10$ logarithm, and all four terms are positive integers. What is $ab$?

## Answer

**D.** $10^{164}$

## Solution

Let $p=\log a$ and $q=\log b$. Since $\sqrt p$ and $p/2=\log\sqrt a$ are integers, write $p=4m^2$ for a positive integer $m$. Similarly write $q=4n^2$.

The given equation becomes

$$2m+2n+2m^2+2n^2=100,$$

or

$$m(m+1)+n(n+1)=50.$$

The only positive integer pair, up to order, is $(m,n)=(4,5)$. Hence

$$\log(ab)=p+q=4m^2+4n^2=4(16+25)=164.$$

Therefore $ab=10^{164}$.

## Knowledge points

- Properties of logarithms
- Integer constraints
- Diophantine equations

## Key idea

The integrality of both $\sqrt{\log a}$ and $\frac12\log a$ forces $\log a$ to be four times a square.

## Common mistakes

- Writing $p=2m$ without also enforcing that $\sqrt p$ is an integer.
- Confusing $\log\sqrt a$ with $\sqrt{\log a}$.
