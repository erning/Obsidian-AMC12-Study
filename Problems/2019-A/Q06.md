# AMC12 2019-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

The figure shows line $\ell$ with a regular, infinite, recurring pattern of squares and line segments. How many of the following four kinds of rigid motion transformations, other than the identity, transform the figure into itself?

- Some rotation around a point of line $\ell$
- Some translation in the direction parallel to line $\ell$
- Reflection across line $\ell$
- Some reflection across a line perpendicular to line $\ell$

## Answer

**C.** $2$

## Solution

A translation by one full period along $\ell$ maps every square and diagonal segment to the next copy, so the translation works.

A $180^\circ$ rotation about a suitable point on $\ell$ exchanges the squares above and below the line. The diagonal segments also match after the rotation, so this transformation works.

Reflection across $\ell$ does not work: the diagonal orientations below the line are not the reflected orientations of the diagonals above it. Reflection across a perpendicular line likewise reverses the pattern in a way that does not match. Therefore exactly two types work.

## Knowledge points

- Symmetry and rigid motions
- Periodic patterns
- Reflection and rotation

## Key idea

Check the orientation of both the squares and the diagonal segments, not only the repeating spacing.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming reflection across the center line works because the squares appear symmetric.
- Counting transformations rather than the four listed types.
