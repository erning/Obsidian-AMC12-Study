# AMC12 2019-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

A box contains $28$ red balls, $20$ green balls, $19$ yellow balls, $13$ blue balls, $11$ white balls, and $9$ black balls. What is the minimum number of balls that must be drawn without replacement to guarantee that at least $15$ balls of a single color will be drawn?

## Answer

**B.** $76$

## Solution

To delay getting $15$ balls of one color as long as possible, draw at most $14$ red, green, and yellow balls, since each of those colors has at least $15$ balls available. We can draw all balls of the other three colors:

$$14+14+14+13+11+9=75.$$

After $75$ balls it is still possible that no color has appeared $15$ times. The next draw must make some color reach $15$, so the required number is $76$.

## Knowledge points

- Pigeonhole principle
- Worst-case counting
- Guarantees in sampling without replacement

## Key idea

Construct the largest draw that avoids the desired event, then add one.

## Common mistakes

- Limiting every color to $14$ even when that color has fewer than $15$ balls.
- Giving the maximum safe number, $75$, instead of the first guaranteed number, $76$.
