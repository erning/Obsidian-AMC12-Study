# AMC12 2019-A Q01

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q01.png]]

## Problem

The area of a pizza with radius $4$ inches is $N$ percent larger than the area of a pizza with radius $3$ inches. What is the integer closest to $N$?

## Answer

**E.** $78$

## Solution

The areas are proportional to the squares of the radii. The ratio of the larger area to the smaller one is

$$\frac{\pi(4)^2}{\pi(3)^2}=\frac{16}{9}.$$

Therefore the percent increase is

$$\left(\frac{16}{9}-1\right)\times100=\frac{700}{9}\approx77.8.$$

The closest integer is $78$.

## Knowledge points

- Area of a circle
- Percent increase
- Ratios

## Key idea

Compare areas by comparing the squares of the radii.

## Common mistakes

- Comparing the radii directly and obtaining $\frac43$ instead of $\frac{16}{9}$.
- Forgetting to subtract $1$ before converting the ratio to a percent increase.
