# AMC12 2019-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Melanie computes the mean $\mu$, the median $M$, and the modes of the $365$ values that are the dates in the months of 2019. Let $d$ be the median of the modes. Which statement is true?

## Answer

**E.** $d<\mu<M$

## Solution

The dates $1$ through $28$ each occur $12$ times, so they are the modes. Their median is

$$d=\frac{14+15}{2}=14.5.$$

In the ordered data, dates $1$ through $15$ account for $180$ entries, so the $183$rd entry is $16$. Hence $M=16$.

The sum of all dates is

$$12(1+\cdots+28)+11(29+30)+7(31)=5738,$$

so

$$\mu=\frac{5738}{365}\approx15.72.$$

Thus $14.5<15.72<16$, or $d<\mu<M$.

## Knowledge points

- Mean, median, and mode
- Frequency counting
- Ordered data

## Key idea

Count how often each date occurs before computing the three statistics.

## Common mistakes

- Treating every date from $1$ to $31$ as equally frequent.
- Using the calendar's average date instead of the mean of all $365$ entries.
