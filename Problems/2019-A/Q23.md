# AMC12 2019-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Define binary operations $\diamond$ and $\heartsuit$ by

$$a\diamond b=a^{\log_7 b},\qquad a\heartsuit b=a^{1/\log_7 b}.$$

The sequence $(a_n)$ is defined by $a_3=3\heartsuit2$ and

$$a_n=(n\heartsuit(n-1))\diamond a_{n-1}\qquad(n\ge4).$$

To the nearest integer, what is $\log_7(a_{2019})$?

## Answer

**D.** $11$

## Solution

Let $L(x)=\log_7x$. The two operations satisfy

$$L(a\diamond b)=L(a)L(b),\qquad L(a\heartsuit b)=\frac{L(a)}{L(b)}.$$

Therefore

$$L(a_3)=\frac{L(3)}{L(2)},$$

and for $n\ge4$,

$$L(a_n)=\frac{L(n)}{L(n-1)}L(a_{n-1}).$$

The product telescopes:

$$L(a_{2019})=\frac{L(3)}{L(2)}\cdot\frac{L(4)}{L(3)}\cdots\frac{L(2019)}{L(2018)}
=\frac{L(2019)}{L(2)}=\log_2 2019.$$

Since $2^{10}=1024<2019<2048=2^{11}$ and $2019$ is close to $2048$, $\log_2 2019\approx10.98$, which rounds to $11$.

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm laws
- Recursive definitions
- Telescoping products

## Key idea

Apply $\log_7$ immediately; the recursive operation becomes a ratio of consecutive logarithms.

## Common mistakes

- Applying the operations as ordinary multiplication and division.
- Losing the initial factor $L(3)/L(2)$ when telescoping.
