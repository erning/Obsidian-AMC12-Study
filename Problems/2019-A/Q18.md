# AMC12 2019-A Q18

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q18.png]]

## Problem

A sphere with center $O$ has radius $6$. A triangle with side lengths $15$, $15$, and $24$ is situated in space so that each of its sides is tangent to the sphere. What is the distance between $O$ and the plane determined by the triangle?

## Answer

**D.** $2\sqrt5$

## Solution

Let $d$ be the distance from $O$ to the triangle's plane. The intersection of the sphere with that plane is a circle of radius

$$r=\sqrt{6^2-d^2}.$$

Because each side of the triangle is tangent to the sphere, it is tangent to this cross-section circle. Thus $r$ is the inradius of the triangle.

The triangle has height $9$ to its base of length $24$, so its area is

$$K=\frac12(24)(9)=108.$$

Its semiperimeter is $27$, hence its inradius is

$$r=\frac K{s}=\frac{108}{27}=4.$$

Therefore

$$6^2-d^2=4^2\quad\Longrightarrow\quad d^2=20,$$

and $d=2\sqrt5$.

## Knowledge points

- Sphere-plane intersection
- Inradius and area
- Isosceles triangle geometry

## Key idea

The three tangent sides make the sphere's planar cross-section the triangle's incircle.

## Common mistakes

- Using the sphere radius $6$ as the triangle's inradius.
- Forgetting to take the distance from the sphere center to the plane into account.
