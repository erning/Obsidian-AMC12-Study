# AMC12 2019-A Q22

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q22.png]]

## Problem

Circles $\omega$ and $\gamma$, both centered at $O$, have radii $20$ and $17$, respectively. Equilateral triangle $ABC$ lies inside $\omega$ and outside $\gamma$, with $A$ on $\omega$. The line containing $BC$ is tangent to $\gamma$. Segments $AO$ and $BC$ intersect at $P$, and $BP/CP=3$. If

$$AB=\frac m{\sqrt n}-\frac p{\sqrt q},$$

where $m,n,p,q$ are positive integers with $\gcd(m,n)=\gcd(p,q)=1$, what is $m+n+p+q$?

## Answer

**E.** $130$

## Solution

Let $s=AB$ and use the ratio $BP:CP=3:1$. Place $P$ at the origin and $BC$ on the horizontal axis, so

$$B=\left(-\frac{3s}{4},0\right),\qquad C=\left(\frac{s}{4},0\right).$$

The altitude of an equilateral triangle is $s\sqrt3/2$, so the third vertex can be represented as

$$A=\left(-\frac{s}{4},\frac{s\sqrt3}{2}\right).$$

Because $A,O,P$ are collinear, write $O=tA$ for a real scalar $t$. The distance from $O$ to the line $BC$ is $17$, giving

$$|t|\frac{s\sqrt3}{2}=17.$$

Also $A$ lies on the circle of radius $20$ centered at $O$. Since

$$|A|=\frac{s\sqrt{13}}4,$$

we have $|t-1|s=80/\sqrt{13}$. Solving the two equations and choosing the configuration in which the triangle is outside the inner circle yields

$$AB=s=\frac{80}{\sqrt{13}}-\frac{34}{\sqrt3}.$$

Both terms are in the required form, so

$$m+n+p+q=80+13+34+3=130.$$

## Knowledge points

- Equilateral triangle geometry
- Coordinate geometry
- Distance from a point to a line
- Ratio division

## Key idea

Place the side on an axis and use collinearity of $A,O,P$ together with the two radii conditions.

## Common mistakes

- Using $BP:CP=1:3$ instead of $3:1$.
- Choosing the algebraic configuration that places the triangle on the wrong side of the inner circle.
