# AMC12 2019-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

For a complex number $c$, the polynomial

$$P(x)=(x^2-2x+2)(x^2-cx+4)(x^2-4x+8)$$

has exactly $4$ distinct roots. What is $|c|$?

## Answer

**E.** $\sqrt{10}$

## Solution

The first and third factors have roots

$$1+i,\ 1-i\qquad\text{and}\qquad2+2i,\ 2-2i,$$

which are already four distinct values. Thus the middle factor must have both roots among these four values.

The product of the two roots of the middle factor is $4$. The only possible pair is one root from each of the first and third pairs, for example

$$ (1+i)(2-2i)=4.$$

The sum of these roots is $3-i$, so the middle factor is $x^2-(3-i)x+4$ and $c=3-i$. Therefore

$$|c|=|3-i|=\sqrt{3^2+1^2}=\sqrt{10}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Complex roots of quadratics
- Viète's formulas
- Distinct-root counting

## Key idea

Use the known four roots and the product of the middle factor's roots to determine its coefficient.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the middle factor can introduce any new root.
- Giving $10$ instead of $\sqrt{10}$ for the modulus.
