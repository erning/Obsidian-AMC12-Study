# AMC12 2019-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Two lines with slopes $\frac12$ and $2$ intersect at $(2,2)$. What is the area of the triangle enclosed by these two lines and the line $x+y=10$?

## Answer

**C.** $6$

## Solution

The two lines through $(2,2)$ are

$$y=\frac12x+1\qquad\text{and}\qquad y=2x-2.$$

Intersecting the first line with $x+y=10$ gives $(6,4)$, and intersecting the second gives $(4,6)$. The triangle's vertices are therefore $(2,2)$, $(6,4)$, and $(4,6)$.

Using the shoelace formula,

$$\text{Area}=\frac12\left|-4+24-8\right|=6.$$

## Knowledge points

- Equations of lines
- Solving linear systems
- Shoelace formula

## Key idea

Find the three pairwise intersections, then compute the area of the resulting triangle.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting that both lines must pass through $(2,2)$.
- Using the slopes as $2$ and $-2$.
