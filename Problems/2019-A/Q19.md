# AMC12 2019-A Q19

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q19.png]]

## Problem

In triangle $ABC$ with integer side lengths,

$$\cos A=\frac{11}{16},\qquad \cos B=\frac78,\qquad \cos C=-\frac14.$$

What is the least possible perimeter?

## Answer

**A.** $9$

## Solution

Using $\sin^2\theta=1-\cos^2\theta$ gives

$$\sin A=\frac{3\sqrt{15}}{16},\qquad
\sin B=\frac{2\sqrt{15}}{16},\qquad
\sin C=\frac{4\sqrt{15}}{16}.$$

By the Law of Sines, the side lengths are in the ratio

$$3:2:4.$$

These lengths satisfy the triangle inequality, since $3+2>4$. The least integer scaling is therefore $(3,2,4)$, whose perimeter is $9$.

## Knowledge points

- Pythagorean identity
- Law of Sines
- Integer scaling and triangle inequality

## Key idea

Convert the cosine data to a side-length ratio, then take the smallest integer scale.

## Common mistakes

- Ignoring that $\sin C$ is positive even though $\cos C$ is negative.
- Choosing a larger common multiple without checking the smallest integer scale.
