# AMC12 2019-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

The figure shows $13$ circles of radius $1$ inside a larger circle. All intersections occur at points of tangency. What is the area of the region inside the larger circle but outside all the circles of radius $1$?

## Answer

**A.** $4\pi\sqrt3$

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q10-draft.svg]]

The $13$ unit circles consist of one center circle, six circles surrounding it, and six outer circles in the gaps. The centers of the six outer circles lie at distance $2\sqrt3$ from the center of the middle circle. Since each outer circle is tangent to the large circle, the large radius is

$$R=2\sqrt3+1.$$

The shaded area is the large circle's area minus the areas of the $13$ unit circles:

$$\pi(2\sqrt3+1)^2-13\pi
=\pi(13+4\sqrt3)-13\pi
=4\pi\sqrt3.$$

## Knowledge points

- Tangent circles
- Regular hexagon geometry
- Area subtraction

## Key idea

Find the distance from the center to the outer ring, then subtract the $13$ unit-circle areas.

## Common mistakes

- Using $2\sqrt3$ as the large radius and forgetting the outer circle's radius.
- Subtracting only the visible white circles rather than all $13$ circles.
