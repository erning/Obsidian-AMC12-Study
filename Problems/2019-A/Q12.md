# AMC12 2019-A Q12

![[assets/problems/2019-A/Q12.png]]

## Problem

Positive real numbers $x\ne1$ and $y\ne1$ satisfy

$$\log_2x=\log_y16\qquad\text{and}\qquad xy=64.$$

What is

$$\left(\log_2\frac{x}{y}\right)^2?$$

## Answer

**B.** $20$

## Solution

Let

$$a=\log_2x,\qquad b=\log_2y.$$

Since $\log_y16=4/b$, the first condition gives $a=4/b$, so $ab=4$. The product condition gives

$$a+b=\log_2(xy)=\log_2 64=6.$$

Therefore

$$\left(\log_2\frac{x}{y}\right)^2=(a-b)^2
=(a+b)^2-4ab
=6^2-4\cdot4=20.$$

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm identities
- Change of base
- Symmetric expressions

## Key idea

Find $a+b$ and $ab$, then use $(a-b)^2=(a+b)^2-4ab$.

## Common mistakes

- Reading $\log_y16$ as $\log_2 16$.
- Forgetting that $\log_2(x/y)=a-b$.
