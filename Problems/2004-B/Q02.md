# AMC12 2004-B Q02

![[assets/problems/2004-B/Q02.png]]

## Problem

In the expression $c \cdot a^b - d,$ the values of $a, b, c,$ and $d$ are $0, 1, 2,$ and $3,$ although not necessarily in that order. What is the maximum possible value of the result?

(A) $5$
(B) $6$
(C) $8$
(D) $9$
(E) $10$

## Answer

**D.** $9$

## Solution

To maximize, set $d = 0.$ With $a, b, c$ taking $1, 2, 3,$ the term $c \cdot a^b$ is largest when $c = 1$ and $a^b = 3^2 = 9.$ This gives $1 \cdot 9 - 0 = 9,$ which beats $2^3 = 8$ and the other assignments.

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Optimization
- Casework

## Key idea

Reduce the objective to a small number of cases and check the extremum.
