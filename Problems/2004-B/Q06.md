# AMC12 2004-B Q06

![[assets/problems/2004-B/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport is $8$ miles southwest of downtown St. Paul and $10$ miles southeast of downtown Minneapolis. Which of the following is closest to the number of miles between downtown St. Paul and downtown Minneapolis?

(A) $13$
(B) $14$
(C) $15$
(D) $16$
(E) $17$

## Answer

**A.** $13$

## Solution

Southwest and southeast are perpendicular, so the airport sits at the right angle of a right triangle with legs $8$ and $10.$ The distance between downtowns is $\sqrt{10^2 + 8^2} = \sqrt{164} \approx 12.8,$ closest to $13.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Pythagorean theorem
- Estimation

## Key idea

Apply the Pythagorean theorem to the right triangle.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
