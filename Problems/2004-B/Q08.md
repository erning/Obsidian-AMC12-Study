# AMC12 2004-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2004-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

A grocer makes a display of cans in which the top row has one can and each lower row has two more cans than the row above it. If the display contains $100$ cans, how many rows does it contain?

(A) $5$
(B) $8$
(C) $9$
(D) $10$
(E) $11$

## Answer

**D.** $10$

## Solution

The rows contain $1, 3, 5, \ldots, (2n - 1)$ cans, and the sum of the first $n$ odd numbers is $n^2.$ Setting $n^2 = 100$ gives $n = 10.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Sum of first $n$ odd numbers

## Key idea

Use the identity $1+3+\cdots+(2n-1)=n^2$.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
