# AMC12 2004-B Q22

![[assets/problems/2004-B/Q22.png]]

## Problem

The square
$$
\begin{array}{|c|c|c|} \hline 50 & b & c \\ \hline d & e & f \\ \hline g & h & 2 \\ \hline \end{array}
$$
 is a multiplicative magic square. That is, the product of the numbers in each row, column, and diagonal is the same. If all the entries are positive integers, what is the sum of the possible values of $g?$

(A) $10$
(B) $25$
(C) $35$
(D) $62$
(E) $136$

## Answer

**C.** $35$

## Solution

From the equal row, column, and diagonal products, every entry can be written in terms of $b:$ $h = \dfrac{100}{b},$ $g = \dfrac{100}{c},$ $f = \dfrac{100}{d}.$ Comparing rows and columns gives $c = \dfrac{20}{b}$ and $d = \dfrac{4}{b},$ hence $g = 5b$ and $e = 10.$

All entries are positive integers exactly when $b = 1, 2,$ or $4,$ giving $g = 5, 10, 20.$ Their sum is $35.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Magic square
- System of equations
- Divisibility

## Key idea

Write the common row/column sum and solve the resulting system.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
