# AMC12 2004-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2004-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

All the students in an algebra class took a $100$-point test. Five students scored $100,$ each student scored at least $60,$ and the mean score was $76.$ What is the smallest possible number of students in the class?

(A) $10$
(B) $11$
(C) $12$
(D) $13$
(E) $14$

## Answer

**D.** $13$

## Solution

Each score of $100$ is $24$ above the mean, so the five contribute $120$ points above $76.$ These must be balanced by points below the mean, and each remaining student is at most $76 - 60 = 16$ below. So at least $\dfrac{120}{16} = 7.5,$ hence $8$ more students are needed, for a total of $13.$ Five $100$s and eight $61$s achieve this.

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

- Bounding to limit cases

## Key idea

Translate the mean into a total sum.
