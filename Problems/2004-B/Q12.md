# AMC12 2004-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2004-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

In the sequence $2001, 2002, 2003, \ldots,$ each term after the third is found by subtracting the previous term from the sum of the two terms that precede that term. For example, the fourth term is $2001 + 2002 - 2003 = 2000.$ What is the $2004$th term in this sequence?

(A) $-2004$
(B) $-2$
(C) $0$
(D) $4003$
(E) $6007$

## Answer

**C.** $0$

## Solution

The rule gives $2001,$ $2002,$ $2003,$ $2000,$ $2005,$ $1998, \ldots$ The even-indexed terms are $2002, 2000, 1998, \ldots,$ decreasing by $2.$ The $2004$th term is the $1002$nd of these: $2002 + 1001(-2) = 0.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.
