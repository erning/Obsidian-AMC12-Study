# AMC12 2004-B Q18

![[assets/problems/2004-B/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Points $A$ and $B$ are on the parabola $y = 4x^2 + 7x - 1,$ and the origin is the midpoint of $\overline{AB}.$ What is the length of $AB?$

(A) $2\sqrt{5}$
(B) $5 + \dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$
(C) $5 + \sqrt{2}$
(D) $7$
(E) $5\sqrt{2}$

## Answer

**E.** $5\sqrt{2}$

## Solution

Let $B = (a, b)$ and $A = (-a, -b).$ Then $4a^2 + 7a - 1 = b$ and $4a^2 - 7a - 1 = -b.$ Subtracting gives $14a = 2b,$ so $b = 7a.$ Then $4a^2 + 7a - 1 = 7a$ gives $a^2 = \dfrac14,$ and $b^2 = 49a^2 = \dfrac{49}{4}.$ So $AB = 2\sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$ $= 2\sqrt{\dfrac{50}{4}} = 5\sqrt{2}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.
