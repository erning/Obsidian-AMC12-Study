# AMC12 2004-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2004-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

An annulus is the region between two concentric circles. The concentric circles in the figure have radii $b$ and $c,$ with $b \gt c.$ Let $\overline{OX}$ be a radius of the larger circle, let $\overline{XZ}$ be tangent to the smaller circle at $Z,$ and let $\overline{OY}$ be the radius of the larger circle that contains $Z.$ Let $a = XZ,$ $d = YZ,$ and $e = XY.$ What is the area of the annulus?

(A) $\pi a^2$
(B) $\pi b^2$
(C) $\pi c^2$
(D) $\pi d^2$
(E) $\pi e^2$

## Answer

**A.** $\pi a^2$

## Solution

The annulus area is $\pi b^2 - \pi c^2.$ Because $\overline{XZ}$ is tangent to the smaller circle at $Z,$ it is perpendicular to radius $\overline{OZ},$ so $\triangle OZX$ is right-angled at $Z.$ Then $b^2 = c^2 + a^2,$ giving $b^2 - c^2 = a^2.$ The area is $\pi a^2.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Annulus
- Tangent line
- Pythagorean theorem

## Key idea

Subtract the inner circle area from the outer circle area.
