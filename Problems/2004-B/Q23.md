# AMC12 2004-B Q23

![[assets/problems/2004-B/Q23.png]]

## Problem

The polynomial $x^3 - 2004x^2 + mx + n$ has integer coefficients and three distinct positive zeros. Exactly one of these is an integer, and it is the sum of the other two. How many values of $n$ are possible?

(A) $250{,}000$
(B) $250{,}250$
(C) $250{,}500$
(D) $250{,}750$
(E) $251{,}000$

## Answer

**C.** $250{,}500$

## Solution

Let the integer zero be $a.$ The other two zeros are irrational conjugates $\dfrac{a}{2} \pm r,$ whose sum $a$ equals the integer zero. Vieta's formula on the $x^2$ coefficient gives $a + a = 2004,$ so $a = 1002$ and the conjugate pair is $501 \pm r.$

The coefficients are integers exactly when $r^2$ is a positive integer, and the zeros are positive and distinct when $1 \le r^2 \le 501^2 - 1 = 251{,}000.$ Since $r$ cannot be an integer, we exclude the $500$ perfect-square values $r^2 = 1^2, \ldots, 500^2,$ leaving $251{,}000 - 500 = 250{,}500$ values of $n.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

- Polynomial
- Counting integers in a range

## Key idea

Use Vieta's formulas instead of solving the polynomial directly.

- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
