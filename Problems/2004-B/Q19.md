# AMC12 2004-B Q19

![[assets/problems/2004-B/Q19.png]]

## Problem

A truncated cone has horizontal bases with radii $18$ and $2.$ A sphere is tangent to the top, bottom, and lateral surface of the truncated cone. What is the radius of the sphere?

(A) $6$
(B) $4\sqrt{5}$
(C) $9$
(D) $10$
(E) $6\sqrt{3}$

## Answer

**A.** $6$

## Solution

The axial cross-section is a trapezoid $ABCD$ with parallel sides $2$ and $18$ and an inscribed circle (a great circle of the sphere). By equal tangent lengths from $B$ and $C,$ the slant side $BC = 18 + 2 = 20.$ Dropping a perpendicular from $C$ to the bottom base gives a right triangle with horizontal leg $18 - 2 = 16,$ so the height is $\sqrt{20^2 - 16^2} = 12.$ The sphere's radius is half the height, $6.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.
