# AMC12 2004-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2004-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

A square has sides of length $10,$ and a circle centered at one of its vertices has radius $10.$ What is the area of the union of the regions enclosed by the square and the circle?

(A) $200 + 25\pi$
(B) $100 + 75\pi$
(C) $75 + 100\pi$
(D) $100 + 100\pi$
(E) $100 + 125\pi$

## Answer

**B.** $100 + 75\pi$

## Solution

The square has area $100$ and the circle has area $100\pi.$ Their overlap is the quarter of the circle lying inside the square, with area $25\pi.$ The union is $100 + 100\pi - 25\pi = 100 + 75\pi.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

- Area decomposition

## Key idea

Use the radius and area formula.
