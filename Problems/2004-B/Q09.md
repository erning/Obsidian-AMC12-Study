## Problem

The point $(-3, 2)$ is rotated $90^\circ$ clockwise around the origin to point $B.$ Point $B$ is then reflected in the line $y = x$ to point $C.$ What are the coordinates of $C?$

(A) $(-3, -2)$
(B) $(-2, -3)$
(C) $(2, -3)$
(D) $(2, 3)$
(E) $(3, 2)$

## Answer

**E.** $(3, 2)$

## Solution

Rotating $(-3, 2)$ by $90^\circ$ clockwise sends $(x, y) \to (y, -x),$ giving $B = (2, 3).$ Reflecting in $y = x$ swaps coordinates, giving $C = (3, 2).$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.
