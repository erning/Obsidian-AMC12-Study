# AMC12 2004-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2004-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

In $\triangle ABC,$ $AB = 13,$ $AC = 5$ and $BC = 12.$ Points $M$ and $N$ lie on $\overline{AC}$ and $\overline{BC},$ respectively, with $CM = CN = 4.$ Points $J$ and $K$ are on $\overline{AB}$ so that $\overline{MJ}$ and $\overline{NK}$ are perpendicular to $\overline{AB}.$ What is the area of pentagon $CMJKN?$

(A) $15$
(B) $\dfrac{81}{5}$
(C) $\dfrac{205}{12}$
(D) $\dfrac{240}{13}$
(E) $20$

## Answer

**D.** $\dfrac{240}{13}$

## Solution

Since $5^2 + 12^2 = 13^2,$ $\triangle ABC$ is right-angled at $C$ with area $\tfrac12 (5)(12) = 30.$ The small right triangles $\triangle AMJ$ and $\triangle NBK$ are each similar to $\triangle ABC,$ with hypotenuses $AM = 5 - 4 = 1$ and $BN = 12 - 4 = 8.$ Their areas are $\left(\dfrac{1}{13}\right)^2 (30)$ and $\left(\dfrac{8}{13}\right)^2 (30).$

The pentagon is what remains:
$$
 \begin{gathered} \left(1 - \dfrac{1}{169} - \dfrac{64}{169}\right)(30) \\ {}= \dfrac{104}{169}(30) = \dfrac{240}{13}. \end{gathered}
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Similarity
- Right triangle
- Area decomposition

## Key idea

Use corresponding side ratios and convert length ratios into area ratios.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
