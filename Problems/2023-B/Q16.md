# AMC12 2023-B Q16

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q16.png]]

## Problem

In Coinland, there are coins worth $6$, $10$, and $15$ cents. Suppose $x$ is the value in cents of the most expensive item that cannot be purchased using these coins with exact change. What is the sum of the digits of $x$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $11$**

## Solution

The consecutive amounts
$$
30,31,32,33,34,35
$$
are all attainable:
$$
30=6\cdot5,\quad
31=6+10+15,\quad
32=6\cdot2+10\cdot2,
$$
$$
33=6\cdot3+15,\quad
34=6\cdot4+10,\quad
35=10\cdot2+15.
$$
Adding another $6$ shows every amount greater than $35$ is attainable. The amount $29$ is impossible because
$$
29-6=23,\qquad 29-10=19,\qquad 29-15=14
$$
are all impossible by checking the smaller amounts. Thus $x=29$, and its digit sum is
$$
2+9=\boxed{11}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Linear combinations
- Coin problems
- Inductive coverage

## Key idea

Find a block of consecutive attainable values whose length includes a coin value.

## Common mistakes

- Checking only that $29$ is not an obvious combination without proving all larger values are attainable.
