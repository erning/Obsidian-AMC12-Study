# AMC12 2023-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

Suppose $a,b,c$ are positive integers such that
$$
\frac a{14}+\frac b{15}=\frac c{210}.
$$
Which of the following statements are necessarily true?

I. If $\gcd(a,14)=1$ or $\gcd(b,15)=1$ or both, then $\gcd(c,210)=1$.

II. If $\gcd(c,210)=1$, then $\gcd(a,14)=1$ or $\gcd(b,15)=1$ or both.

III. $\gcd(c,210)=1$ if and only if $\gcd(a,14)=\gcd(b,15)=1$.

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) II and III only**

## Solution

Multiplying by $210$ gives
$$
c=15a+14b.
$$
Modulo $14$,
$$
c\equiv a\pmod {14},
$$
so
$$
\gcd(c,14)=1\iff\gcd(a,14)=1.
$$
Modulo $15$,
$$
c\equiv-b\pmod {15},
$$
so
$$
\gcd(c,15)=1\iff\gcd(b,15)=1.
$$
Because $14$ and $15$ are relatively prime,
$$
\gcd(c,210)=1
\iff
\gcd(a,14)=\gcd(b,15)=1.
$$
Thus III is true, and II is its forward implication. Statement I is false: one of the two gcd conditions can hold while the other fails. Therefore only II and III are necessarily true.

## Knowledge points

- Greatest common divisors
- Modular arithmetic
- Logical implications

## Key idea

Reduce $c=15a+14b$ modulo $14$ and modulo $15$ separately.

## Common mistakes

- Confusing the “or” in statement I with the “and” required for coprimality with $210$.
