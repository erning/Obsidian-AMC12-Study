# AMC12 2023-B Q18

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Last academic year Yolanda and Zelda took different courses. Yolanda's first-semester quiz average was $3$ points higher than Zelda's. Yolanda's second-semester average was $18$ points higher than her first-semester average and was again $3$ points higher than Zelda's second-semester average. Which statement cannot possibly be true?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) Yolanda's quiz average for the academic year was $22$ points higher than Zelda's**

## Solution

Set Zelda's first-semester average to $0$. Then the four semester averages are
$$
\begin{array}{c|cc}
&\text{first semester}&\text{second semester}\\ \hline
\text{Yolanda}&3&21\\
\text{Zelda}&0&18
\end{array}
$$
Each yearly average is a weighted average of its two semester averages. Therefore Yolanda's yearly average lies between $3$ and $21$, while Zelda's lies between $0$ and $18$. The largest possible difference is at most
$$
21-0=21.
$$
Thus a difference of $22$ is impossible. The other statements can be achieved by choosing suitable numbers of quizzes in the semesters, so the answer is **(A)**.

## Knowledge points

- Weighted averages
- Bounds
- Logical deduction

## Key idea

Bound each yearly average using the range of its two semester averages.

## Common mistakes

- Averaging the two semester averages equally when the courses may have different numbers of quizzes.
