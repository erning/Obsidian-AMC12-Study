# AMC12 2023-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

Jackson's paintbrush makes a narrow strip with a width of $6.5$ millimeters. Jackson has enough paint to make a strip $25$ meters long. How many square centimeters of paper could Jackson cover with paint?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $1625$**

## Solution

Convert both measurements to centimeters:
$$
6.5\text{ mm}=0.65\text{ cm},\qquad
25\text{ m}=2500\text{ cm}.
$$
The painted area is
$$
0.65\cdot2500=\boxed{1625}
$$
square centimeters.

## Knowledge points

- Unit conversion
- Area

## Key idea

Convert the width and length to the same units before multiplying.

## Common mistakes

- Converting meters to millimeters but forgetting to convert the width.
