# AMC12 2023-B Q23

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q23.png]]

## Problem

When $n$ standard six-sided dice are rolled, the product of the numbers rolled can be any of $936$ possible values. What is $n$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $11$**

## Solution

Represent each die face by the exponent vector of its prime factorization in the primes $2,3,5$:
$$
1\mapsto(0,0,0),\quad
2\mapsto(1,0,0),\quad
3\mapsto(0,1,0),
$$
$$
4\mapsto(2,0,0),\quad
5\mapsto(0,0,1),\quad
6\mapsto(1,1,0).
$$
The product of $n$ dice is determined by the sum of the $n$ chosen vectors. Counting distinct attainable sums for $n=1,2,\ldots$ gives
$$
6,18,40,75,126,196,288,405,550,726,936.
$$
The first time the count reaches $936$ is $n=11$. Thus
$$
\boxed{11}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Prime factorization
- Exponent vectors
- Systematic counting

## Key idea

Products are easier to count after converting them into sums of prime exponents.

## Common mistakes

- Counting ordered rolls instead of distinct products.
