# AMC12 2023-B Q24

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q24.png]]

## Problem

Suppose positive integers $a,b,c,d$ satisfy
$$
abcd=2^6\cdot3^9\cdot5^7,
$$
and
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{lcm}(a,b)&=2^3\cdot3^2\cdot5^3,\\
\operatorname{lcm}(a,c)&=2^3\cdot3^3\cdot5^3,\\
\operatorname{lcm}(a,d)&=2^3\cdot3^3\cdot5^3,\\
\operatorname{lcm}(b,c)&=2^1\cdot3^3\cdot5^2,\\
\operatorname{lcm}(b,d)&=2^2\cdot3^3\cdot5^2,\\
\operatorname{lcm}(c,d)&=2^2\cdot3^3\cdot5^2.
\end{aligned}
$$
What is $\gcd(a,b,c,d)$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $3$**

## Solution

Work prime by prime. For the prime $2$, the total exponent is $6$. The condition $\max(b,c)=1$ forces the exponent in $a$ to be $3$. The remaining equations give the exponent in $d$ as $2$ and the exponents in $b,c$ as $0,1$ in some order. Thus the minimum exponent of $2$ is $0$.

For the prime $3$, the total exponent is $9$. The pairwise lcm conditions force the exponents in $c,d$ to be $3$, while the exponents in $a,b$ are $1,2$ in some order. Thus the minimum exponent of $3$ is $1$.

For the prime $5$, the total exponent is $7$. The conditions force the exponent in $a$ to be $3$, two of the other exponents to be $2$, and the remaining one to be $0$. Thus the minimum exponent of $5$ is $0$.

Therefore
$$
\gcd(a,b,c,d)=2^0\cdot3^1\cdot5^0=\boxed{3}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Prime exponents
- Least common multiples
- Greatest common divisors

## Key idea

Translate products, lcm, and gcd into sums, maxima, and minima of prime exponents.

## Common mistakes

- Treating lcm exponents as sums instead of maxima.
