# AMC12 2023-B Q21

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q21.png]]

## Problem

A lampshade is the lateral surface of a right circular cone frustum. Its height is $3\sqrt3$ inches, its top diameter is $6$ inches, and its bottom diameter is $12$ inches. A bug is at the bottom edge and honey is at the farthest point on the top edge. What is the shortest path along the surface from the bug to the honey?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $6\sqrt3+\pi$**

## Solution

The frustum has radii $3$ and $6$, and height $3\sqrt3$, so its slanted height is
$$
\sqrt{(6-3)^2+(3\sqrt3)^2}=6.
$$
Extend it to a full cone. The apex is slant distance $6$ from the top rim and $12$ from the bottom rim. When the surface is unrolled, the bottom circumference $12\pi$ forms a sector of radius $12$ and angle $\pi$. The bug is at radius $12$, while the honey is at radius $6$ on the opposite side of the top edge.

The straight segment from the bug to the honey would pass inside the excluded circle of radius $6$, so the shortest path consists of a tangent segment followed by an arc on the top rim. The tangent length is
$$
\sqrt{12^2-6^2}=6\sqrt3.
$$
In the unrolled sector, the tangent point and the honey are separated by angle $\frac{\pi}{6}$ on the circle of radius $6$. Thus the arc has length
$$
6\cdot\frac{\pi}{6}=\pi.
$$
Hence the shortest path is
$$
\boxed{6\sqrt3+\pi}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Cone frustums
- Developable surfaces
- Tangent lengths

## Key idea

Unroll the frustum and replace the surface path by a planar path constrained outside a circle.

## Common mistakes

- Using the vertical height $3\sqrt3$ as the slanted height.
