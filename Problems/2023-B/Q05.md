# AMC12 2023-B Q05

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q05.png]]

## Problem

A $2\times1$ rectangle covers two adjacent squares of a $3\times3$ grid, oriented either horizontally or vertically, but its location is hidden. A turn consists of guessing a square and being told whether that square is covered. What is the minimum number of turns needed to ensure that at least one guessed square is covered?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $4$**

## Solution

Suppose the guessed squares are fixed. To guarantee a hit, the unguessed squares must contain no adjacent pair; otherwise the hidden domino could occupy that pair and avoid every guess. The largest set of pairwise nonadjacent squares in a $3\times3$ grid has $5$ squares, obtained by taking the checkerboard color class containing the four corners and the center. Therefore at least
$$
9-5=\boxed{4}
$$
guesses are necessary. Guessing the other four squares works, so four turns are sufficient.

## Knowledge points

- Extremal combinatorics
- Grid graphs
- Complementary counting

## Key idea

The unguessed squares must be an independent set in the grid graph.

## Common mistakes

- Counting possible domino positions instead of considering a worst-case hidden placement.
