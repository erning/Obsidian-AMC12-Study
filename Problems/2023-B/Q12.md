# AMC12 2023-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

For complex numbers $u=a+bi$ and $v=c+di$, define
$$
u\otimes v=ac+bdi.
$$
Suppose $z$ is a complex number such that $z\otimes z=z^2+40$. What is $|z|$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $5\sqrt2$**

## Solution

Let $z=a+bi$. Then
$$
z\otimes z=a^2+b^2i,
$$
whereas
$$
z^2+40=(a^2-b^2+40)+2abi.
$$
Equating real parts gives
$$
b^2=40.
$$
Equating imaginary parts gives
$$
b^2=2ab.
$$
Since $b\ne0$, $b=2a$, so $a^2=10$ and $b^2=40$. Therefore
$$
|z|^2=a^2+b^2=50,
\qquad |z|=\boxed{5\sqrt2}.
$$

## Key idea

Expand both sides and equate their real and imaginary components.
