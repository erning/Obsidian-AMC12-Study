# AMC12 2023-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

What is the maximum area of an isosceles trapezoid that has legs of length $1$ and one base twice as long as the other?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $\frac32$**

## Solution

Let the shorter and longer bases be $a$ and $2a$. By symmetry, each leg has horizontal component $\frac a2$, so the height is
$$
h=\sqrt{1-\frac{a^2}{4}}.
$$
The area is
$$
A=\frac{a+2a}{2}h
=\frac{3a}{2}\sqrt{1-\frac{a^2}{4}}.
$$
Maximize its square:
$$
A^2=\frac94\left(a^2-\frac{a^4}{4}\right).
$$
The expression is maximized at $a^2=2$. Then $h=\frac1{\sqrt2}$, and
$$
A=\frac{3\sqrt2}{2}\cdot\frac1{\sqrt2}
=\boxed{\frac32}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Isosceles trapezoids
- Optimization
- Pythagorean theorem

## Key idea

Express the height using one leg and maximize the squared area.

## Common mistakes

- Using $a$ instead of $\frac a2$ for each leg's horizontal offset.
