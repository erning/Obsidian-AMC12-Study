# AMC12 2023-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

For how many integers $n$ does
$$
\sqrt{\frac{\log(n^2)-(\log n)^2}{\log n-3}}
$$
represent a real number, where $\log$ denotes the base-$10$ logarithm?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $901$**

## Solution

Let $L=\log n$. Since $n$ is a positive integer, $L\ge0$. The radicand is
$$
\frac{2L-L^2}{L-3}
=\frac{L(2-L)}{L-3}.
$$
A sign chart shows this is nonnegative when
$$
L=0\quad\text{or}\quad 2\le L<3.
$$
The first case gives $n=1$. The second gives
$$
100\le n<1000,
$$
which contains $900$ integers. Therefore the total is
$$
1+900=\boxed{901}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Logarithms
- Sign analysis
- Inequalities

## Key idea

Substitute $L=\log n$ and analyze the signs of the numerator and denominator.

## Common mistakes

- Including $n=1000$, where the denominator is zero.
