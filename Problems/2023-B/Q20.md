# AMC12 2023-B Q20

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Cyrus the frog jumps $2$ units in a direction, then $2$ more units in another direction. What is the probability that he lands less than $1$ unit away from his starting position?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $\frac{2\arcsin(1/4)}{\pi}$**

## Solution

By rotational symmetry, take the first jump to be $(2,0)$. Let the second jump make angle $\theta$ with the first, where $\theta$ is uniform on $[0,2\pi)$. The squared distance from the start is
$$
R^2=(2+2\cos\theta)^2+(2\sin\theta)^2
=8+8\cos\theta.
$$
The condition $R<1$ becomes
$$
\cos\theta<-\frac78.
$$
The allowed angular measure is $2\arccos\frac78$, so
$$
P=\frac{\arccos(7/8)}{\pi}.
$$
Using
$$
\arccos(1-2u^2)=2\arcsin u
$$
with $u=\frac14$, we obtain
$$
P=\boxed{\frac{2\arcsin(1/4)}{\pi}}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Geometric probability
- Law of Cosines
- Uniform angles

## Key idea

Convert the distance condition into an interval of possible directions.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the second direction is uniformly distributed as a slope rather than as an angle.
