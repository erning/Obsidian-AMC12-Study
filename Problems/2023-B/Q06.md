# AMC12 2023-B Q06

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q06.png]]

## Problem

When the roots of
$$
P(x)=(x-1)^1(x-2)^2(x-3)^3\cdots(x-10)^{10}
$$
are removed from the number line, what remains is the union of $11$ disjoint open intervals. On how many of these intervals is $P(x)$ positive?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $6$**

## Solution

The sign changes at $x=k$ exactly when the multiplicity $k$ is odd. Thus the sign changes at
$$
1,3,5,7,9,
$$
but not at the even roots. For $x>10$, every factor is positive, so $P(x)>0$. Moving leftward and flipping the sign at each odd root, the positive intervals are
$$
(10,\infty),(9,10),(6,7),(5,6),(2,3),(1,2).
$$
There are $\boxed{6}$ such intervals.

## Knowledge points

- Polynomial sign
- Root multiplicity
- Parity

## Key idea

An odd-multiplicity root changes the sign; an even-multiplicity root does not.

## Common mistakes

- Flipping the sign at every root regardless of multiplicity.
