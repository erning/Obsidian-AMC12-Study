# AMC12 2023-B Q17

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Triangle $ABC$ has side lengths in arithmetic progression, and the smallest side has length $6$. If the triangle has an angle of $120^\circ$, what is the area of $\triangle ABC$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $15\sqrt3$**

## Solution

Let the side lengths be
$$
6,\qquad 6+d,\qquad 6+2d.
$$
The $120^\circ$ angle is opposite the longest side, so the Law of Cosines gives
$$
(6+2d)^2
=6^2+(6+d)^2-2\cdot6(6+d)\cos120^\circ.
$$
Using $\cos120^\circ=-\frac12$ and simplifying,
$$
3d^2+6d-72=0.
$$
The positive solution is $d=4$, so the sides are $6,10,14$. The area is
$$
\frac12\cdot6\cdot10\sin120^\circ
=30\cdot\frac{\sqrt3}{2}
=\boxed{15\sqrt3}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Arithmetic progressions
- Law of Cosines
- Triangle area

## Key idea

Use the $120^\circ$ angle to determine the common difference.

## Common mistakes

- Putting the $120^\circ$ angle opposite the wrong side.
