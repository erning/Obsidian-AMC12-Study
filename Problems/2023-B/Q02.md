# AMC12 2023-B Q02

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q02.png]]

## Problem

Running shoes were on sale with prices reduced by $20\%$. Carlos also had to pay a $7.5\%$ sales tax on the discounted price. He had $\$43$. What is the original price of the most expensive shoes he could afford?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $\$50$**

## Solution

If the original price is $p$, the discounted price is $0.8p$. After tax, the final price is
$$
1.075(0.8p)=0.86p.
$$
Affordability requires
$$
0.86p\le43,
$$
so
$$
p\le\frac{43}{0.86}=50.
$$
The greatest possible original price is $\boxed{\$50}$.

## Knowledge points

- Percentages
- Reverse calculations

## Key idea

Apply the discount and tax as successive multiplicative factors.

## Common mistakes

- Applying the tax to the original price instead of the discounted price.
