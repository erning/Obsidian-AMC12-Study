# AMC12 2023-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

A rectangular box $\mathcal P$ has distinct edge lengths $a,b,c$. The sum of the lengths of all $12$ edges is $13$, the sum of the areas of all $6$ faces is $\frac{11}{2}$, and the volume is $\frac12$. What is the length of the longest interior diagonal?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $\frac94$**

## Solution

The edge condition gives
$$
4(a+b+c)=13
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
a+b+c=\frac{13}{4}.
$$
The face-area condition gives
$$
2(ab+bc+ca)=\frac{11}{2}
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
ab+bc+ca=\frac{11}{4}.
$$
The square of the space diagonal is
$$
a^2+b^2+c^2
=(a+b+c)^2-2(ab+bc+ca)
=\frac{169}{16}-\frac{88}{16}
=\frac{81}{16}.
$$
Thus the diagonal is $\boxed{\frac94}$.

## Knowledge points

- Rectangular prisms
- Symmetric polynomials
- Distance formula

## Key idea

Compute the sum of the squares of the edge lengths from the given symmetric sums.

## Common mistakes

- Using the sum of face areas as $ab+bc+ca$ instead of $2(ab+bc+ca)$.
