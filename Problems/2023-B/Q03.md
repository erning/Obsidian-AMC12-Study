# AMC12 2023-B Q03

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q03.png]]

## Problem

A $3$-$4$-$5$ right triangle is inscribed in circle $A$, and a $5$-$12$-$13$ right triangle is inscribed in circle $B$. What is the ratio of the area of circle $A$ to the area of circle $B$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $\frac{25}{169}$**

## Solution

The hypotenuse of an inscribed right triangle is a diameter of its circumcircle. Thus circle $A$ has diameter $5$, while circle $B$ has diameter $13$. Circle areas scale as the square of the diameter, so
$$
\frac{[A]}{[B]}=\left(\frac5{13}\right)^2
=\boxed{\frac{25}{169}}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Right triangles
- Circumcircles
- Area ratios

## Key idea

Use Thales' theorem to identify each circle's diameter.

## Common mistakes

- Using the legs instead of the hypotenuse as the diameter.
