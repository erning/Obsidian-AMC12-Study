# AMC12 2023-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2023-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

For how many ordered pairs $(a,b)$ of integers does the polynomial
$$
x^3+ax^2+bx+6
$$
have three distinct integer roots?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $5$**

## Solution

Let the three integer roots be $p,q,r$. By Vieta,
$$
pqr=-6.
$$
The sets of three distinct integers with product $-6$ are
$$
\{1,2,-3\},\quad
\{1,-2,3\},\quad
\{-1,2,3\},\quad
\{-1,-2,-3\},\quad
\{1,-1,6\}.
$$
For each set, the coefficients are uniquely determined by
$$
a=-(p+q+r),\qquad b=pq+pr+qr.
$$
These five sets produce five different ordered pairs, so the answer is $\boxed{5}$.

## Knowledge points

- Vieta's formulas
- Integer factorization
- Systematic listing

## Key idea

The constant term fixes the product of the three roots.

## Common mistakes

- Counting ordered arrangements of the roots separately; the coefficients do not depend on their order.
