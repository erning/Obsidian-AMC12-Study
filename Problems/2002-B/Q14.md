# AMC12 2002-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Four distinct circles are drawn in a plane. What is the maximum number of points where at least two of the circles intersect?

(A) $8$
(B) $9$
(C) $10$
(D) $12$
(E) $16$

## Answer

**D.** $12$

## Solution

Each pair of circles meets in at most $2$ points, and there are $\binom{4}{2}=6$ pairs, giving at most $6\cdot2=12$ intersection points.

A configuration of four circles achieving all $12$ points is possible.

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- Two distinct circles meet in at most $2$ points
- There are $\binom{4}{2}$ pairs of circles; multiply by $2$
