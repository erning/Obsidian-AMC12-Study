# AMC12 2002-B Q01

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q01.png]]

## Problem

The arithmetic mean of the nine numbers in the set $\{9,$ $99,$ $999,$ $9999,$ $\ldots,$ $999999999\}$ is a $9$-digit number $M,$ all of whose digits are distinct. The number $M$ does not contain the digit

(A) $0$
(B) $2$
(C) $4$
(D) $6$
(E) $8$

## Answer

**A.** $0$

## Solution

Each of the nine numbers is $10^k-1,$ so their sum is $9+99+\cdots+999{,}999{,}999.$ Dividing by $9,$
$$
 \begin{aligned} M &= 1+11+111+\cdots \\ &\quad {}+111{,}111{,}111 \\ &= 123{,}456{,}789. \end{aligned}
$$
 Its digits are $1$ through $9,$ so the missing digit is $0.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Key idea

- The mean is $\dfrac{1}{9}(9+99+\cdots+999{,}999{,}999)$
- Dividing each term by $9$ turns the sum into $1+11+111+\cdots$; add these and see which digit never appears
