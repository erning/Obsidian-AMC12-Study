# AMC12 2002-B Q25

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q25.png]]

## Problem

Let $f(x)=x^2+6x+1,$ and let $R$ denote the set of points $(x,y)$ in the coordinate plane such that
$$
f(x)+f(y)\le0
$$
 and
$$
f(x)-f(y)\le0.
$$
 The area of $R$ is closest to

(A) $21$
(B) $22$
(C) $23$
(D) $24$
(E) $25$

## Answer

**E.** $25$

## Solution

Completing the square,
$$
 \begin{gathered} f(x)+f(y) \\ {}=(x+3)^2+(y+3)^2-16, \end{gathered}
$$
 so the first condition is the disk of radius $4$ centered at $(-3,-3).$

Also
$$
 \begin{gathered} f(x)-f(y) \\ {}=(x-y)(x+y+6), \end{gathered}
$$
 so the second condition $(x-y)(x+y+6)\le0$ describes two half-planes bounded by the perpendicular lines through $(-3,-3)$ of slopes $1$ and $-1.$ These cut the disk into two equal halves.

Thus $R$ has area $\tfrac12\pi\cdot4^2=8\pi\approx25.13,$ which is closest to $25.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Key idea

- Complete the square: $f(x)+f(y)$ $=(x+3)^2+(y+3)^2-16$
- The first condition is a disk of radius $4$; the second, $(x-y)(x+y+6)\le0,$ keeps half of it
