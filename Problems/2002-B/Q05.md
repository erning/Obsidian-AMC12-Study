# AMC12 2002-B Q05

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Let $v,$ $w,$ $x,$ $y,$ and $z$ be the degree measures of the five angles of a pentagon. Suppose $v\lt w\lt x\lt y\lt z$ and $v,$ $w,$ $x,$ $y,$ $z$ form an arithmetic sequence. Find the value of $x.$

(A) $72$
(B) $84$
(C) $90$
(D) $108$
(E) $120$

## Answer

**D.** $108$

## Solution

The five interior angles sum to $540^\circ.$ Writing the sequence as $x-2d,$ $x-d,$ $x,$ $x+d,$ $x+2d,$ the sum is $5x=540,$ so $x=108.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- The interior angles of a pentagon sum to $540^\circ$
- Write the five terms as $x-2d,$ $x-d,$ $x,$ $x+d,$ $x+2d$; their sum is $5x$
