# AMC12 2002-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

How many four-digit numbers $N$ have the property that the three-digit number obtained by removing the leftmost digit is one ninth of $N?$

(A) $4$
(B) $5$
(C) $6$
(D) $7$
(E) $8$

## Answer

**D.** $7$

## Solution

Let $a$ be the leading digit and $x$ the three-digit number after removing it, so $N=1000a+x.$ The condition $N=9x$ gives $1000a=8x,$ i.e. $x=125a.$

For $a=1,\ldots,7$ this makes $x$ a three-digit number, while $a=8$ gives $x=1000.$ So there are $7$ such numbers.

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- Write $N=1000a+x,$ where $a$ is the leading digit and $x$ the last three digits
- $N=9x$ gives $1000a=8x,$ so $x=125a$; count the digits $a$ keeping $x$ three digits
