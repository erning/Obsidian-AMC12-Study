# AMC12 2002-B Q24

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q24.png]]

## Problem

A convex quadrilateral $ABCD$ with area $2002$ contains a point $P$ in its interior such that $PA=24,$ $PB=32,$ $PC=28,$ and $PD=45.$ Find the perimeter of $ABCD.$

(A) $4\sqrt{2002}$
(B) $2\sqrt{8465}$
(C) $2\left(48+\sqrt{2002}\right)$
(D) $2\sqrt{8633}$
(E) $4\left(36+\sqrt{113}\right)$

## Answer

**E.** $4\left(36+\sqrt{113}\right)$

## Solution

For any quadrilateral, the area is at most $\tfrac12\,d_1 d_2$ where $d_1,d_2$ are the diagonals, with equality exactly when they are perpendicular. Here
$$
 \begin{gathered} 2002=\text{Area} \\ {}\le \tfrac12\,AC\cdot BD \\ {}\le \tfrac12(PA+PC)(PB+PD) \\ {}= \tfrac12\cdot52\cdot77 \\ {}= 2002. \end{gathered}
$$

Equality forces the diagonals to be perpendicular and to intersect at $P.$ Then
$$
 \begin{aligned} AB &= \sqrt{24^2+32^2}=40, \\ BC &= \sqrt{28^2+32^2}=4\sqrt{113}, \end{aligned}
$$

$$
 \begin{aligned} CD &= \sqrt{28^2+45^2}=53, \\ DA &= \sqrt{45^2+24^2}=51. \end{aligned}
$$

The perimeter is $144+4\sqrt{113}=4\left(36+\sqrt{113}\right).$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Key idea

- For a quadrilateral with diagonals $d_1,d_2,$ the area is at most $\tfrac12 d_1 d_2,$ with equality when the diagonals are perpendicular
- Here $\tfrac12(PA+PC)(PB+PD)$ $=2002$ forces perpendicular diagonals meeting at $P$; find each side with the Pythagorean theorem
