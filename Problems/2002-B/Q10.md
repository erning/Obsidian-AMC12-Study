# AMC12 2002-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

How many different integers can be expressed as the sum of three distinct members of the set $\{1,4,7,10,13,16,19\}?$

(A) $13$
(B) $16$
(C) $24$
(D) $30$
(E) $35$

## Answer

**A.** $13$

## Solution

Every element is one more than a multiple of $3,$ so any sum of three of them is a multiple of $3.$ The smallest sum is $1+4+7=12$ and the largest is $13+16+19=48,$ and every multiple of $3$ between them is attainable.

There are $13$ multiples of $3$ from $12$ to $48.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Key idea

- Every element is one more than a multiple of $3,$ so each three-element sum is a multiple of $3$
- Count the multiples of $3$ from the smallest sum $1+4+7$ to the largest sum $13+16+19$
