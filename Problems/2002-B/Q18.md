# AMC12 2002-B Q18

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q18.png]]

## Problem

A point $P$ is randomly selected from the rectangular region with vertices $(0,0),$ $(2,0),$ $(2,1),$ $(0,1).$ What is the probability that $P$ is closer to the origin than it is to the point $(3,1)?$

(A) $\dfrac{1}{2}$
(B) $\dfrac{2}{3}$
(C) $\dfrac{3}{4}$
(D) $\dfrac{4}{5}$
(E) $1$

## Answer

**C.** $\dfrac{3}{4}$

## Solution

The points closer to $(0,0)$ than to $(3,1)$ lie on the origin side of the perpendicular bisector of that segment, the line $3x+y=5.$

Within the rectangle, this region is a trapezoid whose parallel sides have lengths $\dfrac53$ (at $y=0$) and $\dfrac43$ (at $y=1$), so its area is $\dfrac12\left(\dfrac53+\dfrac43\right)=\dfrac32.$ The rectangle has area $2,$ so the probability is $\dfrac{3/2}{2}=\dfrac34.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- $P$ is closer to the origin on one side of the perpendicular bisector of the segment from $(0,0)$ to $(3,1)$
- That bisector is the line $3x+y=5$; find the area of the rectangle on the origin side and divide by the total area

- Perpendicular bisector

- Check units, endpoint conditions, and whether the requested quantity is a count, length, area, or probability.
