# AMC12 2002-B Q06

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Suppose that $a$ and $b$ are nonzero real numbers, and that the equation $x^2+ax+b=0$ has solutions $a$ and $b.$ Then the pair $(a,b)$ is

(A) $(-2,1)$
(B) $(-1,2)$
(C) $(1,-2)$
(D) $(2,-1)$
(E) $(4,4)$

## Answer

**C.** $(1,-2)$

## Solution

Since $a$ and $b$ are the roots,
$$
 \begin{gathered} x^2+ax+b \\ {}=(x-a)(x-b) \\ {}=x^2-(a+b)x+ab. \end{gathered}
$$
 Matching coefficients gives $a+b=-a$ and $ab=b.$

As $b\neq0,$ the second equation gives $a=1,$ and then $a+b=-a$ gives $b=-2.$ So $(a,b)=(1,-2).$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- If $a$ and $b$ are the roots, then $x^2+ax+b=(x-a)(x-b)$
- Match coefficients: $a+b=-a$ and $ab=b,$ using $b\neq0$
