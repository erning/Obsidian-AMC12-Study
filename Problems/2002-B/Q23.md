## Problem

In $\triangle ABC,$ we have $AB=1$ and $AC=2.$ Side $BC$ and the median from $A$ to $BC$ have the same length. What is $BC?$

(A) $\dfrac{1+\sqrt{2}}{2}$
(B) $\dfrac{1+\sqrt{3}}{2}$
(C) $\sqrt{2}$
(D) $\dfrac{3}{2}$
(E) $\sqrt{3}$

## Answer

**C.** $\sqrt{2}$

## Solution

Let $M$ be the midpoint of $BC,$ set $AM=2a,$ and let $\theta=\angle AMB,$ so $\angle AMC=180^\circ-\theta.$ With $BM=CM=a,$ so that $BC=2a,$ the Law of Cosines in $\triangle ABM$ and $\triangle AMC$ gives
$$
a^2+4a^2-4a^2\cos\theta=1,
$$

$$
a^2+4a^2+4a^2\cos\theta=4.
$$

Adding, $10a^2=5,$ so $a=\dfrac{\sqrt2}{2}$ and $BC=2a=\sqrt2.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.
