# AMC12 2002-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

The product of three consecutive positive integers is $8$ times their sum. What is the sum of their squares?

(A) $50$
(B) $77$
(C) $110$
(D) $149$
(E) $194$

## Answer

**B.** $77$

## Solution

Let the integers be $n-1,$ $n,$ $n+1.$ Then $(n-1)n(n+1)=8\cdot3n,$ so $n^2-1=24$ and $n=5.$

The three integers $4,$ $5,$ $6$ have squares summing to $16+25+36=77.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Key idea

- Call the integers $n-1,$ $n,$ $n+1$; their product is $n^3-n$ and their sum is $3n$
- $n^2-1=24,$ so find $n$ and then compute $(n-1)^2+n^2+(n+1)^2$
