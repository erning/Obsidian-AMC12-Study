# AMC12 2002-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

If $a,$ $b,$ $c,$ $d$ are positive real numbers such that $a,$ $b,$ $c,$ $d$ form an increasing arithmetic sequence and $a,$ $b,$ $d$ form a geometric sequence, then $\dfrac{a}{d}$ is

(A) $\dfrac{1}{12}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{6}$
(C) $\dfrac{1}{4}$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{3}$
(E) $\dfrac{1}{2}$

## Answer

**C.** $\dfrac{1}{4}$

## Solution

Let $b=a+r,$ $c=a+2r,$ $d=a+3r.$ The geometric condition $b^2=ad$ gives $(a+r)^2=a(a+3r),$ i.e. $r^2=ar,$ so $r=a.$

Then $d=a+3a=4a$ and $\dfrac{a}{d}=\dfrac14.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- Write $b=a+r,$ $c=a+2r,$ $d=a+3r$
- The geometric condition $b^2=ad$ gives $r^2=ar,$ so $r=a$
