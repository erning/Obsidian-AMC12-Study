# AMC12 2002-B Q16

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q16.png]]

## Problem

Juan rolls a fair regular octahedral die marked with the numbers $1$ through $8.$ Then Amal rolls a fair six-sided die. What is the probability that the product of the two rolls is a multiple of $3?$

(A) $\dfrac{1}{12}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{3}$
(C) $\dfrac{1}{2}$
(D) $\dfrac{7}{12}$
(E) $\dfrac{2}{3}$

## Answer

**C.** $\dfrac{1}{2}$

## Solution

The product is a multiple of $3$ if and only if at least one die shows $3$ or $6.$ The octahedral die avoids $3,6$ with probability $\dfrac68=\dfrac34,$ and the six-sided die avoids them with probability $\dfrac46=\dfrac23.$

So neither shows a multiple of $3$ with probability $\dfrac34\cdot\dfrac23=\dfrac12,$ and the answer is $1-\dfrac12=\dfrac12.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- The product is a multiple of $3$ exactly when at least one roll is $3$ or $6$
- Compute $1$ minus the probability that neither die shows a $3$ or $6$
