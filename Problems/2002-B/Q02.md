# AMC12 2002-B Q02

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q02.png]]

## Problem

What is the value of
$$
 \begin{aligned} &(3x-2)(4x+1) \\ &\quad {}-(3x-2)4x+1 \end{aligned}
$$
 when $x=4?$

(A) $0$
(B) $1$
(C) $10$
(D) $11$
(E) $12$

## Answer

**D.** $11$

## Solution

Factor $3x-2$ out of the first two terms:
$$
 \begin{gathered} (3x-2)(4x+1) \\ {}-(3x-2)4x+1 \\ {}=(3x-2)(4x+1-4x)+1 \\ {}=3x-1. \end{gathered}
$$
 At $x=4$ this equals $3\cdot4-1=11.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- Group the two terms containing $3x-2$ using the distributive law
- $(3x-2)(4x+1)-(3x-2)4x$ $=(3x-2)(4x+1-4x)$
