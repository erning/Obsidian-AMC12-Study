# AMC12 2002-B Q19

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q19.png]]

## Problem

If $a,$ $b,$ and $c$ are positive real numbers such that $a(b+c)=152,$ $b(c+a)=162,$ and $c(a+b)=170,$ then $abc$ is

(A) $672$
(B) $688$
(C) $704$
(D) $720$
(E) $750$

## Answer

**D.** $720$

## Solution

Adding the three equations gives $2(ab+bc+ca)=484,$ so $ab+bc+ca=242.$ Subtracting each original equation from this yields $bc=90,$ $ca=80,$ and $ab=72.$

Multiplying, $(abc)^2=90\cdot80\cdot72=720^2,$ and since $abc\gt0,$ we get $abc=720.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- Add all three equations to get $ab+bc+ca$
- Subtract each original equation from that total to find $ab,$ $bc,$ $ca,$ then multiply and take a square root

- Symmetry (algebra)
