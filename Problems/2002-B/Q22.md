## Problem

For all integers $n$ greater than $1,$ define
$$
a_n=\dfrac{1}{\log_n 2002}.
$$
 Let $b=a_2+a_3+a_4+a_5$ and $c=a_{10}+a_{11}+a_{12}+a_{13}+a_{14}.$ Then $b-c$ equals

(A) $-2$
(B) $-1$
(C) $\dfrac{1}{2002}$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{1001}$
(E) $\dfrac{1}{2}$

## Answer

**B.** $-1$

## Solution

By change of base, $a_n=\dfrac{1}{\log_n 2002}=\log_{2002} n.$ So
$$
 \begin{gathered} b-c \\ {}=\log_{2002}\frac{2\cdot3\cdot4\cdot5}{10\cdot11\cdot12\cdot13\cdot14}. \end{gathered}
$$

The fraction equals $\dfrac{120}{240240}=\dfrac{1}{2002},$ so $b-c=\log_{2002}\dfrac{1}{2002}=-1.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Key idea

- $\dfrac{1}{\log_n 2002}=\log_{2002} n$
- Then $b-c$ $=\log_{2002}\dfrac{2\cdot3\cdot4\cdot5}{10\cdot11\cdot12\cdot13\cdot14}$; simplify the fraction
