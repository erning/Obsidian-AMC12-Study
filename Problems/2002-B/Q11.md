# AMC12 2002-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

The positive integers $A,$ $B,$ $A-B,$ and $A+B$ are all prime numbers. The sum of these four primes is

(A) even
(B) divisible by $3$
(C) divisible by $5$
(D) divisible by $7$
(E) prime

## Answer

**E.** prime

## Solution

$A-B$ and $A+B$ have the same parity; being prime, both are odd, so one of $A,B$ is even. Since $A$ lies between the two odd primes $A-B$ and $A+B,$ it is the odd one, forcing $B=2.$

Then $A-2,$ $A,$ $A+2$ are three primes, which must be $3,$ $5,$ $7.$ Their sum together with $2$ is $2+3+5+7=17,$ a prime.

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Key idea

- $A-B$ and $A+B$ have the same parity, and both are prime, so both are odd
- Then $B=2,$ making $A-2,$ $A,$ $A+2$ three primes in arithmetic progression
