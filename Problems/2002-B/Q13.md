# AMC12 2002-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

The sum of $18$ consecutive positive integers is a perfect square. The smallest possible value of this sum is

(A) $169$
(B) $225$
(C) $289$
(D) $361$
(E) $441$

## Answer

**B.** $225$

## Solution

The sum of $n,$ $n+1,\ldots,$ $n+17$ is $18n+\dfrac{17\cdot18}{2}=9(2n+17).$ Since $9$ is a perfect square, $2n+17$ must be one too.

The smallest positive integer $n$ making $2n+17$ a perfect square is $n=4,$ giving $2n+17=25$ and a sum of $9\cdot25=225.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Key idea

- The sum of $n,$ $n+1,\ldots,$ $n+17$ equals $9(2n+17)$
- Since $9$ is already a square, $2n+17$ must be an odd perfect square; take the smallest valid one
