# AMC12 2002-B Q21

![[assets/problems/2002-B/Q21.png]]

## Problem

For all positive integers less than $2002$, let $n$ satisfy
$$
a_n=\begin{cases} 11, & 182\mid n;\\ 13, & 154\mid n;\\ 14, & 143\mid n. \end{cases}
$$
 In all other cases set $a_n=0$. Calculate $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{2001} a_n.$

(A) $448$
(B) $486$
(C) $1560$
(D) $2001$
(E) $2002$

## Answer

**A.** $448$

## Solution

Since $2002=11\cdot13\cdot14$ with $11,$ $13,$ $14$ pairwise coprime, $a_n=11$ when $182\mid n,$ $a_n=13$ when $154\mid n,$ and $a_n=14$ when $143\mid n$ (and no $n\lt2002$ is divisible by all three).

For $n\le2001$ there are $10$ multiples of $182,$ $12$ of $154,$ and $13$ of $143.$ So the sum is
$$
 \begin{gathered} 11\cdot10+13\cdot12+14\cdot13 \\ {}=110+156+182 \\ {}=448. \end{gathered}
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Key idea

- Because $11,$ $13,$ $14$ are pairwise coprime, "divisible by two of them" means divisible by their product
- Count multiples of $182,$ $154,$ $143$ below $2002$; no number under $2002$ is divisible by all three
