# AMC12 2003-B Q21

![[assets/problems/2003-B/Q21.png]]

## Problem

An object moves $8$ cm in a straight line from $A$ to $B,$ turns at an angle $\alpha,$ measured in radians and chosen at random from the interval $(0, \pi),$ and moves $5$ cm in a straight line to $C.$ What is the probability that $AC \lt 7?$

(A) $\dfrac{1}{6}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{5}$
(C) $\dfrac{1}{4}$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{3}$
(E) $\dfrac{1}{2}$

## Answer

**D.** $\dfrac{1}{3}$

## Solution

Let $\beta = \pi - \alpha$ be the interior angle of $\triangle ABC$ at $B.$ By the Law of Cosines,
$$
 \begin{aligned} &AC^2 = 8^2 + 5^2 \\ &\quad {}- 2(8)(5)\cos\beta \\ &= 89 - 80\cos\beta. \end{aligned}
$$

Then $AC \lt 7$ means $89 - 80\cos\beta \lt 49,$ i.e. $\cos\beta \gt \dfrac{1}{2},$ i.e. $\beta \lt \dfrac{\pi}{3}.$

As $\alpha$ is uniform on $(0, \pi),$ so is $\beta.$ The probability is
$$
 \frac{\pi/3}{\pi} = \frac{1}{3}.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- Let $\beta = \pi - \alpha$ be the interior angle at $B;$ apply the Law of Cosines to $\triangle ABC$
- $AC^2 = 89 - 80\cos\beta,$ so $AC \lt 7$ is equivalent to $\cos\beta \gt \dfrac{1}{2}$
