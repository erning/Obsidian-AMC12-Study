# AMC12 2003-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2003-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

Let $\clubsuit(x)$ denote the sum of the digits of the positive integer $x.$ For example, $\clubsuit(8) = 8$ and $\clubsuit(123) = 1 + 2 + 3 = 6.$ For how many two-digit values of $x$ is $\clubsuit(\clubsuit(x)) = 3?$

(A) $3$
(B) $4$
(C) $6$
(D) $9$
(E) $10$

## Answer

**E.** $10$

## Solution

Let $y = \clubsuit(x).$ Since $x \le 99,$ we have $y \le 18.$ Then $\clubsuit(y) = 3$ requires $y = 3$ or $y = 12.$

The two-digit numbers with digit sum $3$ are $12, 21, 30$ ($3$ values), and those with digit sum $12$ are $39, 48, 57, 66, 75, 84, 93$ ($7$ values), for $10$ in all.

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Key idea

- Let $y = \clubsuit(x).$ For a two-digit $x,$ $y$ is between $1$ and $18$
- $\clubsuit(y) = 3$ forces $y = 3$ or $y = 12;$ count two-digit $x$ with each digit sum
