# AMC12 2003-B Q22

![[assets/problems/2003-B/Q22.png]]

## Problem

Let $ABCD$ be a rhombus with $AC = 16$ and $BD = 30.$ Let $N$ be a point on $\overline{AB},$ and let $P$ and $Q$ be the feet of the perpendiculars from $N$ to $\overline{AC}$ and $\overline{BD},$ respectively. Which of the following is closest to the minimum possible value of $PQ?$

(A) $6.5$
(B) $6.75$
(C) $7$
(D) $7.25$
(E) $7.5$

## Answer

**C.** $7$

## Solution

Let $O$ be the intersection of the diagonals. Then $\triangle AOB$ is right-angled at $O$ with legs $OA = 8$ and $OB = 15.$ Quadrilateral $OPNQ$ has right angles at $O,$ $P,$ and $Q,$ so it is a rectangle and $PQ = ON.$

The minimum of $ON$ is the altitude from $O$ to $\overline{AB}$ in $\triangle AOB.$ Since $AB = \sqrt{8^2 + 15^2} = 17,$ equating the two area expressions gives
$$
 \begin{aligned} ON &= \frac{OA \cdot OB}{AB} \\ &= \frac{8 \cdot 15}{17} \\ &= \frac{120}{17} \approx 7.06. \end{aligned}
$$

This is closest to $7.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- Let $O$ be the center; the diagonals meet at right angles, so $OPNQ$ is a rectangle and $PQ = ON$
- $ON$ is smallest when $N$ is the foot of the altitude from $O$ in right triangle $AOB$ with legs $8$ and $15$
