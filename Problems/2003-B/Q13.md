# AMC12 2003-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2003-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

An ice cream cone consists of a sphere of vanilla ice cream and a right circular cone that has the same diameter as the sphere. If the ice cream melts, it will exactly fill the cone. Assume that the melted ice cream occupies $75\%$ of the volume of the frozen ice cream. What is the ratio of the cone's height to its radius?

(A) $2 : 1$
(B) $3 : 1$
(C) $4 : 1$
(D) $16 : 3$
(E) $6 : 1$

## Answer

**B.** $3 : 1$

## Solution

Let $r$ be the common radius and $h$ the cone's height. The melted ice cream fills the cone, so
$$
 \frac{3}{4}\cdot\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h.
$$

This simplifies to $\pi r^3 = \dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h,$ so $h = 3r,$ a ratio of $3 : 1.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Key idea

- The sphere and cone share the same radius $r;$ sphere volume is $\dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3$
- Set $75\%$ of the sphere's volume equal to the cone's volume $\dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$
