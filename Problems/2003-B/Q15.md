# AMC12 2003-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2003-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

A regular octagon $ABCDEFGH$ has an area of one square unit. What is the area of the rectangle $ABEF?$

(A) $1 - \dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$
(B) $\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{4}$
(C) $\sqrt{2} - 1$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{2}$
(E) $\dfrac{1 + \sqrt{2}}{4}$

## Answer

**D.** $\dfrac{1}{2}$

## Solution

Let $O$ be the center of the octagon. Joining $O$ to the vertices splits the octagon into $8$ congruent triangles, so $\triangle AOB$ has area $\dfrac{1}{8}.$

Since $O$ is the midpoint of $\overline{AE},$ triangles $AOB$ and $BOE$ have equal areas, so $\triangle ABE$ has area $\dfrac{1}{4}.$

The rectangle $ABEF$ is split by diagonal $\overline{BE}$ into two congruent triangles, so $\triangle ABE$ is half of it. Hence $ABEF$ has area $\dfrac{1}{2}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- Let $O$ be the center of the octagon, which is the midpoint of diagonal $AE$
- $\triangle AOB$ is one of the eight triangles from the center, so it has area $\dfrac{1}{8}$
