# AMC12 2003-B Q23

![[assets/problems/2003-B/Q23.png]]

## Problem

The number of $x$-intercepts on the graph of $y = \sin(1/x)$ in the interval $(0.0001, 0.001)$ is closest to

(A) $2900$
(B) $3000$
(C) $3100$
(D) $3200$
(E) $3300$

## Answer

**A.** $2900$

## Solution

The intercepts occur where $1/x = k\pi,$ that is $x = \dfrac{1}{k\pi}$ for a nonzero integer $k.$

The condition $0.0001 \lt \dfrac{1}{k\pi} \lt 0.001$ becomes
$$
 \frac{1000}{\pi} \lt k \lt \frac{10000}{\pi}.
$$

The number of such integers is
$$
 \begin{aligned} &\left\lfloor \frac{10000}{\pi} \right\rfloor - \left\lfloor \frac{1000}{\pi} \right\rfloor \\ &= 3183 - 318 = 2865, \end{aligned}
$$
 closest to $2900.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Key idea

- $\sin(1/x) = 0$ when $1/x = k\pi$ for a nonzero integer $k,$ i.e. $x = \dfrac{1}{k\pi}$
- Count integers $k$ with $\dfrac{1000}{\pi} \lt k \lt \dfrac{10000}{\pi}$
