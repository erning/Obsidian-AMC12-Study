# AMC12 2003-B Q25

![[assets/problems/2003-B/Q25.png]]

## Problem

Three points are chosen randomly and independently on a circle. What is the probability that all three pairwise distances between the points are less than the radius of the circle?

(A) $\dfrac{1}{36}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{24}$
(C) $\dfrac{1}{18}$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{12}$
(E) $\dfrac{1}{9}$

## Answer

**D.** $\dfrac{1}{12}$

## Solution

A chord has length less than the radius exactly when the arc it subtends is less than $60^\circ,$ since a chord of a $60^\circ$ arc equals the radius.

All three pairwise chords are shorter than the radius precisely when the three points all lie within some arc of $60^\circ.$

The probability that $n$ random points all lie within some arc of angle $L$ is $n\left(\dfrac{L}{2\pi}\right)^{n-1}.$ With $n = 3$ and $L = \dfrac{\pi}{3}$ (that is, $\dfrac{L}{2\pi} = \dfrac{1}{6}$), the probability is
$$
 3\left(\frac{1}{6}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{12}.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- A chord is shorter than the radius exactly when its arc measures less than $60^\circ$
- All three pairwise chords are short precisely when the three points lie within some $60^\circ$ arc
