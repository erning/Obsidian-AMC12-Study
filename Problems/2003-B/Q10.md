# AMC12 2003-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2003-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

Several figures can be made by attaching two equilateral triangles to the regular pentagon $ABCDE$ in two of the five positions shown. How many non-congruent figures can be constructed in this way?

(A) $1$
(B) $2$
(C) $3$
(D) $4$
(E) $5$

## Answer

**B.** $2$

## Solution

Assume one triangle is attached to side $AB.$ The second triangle can be attached to a side that is one step away or two steps away from $AB.$

Attaching it to $BC$ or $CD$ gives two figures; attaching it to $AE$ or $DE$ gives figures that are mirror images of these across the pentagon's axis of symmetry.

So there are only $2$ non-congruent figures.

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Key idea

- By symmetry, assume one triangle is attached to a fixed side, then place the second
- Only the two sides adjacent versus non-adjacent to the fixed side give different shapes, up to reflection
