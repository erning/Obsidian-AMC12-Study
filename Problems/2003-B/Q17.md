# AMC12 2003-B Q17

![[assets/problems/2003-B/Q17.png]]

## Problem

If $\log(xy^3) = 1$ and $\log(x^2y) = 1,$ what is $\log(xy)?$

(A) $-\dfrac{1}{2}$
(B) $0$
(C) $\dfrac{1}{2}$
(D) $\dfrac{3}{5}$
(E) $1$

## Answer

**D.** $\dfrac{3}{5}$

## Solution

Let $X = \log x$ and $Y = \log y.$ Then
$$
X + 3Y = 1
$$
 and
$$
2X + Y = 1.
$$

Solving gives $X = \dfrac{2}{5}$ and $Y = \dfrac{1}{5},$ so
$$
 \log(xy) = X + Y = \frac{3}{5}.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

- Let $X = \log x$ and $Y = \log y;$ the equations become linear in $X$ and $Y$
- Solve $X + 3Y = 1$ and $2X + Y = 1,$ then compute $X + Y$
