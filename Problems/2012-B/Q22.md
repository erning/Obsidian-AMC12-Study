# AMC12 2012-B Q22

![[assets/problems/2012-B/Q22.png]]

## Problem

A bug travels from $A$ to $B$ along the segments in the hexagonal lattice pictured below. The segments marked with an arrow can be traveled only in the direction of the arrow, and the bug never travels the same segment more than once. How many different paths are there?

(A) $2112$
(B) $2304$
(C) $2368$
(D) $2384$
(E) $2400$

## Answer

**E.** $2400$

## Solution

Label the seven columns of forward (rightward) segments; a path with no back segment simply chooses one forward segment in each column. The numbers of choices are $2,2,4,4,4,2,2,$ giving $2^{10}$ paths.

Let $s_1,s_2,s_3$ be the three left-pointing back segments (in columns $2,4,6$). Analyzing which columns become forced once a back segment is traversed gives $2^8$ paths for each of $\{s_1\}$ and $\{s_3\},$ $2^6$ for $\{s_1,s_3\},$ $2^9$ for $\{s_2\},$ $2^7$ for each of $\{s_1,s_2\}$ and $\{s_2,s_3\},$ and $2^5$ for $\{s_1,s_2,s_3\}.$

Adding, $$ \begin{aligned} &2^{10}+2\cdot2^8+2^6 \ &\quad {}+2^9+2\cdot2^7+2^5=2400. \end{aligned} $$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Casework
- Multiplication principle

## Key idea

Decompose the directed lattice path into columns, count the legal choices in each column, and subtract the cases that use a forbidden segment twice.

## Common mistakes

- Using a quantity in the wrong units or applying a formula before identifying the relevant structure.
- Losing a boundary condition, an excluded case, or a restriction from the original statement.
