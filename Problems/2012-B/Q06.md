# AMC12 2012-B Q06

![[assets/problems/2012-B/Q06.png]]

## Problem

In order to estimate the value of $x-y$ where $x$ and $y$ are real numbers with $x \gt y \gt 0,$ Xiaoli rounded $x$ up by a small amount, rounded $y$ down by the same amount, and then subtracted her rounded values. Which of the following statements is necessarily correct?

(A) Her estimate is larger than $x-y$.
(B) Her estimate is smaller than $x-y$.
(C) Her estimate equals $x-y$.
(D) Her estimate equals $y-x$.
(E) Her estimate is $0$.

## Answer

**A.** Her estimate is larger than $x-y$.

## Solution

Let $d \gt 0$ be the small amount. Xiaoli computes $(x+d)-(y-d)$ $=(x-y)+2d.$

Since $2d \gt 0,$ her estimate exceeds the true value $x-y.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Estimation
- Inequality

## Key idea

Represent the equal rounding adjustment by $d$; rounding one value up and the other down increases their difference by $2d$.

## Common mistakes

- Using a quantity in the wrong units or applying a formula before identifying the relevant structure.
- Losing a boundary condition, an excluded case, or a restriction from the original statement.
