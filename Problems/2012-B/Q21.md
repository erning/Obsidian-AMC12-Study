# AMC12 2012-B Q21

![[assets/problems/2012-B/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Square $AXYZ$ is inscribed in equiangular hexagon $ABCDEF$ with $X$ on $\overline{BC},$ $Y$ on $\overline{DE},$ and $Z$ on $\overline{EF}.$ Suppose that $AB = 40$ and $EF = 41(\sqrt{3} - 1).$ What is the side-length of the square?

(A) $29\sqrt{3}$
(B) $\frac{21\sqrt{2}+41\sqrt{3}}{2}$
(C) $20\sqrt{3}+16$
(D) $20\sqrt{2}+13\sqrt{3}$
(E) $21\sqrt{6}$

## Answer

**A.** $29\sqrt{3}$

## Solution

Extend $EF$ and $CB$ to a line through $A$ perpendicular to both, meeting them at $H$ and $J.$ Since $\angle ABJ=60^\circ,$ we have $BJ=20$ and $AJ=20\sqrt3.$ With $u=BX,$ the Pythagorean theorem gives $s^2=(20+u)^2+(20\sqrt3)^2.$

The equiangular angles make the four corner triangles congruent, and chasing the equal segments along $EF$ yields $$ \begin{aligned} u+20\sqrt3 &= 41(\sqrt3-1) \ &\quad {}+\frac{20+u}{\sqrt3}, \end{aligned} $$ so $u=21\sqrt3-20.$

Since $20+u=21\sqrt3,$ we get $$ \begin{aligned} s^2 &= (21\sqrt3)^2+(20\sqrt3)^2 \ &= 3(441+400)=3\cdot29^2, \end{aligned} $$ giving $s=29\sqrt3.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Equiangular polygon
- Congruence (geometry)
- Pythagorean theorem

## Key idea

Exploit the $60^\circ$ angles of the equiangular hexagon to express the square side with right triangles and equal corner segments.

## Common mistakes

- Using a quantity in the wrong units or applying a formula before identifying the relevant structure.
- Losing a boundary condition, an excluded case, or a restriction from the original statement.
