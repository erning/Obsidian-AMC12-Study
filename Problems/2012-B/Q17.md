# AMC12 2012-B Q17

![[assets/problems/2012-B/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Square $PQRS$ lies in the first quadrant. Points $(3, 0),$ $(5, 0),$ $(7, 0),$ and $(13, 0)$ lie on lines $SP,$ $RQ,$ $PQ,$ and $SR,$ respectively. What is the sum of the coordinates of the center of the square $PQRS?$

(A) $6$
(B) $6.2$
(C) $6.4$
(D) $6.6$
(E) $6.8$

## Answer

**C.** $6.4$

## Solution

Let $\theta$ be the acute angle line $PQ$ makes with the $x$-axis. Sides $SR=PQ$ span the segment from $(3,0)$ to $(5,0)$ as $2\cos\theta,$ while $SP=QR$ span the segment from $(7,0)$ to $(13,0)$ as $6\sin\theta.$

Since the square has equal sides, $2\cos\theta=6\sin\theta,$ so $\tan\theta=\tfrac13.$ Thus lines $SP,RQ$ have slope $3$ and lines $SR,PQ$ have slope $-\tfrac13.$

The center lies on the line through $(4,0)$ with slope $3$ and the line through $(10,0)$ with slope $-\tfrac13:$ $$y=3(x-4),$$ $$y=-\tfrac13(x-10).$$ These meet at $(4.6,1.8).$

The sum of the coordinates is $4.6+1.8=6.4.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Coordinate geometry
- Slope
- System of equations

## Key idea

Use the horizontal and vertical projections of equal square sides to determine the slope, then intersect the two lines through the midpoints of opposite sides.

## Common mistakes

- Using a quantity in the wrong units or applying a formula before identifying the relevant structure.
- Losing a boundary condition, an excluded case, or a restriction from the original statement.
