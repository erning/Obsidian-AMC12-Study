# AMC12 2012-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2012-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Bernardo and Silvia play the following game. An integer between $0$ and $999,$ inclusive, is selected and given to Bernardo. Whenever Bernardo receives a number, he doubles it and passes the result to Silvia. Whenever Silvia receives a number, she adds $50$ to it and passes the result to Bernardo. The winner is the last person who produces a number less than $1000.$ Let $N$ be the smallest initial number that results in a win for Bernardo. What is the sum of the digits of $N?$

(A) $7$
(B) $8$
(C) $9$
(D) $10$
(E) $11$

## Answer

**A.** $7$

## Solution

Bernardo wins after a round when his doubled number $2n+50\ge1000$ but the previous numbers stayed below $1000.$ The smallest $n$ with $2n+50\ge1000$ is $475.$

Working backwards, the smallest starting values that lead to a win after two, three, and four rounds are the smallest integers with $2n+50\ge475,$ $\ge213,$ and $\ge82,$ namely $213,$ $82,$ and $16.$ No start wins after more than four rounds.

So $N=16,$ and the sum of its digits is $1+6=7.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Work backwards
- Inequality

## Key idea

Work backward from the first value at which Bernardo’s doubling step reaches $1000$, then track the alternating transformations to the initial value.

## Common mistakes

- Using a quantity in the wrong units or applying a formula before identifying the relevant structure.
- Losing a boundary condition, an excluded case, or a restriction from the original statement.
