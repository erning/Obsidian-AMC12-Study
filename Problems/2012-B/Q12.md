# AMC12 2012-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2012-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

How many sequences of zeros and/or ones of length $20$ have all the zeros consecutive, or all the ones consecutive, or both?

(A) $190$
(B) $192$
(C) $211$
(D) $380$
(E) $382$

## Answer

**E.** $382$

## Solution

Let $A$ be the sequences in which all zeros are consecutive and $B$ those in which all ones are consecutive.

For $A,$ there is one all-ones sequence, $20$ sequences with exactly one zero, and $\binom{20}{2}=190$ sequences with two or more zeros (choose the first and last zero position). So $|A|=1+20+190=211,$ and by symmetry $|B|=211.$

A sequence in $A\cap B$ is a block of zeros followed by a block of ones, or the reverse; there are $|A\cap B|=40$ of these.

Therefore $|A\cup B|=211+211-40$ $=382.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Inclusion-exclusion
- Combinations

## Key idea

Count sequences with consecutive zeros and sequences with consecutive ones, then subtract their overlap using inclusion-exclusion.
