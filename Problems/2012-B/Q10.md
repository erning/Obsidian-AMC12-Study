# AMC12 2012-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2012-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

What is the area of the polygon whose vertices are the points of intersection of the curves $x^2 + y^2 = 25$ and $(x - 4)^2 + 9y^2 = 81?$

(A) $24$
(B) $27$
(C) $36$
(D) $37.5$
(E) $42$

## Answer

**B.** $27$

## Solution

From $x^2+y^2=25$ we get $y^2=25-x^2.$ Substituting into $(x-4)^2+9y^2=81$ gives $x^2+x-20=0,$ so $x=4$ or $x=-5.$

The intersection points are $(-5,0),$ $(4,3),$ and $(4,-3).$

The vertical side from $(4,3)$ to $(4,-3)$ has length $6,$ and the horizontal distance to $(-5,0)$ is $9,$ so the area is $\tfrac12\cdot6\cdot9=27.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- System of equations
- Substitution
- Triangle area

## Key idea

Subtract the two curve equations to find the intersection points, then recognize the resulting triangle and compute its area.

## Common mistakes

- Using a quantity in the wrong units or applying a formula before identifying the relevant structure.
- Losing a boundary condition, an excluded case, or a restriction from the original statement.
