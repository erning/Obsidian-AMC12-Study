# AMC12 2012-B Q01

![[assets/problems/2012-B/Q01.png]]

## Problem

Each third-grade classroom at Pearl Creek Elementary has $18$ students and $2$ pet rabbits. How many more students than rabbits are there in all $4$ of the third-grade classrooms?

(A) $48$
(B) $56$
(C) $64$
(D) $72$
(E) $80$

## Answer

**C.** $64$

## Solution

Each classroom has $18-2=16$ more students than rabbits.

Across all $4$ classrooms there are $4\cdot16=64$ more students than rabbits.

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Whole number operations

## Key idea

Compute the difference within one classroom first, then multiply by the number of classrooms.

## Common mistakes

- Using a quantity in the wrong units or applying a formula before identifying the relevant structure.
- Losing a boundary condition, an excluded case, or a restriction from the original statement.
