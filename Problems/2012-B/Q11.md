# AMC12 2012-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2012-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

In the equation below, $A$ and $B$ are consecutive positive integers, and $A,$ $B,$ and $A + B$ represent number bases: $$132_A + 43_B = 69_{A+B}.$$ What is $A + B?$

(A) $9$
(B) $11$
(C) $13$
(D) $15$
(E) $17$

## Answer

**C.** $13$

## Solution

Writing the numerals out, $132_A=A^2+3A+2,$ $43_B=4B+3,$ and $69_{A+B}=6(A+B)+9.$

With $B=A+1,$ the equation becomes $A^2+3A+2$ $+4(A+1)+3$ $=6(2A+1)+9,$ which simplifies to $(A-6)(A+1)=0.$ The positive solution is $A=6,$ so $B=7.$

(The case $B=A-1$ gives $A^2-5A-2=0,$ which has no integer solution.)

Therefore $A+B=13.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Number base
- Quadratic

## Key idea

Convert each numeral from its stated base to decimal and solve separately for the two possible orders of consecutive bases.

## Common mistakes

- Using a quantity in the wrong units or applying a formula before identifying the relevant structure.
- Losing a boundary condition, an excluded case, or a restriction from the original statement.
