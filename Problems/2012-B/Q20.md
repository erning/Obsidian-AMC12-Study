# AMC12 2012-B Q20

![[assets/problems/2012-B/Q20.png]]

## Problem

A trapezoid has side lengths $3,$ $5,$ $7,$ and $11.$ The sum of all the possible areas of the trapezoid can be written in the form of $r_1\sqrt{n_1} + r_2\sqrt{n_2} + r_3,$ where $r_1,$ $r_2,$ and $r_3$ are rational numbers and $n_1$ and $n_2$ are positive integers not divisible by the square of a prime. What is the greatest integer less than or equal to $$r_1 + r_2 + r_3 + n_1 + n_2?$$

(A) $57$
(B) $59$
(C) $61$
(D) $63$
(E) $65$

## Answer

**D.** $63$

## Solution

For a trapezoid with parallel sides $a\lt c$ and legs $b,d,$ translating a leg forms a triangle with sides $b,$ $d,$ and $c-a.$ The triangle inequality forces the longer parallel side to be $c=11.$

If $a=3,$ the triangle has sides $5,7,8$ with area $10\sqrt3,$ and the trapezoid has area $\tfrac{35}{2}\sqrt3.$ If $a=5,$ the triangle has sides $3,6,7$ with area $4\sqrt5,$ giving trapezoid area $\tfrac{32}{3}\sqrt5.$ If $a=7,$ the triangle has sides $3,4,5,$ a right triangle, giving trapezoid area $27.$

The total is $\tfrac{35}{2}\sqrt3+\tfrac{32}{3}\sqrt5+27,$ so $$ \begin{gathered} r_1+r_2+r_3+n_1+n_2 \ = \tfrac{35}{2}+\tfrac{32}{3}+27+3+5 \ = 63+\tfrac16. \end{gathered} $$

The greatest integer at most this value is $63.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Trapezoid
- Heron’s formula
- Casework

## Key idea

Choose which side is the shorter parallel side, form the associated triangle, and use Heron’s formula to obtain each possible trapezoid area.

## Common mistakes

- Using a quantity in the wrong units or applying a formula before identifying the relevant structure.
- Losing a boundary condition, an excluded case, or a restriction from the original statement.
