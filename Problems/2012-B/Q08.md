# AMC12 2012-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2012-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

A dessert chef prepares the dessert for every day of a week starting with Sunday. The dessert each day is either cake, pie, ice cream, or pudding. The same dessert may not be served two days in a row. There must be cake on Friday because of a birthday. How many different dessert menus for the week are possible?

(A) $729$
(B) $972$
(C) $1024$
(D) $2187$
(E) $2304$

## Answer

**A.** $729$

## Solution

Friday is fixed as cake. Work outward from Friday.

Each of the other six days (Saturday, then Thursday, Wednesday, Tuesday, Monday, Sunday) can be any dessert except the one served on the neighboring already-chosen day, giving $3$ choices each.

The number of menus is $3^6=729.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Multiplication principle
- Arrangements with restrictions

## Key idea

Fix Friday as cake and count the remaining days by multiplying the three allowed choices at each step, accounting for the adjacent-day restriction.

## Common mistakes

- Using a quantity in the wrong units or applying a formula before identifying the relevant structure.
- Losing a boundary condition, an excluded case, or a restriction from the original statement.
