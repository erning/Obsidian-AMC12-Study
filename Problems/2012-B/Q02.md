# AMC12 2012-B Q02

![[assets/problems/2012-B/Q02.png]]

## Problem

A circle of radius $5$ is inscribed in a rectangle as shown. The ratio of the length of the rectangle to its width is $2:1.$ What is the area of the rectangle?

(A) $50$
(B) $100$
(C) $125$
(D) $150$
(E) $200$

## Answer

**E.** $200$

## Solution

The circle is inscribed, so the width of the rectangle equals the diameter, $2\cdot5=10.$

The length is then $2\cdot10=20,$ so the area is $10\cdot20=200.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Circle
- Rectangle

## Key idea

Use the inscribed circle to determine the rectangle width from the diameter, then apply the given length-to-width ratio.

## Common mistakes

- Using a quantity in the wrong units or applying a formula before identifying the relevant structure.
- Losing a boundary condition, an excluded case, or a restriction from the original statement.
