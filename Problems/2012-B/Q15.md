# AMC12 2012-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2012-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

Jesse cuts a circular paper disk of radius $12$ along two radii to form two sectors, the smaller having a central angle of $120$ degrees. He makes two circular cones, using each sector to form the lateral surface of a cone. What is the ratio of the volume of the smaller cone to that of the larger?

(A) $\frac{1}{8}$
(B) $\frac{1}{4}$
(C) $\frac{\sqrt{10}}{10}$
(D) $\frac{\sqrt{5}}{6}$
(E) $\frac{\sqrt{5}}{5}$

## Answer

**C.** $\frac{\sqrt{10}}{10}$

## Solution

Each sector forms a cone with slant height $12.$ The smaller sector's arc length is $\tfrac{120}{360}\cdot2\pi\cdot12=8\pi,$ so its base radius is $4$ and its height is $\sqrt{12^2-4^2}=8\sqrt2.$

The larger sector (central angle $240^\circ$) has arc length $16\pi,$ base radius $8,$ and height $\sqrt{12^2-8^2}=4\sqrt5.$

The ratio of volumes is $$\frac{\tfrac13\pi\cdot4^2\cdot8\sqrt2}{\tfrac13\pi\cdot8^2\cdot4\sqrt5} =\frac{\sqrt{10}}{10}.$$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Cone
- Volume
- Pythagorean theorem

## Key idea

The sector arc lengths become cone circumferences; use them to find the base radii, then use the common slant height to find both heights and compare volumes.

## Common mistakes

- Using a quantity in the wrong units or applying a formula before identifying the relevant structure.
- Losing a boundary condition, an excluded case, or a restriction from the original statement.
