# AMC12 2009-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2009-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

Triangle $ABC$ has vertices $A = (3, 0),$ $B = (0, 3),$ and $C,$ where $C$ is on the line $x + y = 7.$ What is the area of $\triangle ABC?$

(A) $6$
(B) $8$
(C) $10$
(D) $12$
(E) $14$

## Answer

**A.** $6$

## Solution

Line $AB$ has equation $x + y = 3,$ which is parallel to $x + y = 7,$ so the area is independent of where $C$ lies on that line.

Take $C = (7, 0).$ Then the base $AC = 4$ lies on the $x$-axis with height $3,$ giving area $\frac{1}{2} \cdot 4 \cdot 3 = 6.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Coordinate geometry
- Triangle area
- Parallel lines

## Key idea

Use the line through $A$ and $B$ to find the area under the diagonal, then use the point on the line to determine the intersection area.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
