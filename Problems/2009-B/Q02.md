# AMC12 2009-B Q02

![[assets/problems/2009-B/Q02.png]]

## Problem

Paula the painter had just enough paint for $30$ identically sized rooms. Unfortunately, on the way to work, three cans of paint fell off her truck, so she had only enough paint for $25$ rooms. How many cans of paint did she use for the $25$ rooms?

(A) $10$
(B) $12$
(C) $15$
(D) $18$
(E) $25$

## Answer

**C.** $15$

## Solution

Losing $3$ cans cost her $5$ rooms, so $3$ cans paint $5$ rooms and each room needs $\frac{3}{5}$ of a can.

For $25$ rooms she used $25 \cdot \frac{3}{5} = 15$ cans.

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Ratio and proportion

## Key idea

Set the amount of paint per room equal to total paint divided by the number of rooms, then solve the new-room count from the changed amount.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
