# AMC12 2009-B Q05

![[assets/problems/2009-B/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Kiana has two older twin brothers. The product of their three ages is $128.$ What is the sum of their three ages?

(A) $10$
(B) $12$
(C) $16$
(D) $18$
(E) $24$

## Answer

**D.** $18$

## Solution

Since $128 = 2^7,$ each age is a power of $2.$ The twins share an age $t,$ so Kiana's age is $\frac{128}{t^2}.$

Taking $t = 8$ gives Kiana $\frac{128}{64} = 2,$ who is younger than the twins. (Smaller twins would make Kiana older, which is not allowed.) The sum is $8 + 8 + 2 = 18.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Prime factorization
- Power of 2

## Key idea

Use prime factorization: the product of twin ages is a power of $2$, so both ages must be powers of $2$.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
