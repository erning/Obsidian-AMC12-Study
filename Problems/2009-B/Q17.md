# AMC12 2009-B Q17

![[assets/problems/2009-B/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Each face of a cube is given a single narrow stripe painted from the center of one edge to the center of its opposite edge. The choice of the edge pairing is made at random and independently for each face. What is the probability that there is a continuous stripe encircling the cube?

(A) $\frac{1}{8}$
(B) $\frac{3}{16}$
(C) $\frac{1}{4}$
(D) $\frac{3}{8}$
(E) $\frac{1}{2}$

## Answer

**B.** $\frac{3}{16}$

## Solution

Each of the $6$ faces has $2$ equally likely stripe orientations, for $2^6 = 64$ configurations.

An encircling stripe runs around one of the $3$ pairs of opposite faces. Fixing such a band, the four faces it passes through must be aligned, with probability $\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^4 = \frac{1}{16},$ while the two remaining faces are free. The $3$ possible bands are disjoint events, so the probability is $3 \cdot \frac{1}{16} = \frac{3}{16}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Key idea

For each cube face, count stripe patterns that make a continuous path; use symmetry to combine the cases.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
