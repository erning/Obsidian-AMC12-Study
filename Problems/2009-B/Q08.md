# AMC12 2009-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2009-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

When a bucket is two-thirds full of water, the bucket and water weigh $a$ kilograms. When the bucket is one-half full of water the total weight is $b$ kilograms. In terms of $a$ and $b,$ what is the total weight in kilograms when the bucket is full of water?

(A) $\frac{2}{3}a + \frac{1}{3}b$
(B) $\frac{3}{2}a - \frac{1}{2}b$
(C) $\frac{3}{2}a + b$
(D) $\frac{3}{2}a + 2b$
(E) $3a - 2b$

## Answer

**E.** $3a - 2b$

## Solution

Let $x$ be the bucket's weight and $y$ the weight of a full bucket of water. Then $x + \frac{2}{3}y = a$ and $x + \frac{1}{2}y = b.$

Subtracting gives $\frac{1}{6}y = a - b,$ so $y = 6a - 6b$ and $x = b - \frac{1}{2}y = 4b - 3a.$ The full weight is $x + y = 3a - 2b.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- System of equations
- Substitution

## Key idea

Let the bucket’s capacity be $C$ and compare the two given total weights to solve for the empty bucket and water weights.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
