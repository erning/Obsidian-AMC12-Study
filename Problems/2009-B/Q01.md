# AMC12 2009-B Q01

![[assets/problems/2009-B/Q01.png]]

## Problem

Each morning of her five-day workweek, Jane bought either a $50$-cent muffin or a $75$-cent bagel. Her total cost for the week was a whole number of dollars. How many bagels did she buy?

(A) $1$
(B) $2$
(C) $3$
(D) $4$
(E) $5$

## Answer

**B.** $2$

## Solution

With $b$ bagels she buys $5 - b$ muffins, costing $50(5-b) + 75b = 250 + 25b$ cents. For a whole number of dollars this must be a multiple of $100,$ so $25b$ must end in $50,$ meaning $b$ is even.

Among $b = 2$ and $b = 4,$ only $b = 2$ works: $250 + 50 = 300$ cents $= \$3.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Parity
- Money
- Casework

## Key idea

For each day, compare the amount paid with the two possible prices and use parity to determine which days contribute an odd total.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
