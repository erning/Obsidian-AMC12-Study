# AMC12 2009-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2009-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

The fifth and eighth terms of a geometric sequence of real numbers are $7!$ and $8!$ respectively. What is the first term?

(A) $60$
(B) $75$
(C) $120$
(D) $225$
(E) $315$

## Answer

**E.** $315$

## Solution

The eighth term divided by the fifth term is $r^3 = \frac{8!}{7!} = 8,$ so $r = 2.$

The fifth term is $a r^4 = 7!,$ so $a = \frac{7!}{16} = \frac{5040}{16} = 315.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Geometric sequence
- Factorial

## Key idea

Use the geometric-sequence formula for the fifth and eighth terms and solve the resulting equation for the common ratio.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
