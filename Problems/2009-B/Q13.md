# AMC12 2009-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2009-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Triangle $ABC$ has $AB = 13$ and $AC = 15,$ and the altitude to $BC$ has length $12.$ What is the sum of the two possible values of $BC?$

(A) $15$
(B) $16$
(C) $17$
(D) $18$
(E) $19$

## Answer

**D.** $18$

## Solution

Let $D$ be the foot of the altitude from $A.$ Then $BD = \sqrt{13^2 - 12^2} = 5$ and $DC = \sqrt{15^2 - 12^2} = 9.$

If $D$ lies between $B$ and $C,$ then $BC = 5 + 9 = 14$; if the triangle is obtuse, $BC = 9 - 5 = 4.$ The sum is $14 + 4 = 18.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Pythagorean theorem
- Altitude
- Casework

## Key idea

Use the altitude from $A$ and the Pythagorean theorem; the possible foot positions give a small finite case split.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
