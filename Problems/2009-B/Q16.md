# AMC12 2009-B Q16

![[assets/problems/2009-B/Q16.png]]

## Problem

Trapezoid $ABCD$ has $AD \parallel BC,$ $BD = 1,$ $\angle DBA = 23^\circ,$ and $\angle BDC = 46^\circ.$ The ratio $BC : AD$ is $9 : 5.$ What is $CD?$

(A) $\frac{7}{9}$
(B) $\frac{4}{5}$
(C) $\frac{13}{15}$
(D) $\frac{8}{9}$
(E) $\frac{14}{15}$

## Answer

**B.** $\frac{4}{5}$

## Solution

Draw the line through $D$ parallel to $AB,$ meeting $BC$ at $E,$ so $ABED$ is a parallelogram with $BE = AD.$ Then $\angle BDE = \angle DBA = 23^\circ,$ and since $\angle BDC = 46^\circ,$ segment $DE$ bisects $\angle BDC.$

By the angle bisector theorem in $\triangle BDC,$ $\frac{EC}{BE} = \frac{DC}{DB},$ so $$ \begin{aligned} CD &= DB \cdot \frac{BC - AD}{AD} \\ &= 1 \cdot \left(\frac{9}{5} - 1\right) \\ &= \frac{4}{5}. \end{aligned} $$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Angle bisector theorem
- Trapezoid
- Parallel lines

## Key idea

Apply the angle-bisector theorem to express the two bases and legs, then use the trapezoid area formula.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
