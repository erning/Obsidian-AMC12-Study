# AMC12 2009-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2009-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

Assume $0 \lt r \lt 3.$ Below are five equations for $x.$ Which equation has the largest solution $x?$

(A) $3(1+r)^x=7$
(B) $3(1+r/10)^x=7$
(C) $3(1+2r)^x=7$
(D) $3(1+\sqrt r)^x=7$
(E) $3(1+1/r)^x=7$

## Answer

**B.** $3(1+r/10)^x=7$

## Solution

Each equation gives $x = \frac{\log(7/3)}{\log(1 + f(r))},$ which is largest when the positive quantity $f(r)$ is smallest.

For $0 \lt r \lt 3,$ among $r,\ \frac{r}{10},\ 2r,\ \sqrt{r},\ \frac{1}{r},$ the smallest is $\frac{r}{10}$: it is below $r$ and below $\sqrt{r}$ since $r \lt 3 \lt 100.$ So equation (B) has the largest solution.

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm
- Inequality

## Key idea

Take logarithms of each candidate equation and use the monotonicity of $\log(1+t)$ for $0<r<3$.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
