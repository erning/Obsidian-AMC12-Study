# AMC12 2009-B Q22

![[assets/problems/2009-B/Q22.png]]

## Problem

Parallelogram $ABCD$ has area $1{,}000{,}000.$ Vertex $A$ is at $(0, 0)$ and all other vertices are in the first quadrant. Vertices $B$ and $D$ are lattice points on the lines $y = x$ and $y = kx$ for some integer $k \gt 1,$ respectively. How many such parallelograms are there?

(A) $49$
(B) $720$
(C) $784$
(D) $2009$
(E) $2048$

## Answer

**C.** $784$

## Solution

Let $B = (b, b)$ and $D = (d, kd)$ with $b, d, k$ positive integers and $k \gt 1.$ The area is $(k - 1)bd = 1{,}000{,}000 = 2^6 \cdot 5^6.$

Each parallelogram corresponds to an ordered triple $(k - 1, b, d)$ of positive integers with product $2^6 \cdot 5^6.$ The six $2$'s distribute among the three factors in $\binom{6 + 2}{2} = 28$ ways, and likewise the six $5$'s in $28$ ways, giving $28^2 = 784.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Lattice point
- Stars and bars
- Prime factorization

## Key idea

Translate lattice points in the parallelogram into integer pairs satisfying a linear inequality, then count them by stars and bars.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
