# AMC12 2023-A Q20

![[assets/problems/2023-A/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Rows $1,2,3,4,$ and $5$ of a triangular array are shown. Each row after the first has a $1$ at each end, and each interior entry is $1$ greater than the sum of the two numbers diagonally above it. What is the units digit of the sum of the $2023$ numbers in row $2023$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $5$**

## Solution

Let $S_n$ be the sum of row $n$. The two endpoints of row $n$ contribute $2$. The diagonal sums of the interior entries contribute $2S_{n-1}-2$, because the two endpoint entries of row $n-1$ occur only once. The $n-2$ interior entries each add $1$, so
$$
S_n=2S_{n-1}+(n-2),\qquad S_1=1.
$$
The closed form is
$$
S_n=2^n-n.
$$
Since $2023\equiv3\pmod4$, $2^{2023}$ has units digit $8$. Therefore
$$
S_{2023}\equiv8-3=\boxed{5}\pmod{10}.
$$
