# AMC12 2023-A Q04

![[assets/problems/2023-A/Q04.png]]

## Problem

How many digits does the positive integer $8^5\cdot5^{10}\cdot15^5$ have in base ten?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $18$**

## Solution

Factor the powers:
$$
8^5=2^{15},\qquad 15^5=3^5\cdot5^5.
$$
Therefore
$$
8^5\cdot5^{10}\cdot15^5
=3^5\cdot10^{15}
=243\cdot10^{15}.
$$
This is $243$ followed by $15$ zeroes, so it has
$$
3+15=\boxed{18}
$$
digits.

## Knowledge points

- Prime factorization
- Decimal place value

## Key idea

Pair equal powers of $2$ and $5$ to form powers of $10$.

## Common mistakes

- Counting only the number of trailing zeroes.
