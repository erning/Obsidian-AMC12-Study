# AMC12 2023-A Q02

![[assets/problems/2023-A/Q02.png]]

## Problem

The weight of $\frac13$ of a large pizza together with $3\frac12$ cups of orange slices is the same as the weight of $\frac34$ of a large pizza together with $\frac12$ cup of orange slices. A cup of orange slices weighs $\frac14$ pound. What is the weight, in pounds, of a large pizza?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $1\frac45$**

## Solution

Let $P$ be the weight of one pizza. Then
$$
\frac13P+\frac72\cdot\frac14
=\frac34P+\frac12\cdot\frac14.
$$
Thus
$$
\frac13P+\frac78=\frac34P+\frac18,
$$
so
$$
\frac{5}{12}P=\frac34
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
P=\boxed{\frac95}=1\frac45.
$$

## Key idea

Translate both combined weights into one linear equation.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $3\frac12$ as $\frac32$.
