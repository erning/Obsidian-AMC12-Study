# AMC12 2023-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2023-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Positive real numbers $a,b$ satisfy
$$
(1+2a)(2+2b)(2a+b)=32ab.
$$
What is $a+b$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $1$**

## Solution

Apply AM-GM:
$$
1+2a\ge2\sqrt{2a},\qquad
2+2b\ge4\sqrt b,\qquad
2a+b\ge2\sqrt{2ab}.
$$
Multiplying gives a lower bound of $32ab$, exactly the given value. Equality must hold in every inequality, so
$$
1=2a,\qquad b=1,\qquad 2a=b.
$$
Thus $a=\frac12$, $b=1$, and
$$
a+b=\boxed{1}.
$$

## Key idea

Recognize that the given product equals the AM-GM lower bound.

## Common mistakes

- Applying AM-GM without checking its equality conditions.
