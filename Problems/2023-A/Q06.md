# AMC12 2023-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2023-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Points $A$ and $B$ lie on $y=\log_2x$. The midpoint of $\overline{AB}$ is $(6,2)$. What is the positive difference between the $x$-coordinates of $A$ and $B$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $4\sqrt5$**

## Solution

Let the $x$-coordinates be $x_1,x_2$. The midpoint gives
$$
x_1+x_2=12,\qquad \log_2x_1+\log_2x_2=4.
$$
Therefore $x_1x_2=16$, and the positive difference between the $x$-coordinates is
$$
(x_1-x_2)^2=(x_1+x_2)^2-4x_1x_2=144-64=80.
$$
Hence the requested difference is
$$
\lvert x_1-x_2\rvert=\sqrt{80}=\boxed{4\sqrt5}.
$$

## Key idea

Use the midpoint to obtain the sum and product of the two $x$-coordinates.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the graph as a line and using only the horizontal difference.
