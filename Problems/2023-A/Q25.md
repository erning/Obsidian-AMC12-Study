# AMC12 2023-A Q25

![[assets/problems/2023-A/Q25.png]]

## Problem

There is a unique sequence of integers $a_1,a_2,\ldots,a_{2023}$ such that
$$
\tan(2023x)=\frac{a_1\tan x+a_3\tan^3x+\cdots+a_{2023}\tan^{2023}x}{1+a_2\tan^2x+a_4\tan^4x+\cdots+a_{2022}\tan^{2022}x}
$$
whenever $\tan(2023x)$ is defined. What is $a_{2023}$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $-1$**

## Solution

Put $t=\tan x$. From
$$
(\cos x+i\sin x)^{2023}=\cos(2023x)+i\sin(2023x),
$$
the numerator is the odd part of $(1+it)^{2023}$ and the denominator is its even part. The coefficient of $t^{2023}$ in the numerator is
$$
i^{2023-1}=i^{2022}=(-1)^{1011}=-1.
$$
Therefore
$$
a_{2023}=\boxed{-1}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Multiple-angle identities
- Complex numbers
- Binomial theorem

## Key idea

Read the leading coefficient from the odd part of the binomial expansion.

## Common mistakes

- Ignoring the alternating signs from powers of $i$.
