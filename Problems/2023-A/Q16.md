# AMC12 2023-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2023-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

Consider the set of complex numbers $z$ satisfying $|1+z+z^2|=4$. The maximum value of the imaginary part of $z$ can be written as $\frac{\sqrt m}{n}$, where $m,n$ are relatively prime positive integers. What is $m+n$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $21$**

## Solution

Write $z=x+iy$. Then
$$
1+z+z^2=(1+x+x^2-y^2)+iy(1+2x).
$$
Let $P=1+x+x^2-y^2$. The condition becomes
$$
P^2+y^2(1+2x)^2=16.
$$
At the highest point of this curve, the horizontal-tangent condition gives
$$
(1+2x)(P+2y^2)=0.
$$
The second factor cannot vanish at the maximum, so $x=-\frac12$. The constraint is then
$$
\left(\frac34-y^2\right)^2=16.
$$
For the positive maximum,
$$
\frac34-y^2=-4,
\qquad y=\frac{\sqrt{19}}2.
$$
Thus $m=19,n=2$, and $m+n=\boxed{21}$.

## Key idea

Convert the modulus condition into a real curve and find its highest point.
