# AMC12 2023-A Q17

![[assets/problems/2023-A/Q17.png]]

## Problem

A frog starts at $0$. On each jump it moves forward a positive integer $m$ with probability $\frac1{2^m}$. What is the probability that it eventually lands exactly on $10$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $\frac12$**

## Solution

Let $a_n$ be the probability of ever landing on $n$, with $a_0=1$. Conditioning on the final jump gives
$$
a_n=\sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac1{2^k}a_{n-k}.
$$
We have $a_1=\frac12$ and $a_2=\frac12$. If $a_1,\ldots,a_{n-1}=\frac12$, then
$$
a_n=\frac1{2^n}+\frac12\sum_{k=1}^{n-1}\frac1{2^k}
=\frac12.
$$
By induction, $a_{10}=\boxed{\frac12}$.

## Key idea

Condition on the final jump and use induction.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the frog must land on every intermediate integer.
