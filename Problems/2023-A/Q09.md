# AMC12 2023-A Q09

![[assets/problems/2023-A/Q09.png]]

## Problem

A square of area $2$ is inscribed in a square of area $3$, as shown. What is the ratio of the shorter leg to the longer leg of one of the four congruent shaded right triangles?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $2-\sqrt3$**

## Solution

Let the legs be $p<q$. The outer and inner side lengths are $\sqrt3$ and $\sqrt2$, so
$$
p+q=\sqrt3,\qquad p^2+q^2=2.
$$
Thus $2pq=1$, and $p,q$ are the roots of
$$
t^2-\sqrt3\,t+\frac12=0.
$$
Therefore
$$
p=\frac{\sqrt3-1}{2},\qquad q=\frac{\sqrt3+1}{2}.
$$
The desired ratio is
$$
\frac pq=\frac{\sqrt3-1}{\sqrt3+1}=\boxed{2-\sqrt3}.
$$

Use the sum and sum of squares of the two legs.
