# AMC12 2023-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2023-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

For distinct randomly chosen vertices $Q,R,S$ of a regular icosahedron, let $d(A,B)$ be the minimum number of edges needed to connect vertices $A$ and $B$. What is the probability that $d(Q,R)>d(R,S)$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $\frac7{22}$**

## Solution

Fix $R$. Of the other $11$ vertices, exactly $5$ are at distance $1$ from $R$, $5$ at distance $2$, and $1$ at distance $3$. For ordered distinct choices $(Q,S)$, equality occurs in
$$
5\cdot4+5\cdot4=40
$$
cases. There are $11\cdot10=110$ ordered pairs, so
$$
P(d(Q,R)=d(R,S))=\frac4{11}.
$$
The greater-than and less-than cases have equal probability. Thus
$$
P(d(Q,R)>d(R,S))
=\frac{1-\frac4{11}}2
=\boxed{\frac7{22}}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Graph distance
- Icosahedron symmetry
- Counting

## Key idea

Classify the other vertices by distance from the fixed middle vertex.

## Common mistakes

- Allowing $Q=S$, even though the vertices are distinct.
