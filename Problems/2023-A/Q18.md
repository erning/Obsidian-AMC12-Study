# AMC12 2023-A Q18

![[assets/problems/2023-A/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Circle $C_1$ and $C_2$ each have radius $1$, and the distance between their centers is $\frac12$. Circle $C_3$ is the largest circle internally tangent to both $C_1$ and $C_2$. Circle $C_4$ is internally tangent to both $C_1$ and $C_2$ and externally tangent to $C_3$. What is the radius of $C_4$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $\frac3{28}$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2023-A/Q18-draft.svg]]

Place the centers of $C_1,C_2$ at $\left(-\frac14,0\right)$ and $\left(\frac14,0\right)$. The largest circle $C_3$ is centered at the origin and has radius $\frac34$.

Let $C_4$ have radius $r$ and center $(0,k)$. External tangency to $C_3$ gives
$$
k=\frac34+r.
$$
Internal tangency to either unit circle gives
$$
\left(\frac14\right)^2+k^2=(1-r)^2.
$$
Substitution yields
$$
\frac1{16}+\left(\frac34+r\right)^2=(1-r)^2.
$$
After cancellation,
$$
\frac38+\frac72r=1,
$$
so
$$
r=\boxed{\frac3{28}}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Tangent circles
- Coordinate geometry
- Symmetry

## Key idea

Use symmetry and express both tangencies as center-distance equations.

## Common mistakes

- Using $\frac34-r$ for external tangency.
