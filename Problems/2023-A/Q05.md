# AMC12 2023-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2023-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

A standard die is rolled four times. What is the probability that the running total equals $3$ at some point?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $\frac{49}{216}$**

## Solution

The first time the running total reaches $3$ must occur in one of these disjoint ways:
$$
\text{first roll is }3,
$$
or the first two rolls are $(1,2)$ or $(2,1)$, or the first three rolls are $(1,1,1)$. Hence
$$
\frac16+2\cdot\frac1{36}+\frac1{216}
=\frac{36+12+1}{216}
=\boxed{\frac{49}{216}}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Probability
- Disjoint cases
- Stopping times

## Key idea

Partition by the first time the running total reaches $3$.

## Common mistakes

- Counting paths after the total has already exceeded $3$.
