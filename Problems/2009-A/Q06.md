# AMC12 2009-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2009-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Suppose that $P = 2^m$ and $Q = 3^n.$ Which of the following is equal to $12^{mn}$ for every pair of integers $(m, n)?$

(A) $P^2Q$
(B) $P^nQ^m$
(C) $P^nQ^{2m}$
(D) $P^{2m}Q^n$
(E) $P^{2n}Q^m$

## Answer

**E.** $P^{2n}Q^m$

## Solution

Since $12 = 2^2 \cdot 3,$ $$ \begin{aligned} 12^{mn} &= 2^{2mn} \cdot 3^{mn} \\ &= (2^m)^{2n}(3^n)^m \\ &= P^{2n}Q^m. \end{aligned} $$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Exponent
- Substitution

## Key idea

Substitute $P=2^m$ and $Q=3^n$ into each expression and compare exponents of $2$ and $3$.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
