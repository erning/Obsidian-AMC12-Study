# AMC12 2009-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2009-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

What number is one third of the way from $\frac{1}{4}$ to $\frac{3}{4}?$

(A) $\frac{1}{3}$
(B) $\frac{5}{12}$
(C) $\frac{1}{2}$
(D) $\frac{7}{12}$
(E) $\frac{2}{3}$

## Answer

**B.** $\frac{5}{12}$

## Solution

The gap is $\frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{2}.$ One third of the way adds $\frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{6}.$

So the number is $\frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{3}{12} + \frac{2}{12} = \frac{5}{12}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Fraction
- Ratio and proportion

## Key idea

Use the number line: one third of the way from $\frac14$ to $\frac34$ is the corresponding weighted average.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
