# AMC12 2009-A Q17

![[assets/problems/2009-A/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Let $a + ar_1 + ar_1^2 + ar_1^3 + \cdots$ and $a + ar_2 + ar_2^2 + ar_2^3 + \cdots$ be two different infinite geometric series of positive numbers with the same first term. The sum of the first series is $r_1,$ and the sum of the second series is $r_2.$ What is $r_1 + r_2?$

(A) $0$
(B) $\frac12$
(C) $1$
(D) $\frac{1+\sqrt5}{2}$
(E) $2$

## Answer

**C.** $1$

## Solution

For a series with first term $a$ and ratio $r,$ the sum is $\frac{a}{1 - r} = r,$ so $r^2 - r + a = 0.$

Both $r_1$ and $r_2$ satisfy this same quadratic, and since the two series are different, $r_1 \ne r_2,$ so they are its two distinct roots. By Vieta's formulas, $r_1 + r_2 = 1.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Geometric sequence
- Vieta’s formulas

## Key idea

Relate the two infinite geometric sums to their ratios and use Vieta’s formulas to determine $r_1+r_2$.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
