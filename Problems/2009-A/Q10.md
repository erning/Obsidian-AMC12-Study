# AMC12 2009-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2009-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

In quadrilateral $ABCD,$ $AB = 5,$ $BC = 17,$ $CD = 5,$ $DA = 9,$ and $BD$ is an integer. What is $BD?$

(A) $11$
(B) $12$
(C) $13$
(D) $14$
(E) $15$

## Answer

**C.** $13$

## Solution

In $\triangle BCD,$ the triangle inequality gives $BD + CD \gt BC,$ so $BD + 5 \gt 17$ and $BD \gt 12.$

In $\triangle ABD,$ $AB + DA \gt BD,$ so $BD \lt 5 + 9 = 14.$

The only integer with $12 \lt BD \lt 14$ is $13.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Triangle inequality
- Bounding to limit cases

## Key idea

The diagonal $BD$ must satisfy the triangle inequality in the two triangles sharing it; maximize it at the boundary.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
