# AMC12 2009-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2009-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Let $p(x) = x^3 + ax^2 + bx + c,$ where $a,$ $b,$ and $c$ are complex numbers. Suppose that $$ \begin{gathered} p(2009 + 9002\pi i) \\ = p(2009) \\ = p(9002) = 0. \end{gathered} $$ What is the number of nonreal zeros of $x^{12} + ax^8 + bx^4 + c?$

(A) $4$
(B) $6$
(C) $8$
(D) $10$
(E) $12$

## Answer

**C.** $8$

## Solution

Since $x^{12} + ax^8 + bx^4 + c = p(x^4),$ a value is a zero exactly when $x^4$ equals one of the roots of $p,$ namely $2009 + 9002\pi i,$ $2009,$ or $9002.$

The equation $x^4 = 2009 + 9002\pi i$ has four distinct nonreal roots. Each of $x^4 = 2009$ and $x^4 = 9002$ has two real roots and two nonreal roots.

So the nonreal zeros number $4 + 2 + 2 = 8.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

A cubic with roots among the cube roots of unity is constrained by the coefficient of $x^2$; enumerate the possible complex coefficients.
