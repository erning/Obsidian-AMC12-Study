# AMC12 2009-A Q15

![[assets/problems/2009-A/Q15.png]]

## Problem

For what value of $n$ is $$ \begin{aligned} &i + 2i^2 + 3i^3 + \cdots + ni^n \\ &= 48 + 49i? \end{aligned} $$ Note: here $i = \sqrt{-1}.$

(A) $24$
(B) $48$
(C) $49$
(D) $97$
(E) $98$

## Answer

**D.** $97$

## Solution

For $k$ a multiple of $4,$ $$ \begin{aligned} &(k + 1)i^{k+1} + (k + 2)i^{k+2} \\ &\quad {}+ (k + 3)i^{k+3} + (k + 4)i^{k+4} \\ &= (k + 1)i - (k + 2) \\ &\quad {}- (k + 3)i + (k + 4) \\ &= 2 - 2i. \end{aligned} $$

Summing the first $96$ terms (that is $24$ blocks) gives $24(2 - 2i) = 48 - 48i.$

Adding the next term $97i^{97} = 97i$ yields $48 - 48i + 97i = 48 + 49i.$ So $n = 97.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Complex number
- Pairing and grouping

## Key idea

Pair powers of $i$ in blocks of four and sum the resulting cycle; the condition on $n$ fixes the remainder modulo $4$.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
