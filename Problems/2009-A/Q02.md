# AMC12 2009-A Q02

![[assets/problems/2009-A/Q02.png]]

## Problem

Which of the following is equal to $$1 + \frac{1}{1 + \frac{1}{1 + 1}}?$$

(A) $\frac{5}{4}$
(B) $\frac{3}{2}$
(C) $\frac{5}{3}$
(D) $2$
(E) $3$

## Answer

**C.** $\frac{5}{3}$

## Solution

Starting inside, $1 + 1 = 2,$ so $1 + \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{2}.$ Then $\frac{1}{3/2} = \frac{2}{3},$ and $1 + \frac{2}{3} = \frac{5}{3}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Continued fraction
- Fraction

## Key idea

Evaluate the finite continued fraction from the bottom upward rather than trying to combine all levels at once.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
