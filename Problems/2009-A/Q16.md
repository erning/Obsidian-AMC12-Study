# AMC12 2009-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2009-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

A circle with center $C$ is tangent to the positive $x$- and $y$-axes and externally tangent to the circle centered at $(3, 0)$ with radius $1.$ What is the sum of all possible radii of the circle with center $C?$

(A) $3$
(B) $4$
(C) $6$
(D) $8$
(E) $9$

## Answer

**D.** $8$

## Solution

A circle tangent to both positive axes with radius $r$ has center $(r, r).$ External tangency to the circle at $(3, 0)$ of radius $1$ means the distance between centers is $r + 1$: $$(r - 3)^2 + r^2 = (r + 1)^2.$$

Expanding gives $r^2 - 8r + 8 = 0.$ Both roots $r = 4 \pm 2\sqrt{2}$ are positive, and by Vieta's formulas their sum is $8.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Tangent circles
- Coordinate geometry
- Vieta’s formulas

## Key idea

Use coordinates for the tangent circle and solve the distance equation for the possible center positions.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
