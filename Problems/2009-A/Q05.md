# AMC12 2009-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2009-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

One dimension of a cube is increased by $1,$ another is decreased by $1,$ and the third is left unchanged. The volume of the new rectangular solid is $5$ less than that of the cube. What was the volume of the cube?

(A) $8$
(B) $27$
(C) $64$
(D) $125$
(E) $216$

## Answer

**D.** $125$

## Solution

Let the cube have side length $x.$ The new solid has dimensions $x + 1,$ $x - 1,$ and $x,$ so its volume is $x(x+1)(x-1) = x^3 - x.$

Setting this equal to $x^3 - 5$ gives $x^3 - x = x^3 - 5,$ so $x = 5.$

The cube's volume is $5^3 = 125.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Volume
- Difference of squares

## Key idea

Let the original side length be $s$ and compare the two volumes using difference of cubes.
