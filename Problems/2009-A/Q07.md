# AMC12 2009-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2009-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

The first three terms of an arithmetic sequence are $2x - 3,$ $5x - 11,$ and $3x + 1$ respectively. The $n$th term of the sequence is $2009.$ What is $n?$

(A) $255$
(B) $502$
(C) $1004$
(D) $1506$
(E) $8037$

## Answer

**B.** $502$

## Solution

Equal consecutive differences give $(5x - 11) - (2x - 3)$ $= (3x + 1) - (5x - 11),$ that is $3x - 8 = -2x + 12,$ so $x = 4.$

The first three terms are $5, 9, 13,$ with common difference $4.$

The $n$th term satisfies $2009 = 5 + (n - 1)\cdot 4,$ so $n - 1 = 501$ and $n = 502.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Arithmetic sequence

## Key idea

Use the arithmetic-sequence formula for the sum of the first three terms, then evaluate the sum of the first $100$ terms.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
