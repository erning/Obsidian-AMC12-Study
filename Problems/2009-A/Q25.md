# AMC12 2009-A Q25

![[assets/problems/2009-A/Q25.png]]

## Problem

The first two terms of a sequence are $a_1 = 1$ and $a_2 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}.$ For $n \ge 1,$ $$a_{n+2} = \frac{a_n + a_{n+1}}{1 - a_n a_{n+1}}.$$ What is $|a_{2009}|?$

(A) $0$
(B) $2-\sqrt3$
(C) $\frac{1}{\sqrt3}$
(D) $1$
(E) $2+\sqrt3$

## Answer

**A.** $0$

## Solution

The recursion is exactly the tangent addition formula, and $a_1 = \tan\frac{\pi}{4},$ $a_2 = \tan\frac{\pi}{6}.$

Writing $a_n = \tan\frac{\pi c_n}{12}$ with $c_1 = 3,$ $c_2 = 2,$ and $c_{n+2} \equiv c_n + c_{n+1} \pmod{12},$ the sequence $c_n$ is $$ \begin{gathered} 3, 2, 5, 7, 0, 7, 7, 2, \\ 9, 11, 8, 7, 3, 10, 1, 11, \\ 0, 11, 11, 10, 9, 7, 4, 11, \ldots \end{gathered} $$ which is periodic with period $24.$

Since $2009 = 24\cdot 83 + 17,$ $c_{2009} = c_{17} = 0,$ so $a_{2009} = \tan 0 = 0$ and $|a_{2009}| = 0.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Trigonometric identity
- Recursion
- Modular arithmetic

## Key idea

Use the trigonometric recurrence and angle addition identities to determine the sequence modulo the period.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
