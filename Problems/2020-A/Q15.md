# AMC12 2020-A Q15

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q15.png]]

## Problem

In the complex plane, let $A$ be the set of solutions to $z^3-8=0$ and let $B$ be the set of solutions to $z^3-8z^2-8z+64=0$. What is the greatest distance between a point of $A$ and a point of $B$?

## Answer

**D.** $2\sqrt{21}$

## Solution

The roots of $z^3-8=0$ are

$$2,\qquad -1+\sqrt3i,\qquad -1-\sqrt3i.$$

Factor the second polynomial by grouping:

$$z^3-8z^2-8z+64=(z-8)(z^2-8).$$

Thus

$$B=\{8,2\sqrt2,-2\sqrt2\}.$$

The largest distance occurs between $8$ and either nonreal root of $A$:

$$\sqrt{(8-(-1))^2+(\sqrt3)^2}=\sqrt{81+3}=2\sqrt{21}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Roots of unity
- Factoring by grouping
- Distance in the complex plane

## Key idea

List both finite root sets explicitly, then compare the distances; the farthest point in $B$ is $8$.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the roots of $z^3-8$ are only real.
- Forgetting that complex-plane distance uses both real and imaginary differences.
