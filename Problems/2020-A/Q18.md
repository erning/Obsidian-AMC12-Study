# AMC12 2020-A Q18

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Quadrilateral $ABCD$ satisfies $\angle ABC=\angle ACD=90^\circ$, $AC=20$, and $CD=30$. Diagonals $AC$ and $BD$ intersect at $E$, and $AE=5$. What is the area of quadrilateral $ABCD$?

## Answer

**D.** $360$

## Solution

Place $A=(0,0)$ and $C=(20,0)$. Since $\angle ACD=90^\circ$ and $CD=30$, take $D=(20,30)$. Since $AE=5$, we have $E=(5,0)$ and line $BD$ passes through $E$ and $D$.

The condition $\angle ABC=90^\circ$ means $B$ lies on the circle with diameter $AC$, whose equation is

$$ (x-10)^2+y^2=100.$$

The line $ED$ has equation $y=2x-10$. Substituting gives two intersections; the one between the relevant diagonal endpoints is $B=(2,-6)$. Therefore

$$[ACD]=\frac12(20)(30)=300,qquad [ABC]=\frac12(20)(6)=60.$$

Thus the quadrilateral area is $300+60=360$.

## Knowledge points

- Coordinate geometry
- Thales' theorem and circle with a diameter
- Triangle area

## Key idea

Turn each right-angle condition into a simple coordinate locus, then split the quadrilateral along diagonal $AC$.

## Common mistakes

- Choosing the other intersection of the line and circle.
- Using $30$ as the height of triangle $ABC$ instead of the absolute $y$-coordinate of $B$.
