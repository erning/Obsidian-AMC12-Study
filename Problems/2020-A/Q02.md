# AMC12 2020-A Q02

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q02.png]]

## Problem

The acronym AMC is shown in the rectangular grid below with grid lines spaced $1$ unit apart. In units, what is the sum of the lengths of the line segments that form the acronym AMC?

## Answer

**C.** $13+4\sqrt2$

## Solution

Separate the drawing into horizontal and vertical segments and diagonal segments. The straight segments have total length $13$.

Each diagonal spans one unit horizontally and one unit vertically, so has length $\sqrt2$. There are four such diagonal segments, giving total diagonal length $4\sqrt2$.

Thus the total length is

$$13+4\sqrt2.$$

## Knowledge points

- Distance formula
- Reading a grid diagram

## Key idea

Count the two types of segments separately; every diagonal is the hypotenuse of a $1$-by-$1$ right triangle.

## Common mistakes

- Counting a diagonal as length $2$ instead of $\sqrt2$.
- Counting the gray grid lines as part of the acronym.
