# AMC12 2020-A Q25

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q25.png]]

## Problem

The number $a=\frac pq$, where $p$ and $q$ are relatively prime positive integers, has the property that the sum of all real numbers $x$ satisfying

$$\lfloor x\rfloor\cdot\{x\}=a\cdot x^2$$

is $420$, where $\lfloor x\rfloor$ is the greatest integer less than or equal to $x$ and $\{x\}=x-\lfloor x\rfloor$ is the fractional part of $x$. What is $p+q$?

## Answer

**C.** $929$

## Solution

For $x\in[n,n+1)$ with integer $n\ge1$, the equation becomes

$$n(x-n)=ax^2,$$

or

$$ax^2-nx+n^2=0.$$

The two roots are

$$x=\frac{n\left(1\pm\sqrt{1-4a}\right)}{2a}.$$

Let the smaller root be $\alpha n$, where

$$\alpha=\frac{1-\sqrt{1-4a}}{2a}.$$

The condition $n\le\alpha n<n+1$ says that $\alpha n$ is the admissible root only for a finite initial range $n=1,\ldots,N$. Since the sum of all roots is $420$,

$$\alpha\frac{N(N+1)}2=420.$$

The endpoint condition for the first excluded interval gives $\alpha=1+\frac1{N+1}$. Combining this with the sum gives

$$420=\frac{N(N+1)}2\left(1+\frac1{N+1}\right)=\frac{N(N+2)}2,$$

so $N=28$. Hence $\alpha=\frac{30}{29}$ and the admissible roots are $\frac{30n}{29}$ for $n=1,\ldots,28$. Thus

$$420=\sum_{n=1}^{28}\frac{30n}{29}=\frac{30}{29}\cdot\frac{28\cdot29}{2}.$$

Since $a\alpha^2-\alpha+1=0$, this corresponds to

$$a=\frac{\alpha-1}{\alpha^2}=\frac{1/29}{(30/29)^2}=\frac{29}{900}.$$

Therefore $p+q=29+900=929$.

## Knowledge points

- Floor and fractional-part functions
- Piecewise quadratic equations
- Rational parameter recovery

## Key idea

Fix $\lfloor x\rfloor=n$ first. Each interval contributes a quadratic root, and the total of those roots determines the parameter $a$.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $\{x\}$ as an independent variable rather than $x-\lfloor x\rfloor$.
- Including roots outside the interval $[n,n+1)$.
