# AMC12 2020-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

The $25$ integers from $-10$ to $14$, inclusive, can be arranged to form a $5$-by-$5$ square in which the sum of the numbers in each row, each column, and each main diagonal are all the same. What is the value of this common sum?

## Answer

**C.** $10$

## Solution

The numbers are consecutive and have average

$$\frac{-10+14}{2}=2.$$

Their total is therefore $25\cdot2=50$. The five rows have equal sums and together contain every number, so the common row sum is

$$\frac{50}{5}=10.$$

The column and diagonal conditions are not needed once the equal row sums are known.

## Knowledge points

- Arithmetic mean of consecutive integers
- Magic-square totals

## Key idea

The total of all five equal row sums determines the common sum immediately.

## Common mistakes

- Using $14-(-10)=24$ as the total.
- Dividing by $25$ rather than by the five rows.
