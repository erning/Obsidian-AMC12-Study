# AMC12 2020-A Q22

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q22.png]]

## Problem

Let $(a_n)$ and $(b_n)$ be real sequences such that

$$ (2+i)^n=a_n+b_ni$$

for all integers $n\ge0$, where $i=\sqrt{-1}$. What is

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{a_nb_n}{7^n}?$$

## Answer

**B.** $\frac7{16}$

## Solution

Squaring the defining equation gives

$$ (2+i)^{2n}=a_n^2-b_n^2+2a_nb_ni.$$

Since $(2+i)^2=3+4i$,

$$a_nb_n=\frac12\operatorname{Im}\left((3+4i)^n\right).$$

Therefore

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{a_nb_n}{7^n}
=\frac12\operatorname{Im}\left(\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\left(\frac{3+4i}{7}\right)^n\right).$$

The geometric series equals

$$\frac1{1-\frac{3+4i}{7}}=\frac7{4-4i}=\frac78+\frac78i.$$

Taking half of its imaginary part gives $\frac12\cdot\frac78=\frac7{16}$.

## Knowledge points

- Complex numbers and real/imaginary parts
- Infinite geometric series

## Key idea

Recover the product $a_nb_n$ as the imaginary part of a squared complex power.

## Common mistakes

- Using $(2+i)^n$ directly, which gives $a_n$ and $b_n$ separately but not their product.
- Forgetting the factor $\frac12$ after squaring.
