# AMC12 2020-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

A point is chosen at random within the square with vertices $(0,0)$, $(2020,0)$, $(2020,2020)$, and $(0,2020)$. The probability that the point is within $d$ units of a lattice point is $\frac12$. What is $d$ to the nearest tenth?

## Answer

**B.** $0.4$

## Solution

The pattern repeats in every unit square. In one unit cell, the portions within distance $d$ of its four corner lattice points are four quarter-disks, which together form a disk of area $\pi d^2$ as long as $d<\frac12$.

The probability is therefore $\pi d^2$, and

$$\pi d^2=\frac12\quad\Longrightarrow\quad d=\frac1{\sqrt{2\pi}}\approx0.399.$$

Rounded to the nearest tenth, $d=0.4$.

## Knowledge points

- Periodicity in lattice problems
- Area probability

## Key idea

Reduce the huge square to one unit cell; the four corner quarters have exactly the area of one full circle.

## Common mistakes

- Counting a full disk at every lattice point without accounting for overlap.
- Forgetting the radius must be below $\frac12$ for the four quarter-disks to be disjoint.
