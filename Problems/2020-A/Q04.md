# AMC12 2020-A Q04

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q04.png]]

## Problem

How many $4$-digit positive integers, that is, integers between $1000$ and $9999$ inclusive, having only even digits are divisible by $5$?

## Answer

**B.** $100$

## Solution

A number divisible by $5$ ends in $0$ or $5$. Since every digit must be even, the units digit is forced to be $0$.

The thousands digit can be $2,4,6,$ or $8$, giving $4$ choices. Each of the two middle digits can be any of $0,2,4,6,8$, giving $5$ choices each. Thus the count is

$$4\cdot5\cdot5\cdot1=100.$$

## Knowledge points

- Divisibility rules
- Multiplication principle

## Key idea

Use the divisibility-by-$5$ rule first; it fixes the units digit and leaves independent choices for the other positions.

## Common mistakes

- Allowing $0$ as the leading digit.
- Counting $5$ as an allowed even units digit.
