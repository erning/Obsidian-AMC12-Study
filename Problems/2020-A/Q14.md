# AMC12 2020-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Regular octagon $ABCDEFGH$ has area $n$. Let $m$ be the area of quadrilateral $ACEG$. What is $\frac mn$?

## Answer

**B.** $\frac{\sqrt2}{2}$

## Solution

Use a circumradius of $1$. The regular octagon has area

$$n=\frac82\sin45^\circ=2\sqrt2.$$

The vertices $A,C,E,G$ are every other vertex, so $ACEG$ is a square. Its diagonal is the diameter of the circumcircle, namely $2$, and therefore

$$m=\frac{2^2}{2}=2.$$

Hence

$$\frac mn=\frac{2}{2\sqrt2}=\frac{\sqrt2}{2}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Regular polygon area
- Cyclic quadrilaterals and diagonals

## Key idea

Choose a convenient circumradius; the ratio is scale-invariant.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $ACEG$ as a regular octagon fragment rather than a square.
- Using the side length of the square as its diagonal.
