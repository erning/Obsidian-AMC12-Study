# AMC12 2020-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

In the plane figure shown below, $3$ of the unit squares have been shaded. What is the least number of additional unit squares that must be shaded so that the resulting figure has two lines of symmetry?

## Answer

**D.** $7$

## Solution

The grid is $4$ rows by $5$ columns. A figure with two perpendicular symmetry lines must use the vertical and horizontal center lines of this grid.

Reflect each shaded square across both center lines. The top shaded square is off both center lines, so its orbit contains $4$ squares and requires $3$ additions. The middle shaded square lies on the central column, so its orbit contains $2$ squares and requires $1$ addition. The bottom-right square is again in an orbit of $4$, requiring $3$ additions.

The required number of new squares is

$$3+1+3=7.$$

## Knowledge points

- Reflection symmetry
- Counting orbits under transformations

## Key idea

Each initially shaded square forces all of its reflected images; count only the missing members of each reflection orbit.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting that a square on the central column is fixed by vertical reflection.
- Counting already shaded squares as additional squares.
