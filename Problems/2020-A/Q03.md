# AMC12 2020-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

A driver travels for $2$ hours at $60$ miles per hour, during which her car gets $30$ miles per gallon of gasoline. She is paid $\$0.50$ per mile, and her only expense is gasoline at $\$2.00$ per gallon. What is her net rate of pay, in dollars per hour, after this expense?

## Answer

**E.** $26$

## Solution

In $2$ hours she drives

$$2\cdot60=120\text{ miles},$$

earning $120\cdot0.50=60$ dollars. She uses

$$\frac{120}{30}=4\text{ gallons},$$

which cost $4\cdot2=8$ dollars. Her net earnings are $60-8=52$ dollars, so her net hourly rate is

$$\frac{52}{2}=26.$$

## Knowledge points

- Rate, distance, and time
- Unit conversion and net earnings

## Key idea

Compute total income and total fuel cost over the same two-hour trip, then divide the difference by time.

## Common mistakes

- Dividing the fuel cost by distance instead of time.
- Forgetting that $30$ miles per gallon determines fuel used, not cost directly.
