# AMC12 2020-A Q13

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q13.png]]

## Problem

There are integers $a$, $b$, and $c$, each greater than $1$, such that for all $N>1$,

$$\sqrt[a]{N\sqrt[b]{N\sqrt[c]{N}}}=\sqrt[36]{N^{25}}.$$

What is $b$?

## Answer

**B.** $3$

## Solution

Rewrite both sides as powers of $N$. The left-hand side has exponent

$$\frac1a+\frac1{ab}+\frac1{abc},$$

while the right-hand side has exponent $\frac{25}{36}$. Thus

$$\frac1a+\frac1{ab}+\frac1{abc}=\frac{25}{36}.$$

If $a\ge3$, then because $b,c>1$,

$$\frac1a+\frac1{ab}+\frac1{abc}\le\frac13+\frac16+\frac1{12}=\frac7{12}<\frac{25}{36}.$$

Therefore $a=2$. The equation becomes

$$\frac12+\frac1{2b}+\frac1{2bc}=\frac{25}{36},$$

so

$$\frac1b+\frac1{bc}=\frac7{18}.$$

If $b=2$, the left side is greater than $\frac12$, so it cannot equal $\frac7{18}$. If $b\ge4$, it is at most $\frac{3}{8}<\frac7{18}$. Hence $b=3$, and then

$$\frac13\left(1+\frac1c\right)=\frac7{18}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad c=6.$$

Thus $b=3$.

## Knowledge points

- Exponents of nested radicals
- Rational equations and integer constraints

## Key idea

Replace every nested radical by its exponent on $N$; equality for all $N>1$ forces equality of those exponents.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the index of a radical as multiplication rather than division of the exponent.
- Forgetting that the innermost exponent is divided by all three indices.
