# AMC12 2020-A Q20

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Let $T$ be the triangle with vertices $(0,0)$, $(4,0)$, and $(0,3)$. Consider the five isometries consisting of rotations by $90^\circ$, $180^\circ$, and $270^\circ$ counterclockwise about the origin, reflection across the $x$-axis, and reflection across the $y$-axis. How many of the $125$ ordered sequences of three transformations return $T$ to its original position?

## Answer

**A.** $12$

## Solution

Because $T$ is a scalene right triangle, the only isometry that maps $T$ to itself is the identity. Thus a sequence works exactly when its three transformations compose to the identity.

The five allowed maps are elements of the symmetry group of the square. For an ordered choice of the first two maps, the third is forced to be the inverse of their product. Among the $25$ ordered pairs, $5$ products are the identity and $8$ products are diagonal reflections, neither of which is an allowed third map. The remaining

$$25-5-8=12$$

pairs produce an allowed third transformation.

## Knowledge points

- Isometries and composition
- Finite symmetry groups
- Counting ordered choices

## Key idea

Count valid first-two choices and determine whether the uniquely forced third map belongs to the allowed list.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the triangle as having the full symmetry group of the square.
- Counting unordered triples instead of ordered sequences.
