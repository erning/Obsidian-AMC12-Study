# AMC12 2020-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

There is a unique positive integer $n$ such that

$$\log_2(\log_{16}n)=\log_4(\log_4n).$$

What is the sum of the digits of $n$?

## Answer

**E.** $13$

## Solution

Let $y=\log_4n$. Then $\log_{16}n=\frac y2$, so the equation becomes

$$\log_2\left(\frac y2\right)=\log_4y=\frac12\log_2y.$$

Multiply by $2$ and exponentiate base $2$:

$$\left(\frac y2\right)^2=y.$$

Since $y>0$, this gives $y=4$. Hence

$$n=4^4=256,$$

whose digit sum is $2+5+6=13$.

## Knowledge points

- Change of base for logarithms
- Solving logarithmic equations

## Key idea

Express both logarithms in terms of $y=\log_4n$ so the equation reduces to an algebraic one.

## Common mistakes

- Using $\log_{16}n=2\log_4n$ instead of $\frac12\log_4n$.
- Keeping the extraneous solution $y=0$, which is outside the logarithm's domain.
