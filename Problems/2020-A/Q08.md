# AMC12 2020-A Q08

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q08.png]]

## Problem

What is the median of the following list of $4040$ numbers?

$$1,2,3,\ldots,2020,1^2,2^2,3^2,\ldots,2020^2$$

## Answer

**C.** $1976.5$

## Solution

The median is the average of the $2020$th and $2021$st entries after sorting. There are $44$ perfect squares at most $2020$, because $44^2=1936$ and $45^2=2025$.

Among the numbers at most $1976$, there are $1976$ ordinary integers and $44$ squares, for a total of $2020$. Thus the $2020$th sorted value is $1976$. The next value is $1977$, since $1977$ is not a perfect square. Therefore

$$\text{median}=\frac{1976+1977}{2}=1976.5.$$

## Knowledge points

- Median of an even-sized list
- Counting duplicate values

## Key idea

Count how many entries are at most a candidate threshold, including the square entries separately from the ordinary integers.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the list as the set $\{1,\ldots,2020^2\}$.
- Forgetting that the squares are additional entries and may duplicate ordinary integers.
