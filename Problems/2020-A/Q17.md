# AMC12 2020-A Q17

![[assets/problems/2020-A/Q17.png]]

## Problem

The vertices of a quadrilateral lie on the graph of $y=\ln x$, and the $x$-coordinates of these vertices are consecutive positive integers. The area of the quadrilateral is $\ln\frac{91}{90}$. What is the $x$-coordinate of the leftmost vertex?

## Answer

**D.** $12$

## Solution

Let the four $x$-coordinates be $n,n+1,n+2,n+3$. Applying the shoelace formula to the points $(x,\ln x)$ gives the area

$$\ln\left(\frac{(n+1)(n+2)}{n(n+3)}\right).$$

Set this equal to $\ln\frac{91}{90}$ and cancel the logarithm:

$$\frac{(n+1)(n+2)}{n(n+3)}=\frac{91}{90}.$$

After cross multiplication,

$$90(n^2+3n+2)=91(n^2+3n),$$

so $n^2+3n=180$. Therefore

$$ (n-12)(n+15)=0.$$

The positive solution is $n=12$.

## Knowledge points

- Shoelace formula
- Logarithm identities
- Consecutive integers

## Key idea

The logarithmic area telescopes into a ratio of products of consecutive $x$-coordinates.

## Common mistakes

- Using the area of the trapezoid under $\ln x$ rather than the polygon area.
- Accepting the negative root $n=-15$.
