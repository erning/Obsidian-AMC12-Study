# AMC12 2022-A Q22

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q22.png]]

## Problem

Let $c$ be real, and let $z_1,z_2$ be the two complex roots of
$$
z^2-cz+10=0.
$$
The points $z_1,z_2,\frac1{z_1},\frac1{z_2}$ form a convex quadrilateral $\mathcal Q$. When the area of $\mathcal Q$ is maximized, $c$ is closest to which value?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $4.5$**

## Solution

For the roots to form the stated nondegenerate quadrilateral, they are nonreal conjugates:
$$
z_2=\overline{z_1}.
$$
By Vieta,
$$
|z_1|^2=z_1z_2=10.
$$
Write
$$
z_1=\sqrt{10}\,e^{i\theta}.
$$
Then
$$
c=z_1+z_2=2\sqrt{10}\cos\theta.
$$
Also
$$
\frac1{z_1}=\frac{\overline{z_1}}{10},
\qquad
\frac1{z_2}=\frac{z_1}{10}.
$$
The quadrilateral is a symmetric trapezoid. Its area is
$$
\frac{99}{20}|\sin2\theta|,
$$
which is maximized when $\theta=45^\circ$. Thus
$$
|c|=2\sqrt{10}\cos45^\circ=2\sqrt5\approx4.47.
$$
The closest listed value is $\boxed{4.5}$.

## Knowledge points

- Complex conjugates
- Vieta's formulas
- Geometric area

## Key idea

Parameterize the conjugate roots on the circle $|z|=\sqrt{10}$.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the maximum occurs when $c$ itself is largest rather than when the quadrilateral area is largest.
