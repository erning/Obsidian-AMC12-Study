# AMC12 2022-A Q04

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q04.png]]

## Problem

The least common multiple of a positive divisor $n$ and $18$ is $180$, and the greatest common divisor of $n$ and $45$ is $15$. What is the sum of the digits of $n$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $6$**

## Solution

Factor the relevant numbers:
$$
180=2^2\cdot3^2\cdot5,\qquad
18=2\cdot3^2,\qquad
45=3^2\cdot5.
$$
The lcm condition requires $n$ to contain a factor $5$ and to have a power of $2$ equal to $2^2$; its power of $3$ can be at most $3^2$. The gcd condition
$$
\gcd(n,45)=3\cdot5
$$
requires exactly one factor of $3$ in $n$ and one factor of $5$. Thus
$$
n=2^2\cdot3\cdot5=60.
$$
The digit sum is
$$
6+0=\boxed{6}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Prime factorization
- Greatest common divisor
- Least common multiple

## Key idea

Translate gcd and lcm conditions into constraints on prime exponents.

## Common mistakes

- Requiring $n$ to contain $3^2$ for the lcm, even though $18$ already supplies it.
