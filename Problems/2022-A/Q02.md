# AMC12 2022-A Q02

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q02.png]]

## Problem

The sum of three numbers is $96$. The first number is $6$ times the third number, and the third number is $40$ less than the second number. What is the absolute value of the difference between the first and second numbers?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $5$**

## Solution

Let the third number be $t$. Then the first number is $6t$ and the second is $t+40$. Their sum is
$$
6t+(t+40)+t=96,
$$
so
$$
8t=56,\qquad t=7.
$$
The first and second numbers are $42$ and $47$, respectively. Their absolute difference is
$$
\boxed{5}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Linear equations
- Translation of relationships

## Key idea

Express all three numbers in terms of the third number.

## Common mistakes

- Interpreting “$40$ less than the second number” as the second number being $40$ less than the third.
