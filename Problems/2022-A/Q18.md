# AMC12 2022-A Q18

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Let $T_k$ be the transformation that first rotates the coordinate plane $k$ degrees counterclockwise about the origin and then reflects it across the $y$-axis. What is the least positive integer $n$ such that applying
$$
T_1,T_2,T_3,\ldots,T_n
$$
in sequence returns $(1,0)$ to itself?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $359$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q18-draft.svg]]

Represent a point by its polar angle $\theta$. Rotation by $k^\circ$ followed by reflection across the $y$-axis sends
$$
\theta\longmapsto180^\circ-(\theta+k^\circ).
$$
Starting from angle $0^\circ$, the first few resulting angles are
$$
179^\circ,\ -1^\circ,\ 178^\circ,\ -2^\circ,\ldots.
$$
After $2m$ transformations the angle is $-m^\circ$. After $2m+1$ transformations it is
$$
(179-m)^\circ.
$$
For an odd number of transformations to return to angle $0^\circ$, take $m=179$, giving
$$
n=2(179)+1=\boxed{359}.
$$
The even possibility requires $m=360$, which gives $n=720$ and is larger.

## Knowledge points

- Composition of transformations
- Rotations and reflections
- Modular angles

## Key idea

Track the angle after each transformation and separate even and odd steps.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the reflection across the $y$-axis as a sign change of the angle without the $180^\circ$ shift.
