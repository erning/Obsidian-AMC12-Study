# AMC12 2022-A Q11

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q11.png]]

## Problem

What is the product of all real numbers $x$ such that the distance on the number line between $\log_6x$ and $\log_6 9$ is twice the distance on the number line between $\log_6 10$ and $1$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $81$**

## Solution

Because $\log_6 10>1$,
$$
\left|\log_6 10-1\right|
=\log_6\left(\frac{10}{6}\right)
=\log_6\left(\frac53\right).
$$
The equation is therefore
$$
\left|\log_6x-\log_6 9\right|
=2\log_6\left(\frac53\right)
=\log_6\left(\frac{25}{9}\right).
$$
Thus
$$
\log_6x=\log_6 9\pm\log_6\left(\frac{25}{9}\right),
$$
giving
$$
x=25\quad\text{or}\quad x=\frac{81}{25}.
$$
Their product is
$$
\boxed{81}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Logarithms
- Absolute value
- Distance on a number line

## Key idea

Convert distances between logarithms into logarithms of ratios.

## Common mistakes

- Reading the second point as $\log_6 1$ instead of the number $1$.
