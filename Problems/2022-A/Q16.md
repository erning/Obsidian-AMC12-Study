# AMC12 2022-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

A triangular number is a positive integer
$$
t_n=1+2+\cdots+n.
$$
The three smallest triangular numbers that are also perfect squares are
$$
t_1=1=1^2,\qquad t_8=36=6^2,\qquad t_{49}=1225=35^2.
$$
What is the sum of the digits of the fourth smallest triangular number that is also a perfect square?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $18$**

## Solution

Square triangular numbers arise from the Pell equation
$$
(2n+1)^2-8m^2=1.
$$
The corresponding triangular values satisfy the recurrence
$$
N_{k+1}=34N_k-N_{k-1}+2.
$$
Using the first three values $N_1=1$, $N_2=36$, and $N_3=1225$,
$$
N_4=34\cdot1225-36+2=41616.
$$
The digit sum is
$$
4+1+6+1+6=\boxed{18}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Triangular numbers
- Pell equations
- Recurrences

## Key idea

Use the recurrence for square triangular numbers instead of searching one index at a time.

## Common mistakes

- Confusing the triangular-number index $n$ with the triangular number $t_n$.
