# AMC12 2022-A Q12

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q12.png]]

## Problem

Let $M$ be the midpoint of $\overline{AB}$ in regular tetrahedron $ABCD$. What is $\cos(\angle CMD)$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $\frac13$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q12-draft.svg]]

Take the tetrahedron's edge length to be $1$. Since $M$ is the midpoint of $AB$, the segments $CM$ and $DM$ are medians, and hence altitudes, of equilateral triangles $CAB$ and $DAB$. Therefore
$$
CM=DM=\frac{\sqrt3}{2}.
$$
Also $CD=1$. Apply the Law of Cosines in triangle $CMD$:
$$
\cos(\angle CMD)
=\frac{CM^2+DM^2-CD^2}{2(CM)(DM)}
=\frac{\frac34+\frac34-1}{2\cdot\frac34}
=\boxed{\frac13}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Regular tetrahedra
- Equilateral triangles
- Law of Cosines

## Key idea

Reduce the spatial problem to triangle $CMD$ using the medians of two equilateral faces.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the angle at the midpoint is $60^\circ$ because the faces are equilateral.
