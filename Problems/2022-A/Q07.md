# AMC12 2022-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

A rectangle is partitioned into five regions as shown. Each region is painted one of five colors, with regions that share a boundary required to have different colors. How many different colorings are possible?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $540$**

## Solution

Call the bottom-middle region the central region. It shares a boundary with all four other regions, so it can be colored in
$$
5
$$
ways. After coloring it, the top-left region has $4$ choices.

Each of the remaining three regions touches the central region and one already-colored neighboring region. Those two neighboring regions have different colors, so each remaining region has
$$
5-2=3
$$
choices. Therefore the total number of colorings is
$$
5\cdot4\cdot3^3=\boxed{540}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Graph coloring
- Multiplication principle
- Case-free counting

## Key idea

Choose the region with the most neighbors first.

## Common mistakes

- Treating regions that only meet at a point as sharing a boundary.
