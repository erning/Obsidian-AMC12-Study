# AMC12 2022-A Q25

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q25.png]]

## Problem

A circle with integer radius $r$ is centered at $(r,r)$. Distinct line segments of length $c_i$ connect $(0,a_i)$ to $(b_i,0)$ for $1\le i\le14$ and are tangent to the circle, where $a_i,b_i,c_i$ are positive integers and
$$
c_1\le c_2\le\cdots\le c_{14}.
$$
What is $\frac{c_{14}}{c_1}$ for the least possible value of $r$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $17$**

## Solution

The segment from $(0,a)$ to $(b,0)$ is the hypotenuse of a right triangle with legs $a,b$ and hypotenuse $c$. The line through the segment is
$$
ax+by=ab.
$$
Its distance from the circle center $(r,r)$ must equal $r$:
$$
\frac{|r(a+b)-ab|}{c}=r.
$$
Together with $a^2+b^2=c^2$, this condition can be checked over integer right triangles.

For $r<6$, fewer than $14$ distinct ordered segments are possible. At $r=6$, the solutions give the hypotenuse lengths
$$
5,5,29,29,30,30,34,34,39,39,50,50,85,85.
$$
For example, the first pair comes from the $3$-$4$-$5$ triangle, while the largest pair comes from the $13$-$84$-$85$ triangle. Hence
$$
c_1=5,\qquad c_{14}=85,
$$
and
$$
\frac{c_{14}}{c_1}=\frac{85}{5}=\boxed{17}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Tangent lines
- Right triangles
- Integer solutions

## Key idea

Convert tangency into a distance equation and enumerate integer right-triangle solutions.

## Common mistakes

- Counting a segment and its reversed endpoint order as the same segment; the ordered endpoint pairs are distinct here.
