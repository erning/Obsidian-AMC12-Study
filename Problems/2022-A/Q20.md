# AMC12 2022-A Q20

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Isosceles trapezoid $ABCD$ has parallel sides $\overline{AD}$ and $\overline{BC}$, with $BC<AD$, and $AB=CD$. There is a point $P$ such that
$$
PA=1,\quad PB=2,\quad PC=3,\quad PD=4.
$$
What is $\frac{BC}{AD}$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $\frac13$**

## Solution

Place the trapezoid symmetrically:
$$
A=(-p,0),\quad D=(p,0),\quad
B=(-q,h),\quad C=(q,h),
$$
where $q<p$. Let $P=(x,y)$. From the given distances,
$$
PA^2-PD^2=1^2-4^2=-15.
$$
Using the coordinates, the left side equals $4px$, so
$$
px=-\frac{15}{4}.
$$
Similarly,
$$
PB^2-PC^2=2^2-3^2=-5
$$
gives
$$
qx=-\frac54.
$$
Therefore
$$
\frac qp=\frac{qx}{px}=\frac13.
$$
Since $AD=2p$ and $BC=2q$,
$$
\frac{BC}{AD}=\frac qp=\boxed{\frac13}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Coordinate geometry
- Isosceles trapezoids
- Difference of squared distances

## Key idea

Subtract squared-distance equations to eliminate the unknown height and point coordinates.

## Common mistakes

- Assigning arbitrary coordinates that do not enforce the trapezoid's symmetry.
