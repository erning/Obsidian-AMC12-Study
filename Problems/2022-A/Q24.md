# AMC12 2022-A Q24

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q24.png]]

## Problem

How many strings of length $5$ formed from the digits $0,1,2,3,4$ have the property that for each $j\in\{1,2,3,4\}$, at least $j$ of the digits are less than $j$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $1296$**

## Solution

Sort the five digits of a string:
$$
d_{(1)}\le d_{(2)}\le d_{(3)}\le d_{(4)}\le d_{(5)}.
$$
The condition is equivalent to
$$
d_{(1)}\le0,\quad
d_{(2)}\le1,\quad
d_{(3)}\le2,\quad
d_{(4)}\le3,\quad
d_{(5)}\le4.
$$
This is exactly the definition of a parking function of length $5$. The circular parking-function argument gives $(n+1)^{n-1}$ such strings of length $n$. For $n=5$,
$$
(5+1)^{5-1}=6^4=\boxed{1296}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Combinatorics
- Sorting and inequalities
- Parking functions

## Key idea

Sort the digits to convert the “at least $j$ digits less than $j$” condition into order-statistic bounds.

## Common mistakes

- Counting only multisets and forgetting that the strings are ordered.
