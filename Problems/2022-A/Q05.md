# AMC12 2022-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

The taxicab distance between $(x_1,y_1)$ and $(x_2,y_2)$ is
$$
|x_1-x_2|+|y_1-y_2|.
$$
For how many integer-coordinate points $P$ is the taxicab distance between $P$ and the origin at most $20$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $841$**

## Solution

For each $k\ge1$, the points satisfying
$$
|x|+|y|=k
$$
form a diamond boundary with $4k$ lattice points. The origin contributes one point for $k=0$. Thus the total is
$$
1+\sum_{k=1}^{20}4k
=1+4\cdot\frac{20\cdot21}{2}
=\boxed{841}.
$$

## Key idea

Count lattice points by their exact taxicab distance from the origin.

## Common mistakes

- Counting Euclidean circles instead of taxicab-distance diamonds.
