# AMC12 2022-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

How many ways are there to split the integers $1$ through $14$ into $7$ pairs such that in each pair, the greater number is at least twice the lesser number?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $144$**

## Solution

Any number from $8$ through $14$ must be the greater member of its pair, because twice any number at least $8$ exceeds $14$. Therefore the smaller members are $1,2,\ldots,7$ and the larger members are $8,9,\ldots,14$.

Match the smaller members to the larger members from the most restrictive condition:

- $7$ can only pair with $14$: $1$ choice.
- After that, $6$ has $12$ or $13$ available: $2$ choices.
- The remaining choices for $5,4,3,2,1$ are respectively $3,4,3,2,1$.

Thus the number of matchings is
$$
1\cdot2\cdot3\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1
=\boxed{144}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Matching
- Greedy casework
- Multiplication principle

## Key idea

Separate the forced smaller and larger elements, then match the most restricted smaller element first.

## Common mistakes

- Counting ordered pairs instead of pairings.
