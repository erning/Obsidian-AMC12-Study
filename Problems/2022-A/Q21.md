# AMC12 2022-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Let
$$
P(x)=x^{2022}+x^{1011}+1.
$$
Which of the following polynomials is a factor of $P(x)$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $x^6+x^3+1$**

## Solution

Let $\zeta$ be a root of
$$
x^6+x^3+1.
$$
Then $\zeta^9=1$ and $\zeta^3$ is a primitive cube root of unity. Reduce the exponents modulo $9$:
$$
2022\equiv6\pmod9,\qquad 1011\equiv3\pmod9.
$$
Therefore
$$
P(\zeta)=\zeta^6+\zeta^3+1
=\left(\zeta^3\right)^2+\zeta^3+1
=0.
$$
Every root of $x^6+x^3+1$ is a root of $P$, so it is a factor:
$$
\boxed{x^6+x^3+1}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Roots of unity
- Polynomial factors
- Modular exponents

## Key idea

Test a candidate factor through the behavior of its complex roots.

## Common mistakes

- Reducing the exponents modulo $6$ instead of modulo $9$.
