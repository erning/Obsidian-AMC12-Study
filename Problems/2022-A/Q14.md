# AMC12 2022-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

What is the value of
$$
(\log 5)^3+(\log 20)^3+(\log 8)(\log 0.25),
$$
where $\log$ denotes the base-ten logarithm?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $2$**

## Solution

Let
$$
u=\log2.
$$
Then
$$
\log5=1-u,\quad
\log20=1+u,\quad
\log8=3u,\quad
\log0.25=-2u.
$$
Using $a^3+b^3=(a+b)^3-3ab(a+b)$ with $a=1-u$ and $b=1+u$,
$$
(\log5)^3+(\log20)^3
=2\left(4-3(1-u^2)\right)
=2+6u^2.
$$
The remaining product is
$$
(\log8)(\log0.25)=(3u)(-2u)=-6u^2.
$$
The total is $\boxed{2}$.

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm identities
- Cubic identities
- Algebraic simplification

## Key idea

Express all logarithms in terms of $\log2$ so the $u^2$ terms cancel.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $\log0.25$ as positive instead of $-2\log2$.
