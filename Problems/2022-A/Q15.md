# AMC12 2022-A Q15

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q15.png]]

## Problem

The roots of
$$
10x^3-39x^2+29x-6
$$
are the height, length, and width of a rectangular box. A new box is formed by lengthening each edge of the original box by $2$ units. What is the volume of the new box?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $30$**

## Solution

Let the roots be $r,s,t$. Vieta's formulas give
$$
r+s+t=\frac{39}{10},\qquad
rs+rt+st=\frac{29}{10},\qquad
rst=\frac{6}{10}=\frac35.
$$
The new volume is
$$
(r+2)(s+2)(t+2)
=rst+2(rs+rt+st)+4(r+s+t)+8.
$$
Substituting,
$$
\frac35+2\cdot\frac{29}{10}
+4\cdot\frac{39}{10}+8
=\boxed{30}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Vieta's formulas
- Symmetric expressions
- Polynomial roots

## Key idea

Expand the new volume into the three symmetric sums given by Vieta.

## Common mistakes

- Solving the cubic roots individually when only symmetric sums are needed.
