# AMC12 2022-A Q19

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q19.png]]

## Problem

Suppose $13$ cards numbered $1,2,\ldots,13$ are arranged in a row. The task is to pick them up in increasing order, working repeatedly from left to right. For how many of the $13!$ orderings will all cards be picked up in exactly two passes?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $8178$**

## Solution

Let $\operatorname{pos}(k)$ be the position of card $k$. A new pass is needed before card $k+1$ exactly when
$$
\operatorname{pos}(k+1)<\operatorname{pos}(k).
$$
Thus the number of passes is one plus the number of descents in the sequence
$$
\operatorname{pos}(1),\operatorname{pos}(2),\ldots,\operatorname{pos}(13).
$$
Exactly two passes means exactly one descent. The Eulerian number counting permutations of $13$ elements with exactly one descent is
$$
\left\langle{13\atop1}\right\rangle
=2^{13}-13-1
=\boxed{8178}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Permutations
- Descents
- Eulerian numbers

## Key idea

Translate passes into descents in the positions of consecutive cards.

## Common mistakes

- Counting descents in the original row rather than in the position sequence of card labels.
