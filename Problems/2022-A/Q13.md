# AMC12 2022-A Q13

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Let $\mathcal R$ be the region in the complex plane consisting of all complex numbers $z$ that can be written as $z_1+z_2$, where $z_1$ lies on the segment with endpoints $3$ and $4i$, and $z_2$ has magnitude at most $1$. What integer is closest to the area of $\mathcal R$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $13$**

## Solution

Adding all complex numbers $z_2$ with $|z_2|\le1$ sweeps a disk of radius $1$ around every point of the segment from $3$ to $4i$. Thus $\mathcal R$ is a stadium: a rectangle of width $2$ and length equal to the segment length, together with two semicircles of radius $1$.

The segment length is
$$
|3-4i|=\sqrt{3^2+4^2}=5.
$$
Therefore
$$
\operatorname{Area}(\mathcal R)
=5\cdot2+\pi(1)^2
=10+\pi
\approx13.14.
$$
The closest integer is $\boxed{13}$.

## Knowledge points

- Complex-plane geometry
- Minkowski sums
- Areas of stadiums

## Key idea

Interpret adding a radius-$1$ disk as sweeping that disk along a line segment.

## Common mistakes

- Using the distance between $3$ and $4i$ as an area dimension without adding the semicircles.
