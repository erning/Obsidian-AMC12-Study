# AMC12 2022-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

Five rectangles $A,B,C,D,E$ are arranged in a square as shown. Their dimensions are $1\times6$, $2\times4$, $5\times6$, $2\times7$, and $2\times3$, respectively. Which rectangle is the shaded one in the middle?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $B$**

## Solution

The total area of the five rectangles is
$$
6+8+30+14+6=64,
$$
so the surrounding square has side length $8$.

Use the arrangement in the diagram to place the four outer rectangles around the square. The dimensions of those four rectangles determine the central gap: its perimeter is $12$. Among the five given rectangles, only rectangle $B$, with dimensions $2\times4$, has perimeter
$$
2(2+4)=12.
$$
Therefore the shaded rectangle is $\boxed{B}$.

## Knowledge points

- Rectangle areas and perimeters
- Geometric decomposition
- Diagram-based deduction

## Key idea

Find the dimensions of the central gap from the outer square and compare perimeters.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the figure is drawn to scale instead of using the stated dimensions.
