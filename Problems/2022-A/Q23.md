# AMC12 2022-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Let $h_n$ and $k_n$ be the unique relatively prime positive integers such that
$$
1+\frac12+\frac13+\cdots+\frac1n=\frac{h_n}{k_n}.
$$
Let $L_n$ be the least common multiple of $1,2,\ldots,n$. For how many integers $n$ with $1\le n\le22$ is $k_n<L_n$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $8$**

## Solution

Always $k_n$ divides $L_n$. The strict inequality $k_n<L_n$ occurs exactly when some prime factor of $L_n$ cancels while reducing the harmonic sum.

Write
$$
N_n=\sum_{j=1}^n\frac{L_n}{j},
$$
so that
$$
\sum_{j=1}^n\frac1j=\frac{N_n}{L_n}.
$$
For a prime $p$, let $p^a$ be the largest power of $p$ at most $n$. Modulo $p$, only terms with $v_p(j)=a$ can contribute to $N_n$, because all other terms contain a factor of $p$. Checking these prime-cancellation conditions for $1\le n\le22$ gives exactly
$$
n=6,7,8,18,19,20,21,22.
$$
There are $\boxed{8}$ such values.

## Knowledge points

- Harmonic numbers
- Least common multiples
- Prime valuations

## Key idea

Detect denominator cancellation by examining the numerator modulo primes in $L_n$.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the reduced denominator is always $L_n$.
