# AMC12 2022-A Q08

![[assets/problems/2022-A/Q08.png]]

## Problem

The infinite product
$$
\sqrt[3]{10}\cdot\sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{10}}\cdot
\sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{\sqrt[3]{10}}}\cdots
$$
evaluates to a real number. What is that number?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $\sqrt{10}$**

## Solution

The $k$th factor is
$$
10^{1/3^k}.
$$
Therefore the product is
$$
10^{\frac13+\frac1{3^2}+\frac1{3^3}+\cdots}.
$$
The exponent is a geometric series:
$$
\frac13+\frac19+\frac1{27}+\cdots
=\frac{1/3}{1-1/3}
=\frac12.
$$
Thus the product equals
$$
\boxed{\sqrt{10}}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Infinite geometric series
- Exponent rules
- Infinite products

## Key idea

Rewrite every nested radical as a power of $10$.

## Common mistakes

- Treating every factor as $\sqrt[3]{10}$ instead of noticing the increasing nesting.
