# AMC12 2011-B Q05

![[assets/problems/2011-B/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Let $N$ be the second smallest positive integer that is divisible by every positive integer less than $7.$ What is the sum of the digits of $N?$

(A) $3$
(B) $4$
(C) $5$
(D) $6$
(E) $9$

## Answer

**A.** $3$

## Solution

A number divisible by every integer from $1$ to $6$ must be a multiple of $\operatorname{lcm}(1,2,3,4,5,6)=60.$

The second smallest positive multiple of $60$ is $120,$ whose digit sum is $1+2+0=3.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Least common multiple
- Digits

## Key idea

The second-smallest multiple of the least common multiple must be tested by its prime factors, then its digit sum is immediate.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
