# AMC12 2011-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2011-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

A segment through the focus $F$ of a parabola with vertex $V$ is perpendicular to $\overline{FV}$ and intersects the parabola in points $A$ and $B.$ What is $\cos(\angle AVB)?$

(A) $-\frac{3\sqrt{5}}{7}$
(B) $-\frac{2\sqrt{5}}{5}$
(C) $-\frac{4}{5}$
(D) $-\frac{3}{5}$
(E) $-\frac{1}{2}$

## Answer

**D.** $-\frac{3}{5}$

## Solution

Let $p=FV$ and let the directrix be $\ell.$ Projecting $F$ and $B$ onto $\ell,$ the focus-directrix property gives $FB=2p$ (the distance from $B$ to $\ell$), and by the Pythagorean Theorem $$ \begin{aligned} VB&=\sqrt{FV^2+FB^2} \\ &=\sqrt{p^2+4p^2}=\sqrt5\,p. \end{aligned} $$

Then $\cos(\angle FVB)=\frac{FV}{VB}$ $=\frac{p}{\sqrt5\,p}$ $=\frac{1}{\sqrt5}.$ Since $\angle AVB=2\angle FVB,$ $$ \begin{aligned} \cos(\angle AVB) &=2\cos^2(\angle FVB) \\ &\quad {}-1 \\ &=2\cdot\frac15-1 \\ &=-\frac35. \end{aligned} $$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Parabola
- Trigonometric identity
- Pythagorean theorem

## Key idea

Place the parabola in standard coordinates and use the focus/directrix relation to determine the angle between the two intersection rays.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
