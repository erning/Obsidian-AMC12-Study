# AMC12 2011-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2011-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Let $x$ and $y$ be two-digit positive integers with mean $60.$ What is the maximum value of the ratio $\frac{x}{y}?$

(A) $3$
(B) $\frac{33}{7}$
(C) $\frac{39}{7}$
(D) $9$
(E) $\frac{99}{10}$

## Answer

**B.** $\frac{33}{7}$

## Solution

Since $\frac{x+y}{2}=60,$ we have $x+y=120.$ To maximize $\frac{x}{y}$ we make $y$ small.

Because $x\le99,$ it follows that $y=120-x\ge21.$ Taking $x=99$ and $y=21$ gives the maximum $$ \frac{99}{21}=\frac{33}{7}. $$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Optimization
- Bounding to limit cases

## Key idea

For a fixed sum, make $y$ as small as the two-digit condition permits; then $x=120-y$ is as large as possible.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
