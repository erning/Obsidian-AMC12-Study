# AMC12 2011-B Q17

![[assets/problems/2011-B/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Let $f(x)=10^{10x},$ $g(x)=\log_{10}\!\left(\frac{x}{10}\right),$ $h_1(x)=g(f(x)),$ and $h_n(x)=h_1(h_{n-1}(x))$ for integers $n\ge2.$ What is the sum of the digits of $h_{2011}(1)?$

(A) $16,081$
(B) $16,089$
(C) $18,089$
(D) $18,098$
(E) $18,099$

## Answer

**B.** $16,089$

## Solution

First, $$ \begin{aligned} h_1(x) &=\log_{10}\!\left(\frac{10^{10x}}{10}\right) \\ &=\log_{10}\!\left(10^{10x-1}\right) \\ &=10x-1. \end{aligned} $$

Iterating, $h_n(x)=10^n x$ $-(1+10+\cdots+10^{n-1}).$ Therefore $h_n(1)$ is an $n$-digit integer whose units digit is $9$ and all of whose other digits are $8.$

For $n=2011,$ the digit sum is $$ 8\cdot2010+9=16{,}089. $$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm
- Recursion
- Digits

## Key idea

Apply the logarithm recursion to the decimal exponent, turning the nested functions into a recurrence on the digits.
