# AMC12 2011-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2011-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

A dart board is a regular octagon divided into regions as shown. Suppose that a dart thrown at the board is equally likely to land anywhere on the board. What is the probability that the dart lands within the center square?

(A) $\frac{\sqrt{2}-1}{2}$
(B) $\frac{1}{4}$
(C) $\frac{2-\sqrt{2}}{2}$
(D) $\frac{\sqrt{2}}{4}$
(E) $2-\sqrt{2}$

## Answer

**A.** $\frac{\sqrt{2}-1}{2}$

## Solution

Assume the octagon has edge length $1.$ The four corner triangles are right isosceles with legs $\frac{\sqrt2}{2}$ and area $\frac14$ each. The four rectangles are $1$ by $\frac{\sqrt2}{2}$ with area $\frac{\sqrt2}{2}$ each, and the center square has area $1.$

The total area is $$ 4\cdot\frac14+4\cdot\frac{\sqrt2}{2}+1=2+2\sqrt2. $$ The probability of hitting the center square is $$ \frac{1}{2+2\sqrt2}=\frac{\sqrt2-1}{2}. $$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Geometric probability
- Area decomposition
- Regular polygon

## Key idea

Compare the area of the central square with the area of the regular octagon by subtracting the four congruent right-triangle corners.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
