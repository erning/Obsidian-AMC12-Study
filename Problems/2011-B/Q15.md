# AMC12 2011-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2011-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

How many positive two-digit integers are factors of $2^{24}-1?$

(A) $4$
(B) $8$
(C) $10$
(D) $12$
(E) $14$

## Answer

**D.** $12$

## Solution

Factoring, $$ \begin{aligned} 2^{24}-1 &=(2^{12}-1)(2^{12}+1) \\ &=(2^6-1)(2^6+1) \\ &\quad {}\cdot(2^4+1)(2^8-2^4+1), \end{aligned} $$ which equals $63\cdot65\cdot17\cdot241$ $=3^2\cdot5\cdot7\cdot13\cdot17\cdot241.$

Since $241$ is a three-digit prime, the two-digit factors come from $3^2\cdot5\cdot7\cdot13\cdot17.$ They are $$ \begin{gathered} 13,15,17,21,35,39,45,51, \\ 63,65,85,91, \end{gathered} $$ for a total of $12.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Difference of squares
- Prime factorization
- Factor

## Key idea

Factor $2^{24}-1$ using difference of squares and factor the resulting Fermat-type terms, then count the two-digit divisors.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
