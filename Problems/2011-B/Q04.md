# AMC12 2011-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2011-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

In multiplying two positive integers $a$ and $b,$ Ron reversed the digits of the two-digit number $a.$ His erroneous product was $161.$ What is the correct value of the product of $a$ and $b?$

(A) $116$
(B) $161$
(C) $204$
(D) $214$
(E) $224$

## Answer

**E.** $224$

## Solution

Since $161=7\cdot23,$ the only two-digit factor is $23.$ This must be the reversed value of $a,$ so the true value of $a$ is $32,$ and $b=7.$

The correct product is $$ 32\cdot7=224. $$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Prime factorization
- Digits

## Key idea

Reverse the digit operation algebraically and impose the fact that both original numbers are positive integers.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
