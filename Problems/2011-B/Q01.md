# AMC12 2011-B Q01

![[assets/problems/2011-B/Q01.png]]

## Problem

What is

$$\frac{2+4+6}{1+3+5} - \frac{1+3+5}{2+4+6}?$$

(A) $-1$
(B) $\frac{5}{36}$
(C) $\frac{7}{12}$
(D) $\frac{147}{60}$
(E) $\frac{43}{3}$

## Answer

**C.** $\frac{7}{12}$

## Solution

The sums are $2+4+6=12$ and $1+3+5=9,$ so the expression equals $\frac{12}{9}-\frac{9}{12}=\frac{4}{3}-\frac{3}{4}.$

Over a common denominator this is $$ \frac{16}{12}-\frac{9}{12}=\frac{7}{12}. $$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Fraction

## Key idea

Reduce each even-number sum and odd-number sum to a common value before taking their difference.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
