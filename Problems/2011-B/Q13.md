# AMC12 2011-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2011-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Brian writes down four integers $w \gt x \gt y \gt z$ whose sum is $44.$ The pairwise positive differences of these numbers are $1, 3, 4, 5, 6,$ and $9.$ What is the sum of the possible values for $w?$

(A) $16$
(B) $31$
(C) $48$
(D) $62$
(E) $93$

## Answer

**B.** $31$

## Solution

The largest difference is $w-z=9.$ Writing $9=(w-x)+(x-z)$ style splits, the interior differences pair as $3+6$ and $4+5,$ which forces the smallest difference $x-y=1.$

The second largest difference $6$ is either $w-y$ or $x-z.$ If $w-y=6,$ the numbers are $\{w,w-5,w-6,w-9\},$ so $4w-20=44$ and $w=16.$ If $x-z=6,$ the numbers are $\{w,w-3,w-4,w-9\},$ so $4w-16=44$ and $w=15.$

The possible values are $16$ and $15,$ which sum to $31.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- System of equations
- Casework

## Key idea

The largest difference is $w - z = 9$ and the second largest is $w - y$ or $x - z;$ split into these two cases and use the sum $44$ to solve for $w.$

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
