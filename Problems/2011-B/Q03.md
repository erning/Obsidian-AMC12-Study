# AMC12 2011-B Q03

![[assets/problems/2011-B/Q03.png]]

## Problem

LeRoy and Bernardo went on a week-long trip together and agreed to share the costs equally. Over the week, each of them paid for various joint expenses such as gasoline and car rental. At the end of the trip it turned out that LeRoy had paid $A$ dollars and Bernardo had paid $B$ dollars, where $A \lt B.$ How many dollars must LeRoy give to Bernardo so that they share the costs equally?

(A) $\frac{A+B}{2}$
(B) $\frac{A-B}{2}$
(C) $\frac{B-A}{2}$
(D) $B-A$
(E) $A+B$

## Answer

**C.** $\frac{B-A}{2}$

## Solution

The total cost is $A+B,$ so each person's fair share is $\frac{A+B}{2}.$

LeRoy paid $A,$ which is less than his share, so he must give Bernardo $$ \frac{A+B}{2}-A=\frac{B-A}{2}. $$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Algebraic manipulation

## Key idea

Write the total cost of each person’s share and use the equal-share condition to solve for the requested combination of $A$ and $B$.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
