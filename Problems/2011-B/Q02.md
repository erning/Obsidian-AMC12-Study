# AMC12 2011-B Q02

![[assets/problems/2011-B/Q02.png]]

## Problem

Josanna's test scores to date are $90,$ $80,$ $70,$ $60,$ and $85.$ Her goal is to raise her test average at least $3$ points with her next test. What is the minimum test score she would need to accomplish this goal?

(A) $80$
(B) $82$
(C) $85$
(D) $90$
(E) $95$

## Answer

**E.** $95$

## Solution

The five scores sum to $90+80+70+60+85=385,$ giving an average of $77.$ The goal is a new average of at least $80.$

Six tests averaging $80$ must total $6\cdot80=480,$ so the sixth score must be at least $480-385=95.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Mean

## Key idea

Use the mean to determine the total score required, then choose the smallest possible number of tests consistent with the maximum score on each remaining test.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
