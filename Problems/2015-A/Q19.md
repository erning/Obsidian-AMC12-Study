# AMC12 2015-A Q19

![[assets/problems/2015-A/Q19.png]]

## Problem

A right-angle quadrilateral has integer side lengths, perimeter $p$, $AB=2$, and $CD=AD$. How many possible $p<2015$?

## Answer

**B.** $31$

## Solution

Let the common altitude be $x=2z$ and the remaining horizontal segment be $y$. Pythagoras gives $y=z^2-1$. The perimeter becomes
$$p=2z^2+2z+4.$$
For $z=1,\ldots,31$, $p<2015$; $z=32$ gives $2116$. Hence there are $31$ values.

## Knowledge points

- Pythagorean theorem
- Parametrization

## Key idea

Use the right angles to express all side lengths using one integer parameter.

## Common mistakes

- Counting $z=0$
- Checking only the largest perimeter
