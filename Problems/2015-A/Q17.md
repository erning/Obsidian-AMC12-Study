# AMC12 2015-A Q17

![[assets/problems/2015-A/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Eight people flip fair coins and stand on heads. What is the probability no two adjacent people stand?

## Answer

**A.** $\frac{47}{256}$

## Solution

There are $2^8=256$ outcomes. The numbers of ways to choose $k$ nonadjacent seats around a circle of $8$ seats for $k=0,1,2,3,4$ are $1,8,20,16,2$. Their sum is $47$, so the probability is $47/256$.

## Knowledge points

- Circular combinatorics
- Independent coin flips

## Key idea

Count head patterns with no adjacent positions on a cycle.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the seats as a line
- Forgetting the wraparound adjacency
