# AMC12 2015-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2015-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Two years ago Pete was three times Claire's age; two years before that he was four times her age. When will their age ratio be $2:1$?

## Answer

**B.** $4$ years

## Solution

Let current ages be $p,c$. Then
$$p-2=3(c-2),\qquad p-4=4(c-4).$$
Solving gives $p=20,c=8$. Their difference is $12$, so the ratio becomes $2:1$ when Claire is $12$, four years from now.

## Knowledge points

- Age equations
- Constant age difference

## Key idea

Use the two historical equations to determine present ages.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the ratio as a constant
- Using the same time shift in both equations
