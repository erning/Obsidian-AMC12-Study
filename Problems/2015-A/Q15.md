# AMC12 2015-A Q15

![[assets/problems/2015-A/Q15.png]]

## Problem

What is the minimum number of decimal places needed for $\frac{123456789}{2^{26}\cdot5^4}$?

## Answer

**C.** $26$

## Solution

Multiply numerator and denominator by $5^{22}$:
$$\frac{123456789}{2^{26}5^4}=\frac{123456789\cdot5^{22}}{10^{26}}.$$
The numerator is not divisible by $10$, so exactly $26$ digits are required.

## Knowledge points

- Terminating decimals
- Prime factors of denominators

## Key idea

Make the denominator a power of $10$ with the smallest exponent.

## Common mistakes

- Using $10^{30}$ without minimizing
- Assuming the numerator cancels a factor of $10$
