# AMC12 2015-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2015-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Two right circular cylinders have equal volume. The second radius is $10\%$ larger. Compare their heights.

## Answer

**D.** The first height is $21\%$ more than the second.

## Solution

Let $R=1.1r$. Equal volumes give
$$\pi r^2h=\pi(1.1r)^2H=1.21\pi r^2H.$$
Thus $h=1.21H$: the first height is $21\%$ more than the second.

## Knowledge points

- Cylinder volume
- Percent scaling

## Key idea

Volume scales with the square of radius, so height scales inversely.

## Common mistakes

- Scaling height by $10\%$
- Forgetting to square the radius ratio
