# AMC12 2015-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2015-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

A circle passes through both foci and exactly four points of $x^2/16+y^2=1$. Possible radii form $[a,b]$. Find $a+b$.

## Answer

**D.** $\sqrt{15}+8$

## Solution

The ellipse has foci $(\pm\sqrt{15},0)$. A circle through both has center $(0,k)$ and radius
$$r=\sqrt{k^2+15}.$$
For four intersections, its lower point must lie below the ellipse's bottom, giving $0\le k<7$. Hence $r$ ranges from $\sqrt{15}$ to $\sqrt{64}=8$, so $a+b=\sqrt{15}+8$.

## Knowledge points

- Ellipse foci
- Circle intersections

## Key idea

Parameterize all circles through the two fixed foci by their center height.

## Common mistakes

- Using the ellipse's major radius as a focus distance
- Including the endpoint that creates tangency
