# AMC12 2015-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2015-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Which rounding strategy necessarily makes $a/b-c$ larger than its exact value?

## Answer

**D.** Round $a$ up, $b$ down, and $c$ down.

## Solution

To make $a/b$ larger, increase $a$ and decrease positive $b$. To make the subtraction larger, decrease $c$. Thus the guaranteed strategy is to round $a$ up and $b,c$ down.

## Knowledge points

- Monotonicity of fractions
- Inequality direction

## Key idea

Track the effect of each variable separately.

## Common mistakes

- Rounding $b$ up, which decreases $a/b$
- Rounding $c$ up, which decreases the expression
