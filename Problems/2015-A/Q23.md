# AMC12 2015-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2015-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Two random points on the boundary of a unit square are chosen independently. Find $a+b+c$ if the probability their distance is at least $1/2$ is $(a-b\pi)/c$.

## Answer

**A.** $59$

## Solution

The points are on the same side with probability $1/4$, opposite sides with probability $1/4$, and adjacent sides with probability $1/2$. The conditional probabilities are $1/4$, $1$, and $1-\pi/16$, respectively. Thus
$$P=\frac14\cdot\frac14+\frac14+\frac12\left(1-\frac\pi{16}\right)=\frac{26-\pi}{32},$$
so $a+b+c=26+1+32=59$.

## Knowledge points

- Geometric probability
- Casework on square sides

## Key idea

Separate same, opposite, and adjacent side cases.

## Common mistakes

- Treating all side pairs as equally ordered
- Using a quarter-circle area with the wrong radius
