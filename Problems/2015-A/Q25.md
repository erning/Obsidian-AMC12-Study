# AMC12 2015-A Q25

![[assets/problems/2015-A/Q25.png]]

## Problem

A layered collection of tangent circles begins with radii $70^2$ and $73^2$. Each new circle is tangent to two consecutive circles. Find $\sum 1/r(C)$ over the first seven layers.

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{143}{14}$

## Solution

For three mutually tangent circles on the same line, the reciprocal radius of the inserted circle is the sum of the reciprocals of its two neighbors. Let
$$x=\frac1{70^2}+\frac1{73^2}.$$
The reciprocal-radius sum of layer $k$ is $3^{k-1}x$ for $k\ge1$, while layer $0$ contributes $x$. Hence the first seven layers sum to
$$x\left(1+\sum_{k=1}^{6}3^{k-1}\right)=365x.$$
Since $x=143/5110$, the result is $143/14$.

## Knowledge points

- Descartes-style tangent-circle relation
- Geometric series

## Key idea

Track reciprocal radii, which add when a circle is inserted between two tangent circles.

## Common mistakes

- Adding radii instead of reciprocal radii
- Using the wrong number of layers
