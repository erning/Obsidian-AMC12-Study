# AMC12 2015-A Q20

![[assets/problems/2015-A/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Isosceles triangles with sides $(5,5,8)$ and $(a,a,b)$ have equal area and perimeter but are not congruent. Which integer is closest to $b$?

## Answer

**A.** $3$

## Solution

The first triangle has perimeter $18$ and area $12$. Thus $2a+b=18$. Its area condition gives
$$(b-8)(b^2-b-8)=0.$$
The noncongruent solution is
$$b=\frac{1+\sqrt{33}}2\approx3.37,$$
closest to $3$.

## Knowledge points

- Isosceles triangle area
- Algebraic factorization

## Key idea

Use both equal perimeter and equal area, then discard the congruent root.

## Common mistakes

- Choosing $b=8$
- Using the wrong altitude
