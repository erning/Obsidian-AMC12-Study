# AMC12 2015-A Q09

![[assets/problems/2015-A/Q09.png]]

## Problem

A box has two red, two green, and two yellow marbles. After two people each take two, what is the probability the last person gets two of one color?

## Answer

**C.** $\frac15$

## Solution

The final two marbles are uniformly distributed among pairs. Once the first final marble is fixed, exactly one of the remaining five has the same color. Hence
$$P=\frac15.$$

## Knowledge points

- Symmetry in random sampling
- Conditional probability

## Key idea

Analyze the last pair directly; earlier draws only randomize which pair remains.

## Common mistakes

- Treating colors as equally likely outcomes
- Counting only the first draw
