# AMC12 2015-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2015-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

A tetrahedron has $AB=5,AC=3,BC=4,BD=4,AD=3,CD=\frac{12}{5}\sqrt2$. Find its volume.

## Answer

**C.** $\frac{24}{5}$

## Solution

Triangles $ABC$ and $ABD$ are right $3$-$4$-$5$ triangles, each of area $6$. Their altitudes to $AB$ are both $12/5$, so the segment joining $C,D$ is perpendicular to the base plane. Therefore
$$V=\frac13\cdot6\cdot\frac{12}{5}=\frac{24}{5}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Tetrahedron volume
- Right triangles and perpendicular planes

## Key idea

Find a perpendicular height to one triangular face.

## Common mistakes

- Using $CD$ as the height
- Omitting the factor $1/3$
