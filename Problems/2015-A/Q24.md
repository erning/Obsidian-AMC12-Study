# AMC12 2015-A Q24

![[assets/problems/2015-A/Q24.png]]

## Problem

Rational $a,b\in[0,2)$ with denominators at most $5$ are chosen uniformly. Find the probability $(\cos(a\pi)+i\sin(b\pi))^4$ is real.

## Answer

**D.** $\frac6{25}$

## Solution

There are $20$ possible reduced rationals, hence $400$ ordered pairs. The fourth power is real when $x=\cos(a\pi)$ and $y=\sin(b\pi)$ satisfy $x=0$, $y=0$, or $x=\pm y$. Counting these gives $40+40-4+20=96$ pairs. Thus
$$P=\frac{96}{400}=\frac6{25}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Rational enumeration
- Complex-number realness

## Key idea

For $(x+iy)^4$, characterize when the imaginary part vanishes.

## Common mistakes

- Counting unreduced fractions as distinct
- Double-counting the zero cases
