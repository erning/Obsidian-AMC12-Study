# AMC12 2007-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

A triangle with side lengths in the ratio $3:4:5$ is inscribed in a circle of radius $3.$ What is the area of the triangle?

(A) $8.64$
(B) $12$
(C) $5\pi$
(D) $17.28$
(E) $18$

## Answer

**A.** $8.64$

## Solution

Let the sides be $3x,$ $4x,$ and $5x.$ The triangle is right, so its hypotenuse is a diameter.

Thus $5x=2\cdot 3=6,$ giving $x=\tfrac65.$

The area is $\tfrac12\cdot 3x\cdot 4x=6x^2$ $=6\cdot\tfrac{36}{25}$ $=\tfrac{216}{25}=8.64.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Right triangle
- Circumcircle, circumcenter, and circumradius
- Triangle area

## Key idea

Apply the Pythagorean theorem after identifying the right triangle.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
