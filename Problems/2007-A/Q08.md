# AMC12 2007-A Q08

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q08.png]]

## Problem

A star-polygon is drawn on a clock face by drawing a chord from each number to the fifth number counted clockwise from that number. That is, chords are drawn from $12$ to $5,$ from $5$ to $10,$ from $10$ to $3,$ and so on, ending back at $12.$ What is the degree measure of the angle at each vertex in the star-polygon?

(A) $20$
(B) $24$
(C) $30$
(D) $36$
(E) $60$

## Answer

**C.** $30$

## Solution

Consider the two chords meeting at the number $5.$ They run to $12$ and to $10,$ so the arc they subtend extends from $10$ to $12.$

That arc spans two of the twelve hour-marks, so its measure is $\tfrac{2}{12}\cdot 360^\circ=60^\circ.$

By the Inscribed Angle Theorem, the vertex angle is half the arc, or $\tfrac12\cdot 60^\circ=30^\circ.$ By symmetry every vertex angle equals $30^\circ.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Inscribed angle
- Arc

## Key idea

Relate an inscribed angle to its intercepted arc.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
