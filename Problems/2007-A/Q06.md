# AMC12 2007-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Triangles $ABC$ and $ADC$ are isosceles with $AB=BC$ and $AD=DC.$ Point $D$ is inside $\triangle ABC,$ $\angle ABC=40^\circ,$ and $\angle ADC=140^\circ.$ What is the degree measure of $\angle BAD?$

(A) $20$
(B) $30$
(C) $40$
(D) $50$
(E) $60$

## Answer

**D.** $50$

## Solution

Since $\triangle ABC$ is isosceles, $\angle BAC=\tfrac12(180^\circ-\angle ABC)$ $=70^\circ.$

Since $\triangle ADC$ is isosceles, $\angle DAC=\tfrac12(180^\circ-\angle ADC)$ $=20^\circ.$

Therefore $\angle BAD=\angle BAC-\angle DAC$ $=70^\circ-20^\circ$ $=50^\circ.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Isosceles triangle
- Angle chasing

## Key idea

Exploit equal sides to identify equal base angles before angle chasing.

## Common mistakes
