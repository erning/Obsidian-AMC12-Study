# AMC12 2007-A Q13

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q13.png]]

## Problem

A piece of cheese is located at $(12,10)$ in a coordinate plane. A mouse is at $(4,-2)$ and is running up the line $y=-5x+18.$ At the point $(a,b)$ the mouse starts getting farther from the cheese rather than closer to it. What is $a+b?$

(A) $6$
(B) $10$
(C) $14$
(D) $18$
(E) $22$

## Answer

**B.** $10$

## Solution

The mouse is closest to the cheese at the foot of the perpendicular from $(12,10)$ to the line.

This perpendicular has slope $\tfrac15,$ so its equation is $y=10+\tfrac15(x-12)=\tfrac15 x+\tfrac{38}{5}.$

Setting $\tfrac15 x+\tfrac{38}{5}=-5x+18$ gives $x=2$ and $y=8.$ Thus $(a,b)=(2,8)$ and $a+b=10.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.
