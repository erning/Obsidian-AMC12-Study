# AMC12 2007-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

How many three-digit numbers are composed of three distinct digits such that one digit is the average of the other two?

(A) $96$
(B) $104$
(C) $112$
(D) $120$
(E) $256$

## Answer

**C.** $112$

## Solution

The three distinct digits form an increasing arithmetic progression. Counting by common difference: $8$ with difference $1,$ $6$ with difference $2,$ $4$ with difference $3,$ and $2$ with difference $4,$ for $20$ sets.

Of these, $4$ sets contain $0$ (namely $\{0,1,2\},$ $\{0,2,4\},$ $\{0,3,6\},$ $\{0,4,8\}$); each yields $2\cdot 2!=4$ valid numbers since $0$ cannot lead.

The other $16$ sets each yield $3!=6$ numbers. The total is $4\cdot 4+16\cdot 6=112.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.
