# AMC12 2007-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

The larger of two consecutive odd integers is three times the smaller. What is their sum?

(A) $4$
(B) $8$
(C) $12$
(D) $16$
(E) $20$

## Answer

**A.** $4$

## Solution

Let the smaller integer be $x.$ Then the larger is $x+2.$

So $x+2=3x,$ which gives $x=1.$

The two integers are $1$ and $3,$ and their sum is $4.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Linear equation

## Key idea

Define the unknown quantity and solve the resulting linear equation.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
