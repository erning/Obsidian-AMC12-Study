# AMC12 2007-A Q19

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q19.png]]

## Problem

Triangles $ABC$ and $ADE$ have areas $2007$ and $7002,$ respectively, with $B=(0,0),$ $C=(223,0),$ $D=(680,380),$ and $E=(689,389).$ What is the sum of all possible $x$-coordinates of $A?$

(A) $282$
(B) $300$
(C) $600$
(D) $900$
(E) $1200$

## Answer

**E.** $1200$

## Solution

The altitude $h$ from $A$ in $\triangle ABC$ satisfies $2007=\tfrac12\cdot 223\cdot h,$ so $h=18.$ Thus $A$ lies on $y=18$ or $y=-18.$

Line $DE$ has equation $x-y-300=0.$ The condition on $\triangle ADE$ similarly places $A$ on one of two lines parallel to $DE.$

The four possible positions of $A$ are the vertices of a parallelogram whose center is the intersection of $y=0$ with line $DE,$ namely $(300,0).$ Hence the sum of the four $x$-coordinates is $4\cdot 300=1200.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.
