# AMC12 2007-A Q12

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q12.png]]

## Problem

Integers $a,$ $b,$ $c,$ and $d,$ not necessarily distinct, are chosen independently and at random from $0$ to $2007,$ inclusive. What is the probability that $ad-bc$ is even?

(A) $\dfrac{3}{8}$
(B) $\dfrac{7}{16}$
(C) $\dfrac{1}{2}$
(D) $\dfrac{9}{16}$
(E) $\dfrac{5}{8}$

## Answer

**E.** $\dfrac{5}{8}$

## Solution

Exactly half of the integers from $0$ to $2007$ are odd.

A product $ad$ is odd only when both factors are odd, with probability $\tfrac12\cdot\tfrac12=\tfrac14,$ and even with probability $\tfrac34.$ The same holds for $bc.$

Then $ad-bc$ is even when both products are odd or both are even:
$$
\tfrac14\cdot\tfrac14+\tfrac34\cdot\tfrac34=\tfrac{10}{16}=\tfrac58.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.
