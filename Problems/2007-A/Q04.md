# AMC12 2007-A Q04

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q04.png]]

## Problem

Kate rode her bicycle for $30$ minutes at a speed of $16$ mph, then walked for $90$ minutes at a speed of $4$ mph. What was her overall average speed in miles per hour?

(A) $7$
(B) $9$
(C) $10$
(D) $12$
(E) $14$

## Answer

**A.** $7$

## Solution

Kate rode for $\tfrac12$ hour at $16$ mph, covering $8$ miles.

She walked for $\tfrac32$ hours at $4$ mph, covering $6$ miles.

She covered $14$ miles in $2$ hours, so her average speed was $7$ mph.

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Distance, rate, and time

## Key idea

Use distance $=$ rate $\times$ time and keep all time units consistent.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
