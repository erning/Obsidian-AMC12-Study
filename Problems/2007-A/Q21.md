# AMC12 2007-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

The sum of the zeros, the product of the zeros, and the sum of the coefficients of the function $f(x)=ax^2+bx+c$ are equal. Their common value must also be which of the following?

(A) the coefficient of $x^2$
(B) the coefficient of $x$
(C) the $y$-intercept of the graph of $y=f(x)$
(D) one of the $x$-intercepts of the graph of $y=f(x)$
(E) the mean of the $x$-intercepts of the graph of
$y=f(x)$

## Answer

**A.** the coefficient of $x^2$

## Solution

The product of the zeros is $\tfrac ca$ and the sum of the zeros is $-\tfrac ba.$ Equating them gives $c=-b.$

Then the sum of the coefficients is $a+b+c=a,$ which is the coefficient of $x^2.$

The other choices fail in general: for $f(x)=-2x^2-4x+4$ the common value is $-2,$ but the coefficient of $x$ is $-4,$ the $y$-intercept is $4,$ the $x$-intercepts are $-1\pm\sqrt3,$ and their mean is $-1.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

- Quadratic

## Key idea

Apply the sum and product of roots instead of solving the quadratic directly.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
