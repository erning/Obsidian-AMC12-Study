# AMC12 2007-A Q20

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Corners are sliced off a unit cube so that the six faces each become regular octagons. What is the total volume of the removed tetrahedra?

(A) $\dfrac{5\sqrt2-7}{3}$
(B) $\dfrac{10-7\sqrt2}{3}$
(C) $\dfrac{3-2\sqrt2}{3}$
(D) $\dfrac{8\sqrt2-11}{3}$
(E) $\dfrac{6-4\sqrt2}{3}$

## Answer

**B.** $\dfrac{10-7\sqrt2}{3}$

## Solution

Slicing removes two equal segments of length $x$ from each edge. Each octagon then has side length $x\sqrt2,$ and the edge satisfies $1=2x+x\sqrt2,$ so
$$
x=\frac{1}{2+\sqrt2}=\frac{2-\sqrt2}{2}.
$$

Each removed corner is a tetrahedron with three mutually perpendicular legs of length $x,$ so its volume is $\tfrac16 x^3.$ There are $8$ corners, giving total volume
$$
 \begin{aligned} &8\cdot\tfrac16 x^3 \\ &=\tfrac43\left(\tfrac{2-\sqrt2}{2}\right)^3 \\ &=\frac{10-7\sqrt2}{3}. \end{aligned}
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.
