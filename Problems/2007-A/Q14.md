# AMC12 2007-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Let $a,$ $b,$ $c,$ $d,$ and $e$ be distinct integers such that

$$
 \begin{aligned} &(6-a)(6-b)(6-c) \\ &\quad {}\cdot(6-d)(6-e) \\ &=45. \end{aligned}
$$

What is $a+b+c+d+e?$

(A) $5$
(B) $17$
(C) $25$
(D) $27$
(E) $30$

## Answer

**C.** $25$

## Solution

The five factors are distinct nonzero integers with product $45$, so each is a positive or negative divisor of $45$. Checking $\pm1,\pm3,\pm5,\pm9,\pm15,\pm45$, only the five-element set $\{-3,-1,1,3,5\}$ has product $45$.

Then $a,b,c,d,e$ are $9,7,5,3,1$ in some order, and their sum is $25.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Factoring
- Bounding to limit cases

## Key idea

Factor the expression and bound each factor using the integer constraints.
