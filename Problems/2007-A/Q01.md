# AMC12 2007-A Q01

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q01.png]]

## Problem

One ticket to a show costs $\$20$ at full price. Susan buys $4$ tickets using a coupon that gives her a $25\%$ discount. Pam buys $5$ tickets using a coupon that gives her a $30\%$ discount. How many more dollars does Pam pay than Susan?

(A) $2$
(B) $5$
(C) $10$
(D) $15$
(E) $20$

## Answer

**C.** $10$

## Solution

Susan pays $(4)(0.75)(20)=60$ dollars.

Pam pays $(5)(0.70)(20)=70$ dollars.

So Pam pays $70-60=10$ more dollars than Susan.

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Percentage
- Money

## Key idea

Translate the percentage statement into an equation before calculating the amount.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
