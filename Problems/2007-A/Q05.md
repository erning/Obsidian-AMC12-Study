# AMC12 2007-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Last year Mr. John Q. Public received an inheritance. He paid $20\%$ in federal taxes on the inheritance, and paid $10\%$ of what he had left in state taxes. He paid a total of $\$10{,}500$ for both taxes. How many dollars was the inheritance?

(A) $30{,}000$
(B) $32{,}500$
(C) $35{,}000$
(D) $37{,}500$
(E) $40{,}000$

## Answer

**D.** $37{,}500$

## Solution

After federal taxes, Mr. Public keeps $80\%$ of his inheritance.

He pays $10\%$ of that in state taxes, which is $8\%$ of the inheritance.

His total tax is $20\%+8\%=28\%$ of the inheritance, so the inheritance is $\$10{,}500/0.28=\$37{,}500.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Percentage
- Work backwards

## Key idea

Translate the percentage statement into an equation before calculating the amount.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
