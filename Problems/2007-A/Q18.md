# AMC12 2007-A Q18

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q18.png]]

## Problem

The polynomial $f(x)=x^4+ax^3+bx^2+cx+d$ has real coefficients, and $f(2i)=f(2+i)=0.$ What is $a+b+c+d?$

(A) $0$
(B) $1$
(C) $4$
(D) $9$
(E) $16$

## Answer

**D.** $9$

## Solution

Since $f$ has real coefficients, the conjugates $-2i$ and $2-i$ are also roots. Thus
$$
 \begin{gathered} f(x) = (x^2+4)(x^2-4x+5) \\ = x^4-4x^3+9x^2 \\ {}-16x+20. \end{gathered}
$$

Then $a+b+c+d=-4+9$ $-16+20$ $=9.$ Equivalently, $a+b+c+d=f(1)-1$ $=(1+4)(1+1)-1$ $=9.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.
