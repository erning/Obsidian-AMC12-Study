# AMC12 2007-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2007-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Let $a,$ $b,$ $c,$ $d,$ and $e$ be five consecutive terms in an arithmetic sequence, and suppose that $a+b+c+d+e=30.$ Which of the following can be found?

(A) $a$
(B) $b$
(C) $c$
(D) $d$
(E) $e$

## Answer

**C.** $c$

## Solution

Let $D$ be the common difference. Then $a=c-2D,$ $b=c-D,$ $d=c+D,$ and $e=c+2D,$ so
$$
a+b+c+d+e=5c.
$$

Thus $5c=30,$ giving $c=6.$

The other terms cannot be determined: the sequences $4,5,6,7,8$ and $10,8,6,4,2$ both satisfy the conditions but differ in every term except the middle one.

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Arithmetic sequence
- Symmetry (algebra)

## Key idea

Use the constant difference and symmetry of terms to avoid listing unnecessary terms.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
