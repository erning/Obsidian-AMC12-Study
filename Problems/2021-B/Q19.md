# AMC12 2021-B Q19

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q19.png]]

## Problem

Two fair dice, each with at least $6$ faces, are rolled. On each face of each die is printed a distinct integer from $1$ to the number of faces on that die, inclusive. The probability of rolling a sum of $7$ is $\frac34$ of the probability of rolling a sum of $10$, and the probability of rolling a sum of $12$ is $\frac1{12}$. What is the least possible number of faces on the two dice combined?

## Answer

**B.** $17$

## Solution

Let the dice have $a$ and $b$ faces. There are $ab$ equally likely outcomes. The condition on sum $12$ means the number of sum-$12$ outcomes is $ab/12$.

The smallest feasible pair is $a=8$, $b=9$, giving $ab=72$. For these dice, the numbers of outcomes with sums $7$, $10$, and $12$ are respectively $6$, $8$, and $6$. Thus

$$\frac{6}{72}=\frac34\cdot\frac{8}{72},\qquad \frac{6}{72}=\frac1{12}.$$

Pairs with smaller sum $a+b$ either have too few faces to realize the required sum counts or fail the $3:4$ ratio. Therefore the least combined number is $8+9=17$.

## Knowledge points

- Counting outcomes of sums of two dice
- Probability ratios
- Minimality checking

## Key idea

Convert the probability statements into integer counts and test the smallest feasible face dimensions.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming both dice must have the same number of faces.
- Forgetting that the denominator is $ab$, not $a+b$.
