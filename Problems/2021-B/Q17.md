# AMC12 2021-B Q17

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Let $ABCD$ be an isosceles trapezoid with parallel bases $\overline{AB}$ and $\overline{CD}$, where $AB>CD$. Line segments from a point inside $ABCD$ to the vertices divide the trapezoid into four triangles whose areas are $2,3,4,5$, starting with the triangle with base $CD$ and moving clockwise as shown. What is the ratio $\dfrac{AB}{CD}$?

## Answer

**B.** $2+\sqrt2$

## Solution

Let $AB=a$, $CD=b$, and let the distances from the interior point to $AB$ and $CD$ be $x$ and $y$. Then $x+y=h$. From the two triangles whose bases are the parallel sides,

$$\frac12ax=4,\qquad \frac12by=2,$$

so $ax=8$ and $by=4$. The total trapezoid area is $2+3+4+5=14$, hence

$$\frac12(a+b)(x+y)=14.$$

Substitute $x=8/a$ and $y=4/b$:

$$ (a+b)\left(\frac8a+\frac4b\right)=28.$$

After expansion and setting $r=a/b$, this becomes

$$4r+\frac8r=16,\qquad r^2-4r+2=0.$$

Because $a>b$, the positive branch is required:

$$\frac{a}{b}=2\pm\sqrt2.$$

Because $a>b$, choose the positive larger root, $\frac{a}{b}=2+\sqrt2$.

## Knowledge points

- Area ratios in a trapezoid
- Isosceles trapezoid symmetry
- Algebraic equations involving ratios

## Key idea

The four small areas encode the distances from the interior point to the two parallel bases and the two legs; symmetry reduces the geometry to one ratio.

## Common mistakes

- Reading the four areas in the wrong clockwise order.
- Choosing $2-\sqrt2$, which contradicts $AB>CD$.
