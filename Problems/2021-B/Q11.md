# AMC12 2021-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

Triangle $ABC$ has side lengths $AB=13$, $BC=14$, and $AC=15$. Point $P$ lies on $AC$ with $PC=10$. There are exactly two points $D$ and $E$ on line $BP$ such that $ABCD$ and $ABCE$ are trapezoids. What is $DE$?

## Answer

**D.** $12\sqrt2$

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q11-draft.svg]]

Place $A=(0,0)$ and $C=(15,0)$. From the side lengths,

$$B=\left(\frac{33}{5},\frac{56}{5}\right),\qquad P=(5,0).$$

Thus line $BP$ has slope $7$. For $ABCD$, use $CD\parallel AB$; for $ABCE$, use $AE\parallel BC$. Solving the two line intersections gives

$$D=\left(\frac95,-\frac{112}{5}\right),\qquad E=\left(\frac{21}{5},-\frac{28}{5}\right).$$

Therefore

$$DE=\sqrt{\left(\frac{12}{5}\right)^2+\left(\frac{84}{5}\right)^2}=\sqrt{288}=12\sqrt2.$$

## Knowledge points

- Coordinate geometry
- Law of cosines / distance formula
- Parallel lines

## Key idea

Coordinates make the two trapezoid conditions into two parallel-line intersections on the same known line $BP$.

## Common mistakes

- Choosing the wrong orientation for the $y$-coordinate of $B$.
- Applying only one of the two possible trapezoid parallel-side conditions.
