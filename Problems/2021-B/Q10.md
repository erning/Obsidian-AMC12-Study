# AMC12 2021-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

Two distinct numbers are selected from $\{1,2,3,4,\ldots,36,37\}$ so that the sum of the remaining $35$ numbers is the product of the selected numbers. What is the difference of the two numbers?

## Answer

**E.** $10$

## Solution

The sum of all numbers is $\frac{37\cdot38}{2}=703$. If the selected numbers are $a$ and $b$, then

$$703-a-b=ab.$$

Rearrange and factor:

$$ab+a+b=703\quad\Longrightarrow\quad(a+1)(b+1)=704=2^6\cdot11.$$

The distinct factors that produce values in the required range are $a+1=22$ and $b+1=32$. Thus $(a,b)=(21,31)$ and the difference is $10$.

## Knowledge points

- Arithmetic series
- Symmetric factorization

## Key idea

Convert a product-plus-sum equation into a factorization by adding $1$ to both variables.

## Common mistakes

- Using $37\cdot37/2$ for the total.
- Forgetting the selected numbers are removed from the sum.
