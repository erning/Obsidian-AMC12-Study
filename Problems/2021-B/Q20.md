# AMC12 2021-B Q20

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Let $Q(z)$ and $R(z)$ be the unique polynomials such that

$$z^{2021}+1=(z^2+z+1)Q(z)+R(z),$$

and the degree of $R$ is less than $2$. What is $R(z)$?

## Answer

**A.** $-z$

## Solution

Modulo $z^2+z+1$, we have $z^3-1=(z-1)(z^2+z+1)=0$, so $z^3\equiv1$. Since $2021\equiv2\pmod3$,

$$z^{2021}+1\equiv z^2+1.$$

Also $z^2+z+1\equiv0$, so $z^2+1\equiv-z$. The remainder of degree less than $2$ is therefore $R(z)=-z$.

## Knowledge points

- Polynomial division remainders
- Modular polynomial arithmetic

## Key idea

Reduce powers modulo the relation $z^2+z+1=0$, which makes the powers periodic with period $3$.

## Common mistakes

- Reducing $2021$ modulo $2$ instead of modulo $3$.
- Stopping at $z^2+1$ without reducing it to degree less than $2$.
