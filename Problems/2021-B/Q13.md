# AMC12 2021-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

How many values of $\theta$ in $(0,2\pi]$ satisfy

$$1-3\sin\theta+5\cos3\theta=0?$$

## Answer

**D.** $6$

## Solution

Let $f(\theta)=1-3\sin\theta+5\cos3\theta$. Evaluate $f$ at the convenient multiples of $30^\circ$ in one period. The values alternate in sign across six disjoint subintervals, so the intermediate value theorem gives at least six roots.

To show there are no more, differentiate or use the shape of $\cos3\theta$ between consecutive critical points. On each of those six sign-changing intervals the function is monotone through zero, while the remaining intervals keep one sign. Hence there are exactly $6$ solutions.

## Knowledge points

- Periodic trigonometric functions
- Intermediate value theorem
- Root counting by sign and monotonicity

## Key idea

The $\cos3\theta$ term creates three oscillations; combine a sign chart with monotonicity rather than trying to solve a cubic trigonometric equation.

## Common mistakes

- Counting endpoints twice when the interval is $(0,2\pi]$.
- Assuming every oscillation automatically contributes exactly two roots without checking signs.
