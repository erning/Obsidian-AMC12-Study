# AMC12 2021-B Q16

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q16.png]]

## Problem

Let $g(x)$ be a polynomial with leading coefficient $1$, whose three roots are the reciprocals of the three roots of $f(x)=x^3+ax^2+bx+c$, where $1<a<b<c$. What is $g(1)$ in terms of $a$, $b$, and $c$?

## Answer

**A.** $\dfrac{1+a+b+c}{c}$

## Solution

Let the roots of $f$ be $r,s,t$. Then $rst=-c$ and

$$g(1)=\left(1-\frac1r\right)\left(1-\frac1s\right)\left(1-\frac1t\right)
=\frac{(r-1)(s-1)(t-1)}{rst}.$$

By Vieta,

$$f(1)=1+a+b+c=(1-r)(1-s)(1-t).$$

Therefore $(r-1)(s-1)(t-1)=-(1+a+b+c)$ and $rst=-c$, so

$$g(1)=\frac{1+a+b+c}{c}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Vieta's formulas
- Reciprocal roots
- Polynomial evaluation

## Key idea

Evaluate the transformed polynomial at $1$ using the original polynomial's value $f(1)$.

## Common mistakes

- Losing the two sign changes when replacing $(1-r)$ by $(r-1)$.
- Using $rst=c$ instead of $rst=-c$.
