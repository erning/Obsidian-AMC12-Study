# AMC12 2021-B Q18

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Let $z$ be a complex number satisfying

$$12|z|^2=2|z+2|^2+|z^2+1|^2+31.$$

What is the value of $z+\dfrac6z$?

## Answer

**A.** $-2$

## Solution

Set $p=|z|^2$ and $s=z+\overline z$. Then

$$|z+2|^2=p+2s+4,$$

and

$$|z^2+1|^2=p^2+z^2+\overline z^2+1=p^2+s^2-2p+1.$$

Substitution gives

$$p^2-12p+s^2+4s+40=0,$$

or

$$ (p-6)^2+(s+2)^2=0.$$

Both squares vanish, so $|z|^2=6$ and $z+\overline z=-2$. Since $6/z=\overline z$, the requested value is $-2$.

## Knowledge points

- Complex modulus and conjugates
- Sum of squares
- Completing the square

## Key idea

Rewrite all modulus expressions using $p=|z|^2$ and $s=z+\overline z$ until the condition becomes a sum of nonnegative squares.

## Common mistakes

- Replacing $|z^2+1|^2$ with $|z|^4+1$ and dropping the cross term.
- Using $6/\overline z=z$ instead of $6/z=\overline z$.
