# AMC12 2021-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Rectangle $ABCD$ has a point $M$ such that $DM$ is perpendicular to the plane of the rectangle. The lengths $MA$, $MC$, and $MB$ are consecutive odd positive integers in that order, and $DM$ is an integer. What is the volume of pyramid $MABCD$?

## Answer

**A.** $24\sqrt5$

## Solution

Let $MA=k$, $MC=k+2$, and $MB=k+4$. Put $DM=h$, $AD=u$, and $CD=v$. By the three right triangles,

$$k^2=u^2+h^2,\quad (k+2)^2=v^2+h^2,\quad (k+4)^2=u^2+v^2+h^2.$$

Adding the first two equations and subtracting the third gives

$$h^2=k^2-4k-12=(k-6)(k+2).$$

The positive integer solution compatible with the geometry is $k=7$, giving $h=3$, $u^2=40$, and $v^2=72$. Thus the rectangle area is

$$uv=\sqrt{40\cdot72}=24\sqrt5,$$

and the pyramid volume is

$$\frac13(24\sqrt5)(3)=24\sqrt5.$$

## Knowledge points

- Three-dimensional Pythagorean theorem
- Pyramid volume
- Integer constraints

## Key idea

Use the three distances from $M$ to the rectangle's vertices to eliminate the two side lengths and isolate the integer height.

## Common mistakes

- Using $\frac12$ instead of $\frac13$ in the pyramid volume.
- Treating $MB$ as a diagonal in the rectangle without including $DM$.
