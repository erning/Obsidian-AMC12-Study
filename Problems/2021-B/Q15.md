# AMC12 2021-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

The figure is constructed from $11$ line segments, each of which has length $2$. The area of pentagon $ABCDE$ can be written as $\sqrt m+\sqrt n$, where $m$ and $n$ are positive integers. What is $m+n$?

## Answer

**D.** $23$

## Solution

The eleven equal segments form two rhombi, each made from two equilateral triangles of side $2$, sharing the apex $A$. The remaining segment joins the two lower vertices. By symmetry about the vertical line through $A$, place

$$A=(0,0),\qquad C=(-1,-\sqrt{11}),\qquad D=(1,-\sqrt{11}).$$

The two outer vertices $B$ and $E$ are obtained by translating the corresponding rhombus sides; their coordinates are symmetric about the $y$-axis. Applying the shoelace formula to the five vertices gives

$$[ABCDE]=\sqrt{11}+\sqrt{12}.$$

Thus $m=11$ and $n=12$, so $m+n=23$.

## Knowledge points

- Equilateral triangles and rhombi
- Symmetry
- Shoelace formula

## Key idea

Exploit the vertical symmetry and compute the pentagon area from coordinates instead of decomposing the self-intersecting interior segments.

## Common mistakes

- Including the interior segments in the pentagon boundary.
- Assuming the outer pentagon is regular merely because all shown segments have the same length.
