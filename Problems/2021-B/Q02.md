# AMC12 2021-B Q02

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q02.png]]

## Problem

At a math contest, $57$ students wear blue shirts and $75$ wear yellow shirts. The $132$ students are assigned into $66$ pairs. In exactly $23$ pairs, both students wear blue shirts. In how many pairs do both students wear yellow shirts?

## Answer

**B.** $32$

## Solution

The $23$ all-blue pairs use $46$ blue-shirt students, leaving

$$57-46=11$$

blue students. No additional pair can be all blue, so each of these $11$ students must be in a mixed pair. Thus $11$ yellow students are also used. The remaining yellow students form all-yellow pairs:

$$\frac{75-11}{2}=32.$$

## Knowledge points

- Pairing and conservation of counts
- Complementary counting

## Key idea

After accounting for the known blue-blue pairs, every remaining blue student forces one mixed pair.

## Common mistakes

- Subtracting $23$ instead of $46$ blue students.
- Forgetting that the remaining yellow students must be paired in twos.
