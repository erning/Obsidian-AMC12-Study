# AMC12 2021-B Q06

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q06.png]]

## Problem

An inverted cone has radius $12$ and height $18$. It is filled with water and poured into a cylinder of radius $24$. What is the height of the water in the cylinder?

## Answer

**A.** $1.5$

## Solution

The cone's volume is

$$V=\frac13\pi(12)^2(18)=864\pi.$$

If the cylinder height is $h$, its volume is $\pi(24)^2h=576\pi h$. Equating volumes,

$$576\pi h=864\pi\quad\Longrightarrow\quad h=\frac{864}{576}=1.5.$$

## Knowledge points

- Volumes of cones and cylinders
- Conservation of volume

## Key idea

The pouring process changes the shape, not the amount of water.

## Common mistakes

- Omitting the factor $\frac13$ in the cone volume.
- Confusing radius with diameter.
