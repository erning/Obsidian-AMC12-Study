# AMC12 2021-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Let $N=34\cdot34\cdot63\cdot270$. What is the ratio of the sum of the odd divisors of $N$ to the sum of the even divisors of $N$?

## Answer

**C.** $1:14$

## Solution

Factor the number as $N=2^3M$, where $M$ is odd. Every divisor of $N$ has the form $2^j d$ with $j\in\{0,1,2,3\}$ and $d\mid M$.

The sum of odd divisors is $\sigma(M)$. The sum of even divisors is

$$\left(2+4+8\right)\sigma(M)=14\sigma(M),$$

and the ratio is $1:14$.

## Knowledge points

- Prime factorization
- Sum-of-divisors function
- Separating odd and even divisors

## Key idea

Only the power of $2$ matters for the ratio. If $N=2^kM$ with $M$ odd, the ratio is $1:(2+2^2+\cdots+2^k)$.

## Common mistakes

- Missing the factor $2$ in $270$.
- Treating $34$ as an exponent rather than the integer $34$.
