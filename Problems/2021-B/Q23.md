# AMC12 2021-B Q23

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Three balls are independently tossed into bins numbered by the positive integers. For each ball, the probability of landing in bin $i$ is $2^{-i}$. More than one ball may occupy a bin. The probability that the balls end up evenly spaced in distinct bins is $\frac pq$, where $p$ and $q$ are relatively prime positive integers. What is $p+q$?

## Answer

**A.** $55$

## Solution

Three distinct evenly spaced bin labels have the form $n$, $n+d$, and $n+2d$, where $n,d\ge1$. For a fixed triple, the probability for one assignment of the three labeled balls is

$$2^{-n}2^{-(n+d)}2^{-(n+2d)}=2^{-3n-3d}.$$

There are $3!=6$ assignments. Therefore

$$P=6\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{d=1}^{\infty}2^{-3n-3d}
=6\left(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}2^{-3n}\right)^2
=6\left(\frac17\right)^2=\frac6{49}.$$

Thus $p+q=6+49=55$.

## Knowledge points

- Infinite geometric series
- Counting permutations
- Independent probability

## Key idea

Parameterize the bin triple by its first term and common difference; independence makes the double sum separable.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting the $6$ assignments of the labeled balls.
- Allowing $d=0$, which would violate distinctness.
