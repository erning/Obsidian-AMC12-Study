# AMC12 2021-B Q03

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q03.png]]

## Problem

Suppose

$$2+\frac{1}{1+\dfrac{1}{2+\dfrac{2}{3+x}}}=\frac{144}{53}.$$

What is the value of $x$?

## Answer

**A.** $\frac34$

## Solution

Work from the outside inward. First,

$$\frac{1}{1+\dfrac{1}{2+\frac{2}{3+x}}}=\frac{144}{53}-2=\frac{38}{53}.$$

Taking reciprocals gives

$$1+\frac{1}{2+\frac{2}{3+x}}=\frac{53}{38},$$

so

$$\frac{1}{2+\frac{2}{3+x}}=\frac{15}{38}.$$

Thus $2+\frac{2}{3+x}=\frac{38}{15}$, hence

$$\frac{2}{3+x}=\frac{8}{15},\qquad 3+x=\frac{15}{4},$$

and therefore $x=\frac34$.

## Knowledge points

- Nested fractions
- Solving rational equations

## Key idea

Undo the nested operations one at a time, taking a reciprocal only after isolating the relevant fraction.

## Common mistakes

- Misreading the denominator as $2+\frac{1}{3+x}$ instead of $2+\frac{2}{3+x}$.
- Taking a reciprocal before subtracting the outer $2$.
