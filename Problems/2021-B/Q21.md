# AMC12 2021-B Q21

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Let $S$ be the sum of all positive real numbers $x$ for which

$$x^{2\sqrt2}=(\sqrt2)^{2^x}.$$

Which statement is true?

## Answer

**D.** $2\le S<6$

## Solution

Taking logarithms base $2$ gives

$$2\sqrt2\log_2x=2^{x-1}.$$

Define the difference between the two sides. Its derivative and second derivative show that the equation has exactly two positive roots: one lies between $1$ and $2$, and the other lies between $2$ and $4$. Consequently,

$$2<S<6,$$

and in particular $2\le S<6$. The endpoint inequality is the answer choice requested.

## Knowledge points

- Exponential and logarithmic equations
- Root counting with calculus

## Key idea

Take logarithms first, then locate the two roots by interval estimates instead of solving the transcendental equation explicitly.

## Common mistakes

- Taking logarithms without preserving the exponent $2^x$.
- Assuming the two roots themselves must have simple closed forms.
