# AMC12 2021-B Q24

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q24.png]]

## Problem

Let $ABCD$ be a parallelogram with area $15$. Points $P$ and $Q$ are the projections of $A$ and $C$ onto line $BD$, and points $R$ and $S$ are the projections of $B$ and $D$ onto line $AC$. Suppose $PQ=6$ and $RS=8$. Let $d$ be the length of $BD$, the longer diagonal. Then $d^2=m+n\sqrt p$, where $m,n,p$ are positive integers and $p$ is square-free. What is $m+n+p$?

## Answer

**A.** $81$

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q24-draft.svg]]

Let the diagonal lengths be $u=AC$ and $d=BD$, and let $\theta$ be the angle between them. Orthogonal projection gives

$$u\cos\theta=PQ=6,\qquad d\cos\theta=RS=8.$$

The parallelogram area is half the product of its diagonals times the sine of their angle:

$$\frac12ud\sin\theta=15.$$

Thus $ud\sin\theta=30$ and $ud\cos^2\theta=48$. Dividing gives

$$\frac{\cos^2\theta}{\sin\theta}=\frac85.$$

Solving together with $\sin^2\theta+\cos^2\theta=1$ and $d\cos\theta=8$ yields

$$d^2=32+8\sqrt{41}.$$

Therefore $(m,n,p)=(32,8,41)$ and $m+n+p=81$.

## Knowledge points

- Diagonals of a parallelogram
- Orthogonal projection
- Area using diagonals

## Key idea

Both projection lengths share the cosine of the diagonal angle, while the area supplies the sine relation.

## Common mistakes

- Using $ud\sin\theta=15$ instead of $30$.
- Interchanging the longer diagonal with $AC$.
