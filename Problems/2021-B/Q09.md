# AMC12 2021-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

What is the value of

$$\frac{\log_2 80}{\log_{40}2}-\frac{\log_2 160}{\log_{20}2}?$$

## Answer

**D.** $2$

## Solution

Use reciprocal change of base:

$$\frac{1}{\log_{40}2}=\log_2 40,\qquad \frac{1}{\log_{20}2}=\log_2 20.$$

The expression becomes

$$\log_2 80\log_2 40-\log_2 160\log_2 20.$$

Let $t=\log_2 5$. Then the four logarithms are $4+t$, $3+t$, $5+t$, and $2+t$, respectively. Hence

$$ (4+t)(3+t)-(5+t)(2+t)=2.$$

## Knowledge points

- Change of base for logarithms
- Algebraic cancellation

## Key idea

Turn each reciprocal logarithm into a logarithm with reversed base and argument, then use powers of $2$ times $5$.

## Common mistakes

- Replacing $1/\log_a b$ with $\log_a b$ instead of $\log_b a$.
- Forgetting that $80=16\cdot5$, $40=8\cdot5$, and so on.
