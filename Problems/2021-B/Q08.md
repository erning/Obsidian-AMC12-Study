# AMC12 2021-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

Three equally spaced parallel lines intersect a circle, creating three chords of lengths $38$, $38$, and $34$. What is the distance between two adjacent parallel lines?

## Answer

**B.** $6$

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q08-draft.svg]]

Let the distance between adjacent lines be $d$. The two equal chords must be equally far from the center, so their signed distances from the center are $-\frac d2$ and $\frac d2$. The third line is then at distance $\frac{3d}{2}$ from the center.

For a chord of length $L$ at distance $t$ from the center, $L^2=4(r^2-t^2)$. Therefore

$$r^2-\frac{d^2}{4}=19^2,\qquad r^2-\frac{9d^2}{4}=17^2.$$

Subtracting the equations gives

$$2d^2=19^2-17^2=72,$$

so $d^2=36$ and $d=6$.

## Knowledge points

- Chord length and distance from center
- Symmetry of a circle

## Key idea

Equal chord lengths imply equal distances from the center; use the squared chord formula to eliminate the radius.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming all three lines are on the same side of the center.
- Using chord length rather than half-chord in the Pythagorean relation.
