# AMC12 2021-B Q05

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Point $P(a,b)$ is rotated $90^\circ$ counterclockwise about $(1,5)$ and then reflected across $y=-x$. The final point is $(-6,3)$. What is $b-a$?

## Answer

**D.** $7$

## Solution

Relative to $(1,5)$, the vector from the center to $P$ is $(a-1,b-5)$. After a $90^\circ$ counterclockwise rotation it is $(5-b,a-1)$, so the rotated point is

$$R=(6-b,4+a).$$

Reflection across $y=-x$ maps $(u,v)$ to $(-v,-u)$, giving

$$(-4-a,b-6)=(-6,3).$$

Hence $a=2$ and $b=9$, so $b-a=7$.

## Knowledge points

- Coordinate transformations
- Rotation and reflection formulas

## Key idea

Apply the transformations in their stated order and keep the rotation center separate from the origin.

## Common mistakes

- Rotating about the origin instead of $(1,5)$.
- Using the reflection rule for $y=x$ rather than $y=-x$.
