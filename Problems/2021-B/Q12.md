# AMC12 2021-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2021-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

Suppose that $S$ is a finite set of positive integers. If the greatest integer is removed, the average of the remaining integers is $32$. If the least integer is also removed, the average is $35$. If the greatest integer is then returned, the average rises to $40$. The greatest integer is $72$ greater than the least integer. What is the average of all the integers in $S$?

## Answer

**D.** $36.8$

## Solution

Let $|S|=n$, total sum $T$, least member $L$, and greatest member $M$. The three conditions give

$$T-M=32(n-1),\quad T-M-L=35(n-2),\quad T-L=40(n-1).$$

Subtracting the first and third equations gives

$$M-L=8(n-1).$$

The problem also gives $M-L=72$, so $8(n-1)=72$ and $n=10$. Then

$$L=32(9)-35(8)=8,\qquad M=80.$$

Finally, $T-M=288$, so $T=368$ and

$$\frac{T}{n}=\frac{368}{10}=36.8.$$

## Knowledge points

- Averages as total divided by count
- Translating removals into equations

## Key idea

Use the differences between the three averages to isolate the least and greatest elements before finding the set size.

## Common mistakes

- Changing the denominator incorrectly after removing two elements.
- Treating the average changes as changes in a single element without accounting for the count.
