# AMC12 2024-B Q21

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q21.png]]

## Problem

The smallest angles of three different right triangles sum to $90^\circ$. All three triangles have primitive Pythagorean-triple side lengths. Two are $3$-$4$-$5$ and $5$-$12$-$13$. What is the perimeter of the third?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $154$**

## Solution

Let $\alpha$ and $\beta$ be the smallest angles of the $3$-$4$-$5$ and $5$-$12$-$13$ triangles. Then

$$
\tan\alpha=\frac34,\qquad \tan\beta=\frac5{12}.
$$

The tangent addition formula gives

$$
\tan(\alpha+\beta)
=\frac{\frac34+\frac5{12}}{1-\frac34\cdot\frac5{12}}
=\frac{56}{33}.
$$

The third smallest angle is $\gamma=90^\circ-(\alpha+\beta)$, so

$$
\tan\gamma=\frac{33}{56}.
$$

Thus the third triangle has legs proportional to $33$ and $56$. Its hypotenuse is

$$
\sqrt{33^2+56^2}=\sqrt{4225}=65.
$$

Its perimeter is

$$
33+56+65=\boxed{154}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Tangent addition
- Complementary angles
- Primitive Pythagorean triples

## Key idea

Use the angle sum to obtain the tangent ratio of the third triangle.

## Common mistakes

- Using $4/3$ for the smallest angle of the first triangle.
- Forgetting to take the reciprocal for the complementary angle.
