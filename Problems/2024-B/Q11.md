# AMC12 2024-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

Let

$$
x_n=\sin^2(n^\circ).
$$

What is the mean of $x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_{90}$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $\frac{91}{180}$**

## Solution

Use

$$
\sin^2\theta=\frac{1-\cos2\theta}{2}.
$$

Then

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{90}\sin^2(n^\circ)
=45-\frac12\sum_{n=1}^{90}\cos(2n^\circ).
$$

In the cosine sum, terms pair as

$$
\cos(2n^\circ)+\cos(180^\circ-2n^\circ)=0.
$$

All terms cancel except $\cos180^\circ=-1$. Hence the sum is

$$
45-\frac12(-1)=\frac{91}{2}.
$$

Divide by $90$:

$$
\frac{91/2}{90}=\boxed{\frac{91}{180}}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Power-reduction identity
- Symmetric trigonometric sums
- Arithmetic mean

## Key idea

Convert $\sin^2$ into a cosine sum whose terms cancel in pairs.

## Common mistakes

- Pairing angles incorrectly.
- Dividing by $91$ instead of $90$.
