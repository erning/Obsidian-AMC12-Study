# AMC12 2024-B Q20

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Points $A,B,C$ satisfy $AB=40$ and $AC=42$. Let $x$ be the length of the segment from $A$ to the midpoint of $BC$. If the domain of the area function is $(p,q)$, its maximum is $r$ at $x=s$, find $p+q+r+s$.

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $911$**

## Solution

Let $a=BC$. The median formula gives

$$
x^2=\frac{2(40^2)+2(42^2)-a^2}{4}
=\frac{6728-a^2}{4}.
$$

The triangle inequality requires

$$
|42-40|<a<42+40,
\qquad
2<a<82.
$$

Thus

$$
4<a^2<6724,
$$

which gives

$$
1<x<41.
$$

Therefore $(p,q)=(1,41)$.

The area is maximized when the included angle between sides $40$ and $42$ is $90^\circ$:

$$
r=\frac12\cdot40\cdot42=840.
$$

In this case $a^2=40^2+42^2=3364$, so

$$
s^2=\frac{6728-3364}{4}=841,
\qquad
s=29.
$$

Hence

$$
p+q+r+s=1+41+840+29=\boxed{911}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Median formula
- Triangle inequality
- Area optimization

## Key idea

Translate the possible third-side interval into the domain of the median.

## Common mistakes

- Using $x$ as the third side.
- Maximizing area at an incorrect angle.
