# AMC12 2024-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

Suppose $z$ has positive imaginary part, real part greater than $1$, and $|z|=2$. The four points $0,z,z^2,z^3$ form a quadrilateral of area $15$. What is the imaginary part of $z$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $\frac32$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q12-draft.svg]]

For the quadrilateral with vertices $0,z,z^2,z^3$, the shoelace formula in complex form gives

$$
\text{Area}
=\frac12\operatorname{Im}\left(z\overline{z^2}+z^2\overline{z^3}\right).
$$

Since

$$
z\overline{z^2}=|z|^2\overline z,
\qquad
z^2\overline{z^3}=|z|^4\overline z,
$$

the area is

$$
\frac12(|z|^2+|z|^4)\operatorname{Im}(z).
$$

With $|z|=2$, this becomes

$$
\frac12(4+16)\operatorname{Im}(z)=10\operatorname{Im}(z).
$$

Set this equal to $15$:

$$
\operatorname{Im}(z)=\boxed{\frac32}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Complex-plane coordinates
- Shoelace formula
- Powers and conjugates

## Key idea

The area depends linearly on $\operatorname{Im}(z)$ once $|z|$ is fixed.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $z^2$ and $z^3$ as points on a straight line.
- Forgetting the factor $\frac12$ in the shoelace formula.
