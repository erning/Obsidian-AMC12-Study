# AMC12 2024-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

The dartboard is

$$
B=\{(x,y):|x|+|y|\le8\}.
$$

The target is

$$
T=\{(x,y):(x^2+y^2-25)^2\le49\}.
$$

The probability of landing in $T$ is $\frac{n}{m}\pi$ in lowest terms. What is $m+n$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $71$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q09-draft.svg]]

The board is a diamond with diagonals $16$ and $16$, so

$$
[B]=\frac12\cdot16\cdot16=128.
$$

The target condition becomes

$$
-7\le x^2+y^2-25\le7,
$$

or

$$
18\le x^2+y^2\le32.
$$

Thus $T$ is an annulus with area

$$
\pi(32-18)=14\pi.
$$

The distance from the origin to each side of the diamond is $8/\sqrt2=4\sqrt2$, whose square is $32$. Hence the annulus lies inside the board and is tangent to its sides.

The probability is

$$
\frac{14\pi}{128}=\frac{7}{64}\pi.
$$

Therefore

$$
m+n=64+7=\boxed{71}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Area of a diamond
- Annulus area
- Geometric probability

## Key idea

Recognize the target as an annulus and verify it fits entirely inside the diamond.

## Common mistakes

- Using $25\pm7$ as radii instead of squared radii.
- Forgetting that the board area is $128$.
