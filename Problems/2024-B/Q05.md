# AMC12 2024-B Q05

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q05.png]]

## Problem

In

$$
1+3+5+\cdots+97+99,
$$

some plus signs are changed to minus signs. The resulting expression is negative. What is the least number of plus signs that can be changed?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $15$**

## Solution

The original sum of the first $50$ odd numbers is

$$
50^2=2500.
$$

Changing the sign of an odd number $v$ changes the total by $-2v$. To make the result negative, the changed terms must have sum greater than

$$
\frac{2500}{2}=1250.
$$

To use as few terms as possible, choose the largest odd numbers. The largest $k$ odd numbers are

$$
99,97,\ldots,101-2k,
$$

whose sum is

$$
k(100-k).
$$

For $k=14$ this is $14\cdot86=1204$, not enough. For $k=15$ it is

$$
15\cdot85=1275>1250.
$$

Therefore the minimum number is $\boxed{15}$.

## Knowledge points

- Arithmetic sequences
- Greedy optimization
- Sign changes

## Key idea

For a fixed number of sign changes, flipping the largest terms gives the greatest possible decrease.

## Common mistakes

- Requiring the flipped sum to exceed $2500$ instead of $1250$.
- Choosing small terms first.
