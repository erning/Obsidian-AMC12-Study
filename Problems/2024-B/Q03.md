# AMC12 2024-B Q03

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q03.png]]

## Problem

For how many integer values of $x$ is

$$
|2x|\le7\pi?
$$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $21$**

## Solution

Divide by $2$:

$$
|x|\le\frac{7\pi}{2}.
$$

Using $\pi\approx3.14$,

$$
\frac{7\pi}{2}\approx10.996,
$$

so the integer values are

$$
-10,-9,\ldots,0,\ldots,9,10.
$$

There are

$$
10+10+1=\boxed{21}
$$

values.

## Knowledge points

- Absolute-value inequalities
- Counting consecutive integers

## Key idea

Estimate the boundary accurately enough to identify the largest integer.

## Common mistakes

- Including $\pm11$.
- Forgetting the factor $2$.
