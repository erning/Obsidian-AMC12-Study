# AMC12 2024-B Q23

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q23.png]]

## Problem

A right pyramid has a regular octagon $ABCDEFGH$ of side length $1$ as its base and apex $V$. Segments $AV$ and $DV$ are perpendicular. What is the square of the pyramid's height?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $\frac{1+\sqrt2}{2}$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q23-draft.svg]]

Let $R$ be the circumradius of the octagon and $h$ the height. Every lateral edge has length $L$, so

$$
L^2=h^2+R^2.
$$

Since $AV\perp DV$, triangle $AVD$ is right at $V$:

$$
AD^2=AV^2+DV^2=2L^2.
$$

Vertices $A$ and $D$ are three steps apart, so their central angle is $135^\circ$. Hence

$$
AD^2=2R^2(1-\cos135^\circ)=R^2(2+\sqrt2).
$$

Equating the two expressions:

$$
R^2(2+\sqrt2)=2(h^2+R^2),
$$

so

$$
h^2=\frac{\sqrt2}{2}R^2.
$$

For a regular octagon of side $1$,

$$
R=\frac1{2\sin22.5^\circ},
\qquad
R^2=\frac{2+\sqrt2}{2}.
$$

Therefore

$$
h^2=\frac{\sqrt2}{2}\cdot\frac{2+\sqrt2}{2}
=\boxed{\frac{1+\sqrt2}{2}}.
$$

## Key idea

Relate the perpendicular lateral edges to a chord of the circumcircle.

## Common mistakes

- Using the central angle $45^\circ$ instead of $135^\circ$.
- Confusing circumradius and apothem.
