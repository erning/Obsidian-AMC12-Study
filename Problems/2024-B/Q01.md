# AMC12 2024-B Q01

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q01.png]]

## Problem

In a line of people arranged left to right, the $1013$th person from the left is also the $1010$th person from the right. How many people are in the line?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $2022$**

## Solution

The person has $1013-1=1012$ people to their left. From the right, they have

$$
1010-1=1009
$$

people to their right. Including the person themself, the total is

$$
1012+1+1009=\boxed{2022}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Counting positions from opposite directions

## Key idea

Add the people to the left, the person, and the people to the right.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting to count the identified person once.
- Adding $1013$ and $1010$ directly.
