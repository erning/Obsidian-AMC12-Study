# AMC12 2024-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

How many different remainders can result when the $100$th power of an integer is divided by $125$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $2$**

## Solution

If an integer $n$ is relatively prime to $5$, then it is relatively prime to $125$. Since

$$
\varphi(125)=100,
$$

Euler's theorem gives

$$
n^{100}\equiv1\pmod{125}.
$$

If $5\mid n$, then $n^{100}$ is divisible by $5^{100}$ and hence by $125$, giving remainder $0$.

Thus the only possible remainders are $0$ and $1$, so there are

$$
\boxed2
$$

different remainders.

## Knowledge points

- Euler's theorem
- Prime-power divisibility
- Modular arithmetic

## Key idea

Separate integers into those divisible by $5$ and those relatively prime to $5$.

## Common mistakes

- Applying Euler's theorem to multiples of $5$.
- Counting all $125$ residue classes as possible powers.
