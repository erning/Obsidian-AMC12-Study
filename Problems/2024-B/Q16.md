# AMC12 2024-B Q16

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q16.png]]

## Problem

Sixteen people are partitioned into indistinguishable committees of four. Each committee has one chairperson and one secretary. The number of assignments can be written as $3^rM$, where $3\nmid M$. What is $r$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $5$**

## Solution

The number of partitions into four indistinguishable groups of four is

$$
\frac{16!}{(4!)^4\,4!}.
$$

Each committee chooses a chair and secretary in

$$
4\cdot3=12
$$

ways, so the total count is

$$
\frac{16!}{(4!)^4\,4!}\cdot12^4.
$$

Count factors of $3$:

$$
v_3(16!)=\left\lfloor\frac{16}{3}\right\rfloor+
\left\lfloor\frac{16}{9}\right\rfloor=5+1=6.
$$

The denominator contributes $4+1=5$, because $v_3(4!)=1$. Finally, $12^4$ contributes $4$. Therefore

$$
r=6-5+4=\boxed5.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Multinomial counting
- Indistinguishable groups
- Legendre's formula

## Key idea

Count only the exponent of $3$, not the entire enormous number.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting the extra $4!$ for indistinguishable committees.
- Counting the secretary and chair as the same role.
