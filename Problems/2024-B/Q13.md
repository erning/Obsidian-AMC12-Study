# AMC12 2024-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Real numbers $x,y,h,k$ satisfy

$$
x^2+y^2-6x-8y=h,
$$

$$
x^2+y^2-10x+4y=k.
$$

What is the minimum possible value of $h+k$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $-34$**

## Solution

Add the equations:

$$
h+k=2x^2+2y^2-16x-4y.
$$

Complete the square:

$$
\begin{aligned}
h+k
&=2(x-4)^2+2(y-1)^2-34.
\end{aligned}
$$

The squared terms are nonnegative, so the minimum occurs at $x=4$, $y=1$, and equals

$$
\boxed{-34}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Completing the square
- Quadratic optimization

## Key idea

Rewrite the sum as nonnegative squares plus a constant.

## Common mistakes

- Minimizing $h$ and $k$ separately.
- Losing the factor $2$ when completing squares.
