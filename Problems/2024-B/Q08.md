# AMC12 2024-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

What value of $x$ satisfies

$$
\frac{\log_2x\cdot\log_3x}{\log_2x+\log_3x}=2?
$$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $36$**

## Solution

Let $L=\log x$ with any fixed base. By change of base,

$$
\log_2x=\frac{L}{\log2},
\qquad
\log_3x=\frac{L}{\log3}.
$$

The left side becomes

$$
\frac{1}{\log2+\log3}\,L
=\frac{\log x}{\log6}
=\log_6x.
$$

Thus

$$
\log_6x=2,
\qquad
x=6^2=\boxed{36}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Change of base
- Logarithm identities

## Key idea

The reciprocal-looking expression simplifies to $\log_6x$.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $\log_2x+\log_3x$ as $\log_5x$.
- Forgetting to use a common logarithm base.
