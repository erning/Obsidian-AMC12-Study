# AMC12 2024-B Q19

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q19.png]]

## Problem

An equilateral triangle $ABC$ has side length $14$. It is rotated about its center by $0<\theta<60^\circ$ to form $\triangle DEF$. The area of hexagon $ADBECF$ is $91\sqrt3$. What is $\tan\theta$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $\frac{5\sqrt3}{11}$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q19-draft.svg]]

The circumradius of the original triangle is

$$
R=\frac{14}{\sqrt3},
\qquad
R^2=\frac{196}{3}.
$$

Around the common circumcircle, the six consecutive central angles alternate between $\theta$ and $120^\circ-\theta$. Thus the hexagon area is

$$
\frac12R^2\left(3\sin\theta+3\sin(120^\circ-\theta)\right)
=98\left(\sin\theta+\sin(120^\circ-\theta)\right).
$$

Using sum-to-product,

$$
\sin\theta+\sin(120^\circ-\theta)
=\sqrt3\cos(60^\circ-\theta).
$$

The given area yields

$$
98\sqrt3\cos(60^\circ-\theta)=91\sqrt3,
$$

so

$$
\cos(60^\circ-\theta)=\frac{13}{14},
\qquad
\sin(60^\circ-\theta)=\frac{3\sqrt3}{14}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\tan(60^\circ-\theta)=\frac{3\sqrt3}{13}.
$$

Subtract this angle from $60^\circ$:

$$
\tan\theta
=\frac{\sqrt3-\frac{3\sqrt3}{13}}
{1+\sqrt3\cdot\frac{3\sqrt3}{13}}
=\boxed{\frac{5\sqrt3}{11}}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Rotations about a center
- Cyclic polygon area
- Sum-to-product identities

## Key idea

Compute the hexagon as a sum of six isosceles triangle sectors.

## Common mistakes

- Using the inradius instead of the circumradius.
- Treating all six central angles as equal.
