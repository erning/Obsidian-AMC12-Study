# AMC12 2024-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

A triangle has vertices

$$
A=(\log_2 1,\log_2 2),\quad
B=(\log_2 3,\log_2 4),\quad
C=(\log_2 7,\log_2 8).
$$

What is the area of $\triangle ABC$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $\log_2\left(\frac{3}{\sqrt7}\right)$**

## Solution

Since $\log_2 1=0$, $\log_2 2=1$, $\log_2 4=2$, and $\log_2 8=3$, write

$$
A=(0,1),\quad B=(\log_2 3,2),\quad C=(\log_2 7,3).
$$

The shoelace formula gives

$$
\begin{aligned}
[ABC]
&=\frac12\left|0(2-3)+(\log_2 3)(3-1)+(\log_2 7)(1-2)\right|\\
&=\frac12\left|2\log_2 3-\log_2 7\right|\\
&=\frac12\log_2\left(\frac9{7}\right)\\
&=\log_2\left(\frac3{\sqrt7}\right).
\end{aligned}
$$

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm simplification
- Shoelace formula
- Coordinate geometry

## Key idea

Two coordinates simplify immediately, leaving a two-logarithm area expression.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting the factor $\frac12$.
- Combining $2\log_2 3$ incorrectly.
