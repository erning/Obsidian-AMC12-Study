# AMC12 2024-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Rectangle $WXYZ$ has $WX=4$ and $WZ=8$. Point $M$ lies on $XY$, point $A$ lies on $YZ$, and $\angle WMA=90^\circ$. The areas of $\triangle WXM$ and $\triangle WAZ$ are equal. What is the area of $\triangle WMA$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $15$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2024-B/Q07-draft.svg]]

Use coordinates

$$
X=(0,0),\quad W=(0,4),\quad Y=(8,0),\quad Z=(8,4),
$$

and write

$$
M=(m,0),\qquad A=(8,a).
$$

The perpendicular condition gives

$$
\overrightarrow{MW}\cdot\overrightarrow{MA}
=(-m,4)\cdot(8-m,a)=0,
$$

so

$$
4a=m(8-m).
$$

Equal areas give

$$
\frac12\cdot4m=\frac12\cdot8(4-a),
$$

which simplifies to

$$
m=8-2a.
$$

Substitute $a=(8-m)/2$ into the perpendicular equation:

$$
2(8-m)=m(8-m).
$$

The nondegenerate solution is $m=2$, $a=3$. Finally,

$$
[WMA]
=\frac12\left|2(3-4)+8(4-0)\right|
=\boxed{15}.
$$

## Key idea

Translate perpendicularity into a dot-product equation and area equality into a linear equation.

## Common mistakes

- Using the wrong direction vector for $\overrightarrow{MW}$.
- Choosing the degenerate solution $m=8$.
