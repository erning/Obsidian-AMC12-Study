# AMC12 2012-A Q22

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q22.png]]

## Problem

Distinct planes $p_1,p_2,\ldots,p_k$ intersect the interior of a cube $Q$. Let $S$ be the union of the faces of $Q$ and $P=\bigcup_{j=1}^kp_j$. The intersection $P\cap S$ consists of all segments joining the midpoints of every pair of edges belonging to the same face of $Q$. What is the difference between the maximum and minimum possible values of $k$?

## Answer

**C.** $20$

## Solution

A plane cutting the cube can produce one of four types of sections: a square through midpoints, a rectangle associated with an edge, a triangle associated with a vertex, or a regular hexagon associated with a pair of opposite vertices. The numbers of these planes are respectively
$$3,\qquad12,\qquad8,\qquad4.$$
Using every type gives the maximum
$$k_{\max}=3+12+8+4=27.$$

The complete midpoint-segment figure contains $24$ short segments and $12$ long segments. The four hexagon planes cover all $24$ short segments, while the three square planes cover all $12$ long segments. Hence
$$k_{\min}=4+3=7.$$
The requested difference is
$$27-7=20.$$

## Knowledge points

- Cube cross-sections
- Three-dimensional casework
- Extremal counting

## Key idea

Classify planes by the polygonal cross-section they produce, then cover the required segments as efficiently as possible.

## Common mistakes

- Counting faces rather than planes
- Assuming the maximum and minimum use the same plane types
