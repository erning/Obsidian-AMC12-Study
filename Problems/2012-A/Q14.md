# AMC12 2012-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

The closed curve in the figure is made of $9$ congruent circular arcs, each of length $\frac{2\pi}{3}$. The centers of the corresponding circles are vertices of a regular hexagon of side $2$. What is the enclosed area?

## Answer

**E.** $\pi+6\sqrt3$

## Solution

Each arc has length $\frac{2\pi}{3}$ on a unit circle, so it is a $120^\circ$ sector. Rearranging the nine congruent sectors shows that the enclosed region has the same area as a regular hexagon of side $2$ together with one unit circle.

The hexagon consists of six equilateral triangles of side $2$, so its area is
$$6\cdot\frac{\sqrt3}{4}\cdot2^2=6\sqrt3.$$
Adding the unit circle gives
$$\pi+6\sqrt3.$$

## Knowledge points

- Circular sectors
- Area decomposition
- Regular hexagons

## Key idea

Reassemble the congruent sectors instead of calculating the complicated boundary directly.

## Common mistakes

- Using the arc length as the radius
- Counting only part of the regular hexagon
