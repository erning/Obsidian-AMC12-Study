# AMC12 2012-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

A fruit salad has $280$ pieces of fruit: blueberries, raspberries, grapes, and cherries. There are twice as many raspberries as blueberries, three times as many grapes as cherries, and four times as many cherries as raspberries. How many cherries are there?

## Answer

**D.** $64$

## Solution

Let the number of blueberries be $b$. Then the numbers of raspberries, cherries, and grapes are respectively
$$2b,\qquad4(2b)=8b,\qquad3(8b)=24b.$$
The total is
$$b+2b+8b+24b=35b=280,$$
so $b=8$. The number of cherries is
$$8b=64.$$

## Knowledge points

- Ratios
- Systems of linear equations

## Key idea

Express every fruit count as a multiple of the blueberry count.

## Common mistakes

- Reversing the “three times as many grapes as cherries” relation
- Forgetting that cherries are four times the raspberries
