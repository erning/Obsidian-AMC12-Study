# AMC12 2012-A Q12

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q12.png]]

## Problem

A square region $ABCD$ is externally tangent to the circle $x^2+y^2=1$ at $(0,1)$ on side $CD$. Vertices $A$ and $B$ lie on the circle $x^2+y^2=4$. What is the side length of the square?

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{2\sqrt{19}-4}{5}$

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q12-draft.svg]]

By symmetry write
$$A=(a,b),qquad B=(-a,b),qquad a>0.$$
The square touches the unit circle at $(0,1)$, so its width is $2a$ and its height is $b-1$. Since it is a square,
$$b-1=2a,qquad b=2a+1.$$

Because $A$ lies on $x^2+y^2=4$,
$$a^2+(2a+1)^2=4,$$
so
$$5a^2+4a-3=0.$$
The positive root is
$$a=\frac{\sqrt{19}-2}{5}.$$
The side length is $2a$, namely
$$\frac{2\sqrt{19}-4}{5}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Coordinate geometry
- Symmetry
- Quadratic equations

## Key idea

Use symmetry to reduce the two vertex coordinates to one parameter.

## Common mistakes

- Using $b+1$ instead of the square height $b-1$
- Choosing the negative quadratic root
