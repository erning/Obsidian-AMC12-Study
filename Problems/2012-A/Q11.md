# AMC12 2012-A Q11

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q11.png]]

## Problem

Alex, Mel, and Chelsea play a six-round game. Each round has one winner and the rounds are independent. Alex wins a round with probability $\frac12$, and Mel is twice as likely to win as Chelsea. What is the probability that Alex wins three rounds, Mel wins two, and Chelsea wins one?

## Answer

**B.** $\frac5{36}$

## Solution

The probabilities for Mel and Chelsea sum to $\frac12$, and Mel: Chelsea is $2:1$. Hence
$$P(M)=\frac13,qquad P(C)=\frac16.$$

The number of orders of the outcomes $AAAMMC$ is
$$\frac{6!}{3!2!1!}=60.$$
Thus the probability is
$$
60\left(\frac12\right)^3\left(\frac13\right)^2\left(\frac16\right)
=\frac5{36}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Multinomial probability
- Independent events
- Counting permutations

## Key idea

Multiply the probability of one fixed order by the number of distinct orders.

## Common mistakes

- Using $6!$ without dividing by repeated outcomes
- Assigning Mel probability $\frac23$
