# AMC12 2012-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Let $S$ be the square whose diagonal endpoints are $(0.1,0.7)$ and $(-0.1,-0.7)$. A point $v=(x,y)$ is chosen uniformly from $0\le x,y\le2012$. Let $T(v)$ be a translate of $S$ centered at $v$. What is the probability that $T(v)$ contains exactly two points with integer coordinates in its interior?

## Answer

**C.** $0.16$

## Solution

The diagonal has length
$$\sqrt{0.2^2+1.4^2}=\sqrt2,$$
so $S$ has area $1$. A translate centered at $v$ contains a given lattice point exactly when $v$ lies in the congruent copy of $S$ centered at that lattice point.

By periodicity, it is enough to work in one unit cell. Exactly two interior lattice points occur when the center lies in the overlap of two neighboring copies. The overlap area for a horizontal or vertical neighbor is $0.08$. There are two adjacency directions, and the relevant overlaps do not overlap in the two-point count, so the total favorable area per unit cell is
$$2\cdot0.08=0.16.$$
Thus the probability is $0.16$.

## Knowledge points

- Geometric probability
- Lattice translations
- Area of overlaps

## Key idea

Replace the random translated square by the locus of its center and use unit-cell periodicity.

## Common mistakes

- Using the square's area as the probability
- Counting diagonal lattice-point pairs that cannot occur
