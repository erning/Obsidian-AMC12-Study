# AMC12 2012-A Q17

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Let $S$ be a subset of $\{1,2,3,\ldots,30\}$ such that no pair of distinct elements in $S$ has a sum divisible by $5$. What is the largest possible size of $S$?

## Answer

**B.** $13$

## Solution

Each residue class modulo $5$ contains six numbers. A forbidden sum occurs for residue pairs
$$0+0,\qquad1+4,\qquad2+3.$$
Thus we can take at most one number from residue $0$, one of the two classes $1$ and $4$, and one of the two classes $2$ and $3$. The upper bound is
$$1+6+6=13.$$

It is attainable, for example, with all numbers congruent to $1$ or $2$ modulo $5$, together with $30$. Therefore the maximum is $13$.

## Knowledge points

- Modular arithmetic
- Extremal counting
- Residue classes

## Key idea

Pair residue classes whose sums are $0$ modulo $5$.

## Common mistakes

- Allowing two elements from residue $0$
- Choosing both residues $1$ and $4$
