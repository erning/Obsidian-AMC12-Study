# AMC12 2012-A Q25

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q25.png]]

## Problem

Let $f(x)=|2\{x\}-1|$, where $\{x\}$ is the fractional part of $x$. The number $n$ is the smallest positive integer such that
$$nf(xf(x))=x$$
has at least $2012$ real solutions. What is $n$? Here the fractional part of $x$ is the real number $y=\{x\}$ satisfying $0\le y<1$ and $x-y$ is an integer.

## Answer

**C.** $32$

## Solution

Since $0\le f(x)\le1$, every solution lies in $[0,n]$. The function $f$ is a triangular wave of period $1$. On each half-unit interval $[a,a+\frac12)$ and $[a+\frac12,a+1)$, the composition $f(xf(x))$ meets the line $x/n$ respectively $2a$ and $2(a+1)$ times.

Summing over $a=0,1,\ldots,n-1$ gives
$$
\sum_{a=0}^{n-1}\bigl(2a+2(a+1)\bigr)=2n^2
$$
solutions. We need $2n^2\ge2012$. Since
$$2\cdot31^2=1922<2012<2048=2\cdot32^2,$$
the smallest such integer is
$$n=32.$$

## Knowledge points

- Fractional-part functions
- Periodic piecewise-linear functions
- Counting intersections

## Key idea

Count the intersections on each period rather than trying to solve the equation explicitly.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming $f(x)$ is monotone over an entire unit interval
- Forgetting the two half-interval contributions
