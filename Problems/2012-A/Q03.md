# AMC12 2012-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

A box $2$ centimeters high, $3$ centimeters wide, and $5$ centimeters long holds $40$ grams of clay. A second box has twice the height, three times the width, and the same length. How many grams of clay can it hold?

## Answer

**D.** $240$

## Solution

The volume scale factor is
$$2\cdot3\cdot1=6.$$
Assuming the clay density is unchanged, the mass scales by the same factor. Thus the second box holds
$$6\cdot40=240$$
grams.

## Knowledge points

- Volume scaling
- Ratios and proportions

## Key idea

Multiply the independent scale factors in the three dimensions.

## Common mistakes

- Adding the scale factors instead of multiplying them
- Scaling the mass by only the height factor
