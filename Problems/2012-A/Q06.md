# AMC12 2012-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

The sums of three whole numbers taken in pairs are $12$, $17$, and $19$. What is the middle number?

## Answer

**D.** $7$

## Solution

Let $a<b<c$. Adding the three pairwise sums gives
$$2(a+b+c)=12+17+19=48,$$
so $a+b+c=24$.

The largest pairwise sum is $b+c=19$, the next is $a+c=17$, and the smallest is $a+b=12$. Therefore
$$a=24-19=5,\qquad b=24-17=7,\qquad c=24-12=12.$$
The middle number is $7$.

## Knowledge points

- Systems of equations
- Symmetric sums
- Ordering

## Key idea

Subtract each pairwise sum from the total $a+b+c$.

## Common mistakes

- Matching the pair sums to the wrong variables
- Forgetting that the numbers are ordered
