# AMC12 2012-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Mary divides a circle into $12$ sectors. The central angles are integers and form an arithmetic sequence. What is the smallest possible sector angle?

## Answer

**C.** $8^\circ$

## Solution

Let the smallest angle be $a$ and the common difference be $d\ge0$. The sum of the $12$ angles is
$$12a+\frac{12\cdot11}{2}d=360,$$
so
$$2a+11d=60.$$

Since $2a$ is even, $11d$ is even, so $d$ must be even. To make $a$ small, maximize $d$. The largest possible even $d$ with positive $a$ is $4$, giving
$$2a=60-44=16,qquad a=8.$$

## Knowledge points

- Arithmetic sequences
- Angle sums
- Integer optimization

## Key idea

Use the total angle sum and the parity condition to maximize the common difference.

## Common mistakes

- Using $11a$ instead of $12a$
- Allowing a zero or negative sector angle
