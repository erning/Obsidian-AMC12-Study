# AMC12 2012-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

Circle $C_1$ has center $O$ lying on circle $C_2$. The two circles meet at $X$ and $Y$. Point $Z$ outside $C_1$ lies on $C_2$ and satisfies $XZ=13$, $OZ=11$, and $YZ=7$. What is the radius of $C_1$?

## Answer

**E.** $\sqrt{30}$

## Solution

Let the radius of $C_1$ be $r$. Since $OX=OY=r$, the equal chords $OX$ and $OY$ of $C_2$ subtend equal angles at $Z$:
$$\angle XZO=\angle OZY.$$

Apply the Law of Cosines in triangles $XZO$ and $YZO$:
$$
\frac{13^2+11^2-r^2}{2\cdot13\cdot11}
=\frac{7^2+11^2-r^2}{2\cdot7\cdot11}.
$$
Clearing denominators gives $r^2=30$, so
$$r=\sqrt{30}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Equal chords
- Law of cosines
- Circle geometry

## Key idea

Equal chords in the second circle turn the unknown radius into an equality of cosines.

## Common mistakes

- Using $OX=OY$ as an angle equality without referencing circle $C_2$
- Choosing $r^2=-30$ after rearrangement
