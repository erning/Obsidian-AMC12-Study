# AMC12 2012-A Q18

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Triangle $ABC$ has side lengths $AB=27$, $AC=26$, and $BC=25$. Let $I$ be the intersection of the internal angle bisectors. What is $BI$?

## Answer

**A.** $15$

## Solution

Let $D$ be the foot of the perpendicular from $I$ to $BC$. The semiperimeter is
$$s=\frac{25+26+27}{2}=39.$$
The tangent length from $B$ is
$$BD=s-AC=39-26=13.$$

Using Heron's formula, the inradius satisfies
$$r^2=\frac{(s-25)(s-26)(s-27)}s
=\frac{14\cdot13\cdot12}{39}=56.$$
In right triangle $BDI$,
$$BI^2=BD^2+r^2=13^2+56=225,$$
so
$$BI=15.$$

## Knowledge points

- Incenter and inradius
- Heron's formula
- Pythagorean theorem

## Key idea

Use the tangent length from a vertex and the inradius as the legs of a right triangle.

## Common mistakes

- Using the wrong side in $s-AC$
- Taking $BI=r+BD$ instead of a Pythagorean sum
