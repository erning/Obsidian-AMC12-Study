# AMC12 2012-A Q09

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q09.png]]

## Problem

A year is a leap year exactly when it is divisible by $400$, or divisible by $4$ but not by $100$. February $7$, $2012$ was a Tuesday and marked the $200$th anniversary of Charles Dickens's birth. On what day of the week was Dickens born?

## Answer

**A.** Friday

## Solution

The $200$ years contain $200\cdot365=73000$ ordinary days. Among the years from $1812$ through $2011$, there would be $200/4=50$ leap years, except that $1900$ was not a leap year. Thus there were $49$ leap days, for a total of
$$73000+49=73049$$
days.

Since
$$73049=7\cdot10435+4,$$
February $7$, $1812$ was four days before Tuesday, namely Friday.

## Knowledge points

- Leap-year rules
- Modular arithmetic
- Calendar counting

## Key idea

Reduce the elapsed number of days modulo $7$, taking the exceptional century year into account.

## Common mistakes

- Counting $1900$ as a leap year
- Moving forward instead of backward from Tuesday
