# AMC12 2012-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Let $a$, $b$, and $c$ be positive integers with $a\ge b\ge c$ such that
$$a^2-b^2-c^2+ab=2011$$
and
$$a^2+3b^2+3c^2-3ab-2ac-2bc=-1997.$$
What is $a$?

## Answer

**E.** $253$

## Solution

Adding the equations gives
$$
(a-b)^2+(b-c)^2+(c-a)^2=14.
$$
The only way to express $14$ as a sum of three squares is $9+4+1$. Since $a\ge b\ge c$, we have $a-c=3$, with either
$$a-b=2, b-c=1$$
or
$$a-b=1, b-c=2.$$

The first case gives $(a,b,c)=(c+3,c+1,c)$. Substituting into the first equation yields
$$3(2c+3)+2(c+1)=2011,$$
so $c=250$ and
$$a=253.$$
The other ordering does not give an integer solution.

## Knowledge points

- Algebraic identities
- Diophantine equations
- Sum of squares

## Key idea

Add the equations to reveal a small sum of squared differences.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting the ordering $a\ge b\ge c$
- Assuming every permutation of $9,4,1$ is valid
