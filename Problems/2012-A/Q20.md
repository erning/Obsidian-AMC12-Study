# AMC12 2012-A Q20

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Consider
$$P(x)=\prod_{k=0}^{10}(x^{2^k}+2^k).$$
The coefficient of $x^{2012}$ is $2^a$. What is $a$?

## Answer

**B.** $6$

## Solution

To obtain degree $2012$, choose the $x^{2^k}$ term from factors whose exponents sum to $2012$. The binary representation is
$$2012=11111011100_2,$$
so the choice is unique.

The remaining factors contribute their constant terms. The omitted powers are $2^0$, $2^1$, and $2^5$, so the coefficient is
$$2^0\cdot2^1\cdot2^5=2^6.$$
Thus $a=6$.

## Knowledge points

- Binary representation
- Polynomial products
- Powers of two

## Key idea

Use uniqueness of binary representation to identify the single degree-$2012$ term.

## Common mistakes

- Choosing constants from the factors used for the degree
- Treating the binary representation as nonunique
