# AMC12 2012-A Q13

![[assets/problems/2012-A/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Paula the painter and her two helpers paint at constant, different rates. They start at 8:00 AM and take the same length lunch break each day. Monday all three paint $50\%$ of a house and quit at 4:00 PM. Tuesday the helpers paint $24\%$ and quit at 2:12 PM. Wednesday Paula works alone and finishes at 7:12 PM. How long, in minutes, is each lunch break?

## Answer

**D.** $48$

## Solution

Let the lunch break be $m$ minutes. The working times are $480-m$, $372-m$, and $672-m$ minutes. If Paula's rate is $p$ percent per minute and the helpers' combined rate is $h$, then
$$
(p+h)(480-m)=50,
$$
$$h(372-m)=24,qquad p(672-m)=26.
$$
Adding the last two equations and subtracting the first gives
$$108h-192p=0,qquad h=\frac{16}{9}p.$$
Substitution into either remaining equation gives
$$p=\frac1{24},qquad m=48.$$

## Knowledge points

- Work rates
- Systems of equations
- Time conversion

## Key idea

Translate each day's schedule into a rate-times-time equation, then eliminate the lunch length.

## Common mistakes

- Using $4:00$ PM as $400$ minutes after 8:00 AM
- Treating the two helpers as having Paula's rate
