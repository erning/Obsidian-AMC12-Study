# AMC12 2025-A Q11

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q11.png]]

## Problem

What is the sum of the coordinates of the orthocenter of the triangle with vertices $A(2,31)$, $B(8,27)$, and $C(18,27)$?

**Choices:** (A) $5$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) $17$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) $10+4\sqrt{17}+2\sqrt{13}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) $\frac{113}{3}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) $54$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) 5**

## Solution

Since $B$ and $C$ have the same $y$-coordinate, $BC$ is horizontal. Therefore the altitude from $A$ is vertical:

$$
x=2.
$$

The slope of $AC$ is

$$
\frac{27-31}{18-2}=-\frac14.
$$

Thus the altitude through $B$ has slope $4$, with equation

$$
y-27=4(x-8).
$$

At $x=2$,

$$
y-27=4(2-8)=-24,
\qquad y=3.
$$

The orthocenter is $(2,3)$, so the requested sum is

$$
2+3=\boxed{5}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Slope of a line
- Perpendicular slopes
- Orthocenter as the intersection of altitudes

## Key idea

Use the horizontal side to get one altitude immediately, then intersect it with a second altitude.

## Common mistakes

- Using the slope of $AC$ instead of the perpendicular slope $4$.
- Finding the orthocenter but forgetting that the question asks for the coordinate sum.
- Using a median or angle bisector instead of an altitude.
