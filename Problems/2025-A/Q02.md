# AMC12 2025-A Q02

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q02.png]]

## Problem

A box contains 10 pounds of a nut mix that is 50% peanuts, 20% cashews, and 30% almonds. A second nut mix containing 20% peanuts, 40% cashews, and 40% almonds is added to the box, resulting in a new nut mix that is 40% peanuts. How many pounds of cashews are now in the box?

**Choices:** (A) 3.5 &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) 4 &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) 4.5 &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) 5 &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) 6

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) 4**

## Solution

Let $x$ be the number of pounds of the second nut mix.

The original 10-pound mix contains

- $10(0.50)=5$ pounds of peanuts;
- $10(0.20)=2$ pounds of cashews.

The added mix contains $0.20x$ pounds of peanuts. The total amount of peanuts is therefore $5+0.20x$, and the total mass is $10+x$. Since the final mixture is 40% peanuts,

$$
\frac{5+0.20x}{10+x}=0.40.
$$

Solving,

$$
5+0.20x=4+0.40x
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
x=5.
$$

The second mix contributes $0.40(5)=2$ pounds of cashews. Thus the total amount of cashews is

$$
2+2=\boxed{4}.
$$

## Key idea

Use the percentage that determines how much of the second mixture was added. Once $x$ is known, calculate the requested ingredient separately.

## Common mistakes

- Using 40% as the amount of peanuts instead of the final fraction.
- Forgetting that the total mass after mixing is $10+x$.
- Calculating only the cashews from the second mixture and omitting the original 2 pounds.
