# AMC12 2025-A Q09

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q09.png]]

## Problem

Let $w=2+i$, where $i=\sqrt{-1}$. What real number $r$ has the property that $r,w,w^2$ are three collinear points in the complex plane?

**Choices:** (A) $\frac34$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) $1$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) $\frac75$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) $\frac32$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) $\frac53$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $\frac53$**

## Solution

Compute

$$
w^2=(2+i)^2=3+4i.
$$

Thus $w=(2,1)$ and $w^2=(3,4)$. The slope of the line through them is

$$
\frac{4-1}{3-2}=3.
$$

The line through $(2,1)$ is therefore

$$
y-1=3(x-2),
$$

or $y=3x-5$. Since $r$ is real, its point is $(r,0)$. Substituting $y=0$ gives

$$
0=3r-5
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
r=\boxed{\frac53}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Complex numbers as points in the plane
- Complex multiplication
- Collinearity through equal slopes

## Key idea

Convert $w$ and $w^2$ to coordinates, find their line, and use the fact that a real number lies on the real axis.

## Common mistakes

- Computing $w^2$ as $4+i^2=3$ and omitting the $4i$ term.
- Using $r$ as the point $(0,r)$; a real complex number is $(r,0)$.
- Finding the slope of the line but forgetting to set $y=0$.
