# AMC12 2025-A Q22

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q22.png]]

## Problem

The rendered problem image contains two consecutive statements. The official question, matching the answer choices, is:

Three real numbers are chosen independently and uniformly at random from $[0,1]$. What is the probability that the greatest of the three numbers is greater than 2 times each of the other two? Equivalently, if $a\ge b\ge c$, then $a>2b$.

**Choices:** (A) $\frac1{12}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) $\frac19$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) $\frac18$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) $\frac16$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) $\frac14$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $\frac14$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q22-draft.svg]]

Order the values as $a\ge b\ge c$. By symmetry, it is enough to calculate the volume in this ordered region and multiply by $6$.

For fixed $b$, $c$ ranges from $0$ to $b$, while $a$ ranges from $2b$ to $1$. Thus $0\le b\le\frac12$, and

$$
\begin{aligned}
\Pr(a>2b)
&=6\int_0^{1/2}\int_0^b\int_{2b}^1 da\,dc\,db\\
&=6\int_0^{1/2}b(1-2b)\,db\\
&=6\left(\frac18-\frac1{12}\right)
=\boxed{\frac14}.
\end{aligned}
$$

The image also contains an extra sentence asking whether the greatest number is greater than the sum of the other two. That is a separate condition and is not consistent with the printed choices; the official AMC question and answer are for $a>2b$.

## Knowledge points

- Symmetry in continuous probability
- Volume as probability
- Setting up a triple integral
- Ordering random variables

## Key idea

Sort the values, describe one ordered region, and multiply by $3!$. The inequality $a>b+c$ automatically forces $b<1/2$.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting the factor $6$ from the six possible orderings.
- Integrating over $b\ge1/2$, where the event is impossible.
- Missing the factor $6$ from the six possible orderings.
