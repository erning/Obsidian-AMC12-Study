# AMC12 2025-A Q17

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q17.png]]

## Problem

The polynomial
$$
(z+i)(z+2i)(z+3i)+10
$$
has three roots in the complex plane. What is the area of the triangle formed by these roots?

**Choices:** (A) 6 &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) 8 &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) 10 &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) 12 &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) 14

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) 6**

## Solution

Translate the variable by setting

$$
z=u-2i.
$$

Then

$$
z+i=u-i,\qquad z+2i=u,\qquad z+3i=u+i,
$$

so the equation becomes

$$
(u-i)u(u+i)+10=u(u^2+1)+10=u^3+u+10=0.
$$

Since $u=-2$ is a root,

$$
u^3+u+10=(u+2)(u^2-2u+5).
$$

The other roots are

$$
u=1\pm2i.
$$

Thus, in the $u$-plane the roots are $(-2,0)$, $(1,2)$, and $(1,-2)$. The vertical base has length $4$, and its distance from $(-2,0)$ is $3$. The area is

$$
\frac12(4)(3)=\boxed{6}.
$$

Translation by $-2i$ does not change area.

## Knowledge points

- Translating complex variables
- Factoring polynomials
- Complex roots as Cartesian points
- Triangle area

## Key idea

Choose a translation that centers the three factors at $u-i,u,u+i$, making their product $u(u^2+1)$.

## Common mistakes

- Expanding the original cubic directly and missing the useful translation.
- Forgetting that translating every point preserves distances and area.
- Treating $1\pm2i$ as real numbers instead of points $(1,\pm2)$.
