# AMC12 2025-A Q08

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q08.png]]

## Problem

Pentagon $ABCDE$ is inscribed in a circle, and $\angle BEC=\angle CED=30^\circ$. Let $AC$ and $BD$ intersect at $F$, and suppose that $AB=9$ and $AD=24$. What is $BF$?

**Choices:** (A) $\frac{57}{11}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) $\frac{59}{11}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) $\frac{60}{11}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) $\frac{61}{11}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) $\frac{63}{11}$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $\frac{63}{11}$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q08-draft.svg]]

Because $\angle BEC=30^\circ$, the intercepted arc $BC$ has measure $60^\circ$. Therefore the inscribed angle subtending the same arc is

$$
\angle BAC=30^\circ.
$$

Similarly, $\angle CED=30^\circ$ implies

$$
\angle CAD=30^\circ.
$$

Thus $AC$ bisects $\angle BAD$, and

$$
\angle BAD=60^\circ.
$$

Apply the Law of Cosines in $\triangle ABD$:

$$
BD^2=AB^2+AD^2-2(AB)(AD)\cos60^\circ
$$

$$
=9^2+24^2-2(9)(24)\left(\frac12\right)
=81+576-216=441.
$$

Hence $BD=21$.

Since $F$ lies on the angle bisector from $A$, the Angle Bisector Theorem gives

$$
\frac{BF}{FD}=\frac{AB}{AD}=\frac9{24}=\frac38.
$$

Because $BF+FD=BD=21$,

$$
BF=\frac{3}{3+8}\cdot21=\boxed{\frac{63}{11}}.
$$

## Key idea

The two $30^\circ$ inscribed angles show that $AC$ bisects $\angle BAD$. Then find $BD$ and divide it in the $3:8$ ratio.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting that an inscribed angle is half its intercepted arc.
- Applying the Angle Bisector Theorem to the wrong side ratio.
- Reporting $FD$ instead of $BF$.
- Inverting $\frac{63}{11}$ into $\frac{11}{63}$ because of the printed choice formatting.
