# AMC12 2025-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

There is a unique ordered triple $(a,k,m)$ of nonnegative integers such that

$$
\frac{4^a+4^{a+k}+4^{a+2k}+\cdots+4^{a+mk}}
{2^a+2^{a+k}+2^{a+2k}+\cdots+2^{a+mk}}
=964.
$$

What is $a+k+m$?

**Choices:** (A) 8 &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) 9 &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) 10 &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) 11 &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) 12

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) 8**

## Solution

Let $K=k(m+1)$. Summing both geometric series and using

$$
4^n-1=(2^n-1)(2^n+1),
$$

the ratio simplifies to

$$
2^a\frac{2^K+1}{2^k+1}=964.
$$

Since $964=4\cdot241$, the power-of-two factor gives $a=2$. Thus

$$
\frac{2^K+1}{2^k+1}=241.
$$

To determine $k$: $241$ is prime and $2^{12}=4096\equiv -1\pmod{241}$, so the smallest feasible value is $K=12$. Then

$$
2^{12}+1=4097=241\cdot17=241(2^4+1),
$$

so $2^k+1=17$, i.e. $k=4$. (If $k=0$ then $2^K+1=482$, but $481$ is not a power of $2$, so this is excluded.) Since $K=k(m+1)$,

$$
4(m+1)=12
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
m=2.
$$

Hence

$$
a+k+m=2+4+2=\boxed{8}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Factoring $x^2-1$

## Key idea

Factor the geometric-series quotient until only $2^a$, $2^k+1$, and $2^{k(m+1)}+1$ remain.

## Common mistakes

- Using $m$ instead of $m+1$ terms.
- Applying the geometric-series formula when $k=0$ without checking the special case.
- Forgetting that $4^n=(2^n)^2$.
