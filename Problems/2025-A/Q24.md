# AMC12 2025-A Q24

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q24.png]]

## Problem

A circle of radius $r$ is surrounded by 12 circles of radius 1, externally tangent to the central circle and sequentially tangent to each other. Then $r$ can be written as

$$
\sqrt a+\sqrt b+c,
$$

where $a,b,c$ are integers. What is $a+b+c$?

**Choices:** (A) 3 &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) 5 &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) 7 &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) 9 &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) 11

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) 7**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q24-draft.svg]]

The centers of the 12 outer circles form a regular 12-gon. They lie on a circle of radius $r+1$, and adjacent centers are 2 units apart.

The central angle between adjacent centers is

$$
\frac{360^\circ}{12}=30^\circ.
$$

Using the chord formula,

$$
2=(2(r+1))\sin15^\circ,
$$

so

$$
r+1=\frac1{\sin15^\circ}.
$$

Now

$$
\sin15^\circ
=\sin(45^\circ-30^\circ)
=\frac{\sqrt6-\sqrt2}{4}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\frac1{\sin15^\circ}
=\frac{4}{\sqrt6-\sqrt2}
=\sqrt6+\sqrt2.
$$

Thus

$$
r=\sqrt6+\sqrt2-1,
$$

so

$$
a+b+c=6+2-1=\boxed{7}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Regular polygons
- Chords and central angles
- Exact trigonometric values
- Rationalizing radicals

## Key idea

Work with the centers of the outer circles. Their adjacent-center distance is 2, and the circumradius is $r+1$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $r$ instead of $r+1$ for the radius through the outer centers.
- Using $30^\circ$ instead of half the central angle in the chord formula.
- Simplifying $1/\sin15^\circ$ incorrectly.
