# AMC12 2025-A Q19

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q19.png]]

## Problem

Let $a,b,c$ be the roots of $x^3+kx+1$. Find

$$
a^3b^2+a^2b^3+b^3c^2+b^2c^3+c^3a^2+c^2a^3.
$$

**Choices:** (A) $-k$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) $-k+1$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) $1$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) $k-1$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) $k$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $k$**

## Solution

By Vieta's formulas,

$$
a+b+c=0,\qquad ab+bc+ca=k,\qquad abc=-1.
$$

Group the terms in pairs:

$$
a^2b^2(a+b)+b^2c^2(b+c)+c^2a^2(c+a).
$$

Since $a+b+c=0$,

$$
a+b=-c,\quad b+c=-a,\quad c+a=-b.
$$

Therefore the expression equals

$$
-a^2b^2c-ab^2c^2-a^2bc^2
=-abc(ab+bc+ca).
$$

Substitute $abc=-1$ and $ab+bc+ca=k$:

$$
-(-1)k=\boxed{k}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Vieta's formulas
- Symmetric expressions
- Factoring by grouping

## Key idea

The exponents $3,2$ are designed so that each pair factors as $a^2b^2(a+b)$, after which $a+b+c=0$ simplifies everything.

## Common mistakes

- Using $ab+bc+ca=-k$; the coefficient of $x$ is $k$, so it equals $k$.
- Forgetting the constant-term sign: $abc=-1$.
- Expanding powers instead of grouping cyclic pairs.
