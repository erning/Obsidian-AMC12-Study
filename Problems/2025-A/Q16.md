# AMC12 2025-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

Triangle $ABC$ has side lengths $AB=80$, $BC=45$, and $AC=75$. The bisector of $\angle B$ and the altitude to side $AB$ intersect at $P$. What is $BP$?

**Choices:** (A) 18 &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) 19 &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) 20 &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) 21 &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) 22

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) 21**

## Solution

By the Law of Cosines at $B$,

$$
\cos B
=\frac{AB^2+BC^2-AC^2}{2(AB)(BC)}
=\frac{80^2+45^2-75^2}{2(80)(45)}
=\frac{2800}{7200}
=\frac7{18}.
$$

Let $H$ be the foot of the altitude from $C$ to $AB$. Then

$$
BH=BC\cos B=45\left(\frac7{18}\right)=17.5.
$$

Along the angle bisector, the component in the direction of $AB$ is $BP\cos(B/2)$. Therefore

$$
BP\cos\frac B2=17.5.
$$

The half-angle identity gives

$$
\cos^2\frac B2=\frac{1+\cos B}{2}
=\frac{1+7/18}{2}
=\frac{25}{36},
$$

so $\cos(B/2)=\frac56$. Hence

$$
BP=\frac{17.5}{5/6}=\boxed{21}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Law of Cosines
- Half-angle identities
- Angle bisector geometry
- Projection of a segment

## Key idea

Project $BP$ onto $AB$. The projection reaches the foot of the altitude, so $BP\cos(B/2)$ equals the horizontal distance from $B$ to that foot.

## Common mistakes

- Using the wrong side opposite angle $B$ in the Law of Cosines.
- Confusing the angle bisector direction with the altitude direction.
- Losing fraction bars when simplifying the cosine values.
