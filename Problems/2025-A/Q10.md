# AMC12 2025-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

Major arc $\widehat{AD}$ and minor arc $\widehat{BC}$ have the same center $O$. Point $A$ lies between $O$ and $B$, and $D$ lies between $O$ and $C$. The major arc $\widehat{AD}$, minor arc $\widehat{BC}$, and segments $AB$ and $CD$ each have length $2\pi$. What is $OA$?

**Choices:** (A) $1$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) $1-\pi+\sqrt{\pi^2+1}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) $\frac{\pi}{2}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) $\frac{\sqrt{\pi^2+1}}2$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) $2$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $1-\pi+\sqrt{\pi^2+1}$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q10-draft.svg]]

Let

$$
R=OA=OD,\qquad S=OB=OC,
$$

and let $\theta$ be the central angle subtending the minor arc $BC$. The two arc lengths give

$$
S\theta=2\pi
$$

and

$$
R(2\pi-\theta)=2\pi.
$$

Because $AB=2\pi$ and $A,B$ lie on the same ray from $O$,

$$
S-R=2\pi.
$$

From the arc equations,

$$
S=\frac{2\pi}{\theta},\qquad R=\frac{2\pi}{2\pi-\theta}.
$$

Substitute into $S-R=2\pi$, divide by $2\pi$, and simplify:

$$
\frac1\theta-\frac1{2\pi-\theta}=1.
$$

Combining fractions gives $\dfrac{2\pi-2\theta}{\theta(2\pi-\theta)}=1$, which rearranges into the quadratic

$$
\theta^2-2(1+\pi)\theta+2\pi=0.
$$

The quadratic formula gives

$$
\theta=(1+\pi)\pm\sqrt{(1+\pi)^2-2\pi}=1+\pi\pm\sqrt{1+\pi^2}.
$$

Since $\theta$ subtends the minor arc, take the smaller root:

$$
\theta=\pi+1-\sqrt{\pi^2+1}.
$$

Therefore

$$
R=\frac{2\pi}{2\pi-\theta}
=1-\pi+\sqrt{\pi^2+1}.
$$

Hence

$$
\boxed{OA=1-\pi+\sqrt{\pi^2+1}}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Arc length $s=r\theta$
- Major and minor central angles
- Concentric circles
- Solving an algebraic equation

## Key idea

Use the common central angle $\theta$ for the two arcs. The straight segments give the difference of radii, while the arcs give each radius in terms of $\theta$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $\theta$ for the major arc instead of $2\pi-\theta$.
- Treating $AB$ as a chord; $A,O,B$ are collinear, so $AB=S-R$.
- Forgetting that arc length formulas require radians.
