# AMC12 2025-A Q12

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q12.png]]

## Problem

The harmonic mean of a collection of numbers is the reciprocal of the arithmetic mean of their reciprocals. What is the harmonic mean of all the real roots of

$$
\prod_{k=1}^{2025}(kx^2-4x-3)?
$$

**Choices:** (A) $-\frac53$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) $-\frac32$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) $-\frac65$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) $-\frac56$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) $-\frac23$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $-\frac23$**

## Solution

For one factor

$$
kx^2-4x-3=0,
$$

let its two roots be $r_1,r_2$. By Vieta's formulas,

$$
r_1r_2=-\frac3k,\qquad r_1+r_2=\frac4k.
$$

Therefore

$$
\frac1{r_1}+\frac1{r_2}
=\frac{r_1+r_2}{r_1r_2}
=\frac{4/k}{-3/k}
=-\frac43.
$$

There are $2025$ quadratic factors, hence $4050$ real roots. The sum of the reciprocals of all roots is

$$
2025\left(-\frac43\right)=-2700.
$$

The harmonic mean is

$$
\frac{4050}{-2700}=\boxed{-\frac32}.
$$

The archived answer page labels this as (B), but the displayed choices in the problem image show $-\frac32$ as (B); the result is consistent.

## Knowledge points

- Harmonic mean
- Vieta's formulas
- Reciprocal sums of roots

## Key idea

For a quadratic, the sum of the reciprocals of the roots is $(r_1+r_2)/(r_1r_2)$, which can be obtained immediately from Vieta's formulas.

## Common mistakes

- Taking the reciprocal of the sum of roots instead of summing reciprocals.
- Forgetting that there are $2\cdot2025=4050$ roots.
- Using the wrong harmonic-mean formula: for $N$ numbers it is $N/\sum(1/r_i)$.
