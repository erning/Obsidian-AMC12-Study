# AMC12 2025-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Points $F,G,H$ are collinear with $G$ between $F$ and $H$. The ellipse with foci $G,H$ is internally tangent to the ellipse with foci $F,G$. The ellipses have the same eccentricity $e$, and their area ratio is $2025$. What is $e$?

**Choices:** (A) $\frac35$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) $\frac{16}{25}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) $\frac45$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) $\frac{22}{23}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) $\frac{44}{45}$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $\frac{22}{23}$**

## Solution

For an ellipse with semi-major axis $a$, eccentricity $e$, and semi-minor axis $b$,

$$
b=a\sqrt{1-e^2}.
$$

Since the eccentricities are equal, the factor $\sqrt{1-e^2}$ is the same for both ellipses. Thus area is proportional to $a^2$. The area ratio $2025=45^2$ implies that the larger semi-major axis is $45$ times the smaller one.

Let $a_1$ be the semi-major axis of the larger ellipse and $a_2$ that of the smaller ellipse, so $a_1=45a_2$. Let $c_i=ea_i$.

Using the shared focus $G$, internal tangency gives

$$
a_1-c_1=a_2+c_2.
$$

Substitute $c_i=ea_i$:

$$
a_1(1-e)=a_2(1+e).
$$

With $a_1=45a_2$,

$$
45(1-e)=1+e.
$$

Therefore

$$
46e=44
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
e=\boxed{\frac{22}{23}}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Ellipse area
- Eccentricity $e=c/a$
- Similar scaling
- Internal tangency

## Key idea

Equal eccentricity makes the area ratio equal to the square of the semi-major-axis ratio. The common focus then converts tangency into a linear equation in $e$.

## Common mistakes

- Using the area ratio $2025$ directly as the axis ratio instead of taking its square root.
- Confusing the focal distance $c$ with the semi-major axis $a$.
- Using external tangency rather than internal tangency.
