# AMC12 2025-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

The maximum running speed $v$ of an organism depends on its number of toes $n$ and number of eyes $m$ according to

$$
v=kn^a m^b,
$$

where $k,a,b$ are integer constants. When $n=5$,

$$
\log v=4+2\log m,
$$

and when $m=25$,

$$
\log v=4+4\log n,
$$

where all logarithms are base 10. What is $k+a+b$?

**Choices:** (A) 20 &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) 21 &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) 22 &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) 23 &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) 24

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) 22**

## Solution

Taking the base-10 logarithm of the model gives

$$
\log v=\log k+a\log n+b\log m.
$$

When $n=5$, compare coefficients of $\log m$:

$$
b=2,
$$

and the constant term gives

$$
\log k+a\log5=4. \tag{1}
$$

When $m=25$, compare coefficients of $\log n$:

$$
a=4,
$$

and

$$
\log k+2\log25=4. \tag{2}
$$

Since $2\log25=\log(25^2)=\log625$, equation (2) gives

$$
\log k=4-\log625=\log\left(\frac{10000}{625}\right)=\log16.
$$

Therefore $k=16$, and

$$
k+a+b=16+4+2=\boxed{22}.
$$

- Logarithm rules

## Key idea

Take logarithms first. The exponents $a$ and $b$ become coefficients of $\log n$ and $\log m$, which can be read directly from the two special cases.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $\log(k n^a m^b)$ as $\log k+an^a+bm^b$.
- Forgetting that the coefficient of $\log m$ is $b$, while the coefficient of $\log n$ is $a$.
- Assuming $k=10^4$; the fixed values $n=5$ and $m=25$ also contribute to the constant term.
