# AMC12 2025-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

The outside square contains infinitely many squares, each with the same center and sides parallel to the outside square. The ratio of the side length of a square to the side length of the next inner square is $k$, where $0<k<1$. The spaces between squares are alternately shaded. The shaded portion has area 64% of the original square. What is $k$?

**Choices:** (A) $\frac35$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) $\frac{16}{25}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) $\frac23$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) $\frac34$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) $\frac45$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $\frac34$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q05-draft.svg]]

Let the area of the outside square be $1$. Because side lengths scale by $k$, the areas of the nested squares are

$$
1,\ k^2,\ k^4,\ k^6,\ldots
$$

The first shaded ring has area $1-k^2$. Since every other ring is shaded, the shaded area is

$$
(1-k^2)+k^4(1-k^2)+k^8(1-k^2)+\cdots.
$$

This is a geometric series:

$$
(1-k^2)\sum_{j=0}^{\infty}k^{4j}
=\frac{1-k^2}{1-k^4}
=\frac{1}{1+k^2}.
$$

The shaded area is $64\%=\frac{16}{25}$, so

$$
\frac{1}{1+k^2}=\frac{16}{25}.
$$

Hence

$$
1+k^2=\frac{25}{16}
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
k^2=\frac9{16}.
$$

Since $0<k<1$,

$$
k=\boxed{\frac34}.
$$


## Knowledge points

- Similar figures and area scaling
- Infinite geometric series
- Alternating sums of nested regions

## Key idea

The area ratio is $k^2$, not $k$. The shaded rings form a geometric series with common ratio $k^4$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $k$ instead of $k^2$ for the ratio of square areas.
- Using $k^2$ instead of $k^4$ as the ratio between consecutive shaded rings.
- Choosing the wrong square root without using $0<k<1$.
