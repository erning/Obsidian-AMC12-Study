# AMC12 2025-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2025-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Call a positive integer fair if no digit is used more than once, it has no zeros, and no digit is adjacent to two greater digits. How many fair positive integers are there?

**Choices:** (A) 511 &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) 2584 &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) 9841 &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) 17711 &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) 19682

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) 9841**

## Solution

Only digits $1,\ldots,9$ may be used, with no repetition. The condition “no digit is adjacent to two greater digits” means that no interior digit is smaller than both of its neighbors.

Fix a set of $n$ distinct digits. Build a valid arrangement by placing the digits from largest to smallest. After the larger digits have been placed, a new smaller digit cannot be inserted between two existing digits, because then it would be adjacent to two greater digits. It must be placed at one of the two ends.

The largest digit has one position. Each of the other $n-1$ digits has 2 choices, giving

$$
2^{n-1}
$$

valid arrangements for a fixed $n$-element digit set.

There are $\binom9n$ choices of the digit set, so the total is

$$
\sum_{n=1}^9\binom9n2^{n-1}
=\frac12\left(\sum_{n=1}^9\binom9n2^n\right)
=\frac{3^9-1}{2}
=\boxed{9841}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Restricted permutations
- Binomial theorem
- Constructive counting
- Greedy insertion

## Key idea

Insert digits from largest to smallest. Every new digit must go at an end, producing exactly two choices at each step.

## Common mistakes

- Allowing digit 0.
- Counting $2^n$ arrangements instead of $2^{n-1}$.
- Treating the leading digit differently after the construction has already fixed all positions.
