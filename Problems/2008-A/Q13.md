# AMC12 2008-A Q13

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Points $A$ and $B$ lie on a circle centered at $O,$ and $\angle AOB = 60^\circ.$ A second circle is internally tangent to the first and tangent to both $OA$ and $OB.$ What is the ratio of the area of the smaller circle to that of the larger circle?

(A) $\frac1{16}$
(B) $\frac1{9}$
(C) $\frac1{8}$
(D) $\frac1{6}$
(E) $\frac1{4}$

## Answer

**B.** $\frac1{9}$

## Solution

Let $r$ and $R$ be the radii of the smaller and larger circles, and let $E$ be the center of the smaller circle. By symmetry $E$ lies on the bisector of $\angle AOB,$ so $OE$ makes a $30^\circ$ angle with $OA.$

Dropping the radius $ED$ perpendicular to $OA$ gives a $30$-$60$-$90$ triangle with $OE = 2 \cdot ED = 2r.$ Since the circles are internally tangent, $OE = R - r.$

Then $R - r = 2r,$ so $R = 3r$ and $\tfrac{r}{R} = \tfrac{1}{3}.$ The ratio of areas is $$ \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{9}. $$

Thus, **B** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Tangent circles
- Special right triangle
- Area ratio

## Key idea

Use equal tangent lengths from the circle center and the $60^\circ$ angle to find the area ratio of the two regions.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
