# AMC12 2008-A Q25

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q25.png]]

## Problem

A sequence $(a_1, b_1), (a_2, b_2), (a_3, b_3), \ldots$ of points in the coordinate plane satisfies $$ \begin{aligned} &(a_{n+1}, b_{n+1}) \\ &= \left(\sqrt{3}\,a_n - b_n,\; \sqrt{3}\,b_n + a_n\right) \\ &\quad (n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots) \end{aligned} $$ Suppose that $(a_{100}, b_{100}) = (2, 4).$ What is $a_1 + b_1?$

(A) $\frac1{2^{97}}$
(B) $-\frac1{2^{99}}$
(C) $0$
(D) $\frac1{2^{98}}$
(E) $\frac1{2^{96}}$

## Answer

**D.** $\frac1{2^{98}}$

## Solution

Let $z_n = a_n + b_n i.$ Then $$ \begin{aligned} z_{n+1} &= (\sqrt{3}\,a_n - b_n) \\ &\quad {}+ (\sqrt{3}\,b_n + a_n)i \\ &= (a_n + b_n i)(\sqrt{3} + i), \end{aligned} $$ so $z_{n+1} = z_n(\sqrt{3} + i)$ and $z_{100} = z_1(\sqrt{3} + i)^{99}.$

Since $\sqrt{3} + i = 2(\cos 30^\circ + i\sin 30^\circ),$ De Moivre's theorem gives $(\sqrt{3} + i)^{99}$ $= 2^{99}(\cos 2970^\circ + i\sin 2970^\circ).$ As $2970^\circ$ is coterminal with $90^\circ,$ this equals $2^{99} i.$

Thus $2 + 4i = z_1 \cdot 2^{99} i,$ so $$ z_1 = \frac{2 + 4i}{2^{99} i} = \frac{4 - 2i}{2^{99}}. $$

Then $a_1 = \tfrac{4}{2^{99}}$ and $b_1 = -\tfrac{2}{2^{99}},$ so $$ a_1 + b_1 = \frac{2}{2^{99}} = \frac{1}{2^{98}}. $$

Thus, **D** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Complex number
- De moivre’s theorem

## Key idea

Represent the recurrence as multiplication by a complex number and use De Moivre’s theorem to work backward.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
