# AMC12 2008-A Q11

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q11.png]]

## Problem

Three cubes are each formed from the pattern shown. They are then stacked on a table one on top of another so that the $13$ visible numbers have the greatest possible sum. What is that sum?

(A) $154$
(B) $159$
(C) $164$
(D) $167$
(E) $189$

## Answer

**C.** $164$

## Solution

The six faces of each cube sum to $1 + 2 + 4 + 8 + 16 + 32 = 63.$ From the pattern, the pairs of opposite faces are $1$ & $32,$ $2$ & $16,$ and $4$ & $8.$

Each of the two lower cubes hides a pair of opposite faces (top and bottom); hiding the pair $4 + 8 = 12$ is best. The top cube hides only its bottom face, so hide the $1.$

The greatest sum is $$ \begin{aligned} &3 \cdot 63 - 2 \cdot 12 - 1 \\ &= 189 - 24 - 1 \\ &= 164. \end{aligned} $$

Thus, **C** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Cube geometry
- Optimization

## Key idea

Identify opposite face pairs from the net; the hidden faces should be the cheapest opposite pair (bottom two cubes) and the smallest face (top cube).

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
