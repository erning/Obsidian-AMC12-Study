# AMC12 2008-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

Doug can paint a room in $5$ hours. Dave can paint the same room in $7$ hours. Doug and Dave paint the room together and take a one-hour break for lunch. Let $t$ be the total time, in hours, required for them to complete the job working together, including lunch. Which of the following equations is satisfied by $t?$

(A) $(\frac15+\frac17)(t+1)=1$
(B) $(\frac15+\frac17)t+1=1$
(C) $(\frac15+\frac17)t=1$
(D) $(\frac15+\frac17)(t-1)=1$
(E) $(5+7)t=1$

## Answer

**D.** $(\frac15+\frac17)(t-1)=1$

## Solution

In one hour Doug paints $\tfrac{1}{5}$ of the room and Dave paints $\tfrac{1}{7},$ so together they paint $\tfrac{1}{5} + \tfrac{1}{7}$ of the room per hour.

Of the total time $t,$ one hour is spent at lunch, so they work for $t - 1$ hours. The fraction painted must equal $1,$ giving $$ \left(\frac{1}{5} + \frac{1}{7}\right)(t - 1) = 1. $$

Thus, **D** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Rate
- Linear equation

## Key idea

Model the two painters’ rates and add their contributions over the time interval.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
