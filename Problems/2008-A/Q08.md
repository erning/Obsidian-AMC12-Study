# AMC12 2008-A Q08

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q08.png]]

## Problem

What is the volume of a cube whose surface area is twice that of a cube with volume $1?$

(A) $\sqrt2$
(B) $2$
(C) $2\sqrt2$
(D) $4$
(E) $8$

## Answer

**C.** $2\sqrt2$

## Solution

The cube with volume $1$ has side $1$ and surface area $6.$ The larger cube has surface area $12,$ so if its side is $s,$ then $6s^2 = 12,$ giving $s = \sqrt{2}.$

Its volume is $$ (\sqrt{2})^3 = 2\sqrt{2}. $$

Thus, **C** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Cube geometry
- Surface area
- Volume

## Key idea

Use the surface-area and volume formulas for a cube to solve for its side length.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
