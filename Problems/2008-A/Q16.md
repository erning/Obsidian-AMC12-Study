# AMC12 2008-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

The numbers $\log(a^3 b^7),$ $\log(a^5 b^{12}),$ and $\log(a^8 b^{15})$ are the first three terms of an arithmetic sequence, and the $12$th term of the sequence is $\log(b^n).$ What is $n?$

(A) $40$
(B) $56$
(C) $76$
(D) $112$
(E) $143$

## Answer

**D.** $112$

## Solution

The three terms are $3\log a + 7\log b,$ $5\log a + 12\log b,$ and $8\log a + 15\log b.$ Setting the two consecutive differences equal, $$ \begin{aligned} &2\log a + 5\log b \\ &= 3\log a + 3\log b, \end{aligned} $$ so $\log a = 2\log b.$

The first term is then $(3 \cdot 2 + 7)\log b = 13\log b,$ and the common difference is $(2 \cdot 2 + 5)\log b = 9\log b.$

The $12$th term is $$ \begin{aligned} (13 + 11 \cdot 9)\log b &= 112\log b \\ &= \log(b^{112}), \end{aligned} $$ so $n = 112.$

Thus, **D** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Arithmetic sequence
- Logarithm

## Key idea

Convert the logarithms into an arithmetic sequence in the exponents of $a$ and $b$, then find the next term.
