## Problem

What is the reciprocal of $$\frac{1}{2} + \frac{2}{3}?$$

(A) $\frac{6}{7}$
(B) $\frac{7}{6}$
(C) $\frac{5}{3}$
(D) $3$
(E) $\frac{7}{2}$

## Answer

**A.** $\frac{6}{7}$

## Solution

Using a common denominator, $$ \frac{1}{2} + \frac{2}{3} = \frac{3}{6} + \frac{4}{6} = \frac{7}{6}. $$

The reciprocal of $\frac{7}{6}$ is $\frac{6}{7}.$

Thus, **A** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Fraction

## Key idea

Evaluate the sum first, then invert the resulting fraction.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
