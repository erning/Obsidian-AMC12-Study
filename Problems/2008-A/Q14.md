# AMC12 2008-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

What is the area of the region defined by the inequality $$|3x - 18| + |2y + 7| \le 3?$$

(A) $3$
(B) $\frac72$
(C) $4$
(D) $\frac92$
(E) $5$

## Answer

**A.** $3$

## Solution

The region is a rhombus centered at $\left(6, -\tfrac{7}{2}\right).$ Setting $2y + 7 = 0$ gives $|3x - 18| \le 3,$ so $x \in [5, 7],$ a horizontal diagonal of length $2.$

Setting $3x - 18 = 0$ gives $|2y + 7| \le 3,$ so $y \in [-5, -2],$ a vertical diagonal of length $3.$

The area of the rhombus is half the product of its diagonals, $$ \frac{1}{2} \cdot 2 \cdot 3 = 3. $$

Thus, **A** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Absolute value
- Rhombus
- Area

## Key idea

Solve the absolute-value inequality by splitting at the breakpoints and compute the resulting region area.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
