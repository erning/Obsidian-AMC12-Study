# AMC12 2008-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Heather compares the price of a new computer at two different stores. Store A offers $15\%$ off the sticker price followed by a $90 rebate, and store B offers $25\%$ off the same sticker price with no rebate. Heather saves $15 by buying the computer at store A instead of store B. What is the sticker price of the computer, in dollars?

(A) $750$
(B) $900$
(C) $1000$
(D) $1050$
(E) $1500$

## Answer

**A.** $750$

## Solution

Let $x$ be the sticker price in dollars. Store A charges $0.85x - 90$ dollars, and store B charges $0.75x$ dollars.

Since store A is $15$ dollars cheaper, $$ 0.85x - 90 = 0.75x - 15, $$ so $0.10x = 75$ and $x = 750.$

Thus, **A** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Percentage
- Linear equation

## Key idea

Compare the two discounts as percentages of their respective original prices, then solve for the unknown original price.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
