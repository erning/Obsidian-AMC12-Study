# AMC12 2008-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Suppose that $$\frac{2x}{3} - \frac{x}{6}$$ is an integer. Which of the following statements must be true about $x?$

(A) It is negative.
(B) It is even, but not necessarily a multiple of 3.
(C) It is a multiple of 3, but not necessarily even.
(D) It is a multiple of 6, but not necessarily a multiple of 12.
(E) It is a multiple of 12.

## Answer

**B.** It is even, but not necessarily a multiple of 3.

## Solution

Combining the fractions, $$ \frac{2x}{3} - \frac{x}{6} = \frac{4x - x}{6} = \frac{x}{2}. $$

This is an integer exactly when $x$ is even. The example $x = 4$ is even but not a multiple of $3,$ which rules out every other statement.

Thus, **B** is the correct answer.

- Parity

## Key idea

Clear denominators and analyze the parity and divisibility of the resulting integer expression.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
