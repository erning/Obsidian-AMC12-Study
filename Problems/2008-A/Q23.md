# AMC12 2008-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

The solutions of the equation $$z^4 + 4z^3 i - 6z^2 - 4zi - i = 0$$ are the vertices of a convex polygon in the complex plane. What is the area of the polygon?

(A) $2^5/8$
(B) $2^3/4$
(C) $2$
(D) $2^5/4$
(E) $2^3/2$

## Answer

**D.** $2^5/4$

## Solution

Adding $1 + i$ to both sides, the left side becomes $$ \begin{aligned} &z^4 + 4z^3 i - 6z^2 - 4zi + 1 \\ &= (z + i)^4, \end{aligned} $$ so $(z + i)^4 = 1 + i.$

The four solutions for $w = z + i$ are equally spaced on a circle of radius $|1 + i|^{1/4} = (2^{1/2})^{1/4} = 2^{1/8},$ and they form a square. Subtracting $i$ merely translates it.

A square inscribed in a circle of radius $2^{1/8}$ has diagonal $2 \cdot 2^{1/8} = 2^{9/8},$ so its side is $\tfrac{2^{9/8}}{\sqrt{2}} = 2^{5/8}.$

The area is $$ \left(2^{5/8}\right)^2 = 2^{5/4}. $$

Thus, **D** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Binomial theorem
- Complex number
- Roots of unity

## Key idea

Apply the binomial theorem and identify the four roots as a translated set of fourth roots of unity.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
