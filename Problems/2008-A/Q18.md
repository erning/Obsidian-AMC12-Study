# AMC12 2008-A Q18

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Triangle $ABC,$ with sides of length $5, 6,$ and $7,$ has one vertex on the positive $x$-axis, one on the positive $y$-axis, and one on the positive $z$-axis. Let $O$ be the origin. What is the volume of tetrahedron $OABC?$

(A) $√85$
(B) $√90$
(C) $√95$
(D) $10$
(E) $√105$

## Answer

**C.** $√95$

## Solution

Let $A = (a, 0, 0),$ $B = (0, b, 0),$ $C = (0, 0, c).$ Assigning the sides, $$ \begin{aligned} a^2 + b^2 &= 25, \\ b^2 + c^2 &= 36, \\ a^2 + c^2 &= 49. \end{aligned} $$

Adding gives $a^2 + b^2 + c^2 = 55,$ so $a^2 = 19,$ $b^2 = 6,$ and $c^2 = 30.$

The volume is $$ \begin{aligned} \frac{1}{6}abc &= \frac{1}{6}\sqrt{19 \cdot 6 \cdot 30} \\ &= \frac{1}{6}\sqrt{3420} \\ &= \sqrt{95}. \end{aligned} $$

Thus, **C** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- 3d geometry
- System of equations
- Volume

## Key idea

Place the vertices on the axes at $(a,0,0),$ $(0,b,0),$ $(0,0,c),$ solve the side-length system for $a^2, b^2, c^2,$ and use $V = \tfrac16 abc.$

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
