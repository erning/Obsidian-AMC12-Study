# AMC12 2008-A Q01

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q01.png]]

## Problem

A bakery owner turns on his doughnut machine at $8\!:\!30$ am. At $11\!:\!10$ am the machine has completed one third of the day's job. At what time will the doughnut machine complete the job?

(A) 1:50 pm
(B) 3:00 pm
(C) 3:30 pm
(D) 4:30 pm
(E) 5:50 pm

## Answer

**D.** 4:30 pm

## Solution

From $8\!:\!30$ am to $11\!:\!10$ am is $2$ hours $40$ minutes, or $160$ minutes, to complete one third of the job.

The whole job then takes $3 \cdot 160 = 480$ minutes, or $8$ hours. Adding $8$ hours to $8\!:\!30$ am gives $4\!:\!30$ pm.

Thus, **D** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Ratio and proportion
- Date and time

## Key idea

Convert both times to minutes after midnight and compare the elapsed time with the end-to-end time shown on the clock.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
