# AMC12 2008-A Q09

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q09.png]]

## Problem

Older television screens have an aspect ratio of $4:3.$ That is, the ratio of the width to the height is $4:3.$ The aspect ratio of many movies is not $4:3,$ so they are sometimes shown on a television screen by "letterboxing" — darkening strips of equal height at the top and bottom of the screen, as shown. Suppose a movie has an aspect ratio of $2:1$ and is shown on an older television screen with a $27$-inch diagonal. What is the height, in inches, of each darkened strip?

(A) $2$
(B) $2.25$
(C) $2.5$
(D) $2.7$
(E) $3$

## Answer

**D.** $2.7$

## Solution

Since the sides and diagonal are in ratio $3:4:5,$ the height is $\tfrac{3}{5} \cdot 27 = 16.2$ inches and the width is $\tfrac{4}{5} \cdot 27 = 21.6$ inches.

The movie has aspect ratio $2:1,$ so its height is $\tfrac{21.6}{2} = 10.8$ inches.

Each darkened strip therefore has height $$ \frac{16.2 - 10.8}{2} = 2.7 $$ inches.

Thus, **D** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Pythagorean triple
- Ratio and proportion

## Key idea

Use the $4:3$ aspect ratio and the Pythagorean theorem to compare the diagonal with the screen width.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
