# AMC12 2008-A Q15

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q15.png]]

## Problem

Let $k = 2008^2 + 2^{2008}.$ What is the units digit of $k^2 + 2^k?$

(A) $0$
(B) $2$
(C) $4$
(D) $6$
(E) $8$

## Answer

**D.** $6$

## Solution

The units digit of $2008^2$ is $4.$ Since $2008$ is a multiple of $4,$ the units digit of $2^{2008}$ is $6.$ Thus $k$ has units digit $0,$ and so does $k^2.$

Both $2008^2$ and $2^{2008}$ are multiples of $4,$ so $k$ is a multiple of $4.$ Therefore the units digit of $2^k$ is $6.$

The units digit of $k^2 + 2^k$ is then $0 + 6 = 6.$

Thus, **D** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Units digit
- Modular exponentiation

## Key idea

Use modular arithmetic for $2008^2$ and for powers of $2$ modulo $10$ to find the units digit.
