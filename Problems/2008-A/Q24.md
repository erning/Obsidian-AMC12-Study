# AMC12 2008-A Q24

![[assets/problems/2008-A/Q24.png]]

## Problem

Triangle $ABC$ has $\angle C = 60^\circ$ and $BC = 4.$ Point $D$ is the midpoint of $BC.$ What is the largest possible value of $\tan(\angle BAD)?$

(A) $\frac{\sqrt3}{6}$
(B) $\frac{\sqrt3}{3}$
(C) $\frac{\sqrt3}{2\sqrt2}$
(D) $\frac{\sqrt3}{4\sqrt2-3}$
(E) $1$

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{\sqrt3}{4\sqrt2-3}$

## Solution

Place $C = (0, 0),$ $B = (2, 2\sqrt{3})$ so that $\angle C = 60^\circ$ and $BC = 4,$ and let $A = (x, 0)$ with $x \gt 0.$ Then $D = (1, \sqrt{3})$ is the midpoint of $BC.$

The lines $AD$ and $AB$ have slopes $\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{1 - x}$ and $\tfrac{2\sqrt{3}}{2 - x}.$ Using the tangent-difference formula and simplifying, $$ \tan(\angle BAD) = \frac{\sqrt{3}\,x}{x^2 - 3x + 8}. $$

Setting the derivative to zero gives $x^2 = 8,$ so $x = 2\sqrt{2}.$ Substituting, $$ \begin{aligned} \tan(\angle BAD) &= \frac{2\sqrt{6}}{16 - 6\sqrt{2}} \\ &= \frac{\sqrt{6}}{8 - 3\sqrt{2}} \\ &= \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4\sqrt{2} - 3}. \end{aligned} $$

Thus, **D** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Coordinate geometry
- Trigonometric identity
- Optimization

## Key idea

Place $B$ and $C$ in coordinates with $A$ on the $x$-axis, write $\tan(\angle BAD)$ via the tangent-difference formula, and maximize over $x.$

- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
