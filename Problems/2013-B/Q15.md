# AMC12 2013-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

The number $2013$ is expressed as
$$2013=\frac{a_1!a_2!\cdots a_m!}{b_1!b_2!\cdots b_n!},$$
where the $a_i$ and $b_i$ are positive integers in nonincreasing order and $a_1+b_1$ is as small as possible. What is $|a_1-b_1|$?

## Answer

**B.** $2$

## Solution

Factor
$$2013=3\cdot11\cdot61.$$
The numerator must contain a factorial at least $61!$, so $a_1\ge61$. But $61!$ contains the prime $59$, which does not occur in $2013$, so the denominator must contain a factorial at least $59!$, giving $b_1\ge59$.

These bounds are attained by
$$2013=\frac{61!\,11!\,3!}{59!\,10!\,5!}.$$
Thus $(a_1,b_1)=(61,59)$ and
$$|a_1-b_1|=2.$$

## Knowledge points

- Prime factorization
- Factorials
- Minimality arguments

## Key idea

The primes $61$ and $59$ force the largest numerator and denominator factorials.

## Common mistakes

- Checking only the prime $61$
- Forgetting that the denominator must cancel the unwanted factor $59$
