# AMC12 2013-B Q25

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q25.png]]

## Problem

Let $G$ be the set of polynomials
$$P(z)=z^n+c_{n-1}z^{n-1}+\cdots+c_1z+50,$$
where the coefficients are integers and $P$ has $n$ distinct roots of the form $a+ib$ with $a,b$ integers. How many polynomials are in $G$?

## Answer

**B.** $528$

## Solution

Because the coefficients are real, every nonreal root occurs with its conjugate. Thus the basic factors are integer linear factors and conjugate quadratic factors
$$
(z-(a+ib))(z-(a-ib))=z^2-2az+(a^2+b^2).
$$
Each basic factor's constant term must divide $50$.

For constant-term magnitudes $d=1,2,5,10,25,50$, the numbers of admissible basic factors are respectively
$$|B_1|=3,\quad|B_2|=4,\quad|B_5|=6,\quad|B_{10}|=6,\quad|B_{25}|=7,\quad|B_{50}|=8.$$
Accounting for the ways to build the total constant term $50$, including the independent factors $z+1$ and $z^2+1$ and the forced sign factor, gives
$$
2^2\left(8+7\cdot4+6\cdot6+4\binom62\right).
$$
Therefore
$$4(8+28+36+60)=528.$$

## Knowledge points

- Complex conjugate roots
- Polynomial factorization
- Integer lattice points
- Casework

## Key idea

Classify all possible irreducible factors by their integer constant terms, then count compatible products whose constant term is $50$.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting that nonreal roots must come in conjugate pairs
- Counting roots instead of distinct monic polynomials
