# AMC12 2013-B Q22

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q22.png]]

## Problem

Let $m>1$ and $n>1$ be integers. Suppose the product of the solutions for $x$ of
$$8(\log_nx)(\log_mx)-7\log_nx-6\log_mx-2013=0$$
is the smallest possible integer. What is $m+n$?

## Answer

**A.** $12$

## Solution

Set $u=\log x$. Then
$$\log_nx=\frac{u}{\log n},qquad\log_mx=\frac{u}{\log m}.$$
The equation is quadratic in $u$. If its two solutions correspond to $x_1$ and $x_2$, Vieta's formulas give
$$\log(x_1x_2)=\frac18(7\log m+6\log n).$$
Let $N=x_1x_2$. Exponentiating,
$$N^8=m^7n^6.$$

For each prime, the exponent in $7\log m+6\log n$ must be divisible by $8$. The smallest choice with $m,n>1$ is
$$m=2^2=4,qquad n=2^3=8,$$
which gives $N=16$. Any additional prime or larger exponent increases $N$. Therefore
$$m+n=4+8=12.$$

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm change of base
- Vieta's formulas
- Prime-exponent minimization

## Key idea

Apply Vieta to the quadratic in $\log x$, then minimize the integer satisfying $N^8=m^7n^6$.

## Common mistakes

- Applying Vieta directly to $x$ instead of $\log x$
- Choosing $m=2$ even though the exponent congruence cannot be satisfied
