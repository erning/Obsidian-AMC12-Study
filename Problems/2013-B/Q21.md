# AMC12 2013-B Q21

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Consider the $30$ parabolas with focus $(0,0)$ and directrices $y=ax+b$, where $a\in\{-2,-1,0,1,2\}$ and $b\in\{-3,-2,-1,1,2,3\}$. No three of these parabolas have a common point. How many points in the plane lie on two of these parabolas?

## Answer

**C.** $810$

## Solution

Two parabolas with the same focus generally meet in two points. They fail to meet only when their directrices are parallel and the focus lies outside the strip between them. Thus the non-intersecting pairs have the same slope and intercepts with the same sign.

For each of the $5$ slopes, there are
$$2\binom32=6$$
such same-sign pairs. Hence the number of intersecting pairs is
$$\binom{30}{2}-5\cdot6=435-30=405.$$
Each pair contributes two intersection points, and the no-three-common-points condition prevents overcounting. Therefore
$$2\cdot405=810.$$

## Key idea

Count pairs of parabolas, subtract the parallel directrix pairs that do not intersect, then multiply by two.

## Common mistakes

- Counting directrices instead of parabolas
- Forgetting the factor of two intersection points per valid pair
