# AMC12 2013-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Two non-decreasing sequences of nonnegative integers have different first terms. Each sequence has the property that every term beginning with the third is the sum of the previous two terms, and the seventh term of each sequence is $N$. What is the smallest possible value of $N$?

## Answer

**C.** $104$

## Solution

For a Fibonacci-type sequence with first terms $a_1,a_2$, the seventh term is
$$5a_1+8a_2.$$
Let the other sequence begin with $b_1,b_2$. Equality of the seventh terms gives
$$5(b_1-a_1)=8(a_2-b_2).$$
Since $\gcd(5,8)=1$, write
$$b_1-a_1=8t,qquad a_2-b_2=5t$$
for a positive integer $t$. Taking $t=1$ and using nondecreasing terms gives the smallest choice
$$a_1=0,\quad b_1=b_2=8,\quad a_2=13.$$
The common seventh term is then
$$N=5\cdot0+8\cdot13=104.$$

## Knowledge points

- Fibonacci recurrences
- Diophantine equations
- Nondecreasing sequences

## Key idea

Express the seventh term in terms of the first two terms and use coprimality of $5$ and $8$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $3a_1+5a_2$, which is the fifth-term formula
- Allowing a negative or decreasing initial pair
