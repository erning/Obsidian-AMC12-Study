# AMC12 2013-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Jo and Blair take turns counting from $1$ to one more than the last number said by the other person. Jo starts by saying “1,” so Blair says “1,2.” Jo then says “1,2,3,” and so on. What is the $53$rd number said?

## Answer

**E.** $8$

## Solution

After the turn that counts up to $k$, the number of numbers spoken is
$$1+2+\cdots+k=\frac{k(k+1)}2.$$
For $k=9$, this total is $45$. The next turn speaks the sequence $1,2,\ldots$, so the $53$rd number is the eighth number of that turn:
$$53-45=8.$$

## Knowledge points

- Triangular numbers
- Sequence indexing

## Key idea

Locate the $53$rd position between consecutive triangular numbers.

## Common mistakes

- Counting the turns instead of the individual numbers
- Starting the next turn at $0$
