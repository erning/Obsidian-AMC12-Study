# AMC12 2013-B Q01

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q01.png]]

## Problem

On a particular January day, the high temperature in Lincoln, Nebraska, was $16$ degrees higher than the low temperature, and the average of the high and low temperatures was $3^\circ$. In degrees, what was the low temperature?

## Answer

**C.** $-5^\circ$

## Solution

Let the low temperature be $L$. The high temperature is $L+16$, so
$$\frac{L+(L+16)}2=3.$$
Thus $L+8=3$, giving
$$L=-5.$$

## Knowledge points

- Mean
- Linear equations

## Key idea

The average lies exactly $8$ degrees above the low temperature.

## Common mistakes

- Using $16$ as the distance from the low to the average
- Losing the negative sign
