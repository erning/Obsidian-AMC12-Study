# AMC12 2013-B Q20

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q20.png]]

## Problem

For $135^\circ<x<180^\circ$, let
$$P=(\cos x,\cos^2x),\quad Q=(\cot x,\cot^2x),$$
$$R=(\sin x,\sin^2x),\quad S=(\tan x,\tan^2x).$$
These points are the vertices of a trapezoid. What is $\sin(2x)$?

## Answer

**A.** $2-2\sqrt2$

## Solution

All four points lie on the parabola $y=t^2$. The chord through parameters $t_1$ and $t_2$ has slope $t_1+t_2$. In the given interval, the parallel sides are $QR$ and $PS$, so their slopes are equal:
$$\cot x+\sin x=\tan x+\cos x.$$
Multiplying by $\sin x\cos x$ gives
$$\cos x+\sin x-\sin x\cos x=0.$$
Squaring and using $2\sin x\cos x=\sin2x$ yields
$$1+\sin2x=\frac14\sin^22x.$$
The root in the allowed interval is
$$\sin2x=2-2\sqrt2.$$

## Knowledge points

- Parabola chords
- Trigonometric identities
- Trapezoid parallel sides

## Key idea

Use the chord-slope formula for $y=t^2$ to convert the trapezoid condition into one trigonometric equation.

## Common mistakes

- Pairing the wrong sides as parallel
- Keeping a squared root that is outside the interval for $x$
