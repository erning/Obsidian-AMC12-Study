# AMC12 2013-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

Ray's car averages $40$ miles per gallon and Tom's car averages $10$ miles per gallon. Ray and Tom each drive the same number of miles. What is the cars' combined rate in miles per gallon?

## Answer

**B.** $16$

## Solution

Suppose each car travels $D$ miles. The total distance is $2D$, while the gasoline used is
$$\frac D{40}+\frac D{10}=\frac D8.$$
Thus the combined mileage is
$$\frac{2D}{D/8}=16\text{ miles per gallon}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Rates
- Harmonic mean
- Unit analysis

## Key idea

Combine total distance divided by total fuel, not the arithmetic mean of the two rates.

## Common mistakes

- Averaging $40$ and $10$ directly
- Adding miles-per-gallon rates instead of fuel amounts
