# AMC12 2013-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

Two bees start at the same spot and fly at the same rate. Bee $A$ travels $1$ foot north, then $1$ foot east, then $1$ foot up, repeating this pattern. Bee $B$ travels $1$ foot south, then $1$ foot west, repeating this pattern. In what directions are the bees traveling when they are exactly $10$ feet apart?

## Answer

**A.** Bee $A$ east, Bee $B$ west

## Solution

Use coordinates $(\text{east},\text{north},\text{up})$. After $7$ feet, Bee $A$ is at $(2,3,2)$ and Bee $B$ is at $(-3,-4,0)$. Their distance is
$$\sqrt{5^2+7^2+2^2}=\sqrt{78}<10.$$

During the next foot, $A$ travels east and $B$ travels west. At the end of that foot their positions are $(3,3,2)$ and $(-4,-4,0)$, whose distance is
$$\sqrt{7^2+7^2+2^2}=\sqrt{102}>10.$$
Thus they pass through a distance of $10$ while $A$ travels east and $B$ travels west.

## Knowledge points

- Three-dimensional distance
- Periodic motion
- Coordinate modeling

## Key idea

Check the first unit interval in which the distance changes from below $10$ to above $10$.

## Common mistakes

- Comparing positions only at integer times
- Forgetting the vertical coordinate of Bee $A$
