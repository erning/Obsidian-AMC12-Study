# AMC12 2013-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

What is the sum of the exponents of the prime factors of the square root of the largest perfect square that divides $12!$?

## Answer

**C.** $8$

## Solution

The prime factorization is
$$12!=2^{10}\cdot3^5\cdot5^2\cdot7\cdot11.$$
The largest square divisor uses the largest even exponent for each prime:
$$2^{10}\cdot3^4\cdot5^2.$$
Its square root is
$$2^5\cdot3^2\cdot5.$$
The requested sum is
$$5+2+1=8.$$

## Knowledge points

- Prime factorization
- Legendre's formula
- Perfect squares

## Key idea

Reduce each prime exponent to the greatest even exponent not exceeding it.

## Common mistakes

- Taking the square root before making all exponents even
- Including primes $7$ and $11$ in the square root
