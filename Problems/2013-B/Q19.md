# AMC12 2013-B Q19

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q19.png]]

## Problem

In $\triangle ABC$, $AB=13$, $BC=14$, and $CA=15$. Distinct points $D$, $E$, and $F$ lie on $\overline{BC}$, $\overline{CA}$, and $\overline{DE}$, respectively, such that $AD\perp BC$, $DE\perp AC$, and $AF\perp BF$. If $DF=\frac mn$ in lowest terms, what is $m+n$?

## Answer

**B.** $21$

## Solution

The $13$-$14$-$15$ triangle has altitude $AD$ to $BC$ with
$$BD=5,qquad CD=9,qquad AD=12.$$

Since $DE\perp AC$, triangles $AED$ and $ADC$ are similar. Therefore
$$DE=\frac{36}{5},qquad AE=\frac{48}{5}.$$

Because $AF\perp BF$ and $AD\perp BD$, quadrilateral $ABDF$ is cyclic. This gives the similarity of right triangles $ABD$ and $AFE$, so
$$\frac{FE}{5}=\frac{48/5}{12},qquad FE=4.$$
Hence
$$DF=DE-FE=\frac{36}{5}-4=\frac{16}{5}.$$
Thus $m+n=16+5=21$.

## Knowledge points

- Altitude in a $13$-$14$-$15$ triangle
- Similar triangles
- Cyclic quadrilaterals

## Key idea

Use the altitude to expose a $5$-$12$-$13$ triangle, then chain two similarities.

## Common mistakes

- Assigning $BD=9$ instead of $5$
- Using $DF=DE+FE$
