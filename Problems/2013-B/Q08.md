# AMC12 2013-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

Line $\ell_1$ has equation $3x-2y=1$ and goes through $A=(-1,-2)$. Line $\ell_2$ has equation $y=1$ and meets $\ell_1$ at $B$. Line $\ell_3$ has positive slope, goes through $A$, and meets $\ell_2$ at $C$. The area of $\triangle ABC$ is $3$. What is the slope of $\ell_3$?

## Answer

**B.** $\frac34$

## Solution

Solving $3x-2y=1$ with $y=1$ gives $B=(1,1)$. The distance from $A=(-1,-2)$ to the horizontal line $y=1$ is $3$. If $BC$ is the base,
$$\frac12\cdot BC\cdot3=3,$$
so $BC=2$.

Thus $C=(3,1)$ or $C=(-1,1)$. The second choice makes $AC$ vertical, so the positive-slope line uses $C=(3,1)$. Its slope is
$$\frac{1-(-2)}{3-(-1)}=\frac34.$$

## Key idea

Use the known altitude to determine the horizontal base length.

## Common mistakes

- Choosing the vertical line through $A$
- Using $AB$ as the triangle base without checking its direction
