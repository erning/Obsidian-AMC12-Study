# AMC12 2013-B Q24

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q24.png]]

## Problem

In triangle $ABC$, $M$ is the midpoint of $AC$, and $CN$ is the angle bisector of $\angle ACB$ with $N$ on $AB$. Let $X$ be the intersection of median $BM$ and bisector $CN$. In addition, $\triangle BXN$ is equilateral and $AC=2$. What is $BN^2$?

## Answer

**A.** $\frac{10-6\sqrt2}{7}$

## Solution

Let $\alpha=\angle ACN=\angle NCB$ and let $x=BN$. Since $BXN$ is equilateral, the relevant angle at $X$ is $120^\circ$. The similarity relations
$$\triangle ABC\sim\triangle BMC,qquad \triangle ANC\sim\triangle BXC$$
give, using $MC=1$,
$$\frac{BC}{2}=\frac{MC}{BC},qquad BC=\sqrt2,$$
and
$$CX=(\sqrt2+1)x.$$

Apply the Law of Cosines in $\triangle BCX$:
$$
2=x^2+(\sqrt2+1)^2x^2+(\sqrt2+1)x^2
=(5+3\sqrt2)x^2.
$$
Thus
$$BN^2=x^2=\frac2{5+3\sqrt2}=\frac{10-6\sqrt2}{7}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Angle bisectors
- Similar triangles
- Law of cosines

## Key idea

Use the equilateral triangle to identify a $120^\circ$ angle and combine the two similarity relations.

## Common mistakes

- Using $MC=AC$ instead of $MC=\frac12AC$
- Rationalizing the final denominator incorrectly
