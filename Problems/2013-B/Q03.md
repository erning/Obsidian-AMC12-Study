# AMC12 2013-B Q03

![[assets/problems/2013-B/Q03.png]]

## Problem

When counting from $3$ to $201$, $53$ is the $51$st number counted. When counting backwards from $201$ to $3$, $53$ is the $n$th number counted. What is $n$?

## Answer

**D.** $149$

## Solution

Counting down from $201$, the number $x$ appears after all integers from $201$ through $x+1$. Its position is therefore
$$202-x.$$
For $x=53$,
$$n=202-53=149.$$

## Knowledge points

- Counting intervals
- Arithmetic sequences

## Key idea

The first term is $201$, so the position of $x$ is $201-x+1$.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting the final $+1$
- Using $201-53$ instead of $202-53$
