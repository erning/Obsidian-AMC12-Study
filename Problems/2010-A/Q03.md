# AMC12 2010-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2010-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

Rectangle $ABCD,$ pictured below, shares $50\%$ of its area with square $EFGH.$ Square $EFGH$ shares $20\%$ of its area with rectangle $ABCD.$ What is $\frac{AB}{AD}?$

(A) $4$
(B) $5$
(C) $6$
(D) $8$
(E) $10$

## Answer

**E.** $10$

## Solution

Let $s$ be the side length of square $EFGH.$ The shaded overlap has width $s$ and height $AD,$ so its area is $s\cdot AD.$

Because the overlap is $50\%$ of the rectangle, $s\cdot AD=\tfrac12\,AB\cdot AD,$ so $AB=2s.$ Because it is $20\%$ of the square, $s\cdot AD=\tfrac15 s^2,$ so $AD=\tfrac{s}{5}.$

Therefore $$\frac{AB}{AD}=\frac{2s}{s/5}=10.$$

Thus, **E** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Area ratio
- Percentage

## Key idea

Use the shared 50% overlap to express the two rectangle areas in terms of the same intersection, then compute the requested ratio.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
