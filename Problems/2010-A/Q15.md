# AMC12 2010-A Q15

![[assets/problems/2010-A/Q15.png]]

## Problem

A coin is altered so that the probability that it lands on heads is less than $\frac12,$ and when the coin is flipped four times, the probability of an equal number of heads and tails is $\frac{1}{6}.$ What is the probability that the coin lands on heads?

(A) $\frac{\sqrt{15}-3}{6}$
(B) $\frac{6-\sqrt{6\sqrt{6}+2}}{12}$
(C) $\frac{\sqrt{2}-1}{2}$
(D) $\frac{3-\sqrt{3}}{6}$
(E) $\frac{\sqrt{3}-1}{2}$

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{3-\sqrt{3}}{6}$

## Solution

Let $p$ be the probability of heads. The chance of two heads and two tails in four flips is $$ \begin{aligned} \binom{4}{2}p^2(1-p)^2 &= 6p^2(1-p)^2 \\ &= \frac16. \end{aligned} $$

Thus $p^2(1-p)^2=\frac{1}{36},$ so $p(1-p)=\frac16.$

This gives $6p^2-6p+1=0,$ so $p=\frac{3\pm\sqrt3}{6}.$ Since $p\lt\frac12,$ we take $p=\frac{3-\sqrt3}{6}.$

Thus, **D** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Binomial probability
- Quadratic

## Key idea

Let the head probability be $p$; the four-flip tie probability is $6p^2(1-p)^2$, giving a quadratic equation with the required root.

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
