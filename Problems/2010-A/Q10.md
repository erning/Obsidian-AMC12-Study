# AMC12 2010-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2010-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

The first four terms of an arithmetic sequence are $p,$ $9,$ $3p-q,$ and $3p+q.$ What is the $2010$th term of this sequence?

(A) $8041$
(B) $8043$
(C) $8045$
(D) $8047$
(E) $8049$

## Answer

**A.** $8041$

## Solution

Consecutive terms differ by a common difference $d.$ From the last two terms, $d=(3p+q)-(3p-q)=2q.$

From the first two terms, $9-p=d=2q,$ and from the second and third, $(3p-q)-9=d=2q.$ Solving this system gives $p=5,$ $q=2,$ and $d=4.$

The $2010$th term is $$ \begin{aligned} p+2009d &= 5+2009\cdot4 \\ &= 8041. \end{aligned} $$

Thus, **A** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Arithmetic sequence
- System of equations

## Key idea

Use the arithmetic-sequence relations to express $p,q,r,s$ in terms of two variables, then impose the given product equation.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
