# AMC12 2010-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2010-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Logan is constructing a scaled model of his town. The city's water tower stands $40$ meters high, and the top portion is a sphere that holds $100,000$ liters of water. Logan's miniature water tower holds $0.1$ liters. How tall, in meters, should Logan make his tower?

(A) $0.04$
(B) $(0.4)/(π)$
(C) $0.4$
(D) $(4)/(π)$
(E) $4$

## Answer

**C.** $0.4$

## Solution

The miniature tower holds $$ \frac{100,000}{.1} = 1,000,000 $$ times less water than the actual tower. Since this is the ratio for volumes, the ratio of heights is $$ (1,000,000)^{1 / 3} = 100. $$ This means that the height of the miniature tower is $$ \frac{40}{100} = .4. $$

Thus, **C** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Power scaling of length, area, and volume
- Volume

## Key idea

Scale length, area, and volume by the model factor separately; the number of gallons is controlled by the volume scale.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
