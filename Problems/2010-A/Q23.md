# AMC12 2010-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2010-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

The number obtained from the last two nonzero digits of $90!$ is equal to $n.$ What is $n?$

(A) $12$
(B) $32$
(C) $48$
(D) $52$
(E) $68$

## Answer

**A.** $12$

## Solution

The number of trailing zeroes in $90!$ is $\left\lfloor\frac{90}{5}\right\rfloor+\left\lfloor\frac{90}{25}\right\rfloor=21.$ Let $N=\frac{90!}{10^{21}}.$

There are still more than two factors of $2$ left after removing $10^{21},$ so $N\equiv0 \pmod4.$

Let $A$ be the product of factors of $90!$ not divisible by $5,$ and let $B$ be the product of the factors divisible by $5.$ Grouping residues modulo $25$ gives $A\equiv1\pmod{25}$ and $\frac{B}{5^{21}}\equiv-1\pmod{25}.$

Therefore $\frac{90!}{5^{21}}\equiv-1\pmod{25}.$ Since $2^{21}\equiv2\pmod{25},$ $N=\frac{90!}{5^{21}\cdot2^{21}}\equiv-13\equiv12\pmod{25}.$

The number congruent to $0\pmod4$ and $12\pmod{25}$ is $12\pmod{100},$ so the last two nonzero digits form $12.$

Thus, **A** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Modular arithmetic
- Chinese remainder theorem
- Trailing zeros

## Key idea

Track powers of $2$ and $5$ in the factorial-like product after removing zeros; modular arithmetic determines the final two nonzero digits.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
