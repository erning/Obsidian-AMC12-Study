# AMC12 2010-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2010-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Halfway through a $100$-shot archery tournament, Chelsea leads by $50$ points. For each shot a bullseye scores $10$ points, with other possible scores being $8,$ $4,$ $2,$ and $0$ points. Chelsea always scores at least $4$ points on each shot. If Chelsea's next $n$ shots are bullseyes she will be guaranteed victory. What is the minimum value for $n?$

(A) $38$
(B) $40$
(C) $42$
(D) $44$
(E) $46$

## Answer

**C.** $42$

## Solution

The opponent can score at most $50\cdot10=500$ on the last $50$ shots. Since Chelsea leads by $50,$ she must score more than $500-50=450$ points on her remaining shots to guarantee victory.

Her $n$ bullseyes give $10n$ points, and her other $50-n$ shots give at least $4(50-n)$ points, so $$10n+4(50-n)\gt450.$$ This simplifies to $6n\gt250,$ i.e. $n\gt41\tfrac23.$

Therefore Chelsea needs at least $42$ bullseyes.

Thus, **C** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Inequality
- Bounding to limit cases

## Key idea

After 50 shots, maximize the total score by using the largest possible scores on the remaining shots and test the boundary case.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
