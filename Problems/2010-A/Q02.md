# AMC12 2010-A Q02

![[assets/problems/2010-A/Q02.png]]

## Problem

A ferry boat shuttles tourists to an island every hour starting at $10$ am until its last trip, which starts at $3$ pm. One day the boat captain notes that on the $10$ am trip there were $100$ tourists on the ferry boat, and that on each successive trip, the number of tourists was $1$ fewer than on the previous trip. How many tourists did the ferry take to the island that day?

(A) $585$
(B) $594$
(C) $672$
(D) $679$
(E) $694$

## Answer

**A.** $585$

## Solution

The ferry makes $6$ trips: at $10,$ $11,$ $12,$ $1,$ $2,$ and $3.$

The numbers of tourists are $100,99,98,97,96,95,$ so the total is $$ \begin{gathered} 6\cdot100-(1+2+3+4+5) \\ =600-15 \\ =585. \end{gathered} $$

Thus, **A** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Arithmetic sequence
- Summation

## Key idea

Model the ferry’s departures as an arithmetic sequence, then sum the fares for the trips that occur within the stated time window.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
