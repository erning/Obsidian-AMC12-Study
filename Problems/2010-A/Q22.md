# AMC12 2010-A Q22

![[assets/problems/2010-A/Q22.png]]

## Problem

What is the minimum value of $$ \begin{gathered} f(x) = |x-1|+|2x-1| \\ {}+|3x-1|+\cdots+|119x-1|? \end{gathered} $$

(A) $49$
(B) $50$
(C) $51$
(D) $52$
(E) $53$

## Answer

**A.** $49$

## Solution

The function $f$ is piecewise linear with breakpoints at $x=\tfrac1k.$ On the interval $\left[\tfrac1m,\tfrac1{m-1}\right]$ its slope is $$ \begin{aligned} &\sum_{k=m}^{119}k \\ &\quad {}-\sum_{k=1}^{m-1}k=7140-(m-1)m, \end{aligned} $$ where $7140=\tfrac{119\cdot120}{2}.$

This slope is zero when $(m-1)m=7140,$ i.e. $m=85,$ so the minimum occurs at the right endpoint $x=\tfrac1{84}.$

There, terms with $k\le84$ contribute $\tfrac{84-k}{84}$ and terms with $k\ge85$ contribute $\tfrac{k-84}{84},$ so $$ \begin{aligned} f\left(\tfrac1{84}\right) &= \frac{3486}{84}+\frac{630}{84} \\ &= 41.5+7.5=49. \end{aligned} $$

Thus, **A** is the correct answer.

## Key idea

Break the absolute-value sum into intervals at its integer breakpoints and minimize each linear piece, then add the resulting arithmetic series.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
