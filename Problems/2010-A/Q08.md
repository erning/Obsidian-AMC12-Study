# AMC12 2010-A Q08

![[assets/problems/2010-A/Q08.png]]

## Problem

Triangle $ABC$ has $AB=2 \cdot AC.$ Let $D$ and $E$ be on $\overline{AB}$ and $\overline{BC},$ respectively, such that $\angle BAE = \angle ACD.$ Let $F$ be the intersection of segments $AE$ and $CD,$ and suppose that $\triangle CFE$ is equilateral. What is $\angle ACB?$

(A) $60^\circ$
(B) $75^\circ$
(C) $90^\circ$
(D) $105^\circ$
(E) $120^\circ$

## Answer

**C.** $90°$

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2010-A/Q08-draft.svg]]

Let $\angle BAE = \angle ACD = x.$ Note that $\angle CFE = 60^{\circ}$ since $\triangle CFE$ is equilateral.

We then have that $$ \begin{aligned} \angle AFC = 180^{\circ} &- \angle CFE \\ &= 120^{\circ}. \end{aligned} $$

Then: $$\begin{align*} \angle FAC &= 180^{\circ} - 120^{\circ} - x\\ &=60^{\circ} - x \\ &= \angle EAC.\end{align*} $$

We then get that $$ \begin{align*} \angle BAC &= \angle BAE + \angle EAC\\ &= x + 60^{\circ} - x \\&= 60^{\circ}. \end{align*}$$

Since $AB = 2 \cdot AC$ and $\angle BAC = 60^{\circ},$ we have that $\triangle ABC$ is a $30-60-90$ triangle.

Thus, **C** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Angle chasing
- Equilateral triangle

## Key idea

Transfer the equal-angle information to an equilateral triangle and use a $30$-$60$-$90$ triangle to relate the side lengths.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
