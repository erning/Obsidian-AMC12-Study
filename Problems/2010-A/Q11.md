## Problem

The solution of the equation $7^{x+7}=8^x$ can be expressed in the form $x=\log_b 7^7.$ What is $b?$

(A) $\frac{7}{15}$
(B) $\frac{7}{8}$
(C) $\frac{8}{7}$
(D) $\frac{15}{8}$
(E) $\frac{15}{7}$

## Answer

**C.** $\frac{8}{7}$

## Solution

Since $x=\log_b 7^7,$ we have $b^x=7^7.$

Then $$ \begin{aligned} (7b)^x=7^x\cdot b^x &= 7^x\cdot7^7 \\ &= 7^{x+7}=8^x. \end{aligned} $$

Because $x\gt0,$ it follows that $7b=8,$ so $b=\frac{8}{7}.$

Thus, **C** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm
- Exponent

## Key idea

Take logarithms of both sides and solve the resulting linear equation in $x$.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
