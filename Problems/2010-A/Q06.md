# AMC12 2010-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2010-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

A palindrome, such as $83438,$ is a number that remains the same when its digits are reversed. The numbers $x$ and $x + 32$ are three-digit and four-digit palindromes, respectively. What is the sum of the digits of $x?$

(A) $20$
(B) $21$
(C) $22$
(D) $23$
(E) $24$

## Answer

**E.** $24$

## Solution

Note that $x$ is at most $999.$ This means that $x + 32$ has a maximum of $1031.$

Similarly, we have that the minimum value of $x + 32$ is $1000.$

The only palindrome in this range is $1001,$ so this is what $x + 32$ equals.

Then $$ x + 32 = 1001 $$$$ x = 969. $$

The sum of the digits is then $$ 9 + 6 + 9 = 24. $$

Thus, **E** is the correct answer.

## Knowledge points

- Palindrome
- Digits
- Bounding to limit cases

## Key idea

Bound $x + 32$ between $1000$ and $1031;$ the only palindrome in that range is $1001,$ which determines $x.$

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
