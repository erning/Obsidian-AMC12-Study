# AMC12 2024-A Q08

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q08.png]]

## Problem

How many angles $\theta$ with $0\le\theta\le2\pi$ satisfy

$$
\log(\sin 3\theta)+\log(\cos 2\theta)=0?
$$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $0$**

## Solution

The logarithms require both sine and cosine factors to be positive. Combining the logarithms gives

$$
\log\bigl(\sin(3\theta)\cos(2\theta)\bigr)=0,
$$

so

$$
\sin(3\theta)\cos(2\theta)=1.
$$

Each factor is at most $1$. Their product can equal $1$ only if both factors equal $1$:

$$
\sin(3\theta)=1,\qquad \cos(2\theta)=1.
$$

But $\cos(2\theta)=1$ forces $\theta\in\{0,\pi,2\pi\}$, and then $\sin(3\theta)=0$, not $1$. Therefore there are $\boxed0$ solutions.

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm laws
- Domain restrictions
- Trigonometric bounds

## Key idea

The product of two numbers at most $1$ can equal $1$ only when both are exactly $1$.

## Common mistakes

- Ignoring the positivity requirements of the logarithms.
- Treating $\log u+\log v=0$ as $u+v=1$.
