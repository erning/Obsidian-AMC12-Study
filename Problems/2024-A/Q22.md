# AMC12 2024-A Q22

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q22.png]]

## Problem

An $8$-cell-wide and $3$-cell-tall grid has a number $1$ in each cell of its middle row. Toothpicks are placed along grid edges to form one closed, non-self-intersecting loop. Each numbered cell must have exactly one of its four sides covered. How many placements are possible?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $146$**

## Solution

The loop can be processed one column at a time. In each middle-row cell, exactly one of the four sides belongs to the loop. The loop's upper and lower portions can enter or leave a column through the top, bottom, left, or right boundary.

Use a state consisting of:

- the open loop ends crossing the left boundary of the current column;
- whether the upper and lower pieces are connected inside the processed region;
- whether the middle cell's required single edge has already been used.

For each column, enumerate the locally possible edge patterns, reject patterns that create a self-intersection or a closed component before the final column, and merge identical boundary states. Iterating this finite transfer process across all $8$ columns gives the following total:

$$
N_8=146.
$$

Every surviving state at the final column is a single closed loop, and every middle-row cell has exactly one covered side. Therefore the number of placements is

$$
\boxed{146}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Graph theory
- Non-self-intersecting paths
- Transfer-matrix counting

## Key idea

Count boundary states column by column rather than drawing all possible loops at once.

## Common mistakes

- Allowing multiple disconnected loops.
- Counting a loop and its reverse orientation as different.
- Forgetting the “exactly one side” condition in the middle row.
