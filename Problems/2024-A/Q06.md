# AMC12 2024-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

The product of three integers is $60$. What is the least possible positive sum of the three integers?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $3$**

## Solution

For a positive product and a potentially small sum, use two negative integers and one positive integer. Write the numbers as $-p,-q,r$, where $pqr=60$. The sum is

$$
r-p-q.
$$

The choice

$$
(-1,-6,10)
$$

has product $60$ and sum

$$
10-1-6=3.
$$

To see that no smaller positive sum is possible, enumerate the factor triples of $60$ with two negative factors. The alternatives either give a nonpositive sum or a sum at least $3$; all-positive triples are much larger. Hence the least positive sum is $\boxed3$.

## Knowledge points

- Factorization
- Sign analysis
- Finite casework

## Key idea

Two negative factors allow the sum to be small while preserving a positive product.

## Common mistakes

- Restricting all three integers to be positive.
- Confusing the least sum with the least positive sum.
