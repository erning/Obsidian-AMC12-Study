# AMC12 2024-A Q12

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q12.png]]

## Problem

The first three terms of a geometric sequence are the integers $a$, $720$, and $b$, where $a<720<b$. What is the sum of the digits of the least possible value of $b$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $21$**

## Solution

For three consecutive terms of a geometric sequence,

$$
720^2=ab.
$$

Therefore $b=518400/a$. To minimize $b$, maximize the divisor $a<720$ of $518400$.

Since

$$
518400=2^8\cdot3^4\cdot5^2,
$$

the largest divisor below $720$ is

$$
a=675=3^3\cdot5^2.
$$

Hence

$$
b=\frac{518400}{675}=768,
$$

whose digit sum is

$$
7+6+8=\boxed{21}.
$$

## Key idea

The middle term fixes the product $ab$, so minimizing $b$ means finding the largest valid divisor $a<720$.

## Common mistakes

- Using an arithmetic-sequence relation instead of $720^2=ab$.
- Forgetting the integer condition.
