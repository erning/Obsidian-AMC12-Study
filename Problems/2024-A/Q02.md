# AMC12 2024-A Q02

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q02.png]]

## Problem

A hiking-time model has the form

$$
T=aL+bG,
$$

where $T$ is measured in minutes, $L$ is trail length in miles, and $G$ is altitude gain in feet. The model gives $69$ minutes for both $(L,G)=(1.5,800)$ and $(1.2,1100)$. What does it give for $(L,G)=(4.2,4000)$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $246$**

## Solution

The two known cases give

$$
1.5a+800b=69,
\qquad
1.2a+1100b=69.
$$

Subtracting the equations,

$$
0.3a-300b=0,
\qquad
a=1000b.
$$

Substitute into the first equation:

$$
1.5(1000b)+800b=69,
$$

so

$$
2300b=69,\qquad b=0.03,\qquad a=30.
$$

For the requested trail,

$$
T=30(4.2)+0.03(4000)=126+120=\boxed{246}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Systems of linear equations
- Linear modeling
- Substitution

## Key idea

Use the two equal-time observations to determine the two model constants.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $G$ as measured in thousands of feet.
- Subtracting the equations with inconsistent units.
