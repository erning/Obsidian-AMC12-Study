# AMC12 2024-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Find the value of

$$
\tan^2\frac{\pi}{16}\tan^2\frac{3\pi}{16}
+\tan^2\frac{\pi}{16}\tan^2\frac{5\pi}{16}
+\tan^2\frac{3\pi}{16}\tan^2\frac{7\pi}{16}
+\tan^2\frac{5\pi}{16}\tan^2\frac{7\pi}{16}.
$$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $68$**

## Solution

Set

$$
a=\tan^2\frac{\pi}{16},\quad
b=\tan^2\frac{3\pi}{16},\quad
c=\tan^2\frac{5\pi}{16},\quad
d=\tan^2\frac{7\pi}{16}.
$$

The expression factors:

$$
ab+ac+bd+cd=(a+d)(b+c).
$$

Since $\tan(7\pi/16)=\cot(\pi/16)$,

$$
a+d=\tan^2\theta+\cot^2\theta
=\frac4{\sin^2(2\theta)}-2
=14+8\sqrt2,
$$

where $\theta=\pi/16$. Similarly,

$$
b+c=14-8\sqrt2.
$$

Therefore

$$
(a+d)(b+c)
=(14+8\sqrt2)(14-8\sqrt2)
=196-128=\boxed{68}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Factoring
- Complementary-angle tangent identities
- Special-angle trigonometry

## Key idea

Regroup the four products into $(a+d)(b+c)$.

## Common mistakes

- Pairing the terms incorrectly.
- Using $\tan(7\pi/16)=\tan(\pi/16)$ instead of the cotangent relation.
