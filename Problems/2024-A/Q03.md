# AMC12 2024-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

The number $2024$ is written as a sum of, not necessarily distinct, two-digit numbers. What is the least number of two-digit numbers needed?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $21$**

## Solution

Every two-digit number is at most $99$. If $k$ such numbers are used, their sum is at most $99k$. Since

$$
99\cdot20=1980<2024,
$$

at least $21$ numbers are necessary.

This bound is attainable:

$$
20\cdot99+44=1980+44=2024.
$$

Thus the least possible number is $\boxed{21}$.

## Key idea

Prove a lower bound using the largest allowed summand, then construct an example attaining it.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting that two-digit numbers may repeat.
- Using $100$ instead of the maximum two-digit number $99$.
