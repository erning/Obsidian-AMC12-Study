# AMC12 2024-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

A set of $12$ tokens consists of $3$ red, $2$ white, $1$ blue, and $6$ black tokens. They are distributed at random to $3$ players, $4$ tokens per player. The probability that one player gets all red tokens, another gets all white tokens, and the remaining player gets the blue token is $\frac{n}{m}$ in lowest terms. Find $m+n$.

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $389$**

## Solution

Treat the tokens as distinct. The total number of equal-size deals is

$$
\binom{12}{4,4,4}=\frac{12!}{4!4!4!}=34650.
$$

For a favorable deal, assign the red, white, and blue roles to the players in $3!=6$ ways. The red, white, and blue players then need respectively $1$, $2$, and $3$ black tokens. The black tokens can be distributed in

$$
\binom61\binom52\binom33=60
$$

ways.

Thus

$$
\Pr=\frac{6\cdot60}{34650}=\frac4{385}.
$$

Therefore

$$
m+n=385+4=\boxed{389}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Multinomial counting
- Combinations
- Simplifying rational probabilities

## Key idea

Assign the colored-token roles first, then fill the remaining spaces with black tokens.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting the $3!$ assignment of roles to players.
- Mixing distinct-token and indistinguishable-token counting.
