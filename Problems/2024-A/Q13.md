# AMC12 2024-A Q13

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q13.png]]

## Problem

The graph of

$$
y=e^{x+1}+e^{-x}-2
$$

has an axis of symmetry. What is the reflection of $\left(-1,\frac12\right)$ over this axis?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $\left(0,\frac12\right)$**

## Solution

The axis is the vertical line through the minimum. Differentiate:

$$
\frac{dy}{dx}=e^{x+1}-e^{-x}.
$$

At the minimum,

$$
e^{x+1}=e^{-x}
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
x=-\frac12.
$$

Thus the axis is $x=-\frac12$. Reflecting $x=-1$ across this line gives

$$
x'=2\left(-\frac12\right)-(-1)=0.
$$

The $y$-coordinate is unchanged, so the image is

$$
\boxed{\left(0,\frac12\right)}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Axis of symmetry
- Exponential functions
- Reflection across a vertical line

## Key idea

The two exponential terms balance at the minimum, determining the symmetry axis.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the axis is $x=-1$.
- Changing the $y$-coordinate under a vertical reflection.
