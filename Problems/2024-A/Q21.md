# AMC12 2024-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Suppose $a_1=2$ and

$$
\frac{a_n-1}{n-1}=\frac{a_{n-1}+1}{n}
$$

for all $n\ge2$. What is

$$
\left\lfloor\sum_{n=1}^{100}a_n^2\right\rfloor?
$$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $338551$**

## Solution

Rearrange the recurrence:

$$
n(a_n-1)=(n-1)(a_{n-1}+1).
$$

Thus

$$
a_n=1+\frac{n-1}{n}(a_{n-1}+1).
$$

The formula

$$
a_n=n+\frac1n
$$

fits $a_1=2$. Substitution verifies it:

$$
1+\frac{n-1}{n}\left(n-1+\frac1{n-1}+1\right)
=1+\frac{n-1}{n}\left(n+\frac1{n-1}\right)
=n+\frac1n.
$$

Therefore

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{100}a_n^2
=\sum_{n=1}^{100}\left(n+\frac1n\right)^2
=\sum_{n=1}^{100}n^2+200+\sum_{n=1}^{100}\frac1{n^2}.
$$

Now

$$
\sum_{n=1}^{100}n^2=\frac{100\cdot101\cdot201}{6}=338350,
$$

and

$$
1<\sum_{n=1}^{100}\frac1{n^2}<2.
$$

Hence the total lies strictly between $338551$ and $338552$, so its floor is

$$
\boxed{338551}.
$$

## Key idea

Guess and verify a simple closed form, then isolate the small fractional remainder.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting the floor function.
- Treating $\sum 1/n^2$ as exactly $1$.
