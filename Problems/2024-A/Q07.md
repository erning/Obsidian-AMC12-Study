# AMC12 2024-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

In right isosceles triangle $ABC$, $\angle ABC=90^\circ$ and $BA=BC=\sqrt2$. Points $P_1,\ldots,P_{2024}$ divide the hypotenuse $AC$ into $2025$ equal segments. What is the length of

$$
\overrightarrow{BP_1}+\overrightarrow{BP_2}+\cdots+\overrightarrow{BP_{2024}}?
$$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $2024$**

## Solution

The points $P_k$ are symmetric about the midpoint $M$ of $AC$. Pair $P_k$ with $P_{2025-k}$. Then

$$
\overrightarrow{BP_k}+\overrightarrow{BP_{2025-k}}
=2\overrightarrow{BM}.
$$

There are $2024$ points, so the complete vector sum is

$$
2024\overrightarrow{BM}.
$$

Because $BA=BC=\sqrt2$,

$$
AC=\sqrt{(\sqrt2)^2+(\sqrt2)^2}=2.
$$

The median from the right angle to the hypotenuse has length half the hypotenuse, so $BM=1$. Therefore the requested length is

$$
2024\cdot1=\boxed{2024}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Vector sums
- Symmetry and midpoint pairing
- Median of a right triangle

## Key idea

Pair symmetric points before attempting coordinate calculations.

## Common mistakes

- Pairing $P_k$ with $P_{2024-k}$ instead of $P_{2025-k}$.
- Forgetting that vector sums are directed.
