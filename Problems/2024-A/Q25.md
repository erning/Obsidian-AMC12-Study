# AMC12 2024-A Q25

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q25.png]]

## Problem

For how many integer quadruples $(a,b,c,d)$, where $|a|,|b|,|c|,|d|\le5$ and $c,d$ are not both $0$, is the graph of

$$
y=\frac{ax+b}{cx+d}
$$

symmetric about the line $y=x$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $1292$**

## Solution

Reflection in $y=x$ sends a function to its inverse. A non-identity fractional linear transformation is its own inverse exactly when its matrix

$$
\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{pmatrix}
$$

has trace zero:

$$
a+d=0,
$$

and nonzero determinant. With $d=-a$, the determinant condition becomes

$$
ad-bc=-a^2-bc\ne0,
\qquad\text{or}\qquad
a^2+bc\ne0.
$$

There are $11^3=1331$ choices for $(a,b,c)$ in $[-5,5]^3$. Count the invalid choices satisfying $a^2+bc=0$:

- $a=0$: $bc=0$, giving $21$ pairs.
- $a=\pm1$: $bc=-1$, giving $4$ pairs in total.
- $a=\pm2$: $bc=-4$, giving $12$ pairs in total.
- $a=\pm3$: $bc=-9$, giving $4$ pairs in total.
- $a=\pm4$: $bc=-16$, giving $4$ pairs in total.
- $a=\pm5$: $bc=-25$, giving $4$ pairs in total.

Thus the non-identity count is

$$
1331-(21+4+12+4+4+4)=1282.
$$

The identity function occurs when $b=c=0$ and $a=d\ne0$, giving $10$ additional quadruples. Therefore the total is

$$
1282+10=\boxed{1292}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Fractional linear transformations
- Inverse functions
- Symmetry and finite counting

## Key idea

Self-inversion corresponds to a trace-zero matrix, with the identity handled separately.

## Common mistakes

- Counting singular transformations whose graphs are not valid functions.
- Forgetting the identity cases $a=d\ne0$, $b=c=0$.
