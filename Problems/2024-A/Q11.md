# AMC12 2024-A Q11

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q11.png]]

## Problem

There are exactly $K$ positive integers $b$ with $5\le b\le2024$ such that the base-$b$ integer $2024_b$ is divisible by $16$. What is the sum of the digits of $K$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $20$**

## Solution

The base-$b$ numeral is

$$
2024_b=2b^3+0b^2+2b+4=2(b^3+b+2).
$$

For it to be divisible by $16$, we need

$$
b^3+b+2\equiv0\pmod8.
$$

Checking the eight residue classes modulo $8$ gives

$$
b\equiv3,6,7\pmod8.
$$

The values in $[5,2024]$ are:

- $b\equiv3$: $11,19,\ldots,2019$, giving $252$ values.
- $b\equiv6$: $6,14,\ldots,2022$, giving $253$ values.
- $b\equiv7$: $7,15,\ldots,2023$, giving $253$ values.

Thus $K=252+253+253=758$, and its digit sum is

$$
7+5+8=\boxed{20}.
$$

## Key idea

Convert the base-$b$ numeral into a polynomial in $b$.

## Common mistakes

- Reading $2024_b$ as decimal $2024$.
- Forgetting the factor $2$ when applying divisibility by $16$.
