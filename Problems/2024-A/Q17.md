# AMC12 2024-A Q17

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Integers $a,b,c$ satisfy

$$
ab+c=100,\qquad bc+a=87,\qquad ca+b=60.
$$

What is $ab+bc+ca$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $276$**

## Solution

Subtract pairs of equations:

$$
(ab+c)-(bc+a)=13
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
(a-c)(b-1)=13,
$$

$$
(ab+c)-(ca+b)=40
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
(a-1)(b-c)=40,
$$

and

$$
(bc+a)-(ca+b)=27
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
(b-a)(c-1)=27.
$$

Because $13$ is prime, $(a-c)(b-1)=13$ has only four same-sign factorizations: $(a-c,b-1)\in\{(1,13),(13,1),(-1,-13),(-13,-1)\}$. For each, solve the other two factored equations for a candidate $(a,b,c)$, then check it in the original equation $ab+c=100$ (the factored system alone is underdetermined, so this check is essential); only one survives:

$$
(a,b,c)=(-9,-12,-8).
$$

Indeed, the original equations become $108-8=100$, $96-9=87$, and $72-12=60$. Hence

$$
ab+bc+ca=108+96+72=\boxed{276}.
$$

- Subtracting nonlinear equations

## Key idea

Pairwise subtraction factors the system and uses the primality of $13$.

## Common mistakes

- Checking a factor-case solution only in the subtracted equations.
- Assuming $a,b,c$ are positive.
