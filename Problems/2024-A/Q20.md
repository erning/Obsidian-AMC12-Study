# AMC12 2024-A Q20

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Points $P$ and $Q$ are chosen uniformly and independently on sides $AB$ and $AC$ of an equilateral triangle. Which interval contains the probability that the area of $\triangle APQ$ is less than half the area of $\triangle ABC$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $\left(\frac34,\frac78\right]$**

## Solution

Let

$$
x=\frac{AP}{AB},\qquad y=\frac{AQ}{AC}.
$$

Both $x$ and $y$ are independent uniform random variables on $[0,1]$. Since $\triangle APQ$ and $\triangle ABC$ share the included angle at $A$,

$$
\frac{[APQ]}{[ABC]}=xy.
$$

We want $\Pr(xy<1/2)$. It is easier to compute the complement:

$$
\Pr\left(xy\ge\frac12\right)
=\int_{1/2}^1\left(1-\frac1{2x}\right)\,dx
=\frac12-\frac12\ln2.
$$

Therefore

$$
\Pr\left(xy<\frac12\right)
=\frac12+\frac12\ln2
\approx0.8466.
$$

Since

$$
\frac34<0.8466\le\frac78,
$$

the probability lies in $\boxed{\left(\frac34,\frac78\right]}$.

## Key idea

The area ratio is the product of two independent uniform variables.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the area ratio is uniformly distributed.
- Computing $xy<1/2$ directly without identifying the curved boundary $y=1/(2x)$.
