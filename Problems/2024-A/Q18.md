# AMC12 2024-A Q18

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q18.png]]

## Problem

A rectangular card has side lengths $1$ and $2+\sqrt3$. Identical cards are placed successively so that consecutive diagonals line up, with each new card rotated clockwise. How many cards are needed until a vertex of a new card lands exactly on the vertex labeled $B$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $6$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q18-draft.svg]]

Let $\theta$ be the angle the diagonal makes with the long side. Then

$$
\tan\theta=\frac{1}{2+\sqrt3}=2-\sqrt3=\tan15^\circ.
$$

Therefore each successive card is rotated by $15^\circ$ about the common center of the circle containing the equal diagonals.

The target vertex $B$ is reached after a total rotation of $90^\circ$. Hence the number of cards is

$$
\frac{90^\circ}{15^\circ}=\boxed6.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Special-angle identities
- Rotations
- Chords of a circle

## Key idea

The shared diagonal fixes the rotation angle between consecutive cards.

## Common mistakes

- Using the angle of the diagonal with the short side.
- Counting rotations instead of total cards.
