# AMC12 2024-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

Let $\alpha$ be the smallest angle in a $3$-$4$-$5$ right triangle, and let $\beta$ be the smallest angle in a $7$-$24$-$25$ right triangle. In terms of $\alpha$, what is $\beta$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $\frac{\pi}{2}-2\alpha$**

## Solution

For the smallest angle $\alpha$ in a $3$-$4$-$5$ triangle,

$$
\tan\alpha=\frac34.
$$

Then

$$
\tan(2\alpha)
=\frac{2\tan\alpha}{1-\tan^2\alpha}
=\frac{2\cdot\frac34}{1-\frac9{16}}
=\frac{24}{7}.
$$

The smallest angle $\beta$ in a $7$-$24$-$25$ triangle satisfies

$$
\tan\beta=\frac7{24}.
$$

Since $\tan(2\alpha)=24/7$, the acute complementary angle has tangent $7/24$. Hence

$$
\beta=\frac{\pi}{2}-2\alpha.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Tangent in right triangles
- Double-angle identity
- Complementary angles

## Key idea

The tangent of twice the smaller $3$-$4$-$5$ angle is the reciprocal of the tangent of $\beta$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $\tan\alpha=4/3$ for the smallest angle.
- Choosing an obtuse angle instead of the acute $\beta$.
