# AMC12 2024-A Q24

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q24.png]]

## Problem

A disphenoid is a tetrahedron whose four triangular faces are congruent. What is the least total surface area of a disphenoid whose faces are scalene triangles with integer side lengths?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $15\sqrt7$**

## Solution

A disphenoid with a given face triangle exists exactly when the common face triangle is acute. Since all four faces are congruent, the total surface area is four times the area of one face.

We therefore seek the smallest-area acute scalene integer triangle. Small candidates fail:

- $(2,3,4)$ is obtuse because $2^2+3^2<4^2$.
- $(3,5,6)$ is obtuse because $3^2+5^2<6^2$.
- $(3,4,5)$ is right and produces a degenerate limiting disphenoid.

The triangle $(4,5,6)$ is acute because

$$
4^2+5^2>6^2.
$$

Its semiperimeter is $15/2$, so Heron's formula gives

$$
\begin{aligned}
K
&=\sqrt{\frac{15}{2}\cdot\frac72\cdot\frac52\cdot\frac32}\\
&=\frac{15\sqrt7}{4}.
\end{aligned}
$$

The total surface area is

$$
4K=\boxed{15\sqrt7}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Acute-triangle condition
- Heron's formula
- Three-dimensional geometry

## Key idea

Minimize one common face area, then multiply by four.

## Common mistakes

- Using a right triangle, which gives a degenerate disphenoid.
- Forgetting the four congruent faces.
