# AMC12 2024-A Q04

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q04.png]]

## Problem

What is the least value of $n$ such that $n!$ is a multiple of $2024$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $23$**

## Solution

Factor $2024$:

$$
2024=8\cdot253=2^3\cdot11\cdot23.
$$

For $n!$ to be divisible by $2024$, it must contain a factor of $23$. No factorial $n!$ with $n<23$ contains $23$, so $n\ge23$.

At $n=23$, the factor $23$ is present, the factor $11$ is present, and $23!$ contains far more than three factors of $2$. Hence $23!$ is divisible by $2^3\cdot11\cdot23$.

Therefore the least value is $\boxed{23}$.

## Knowledge points

- Prime factorization
- Divisibility of factorials

## Key idea

The largest prime factor, $23$, forces the lower bound immediately.

## Common mistakes

- Checking only whether $2024\le n!$.
- Forgetting that the prime factor $23$ must itself appear.
