# AMC12 2024-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

A data set contains $20$ numbers, some of which are $6$, and has mean $45$. When all the $6$s are removed, the remaining data set has mean $66$. How many $6$s were in the original data set?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $7$**

## Solution

Let $k$ be the number of $6$s. The original sum is

$$
20\cdot45=900.
$$

After removing the $k$ sixes, there are $20-k$ numbers with sum $900-6k$. Their mean is $66$, so

$$
900-6k=66(20-k).
$$

Solving,

$$
900-6k=1320-66k,
\qquad
60k=420,
\qquad
k=\boxed7.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Arithmetic mean
- Translating word conditions into equations

## Key idea

Track the total sum before and after removing the repeated values.

## Common mistakes

- Removing $6k$ from the number of data points instead of from the sum.
- Using $20-k$ as the original count.
