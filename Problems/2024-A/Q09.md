# AMC12 2024-A Q09

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q09.png]]

## Problem

Let $M$ be the greatest integer such that both $M+1213$ and $M+3773$ are perfect squares. What is the units digit of $M$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $8$**

## Solution

Write

$$
M+1213=a^2,\qquad M+3773=b^2.
$$

Subtracting gives

$$
b^2-a^2=2560,
\qquad
(b-a)(b+a)=2560.
$$

The two factors have the same parity, so both are even. To maximize $M=a^2-1213$, maximize $a$, which means make $b-a$ as small as possible. The smallest possible positive even factor is $2$, giving

$$
b-a=2,\qquad b+a=1280.
$$

Thus $a=639$, and

$$
M=639^2-1213=408321-1213=407108.
$$

The units digit is $\boxed{8}$.

## Knowledge points

- Difference of squares
- Factor pairs
- Optimization

## Key idea

Maximizing the smaller square root is equivalent to minimizing the positive factor $b-a$.

## Common mistakes

- Maximizing $b$ instead of $a$.
- Allowing factor pairs of different parity.
