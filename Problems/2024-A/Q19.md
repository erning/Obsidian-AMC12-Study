# AMC12 2024-A Q19

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q19.png]]

## Problem

Cyclic quadrilateral $ABCD$ has $BC=CD=3$, $DA=5$, and $\angle CDA=120^\circ$. What is the length of the shorter diagonal?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $\frac{39}{7}$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q19-draft.svg]]

In triangle $ACD$, the Law of Cosines gives

$$
AC^2=3^2+5^2-2(3)(5)\cos120^\circ
=9+25+15=49.
$$

Thus $AC=7$.

Opposite angles in a cyclic quadrilateral are supplementary, so

$$
\angle ABC=180^\circ-120^\circ=60^\circ.
$$

In triangle $ABC$, let $AB=x$. Then

$$
7^2=x^2+3^2-2(3)x\cos60^\circ
=x^2+9-3x.
$$

The positive solution is $x=8$.

Ptolemy's theorem gives

$$
AC\cdot BD=AB\cdot CD+BC\cdot DA
=8\cdot3+3\cdot5=39.
$$

Hence

$$
BD=\frac{39}{7}<7=AC,
$$

so the shorter diagonal is $\boxed{\frac{39}{7}}$.

## Knowledge points

- Cyclic quadrilaterals
- Law of Cosines
- Ptolemy's theorem

## Key idea

Find one diagonal using the given $120^\circ$ angle, then use Ptolemy for the other.

## Common mistakes

- Using $120^\circ$ instead of the supplementary angle in triangle $ABC$.
- Forgetting to compare the two diagonal lengths.
