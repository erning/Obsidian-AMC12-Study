# AMC12 2024-A Q15

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q15.png]]

## Problem

The roots of

$$
P(x)=x^3+2x^2-x+3
$$

are $p,q,r$. What is

$$
(p^2+4)(q^2+4)(r^2+4)?
$$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $125$**

## Solution

Since

$$
p^2+4=(p-2i)(p+2i),
$$

and similarly for $q$ and $r$,

$$
\prod_{t\in\{p,q,r\}}(t^2+4)=P(2i)P(-2i).
$$

Compute:

$$
P(2i)=-5-10i,\qquad P(-2i)=-5+10i.
$$

Therefore

$$
P(2i)P(-2i)=(-5)^2+10^2=\boxed{125}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Polynomial evaluation
- Complex conjugates
- Roots and factorization

## Key idea

Evaluate at $\pm2i$ instead of solving for the roots.

## Common mistakes

- Trying to find the cubic roots.
- Using $P(2)P(-2)$ instead of $P(2i)P(-2i)$.
