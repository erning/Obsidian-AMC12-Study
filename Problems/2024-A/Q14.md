# AMC12 2024-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2024-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

The entries in each row and each column of a $5\times5$ integer array form arithmetic progressions. The entries at positions $(5,5)$, $(2,4)$, $(4,3)$, and $(3,1)$ are $0$, $48$, $16$, and $12$, respectively. What is the entry at $(1,2)$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $29$**

## Solution

If every row and column is an arithmetic progression, the entry in row $i$, column $j$ has the form

$$
f(i,j)=\alpha+\beta i+\gamma j+\delta ij.
$$

The four known entries yield

$$
\begin{aligned}
\alpha+5\beta+5\gamma+25\delta&=0,\\
\alpha+2\beta+4\gamma+8\delta&=48,\\
\alpha+4\beta+3\gamma+12\delta&=16,\\
\alpha+3\beta+\gamma+3\delta&=12.
\end{aligned}
$$

Solving gives

$$
\alpha=-10,\qquad\beta=5,\qquad\gamma=22,\qquad\delta=-5.
$$

Therefore

$$
f(1,2)=-10+5+44-10=\boxed{29}.
$$

## Key idea

The row and column common differences may vary, which creates the mixed term $\delta ij$.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the entire array has one common difference.
- Omitting the $ij$ term.
