# AMC12 2013-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2013-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Tom, Dorothy, and Sammy went on a vacation and agreed to split the costs evenly. During their trip Tom paid $105, Dorothy paid $125, and Sammy paid $175. In order to share the costs equally, Tom gave Sammy $t$ dollars, and Dorothy gave Sammy $d$ dollars. What is $t-d$?

## Answer

**B.** $20$

## Solution

The total cost is
$$105+125+175=405,$$
so each person's fair share is
$$\frac{405}{3}=135.$$

Tom paid $30$ less than his share and therefore gives Sammy $t=30$. Dorothy gives $d=10$. Hence
$$t-d=30-10=20.$$

## Knowledge points

- Averages
- Equal sharing
- Linear arithmetic

## Key idea

Compute the common share first, then compare each payment with it.

## Common mistakes

- Using the average of the three payments incorrectly
- Reversing $t-d$ and $d-t$
