# AMC12 2013-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2013-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

A flower bouquet contains pink roses, red roses, pink carnations, and red carnations. One third of the pink flowers are roses, three fourths of the red flowers are carnations, and six tenths of the flowers are pink. What percent of the flowers are carnations?

## Answer

**E.** $70\%$

## Solution

Take the total number of flowers to be $10$ units. Pink flowers account for $6$ units and red flowers for $4$ units.

One third of the pink flowers are roses, so two thirds are carnations. Pink carnations therefore account for
$$\frac23\cdot 6=4$$
units. Red carnations account for
$$\frac34\cdot 4=3$$
units. Thus carnations make up $7$ of the $10$ units, or
$$70\%.$$

## Knowledge points

- Percentages
- Complementary fractions

## Key idea

Separate the flowers into pink and red groups before applying the given fractions.

## Common mistakes

- Treating one third of all flowers as pink roses
- Applying the red-carnation fraction to the whole bouquet
