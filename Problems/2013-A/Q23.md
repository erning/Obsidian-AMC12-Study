# AMC12 2013-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2013-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

$ABCD$ is a square of side length $\sqrt3+1$. Point $P$ is on $\overline{AC}$ such that $AP=\sqrt2$. The square is rotated $90^\circ$ counterclockwise about $P$, sweeping out a region whose area is $\frac1c(a\pi+b)$, where $a$, $b$, and $c$ are positive integers with $\gcd(a,b,c)=1$. What is $a+b+c$?

## Answer

**C.** $19$

## Solution

Let $A'$, $B'$, $C'$, and $D'$ be the rotated images of the vertices. The swept region decomposes into four circular sectors and four triangles.

The diagonal of the square is $\sqrt2(\sqrt3+1)=\sqrt6+\sqrt2$, so
$$AP=\sqrt2,qquad PC=\sqrt6.$$
Thus the sectors at $A$ and $C$ have areas
$$\frac{\pi}{2}\quad\text{and}\quad\frac{3\pi}{2}.$$
Also $PB=2$, and the two $60^\circ$ sectors along $BC$ each have area
$$\frac{60^\circ}{360^\circ}\pi(2)^2=\frac{2\pi}{3}.$$
The four triangular pieces together have area
$$\left(\sqrt3-1\right)+\left(3-\sqrt3\right)=2.$$

Therefore the swept area is
$$
\frac\pi2+\frac{3\pi}{2}+2\cdot\frac{2\pi}{3}+2
=\frac{10\pi+6}{3}.
$$
So $(a,b,c)=(10,6,3)$ and
$$a+b+c=19.$$

## Knowledge points

- Rotations
- Circular sectors
- Area decomposition

## Key idea

Track the vertex orbits under rotation and partition the swept region into sectors and triangles.

## Common mistakes

- Using the side length instead of the distance from $P$ to each vertex
- Counting the sector angles as all $90^\circ$
