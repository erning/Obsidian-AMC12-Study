# AMC12 2013-A Q02

![[assets/problems/2013-A/Q02.png]]

## Problem

A softball team played ten games, scoring $1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9,$ and $10$ runs. They lost by one run in exactly five games. In each of the other games, they scored twice as many runs as their opponent. How many total runs did their opponents score?

## Answer

**C.** $45$

## Solution

In a game where the team scored twice as many runs as its opponent, its score must be even. Thus the five such games have scores $2,4,6,8,10$, and the opponents scored
$$1+2+3+4+5=15.$$

The remaining scores $1,3,5,7,9$ are one-run losses, so the opponents scored $2,4,6,8,10$, totaling $30$. Hence the total is
$$15+30=45.$$

## Knowledge points

- Parity
- Casework
- Sum of consecutive integers

## Key idea

The games with a doubled score must be exactly the games with even team scores.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the five losses can be any five scores
- Forgetting that the opponents score one more run in a loss
