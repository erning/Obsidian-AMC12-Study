# AMC12 2013-A Q19

![[assets/problems/2013-A/Q19.png]]

## Problem

In $\triangle ABC$, $AB=86$ and $AC=97$. A circle centered at $A$ with radius $AB$ intersects $\overline{BC}$ at $B$ and $X$. Moreover, $BX$ and $CX$ have integer lengths. What is $BC$?

## Answer

**D.** $61$

## Solution

Apply the power of a point theorem at $C$. The secant through $C$ meets the circle at $X$ and $B$, while $CA=97$ and the radius is $AB=86$. Hence
$$BC\cdot CX=CA^2-AB^2=97^2-86^2=2013.$$

Since $BC=BX+CX$ and both segments are integers, $CX$ and $BC$ are complementary factors of
$$2013=3\cdot11\cdot61.$$
Also $CX<BC<AB+AC=183$. The only factor pair satisfying these geometric bounds is
$$CX=33,qquad BC=61.$$

## Knowledge points

- Power of a point
- Factorization
- Triangle inequality

## Key idea

Turn the circle condition into a product of two integer lengths.

## Common mistakes

- Using $AB^2-AC^2$ with the wrong sign
- Treating $BX$ rather than $BC$ as the second secant length
