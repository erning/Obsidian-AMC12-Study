# AMC12 2013-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2013-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

The sequence
$$\log_{12}162,\ \log_{12}x,\ \log_{12}y,\ \log_{12}z,\ \log_{12}1250$$
is an arithmetic progression. What is $x$?

## Answer

**B.** $270$

## Solution

Exponentiating an arithmetic progression of logarithms produces a geometric progression. Let its common ratio be $r$. Then
$$162r^4=1250,$$
so
$$r^4=\frac{1250}{162}=\frac{625}{81}=\left(\frac53\right)^4.$$
Thus $r=\frac53$, and
$$x=162\cdot\frac53=270.$$

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm properties
- Arithmetic and geometric progressions
- Rational simplification

## Key idea

The logarithms being equally spaced means the original positive numbers have a constant multiplicative ratio.

## Common mistakes

- Taking an arithmetic mean of $162$ and $1250$
- Using the fifth root instead of the fourth root
