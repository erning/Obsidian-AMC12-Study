# AMC12 2013-A Q09

![[assets/problems/2013-A/Q09.png]]

## Problem

In $\triangle ABC$, $AB=AC=28$ and $BC=20$. Points $D$, $E$, and $F$ are on sides $\overline{AB}$, $\overline{BC}$, and $\overline{AC}$, respectively, such that $\overline{DE}\parallel\overline{AC}$ and $\overline{EF}\parallel\overline{AB}$. What is the perimeter of parallelogram $ADEF$?

## Answer

**C.** $56$

## Solution

Because $EF\parallel AB$, triangles $FEC$ and $ABC$ are similar. Since $ABC$ is isosceles, $FE=FC$.

The sides of parallelogram $ADEF$ satisfy $AD=FE$ and $AF=DE$. Hence half its perimeter is
$$AF+FE=AF+FC=AC=28.$$
Therefore the full perimeter is
$$2\cdot28=56.$$

## Knowledge points

- Similar triangles
- Parallelogram properties
- Isosceles triangles

## Key idea

Replace one parallelogram side by an equal segment so that the two pieces form $AC$.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming $D$, $E$, and $F$ divide the sides equally
- Using $BC=20$ instead of $AC=28$ in the final sum
