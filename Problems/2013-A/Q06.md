# AMC12 2013-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2013-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

In a recent basketball game, Shenille attempted only three-point shots and two-point shots. She was successful on $20\%$ of her three-point shots and $30\%$ of her two-point shots. Shenille attempted $30$ shots. How many points did she score?

## Answer

**B.** $18$

## Solution

Suppose she attempted $x$ three-point shots and $30-x$ two-point shots. Her expected scored points, which here is also forced by the given success rates, are
$$
0.2\cdot3x+0.3\cdot2(30-x)
=0.6x+0.6(30-x)
=0.6\cdot30=18.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Percentages
- Linear expressions
- Weighted averages

## Key idea

Each attempted shot contributes the same expected number of points: $0.6$.

## Common mistakes

- Multiplying the two-point success rate by $3$
- Trying to determine $x$ even though it cancels
