# AMC12 2013-A Q11

![[assets/problems/2013-A/Q11.png]]

## Problem

Triangle $ABC$ is equilateral with $AB=1$. Points $E$ and $G$ are on $\overline{AC}$ and points $D$ and $F$ are on $\overline{AB}$ such that both $\overline{DE}$ and $\overline{FG}$ are parallel to $\overline{BC}$. Furthermore, triangle $ADE$ and trapezoids $DFGE$ and $FBCG$ all have the same perimeter. What is $DE+FG$?

## Answer

**C.** $\frac{21}{13}$

## Solution

Let
$$x=DE,qquad y=FG.$$
Because the figures are formed by parallel cuts in an equilateral triangle, their perimeters are
$$
P_{ADE}=3x,qquad P_{DFGE}=3y-x,qquad P_{FBCG}=3-y.
$$
Equating the first two gives $4x=3y$, while equating the first and third gives $3x=3-y$. Solving,
$$x=\frac9{13},qquad y=\frac{12}{13}.$$
Hence
$$DE+FG=x+y=\frac{21}{13}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Equilateral triangle geometry
- Similarity under parallel lines
- Linear equations

## Key idea

Express every perimeter in terms of the two parallel segment lengths.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the trapezoid perimeter as $3y+x$
- Assuming the two cuts are equally spaced
