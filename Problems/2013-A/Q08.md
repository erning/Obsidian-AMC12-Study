# AMC12 2013-A Q08

![[assets/problems/2013-A/Q08.png]]

## Problem

Given that $x$ and $y$ are distinct nonzero real numbers such that
$$x+\frac2x=y+\frac2y,$$
what is $xy$?

## Answer

**D.** $2$

## Solution

Multiply by $xy$:
$$x^2y+2y=xy^2+2x.$$
Rearranging and factoring gives
$$
x^2y-xy^2-2x+2y
=(x-y)(xy-2)=0.
$$
Because $x$ and $y$ are distinct, $x-y\ne0$. Therefore $xy-2=0$, so
$$xy=2.$$

## Knowledge points

- Algebraic manipulation
- Factoring
- Nonzero denominators

## Key idea

Clear the denominators and factor out the known nonzero factor $x-y$.

## Common mistakes

- Dividing by $x-y$ without using that $x\ne y$
- Forgetting the nonzero condition when multiplying by $xy$
