# AMC12 2013-A Q15

![[assets/problems/2013-A/Q15.png]]

## Problem

Rabbits Peter and Pauline have three offspring—Flopsie, Mopsie, and Cottontail. These five rabbits are to be distributed to four different pet stores so that no store gets both a parent and a child. It is not required that every store gets a rabbit. In how many different ways can this be done?

## Answer

**D.** $204$

## Solution

First suppose the two parents go to the same store. There are $4$ choices for that store, and each of the three children can go to any of the other three stores:
$$4\cdot3^3=108.$$

If the parents go to different stores, there are $4\cdot3=12$ ordered assignments for Peter and Pauline. Each child can then go to either of the two stores not occupied by a parent:
$$12\cdot2^3=96.$$

The total number of distributions is
$$108+96=204.$$

## Knowledge points

- Multiplication principle
- Casework
- Counting with restrictions

## Key idea

Split according to whether the two parents share a store.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the three children as one indistinguishable group
- Forgetting that the parent assignments are ordered
