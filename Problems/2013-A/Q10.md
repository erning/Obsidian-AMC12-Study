# AMC12 2013-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2013-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

Let $S$ be the set of positive integers $n$ for which $\frac1n$ has the repeating decimal representation $0.\overline{ab}=0.ababab\ldots$, with $a$ and $b$ different digits. What is the sum of the elements of $S$?

## Answer

**D.** $143$

## Solution

The repeating block gives
$$0.\overline{ab}=\frac{10a+b}{99}.$$
Thus $99/n$ must be a two-digit block, allowing a leading zero, whose digits are different. The positive divisors of $99$ are
$$1,3,9,11,33,99.$$

The corresponding values of $99/n$ for $n=11,33,99$ are $09,03,01$, each with different digits. The other divisors produce repeated digits. Therefore
$$11+33+99=143.$$

## Knowledge points

- Repeating decimals
- Divisors
- Fraction representations

## Key idea

Convert the two-digit repeating block into the fraction $(10a+b)/99$.

## Common mistakes

- Excluding blocks such as $09$ because of the leading zero
- Forgetting that the digits must be different
