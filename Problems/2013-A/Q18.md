# AMC12 2013-A Q18

![[assets/problems/2013-A/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Six spheres of radius $1$ have centers at the vertices of a regular hexagon of side length $2$. They are internally tangent to a larger sphere centered at the hexagon center. An eighth sphere is externally tangent to the six smaller spheres and internally tangent to the larger sphere. What is the radius of the eighth sphere?

## Answer

**B.** $\frac32$

## Solution

The centers of the six small spheres are $2$ from the center $O$, and each small sphere has radius $1$, so the large sphere has radius $3$.

Let the eighth sphere have radius $r$ and center $G$, with $OG=x$. Internal tangency gives
$$x+r=3.$$

The center $G$ is equidistant from two opposite vertices of the hexagon. The right triangle formed by $O$, $G$, and one small-sphere center gives
$$
(r+1)^2=2^2+x^2=4+(3-r)^2.
$$
Simplifying,
$$2r+1=13-6r,$$
so
$$r=\frac32.$$

## Knowledge points

- Sphere tangency
- Three-dimensional Pythagorean theorem
- Regular hexagon geometry

## Key idea

Use the large-sphere tangency to replace the unknown center distance by $3-r$.

## Common mistakes

- Using the hexagon side length as the large radius
- Setting the distance between sphere centers equal to $r$ instead of $r+1$
