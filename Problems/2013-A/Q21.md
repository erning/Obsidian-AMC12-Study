# AMC12 2013-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2013-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Consider
$$
A=\log\left(2013+\log\left(2012+\log\left(2011+\log(\cdots+\log(3+\log2)\cdots)\right)\right)\right).
$$
Which interval contains $A$?

## Answer

**A.** $(\log2016,\log2017)$

## Solution

Let
$$A_n=\log\left(n+\log\left((n-1)+\cdots+\log(3+\log2)\cdots\right)\right).$$
Using common logarithms, direct bounds propagate through the recursion:
$$3<A_{2012}<4.$$
Therefore
$$2016<2013+A_{2012}<2017.$$
Applying the increasing logarithm gives
$$\log2016<A<\log2017.$$

## Knowledge points

- Nested logarithms
- Inductive bounds
- Monotonicity of logarithms

## Key idea

Bound the entire inner expression by bounding its last logarithmic value between $3$ and $4$.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the nested expression as a sum of logarithms
- Forgetting that the logarithm is increasing
