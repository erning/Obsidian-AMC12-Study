# AMC12 2022-B Q19

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q19.png]]

## Problem

In $\triangle ABC$, medians $AD$ and $BE$ intersect at $G$, and $\triangle AGE$ is equilateral. Then $\cos(C)$ can be written as $\frac{m\sqrt n}{p}$, where $m$ and $p$ are relatively prime positive integers and $n$ is squarefree. What is $m+n+p$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $44$**

## Solution

Let
$$
a=BC,\qquad b=CA,\qquad c=AB.
$$
Since $E$ is the midpoint of $AC$, $AE=b/2$. Because $AGE$ is equilateral,
$$
AG=GE=AE=\frac b2.
$$
The centroid divides each median in a $2:1$ ratio. Hence
$$
AD=\frac32AG=\frac{3b}{4},
\qquad BE=3GE=\frac{3b}{2}.
$$
Apply the median-length formulas:
$$
AD^2=\frac{2b^2+2c^2-a^2}{4},
\qquad
BE^2=\frac{2a^2+2c^2-b^2}{4}.
$$
Substitution gives
$$
2c^2-a^2=\frac{b^2}{4},
\qquad a^2+c^2=5b^2.
$$
Solving,
$$
a^2=\frac{13b^2}{4},\qquad c^2=\frac{7b^2}{4}.
$$
Therefore, by the law of cosines at $C$,
$$
\cos C=\frac{a^2+b^2-c^2}{2ab}
=\frac{5}{2\sqrt{13}}=\frac{5\sqrt{13}}{26}.
$$
Thus $m=5$, $n=13$, $p=26$, and the requested sum is $44$.

## Knowledge points

- Centroid ratios
- Median-length formulas
- Law of cosines

## Key idea

The equilateral condition determines both median lengths in terms of $b$.

## Common mistakes

- Reversing the centroid ratio $2:1$.
- Using the wrong side opposite angle $C$ in the law of cosines.
