# AMC12 2022-B Q16

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q16.png]]

## Problem

Suppose $x$ and $y$ are positive real numbers such that
$$
x^y=2^{64}
\quad\text{and}\quad
(\log_2x)^{\log_2y}=2^7.
$$
What is the greatest possible value of $\log_2y$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $3+\sqrt2$**

## Solution

Set
$$
a=\log_2x,\qquad b=\log_2y.
$$
Then $x=2^a$ and $y=2^b$. The first condition becomes
$$
(2^a)^{2^b}=2^{a2^b}=2^{64},
$$
so $a2^b=64$. Taking $log_2$ of the second condition gives
$$
b\log_2a=7.
$$
Thus $a=2^{7/b}$, and substituting into $a2^b=2^6$ yields
$$
\frac7b+b=6.
$$
Therefore
$$
b^2-6b+7=0,
\qquad b=3\pm\sqrt2.
$$
The greater value is $\log_2y=b=3+\sqrt2$.

## Knowledge points

- Change of variables with logarithms
- Exponential equations
- Quadratic equations

## Key idea

Use $a=\log_2x$ and $b=\log_2y$ to turn the nested exponent conditions into a quadratic in $b$.

## Common mistakes

- Confusing $\log_2(y)$ with $y$.
- Taking logarithms of the second equation without using $\log_2(a^b)=b\log_2a$.
