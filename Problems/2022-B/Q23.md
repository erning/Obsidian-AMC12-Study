# AMC12 2022-B Q23

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Let $x_0,x_1,x_2,\ldots$ be a sequence in which each $x_k$ is $0$ or $1$. For each positive integer $n$, define
$$
S_n=\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}x_k2^k.
$$
Suppose $7S_n\equiv1\pmod{2^n}$ for all $n\ge1$. What is
$$
x_{2019}+2x_{2020}+4x_{2021}+8x_{2022}?
$$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $6$**

## Solution

The congruence says that $S_n$ is the inverse of $7$ modulo $2^n$. Thus the $x_k$ are the binary digits of the 2-adic number $1/7$.

Computing the inverse successively modulo $2$, $4$, and $8$ gives
$$
x_0=1,\qquad x_1=1,\qquad x_2=1.
$$
For $k\ge3$, the digits repeat with period $3$:
$$
x_k=
\begin{cases}
0,&k\equiv0\pmod3,\\
1,&k\equiv1,2\pmod3.
\end{cases}
$$
This can also be checked by multiplying the repeating low-bit pattern $111011011\ldots$ by $7$ and observing that every finite truncation has product congruent to $1$ modulo the corresponding power of $2$.

Now $2019\equiv0\pmod3$, $2020\equiv1\pmod3$, $2021\equiv2\pmod3$, and $2022\equiv0\pmod3$. Hence
$$
(x_{2019},x_{2020},x_{2021},x_{2022})=(0,1,1,0),
$$
and the requested value is
$$
0+2(1)+4(1)+8(0)=6.
$$

## Key idea

The condition determines the binary digits of the 2-adic inverse $1/7$.

## Common mistakes

- Reading the binary digits in the opposite order.
- Using ordinary real-number decimal intuition for a 2-adic expansion.
