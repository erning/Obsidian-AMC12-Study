# AMC12 2022-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

One of the following numbers is not divisible by any prime number less than $10$. Which is it?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $2^{607}-1$**

## Solution

The only primes below $10$ are $2,3,5,7$. Every option is odd, so only $3,5,7$ need checking.

For the other choices:

- $2^{606}-1$ is divisible by $3$ because $2^{606}\equiv1\pmod3$.
- $2^{606}+1$ is divisible by $5$ because $606\equiv2\pmod4$ and $2^{606}\equiv4\pmod5$.
- $2^{607}+1$ is divisible by $3$ because $2^{607}\equiv2\pmod3$.
- $2^{607}+3^{607}$ is divisible by $5$ because $2^{607}\equiv3\pmod5$ and $3^{607}\equiv2\pmod5$.

For $2^{607}-1$,
$$
2^{607}-1\equiv1\pmod3,
\quad 2^{607}-1\equiv2\pmod5,
\quad 2^{607}-1\equiv1\pmod7.
$$
It is divisible by none of the relevant primes.

## Knowledge points

- Modular arithmetic
- Periodic powers modulo a prime

## Key idea

Reduce the exponent modulo the period of $2$ for each small prime.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting to test $7$.
- Checking divisibility by composite numbers instead of the primes.
