# AMC12 2022-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

Regular hexagon $ABCDEF$ has side length $2$. Let $G$ be the midpoint of $AB$ and let $H$ be the midpoint of $DE$. What is the perimeter of $GCHF$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $4\sqrt7$**

## Solution

Place the hexagon with center at the origin:
$$
A=(-1,\sqrt3),\ B=(1,\sqrt3),\ C=(2,0),
$$
$$
D=(1,-\sqrt3),\ E=(-1,-\sqrt3),\ F=(-2,0).
$$
Thus $G=(0,\sqrt3)$ and $H=(0,-\sqrt3)$. By symmetry, all four sides of $GCHF$ have the same length. For example,
$$
GC=\sqrt{(2-0)^2+(0-\sqrt3)^2}=\sqrt7.
$$
Therefore the perimeter is $4\sqrt7$.

## Knowledge points

- Regular hexagon coordinates
- Distance formula
- Symmetry

## Key idea

Use symmetry to compute one side of the quadrilateral and multiply by four.

## Common mistakes

- Using circumradius $1$ instead of the side length $2$.
- Assuming $GCHF$ is a rectangle.
