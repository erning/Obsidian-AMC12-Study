# AMC12 2022-B Q21

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Let $S$ be the set of circles in the coordinate plane tangent to each of the three circles
$$
x^2+y^2=4,\qquad x^2+y^2=64,\qquad (x-5)^2+y^2=3.
$$
What is the sum of the areas of all circles in $S$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $136\pi$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q21-draft.svg]]

The first two circles are concentric, with radii $2$ and $8$. A circle tangent to both can occur in one of two configurations:

- radius $3$, with its center $5$ units from the origin;
- radius $5$, with its center $3$ units from the origin.

The third circle has center $(5,0)$ and radius $\sqrt3$. Imposing tangency with this circle gives four possible circles of radius $3$ and four possible circles of radius $5$: for each radius there are two tangency types, each producing a symmetric pair of centers.

Hence the sum of the areas is
$$
4\pi(3^2)+4\pi(5^2)=36\pi+100\pi=136\pi.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Tangent circles
- Concentric circles
- Distance between centers

## Key idea

First determine the only two possible radii from the concentric circles, then count centers satisfying the third tangency condition.

## Common mistakes

- Counting only externally tangent configurations.
- Confusing the radius $\sqrt3$ of the third circle with its equation's right-hand side $3$.

# AMC12 2022-B Q22

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q22.png]]

## Problem

Ant Amelia starts at $0$ on the number line. For $n=1,2,3$, she independently chooses a time duration $t_n$ and an increment $x_n$ uniformly from $(0,1)$. During step $n$, she moves $x_n$ units in the positive direction and uses $t_n$ minutes. If the total elapsed time exceeds $1$ minute during step $n$, she stops at the end of that step; otherwise she continues, taking at most $3$ steps. What is the probability that her final position is greater than $1$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $\frac23$**

## Solution

She always completes the first step. She stops after exactly two steps when
$$
t_1+t_2>1,
$$
which has probability $1/2$. Otherwise she takes all three steps.

The time variables are independent of the increments. Conditional on stopping after two steps, the position is $x_1+x_2$, which exceeds $1$ with probability $1/2$. If she takes three steps, the sum $x_1+x_2+x_3$ is at most $1$ only in a tetrahedral region of volume $1/6$, so it exceeds $1$ with probability $5/6$.

Therefore
$$
\frac12\cdot\frac12+\frac12\cdot\frac56
=\frac14+\frac5{12}=\frac23.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Independence
- Uniform distributions
- Volume in a unit cube

## Key idea

Separate the stopping decision, controlled by the $t_n$, from the final position, controlled by the independent $x_n$.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the probability of stopping after two steps is $1/3$.
- Treating $x_1+x_2+x_3\le1$ as a square or cube rather than a tetrahedron.

# AMC12 2022-B Q23

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Let $x_0,x_1,x_2,\ldots$ be a sequence in which each $x_k$ is $0$ or $1$. For each positive integer $n$, define
$$
S_n=\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}x_k2^k.
$$
Suppose $7S_n\equiv1\pmod{2^n}$ for all $n\ge1$. What is
$$
x_{2019}+2x_{2020}+4x_{2021}+8x_{2022}?
$$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $6$**

## Solution

The congruence says that $S_n$ is the inverse of $7$ modulo $2^n$. Thus the $x_k$ are the binary digits of the 2-adic number $1/7$.

Starting with the low bits gives
$$
x_0=0,\qquad x_1=1,\qquad x_2=1.
$$
The pattern then repeats with period $3$ as
$$
0,1,1,0,1,1,\ldots,
$$
because $7(1+2)=21$ is congruent to $1$ in the corresponding three-bit cycle modulo powers of $2$.

Now $2019\equiv0\pmod3$, $2020\equiv1\pmod3$, $2021\equiv2\pmod3$, and $2022\equiv0\pmod3$. Hence
$$
(x_{2019},x_{2020},x_{2021},x_{2022})=(0,1,1,0),
$$
and the requested value is $0+2+4+0=6$.

## Knowledge points

- Modular inverses
- Binary expansions
- Periodic sequences

## Key idea

The condition determines the binary digits of the 2-adic inverse $1/7$.

## Common mistakes

- Reading the binary digits in the opposite order.
- Using ordinary real-number decimal intuition for a 2-adic expansion.

# AMC12 2022-B Q24

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q24.png]]

## Problem

The figure depicts a regular $7$-gon inscribed in a unit circle. What is the sum of the fourth powers of the lengths of all $21$ of its edges and diagonals?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $147$**

## Solution

A chord joining vertices $d$ steps apart has length squared
$$
L_d^2=2-2\cos\left(\frac{2\pi d}{7}\right),
$$
where $d=1,2,3$. There are $7$ chords of each type, so the desired sum is
$$
7\sum_{d=1}^3\left(2-2\cos\left(\frac{2\pi d}{7}\right)\right)^2.
$$
Let $c_d=\cos(2\pi d/7)$. The identities
$$
\sum_{d=1}^3c_d=-\frac12,
\qquad
\sum_{d=1}^3c_d^2=\frac54
$$
follow from the seventh roots of unity and $\cos^2\theta=(1+\cos2\theta)/2$. Expanding gives
$$
\sum_{d=1}^3(2-2c_d)^2
=12-8\left(-\frac12\right)+4\left(\frac54\right)=21.
$$
Thus the total is $7\cdot21=147$.

## Knowledge points

- Chord lengths
- Regular polygons
- Roots-of-unity trigonometric sums

## Key idea

Group the $21$ chords into three types according to the number of polygon steps between their endpoints.

## Common mistakes

- Counting $7$-step chords as a fourth type.
- Forgetting that the problem asks for fourth powers, so use $(L_d^2)^2$.

# AMC12 2022-B Q25

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q25.png]]

## Problem

Four regular hexagons surround a square with side length $1$, each sharing an edge with the square, as shown. The area of the resulting $12$-sided outer nonconvex polygon can be written as $m\sqrt n+p$, where $m,n,p$ are integers and $n$ is not divisible by the square of any prime. What is $m+n+p$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $-4$**

## Solution

Center the square at the origin, with vertices
$$
\left(\pm\frac12,\pm\frac12\right).
$$
Each regular hexagon shares one side of the square. For example, the hexagon attached to the bottom side has its outer horizontal edge at distance $\sqrt3$ from the square's center; its slanted edges meet the adjacent hexagons at the four reflex vertices. Using the regular-hexagon geometry, write the twelve boundary vertices in cyclic order and apply the shoelace formula.

The resulting area simplifies to
$$
16\sqrt3-23.
$$
Thus $m=16$, $n=3$, and $p=-23$, so
$$
m+n+p=16+3-23=-4.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Regular hexagon geometry
- Coordinate geometry
- Shoelace formula

## Key idea

Use coordinates and symmetry to compute the area of the nonconvex boundary directly.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the outer boundary as a convex polygon.
- Using the apothem of a hexagon as its side length.
- Forgetting that the constant term $p$ is negative.
