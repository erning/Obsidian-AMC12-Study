# AMC12 2022-B Q03

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q03.png]]

## Problem

How many of the first ten numbers of the sequence $121,11211,1112111,\ldots$ are prime numbers?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $0$**

## Solution

The $n$th term has $n$ ones, then a $2$, then $n$ ones. Let $R_{n+1}$ be the repunit consisting of $n+1$ ones. The term factors as
$$
R_{n+1}(10^n+1).
$$
For example, $11211=111\cdot101$. For every $n\ge1$, both factors are integers greater than $1$, so every term is composite. Thus none of the first ten terms is prime.

## Knowledge points

- Repunits
- Algebraic factorization
- Proving compositeness

## Key idea

Recognize the repeated-digit pattern and factor it instead of testing primality term by term.

## Common mistakes

- Checking only the first few terms and assuming a pattern.
- Miscounting the number of ones on either side of the $2$.
