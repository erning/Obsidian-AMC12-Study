# AMC12 2022-B Q20

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Let $P(x)$ be a polynomial with rational coefficients such that the remainder when $P(x)$ is divided by $x^2+x+1$ is $x+2$, and the remainder when $P(x)$ is divided by $x^2+1$ is $2x+1$. There is a unique polynomial of least degree with these properties. What is the sum of the squares of its coefficients?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $23$**

## Solution

Write
$$
P(x)=x+2+(x^2+x+1)(px+q).
$$
Modulo $x^2+1$, we have $x^2\equiv-1$, so
$$
P(x)\equiv x+2+x(px+q)
\equiv(q+1)x+(2-p).
$$
This must equal $2x+1$, giving $q=1$ and $p=1$. Thus
$$
P(x)=x+2+(x^2+x+1)(x+1)=x^3+2x^2+3x+3.
$$
The sum of the coefficient squares is
$$
1^2+2^2+3^2+3^2=23.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Polynomial remainders
- Modular polynomial arithmetic
- Interpolation by congruences

## Key idea

Use the first remainder to parameterize $P$, then impose the second remainder modulo $x^2+1$.

## Common mistakes

- Expanding before reducing $x^2$ modulo $x^2+1$.
- Using $x^2\equiv1$ instead of $x^2\equiv-1$.
