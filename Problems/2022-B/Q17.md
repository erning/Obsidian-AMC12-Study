# AMC12 2022-B Q17

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q17.png]]

## Problem

How many $4\times4$ arrays whose entries are $0$s and $1$s are there such that the row sums are $1,2,3,4$ in some order, and the column sums are also $1,2,3,4$ in some order?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $576$**

## Solution

Choose the row whose sum is $4$ in $4$ ways and the column whose sum is $4$ in $4$ ways. Delete that row and column. In the remaining $3\times3$ array, the row sums and column sums are each $0,1,2$ in some order.

For this $3\times3$ array, choose the zero row and zero column in $3\cdot3=9$ ways. The remaining $2\times2$ array must have row sums $1,2$ and column sums $1,2$. Choose which row has sum $2$ and which column has sum $2$ in $2\cdot2=4$ ways; the entries are then forced. Thus the reduced array has $9\cdot4=36$ possibilities.

The total is
$$
4\cdot4\cdot36=576.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Binary matrices
- Row and column sums
- Structured counting

## Key idea

The rows and columns with sum $4$ force a reduction to a small $3\times3$ counting problem.

## Common mistakes

- Treating row and column sum orders as fixed.
- Counting the forced entries as independent choices.
