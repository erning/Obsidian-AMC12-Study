# AMC12 2022-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

Kayla rolls four fair $6$-sided dice. What is the probability that at least one of the numbers rolled is greater than $4$ and at least two of the numbers are greater than $2$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $\frac{61}{81}$**

## Solution

Classify each die as low ($1,2$), middle ($3,4$), or high ($5,6$). Each category has probability $1/3$, so the $3^4=81$ category patterns are equally likely.

Let $A$ be the event that there is no high die. There are $2^4=16$ such patterns. Let $B$ be the event that at most one die is non-low; there are
$$
1+\binom41\cdot2=9
$$
such patterns. The overlap $A\cap B$ has one all-low pattern and four patterns with exactly one middle die, for a total of $5$.

The desired event is the complement of $A\cup B$, so its count is
$$
81-16-9+5=61.
$$
The probability is therefore $61/81$.

## Knowledge points

- Complementary events
- Inclusion-exclusion
- Equally likely categories

## Key idea

Count the complement: either no die is high, or fewer than two dice are above $2$.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the six face values as three equally likely individual outcomes.
- Counting the overlap twice without adding it back.
