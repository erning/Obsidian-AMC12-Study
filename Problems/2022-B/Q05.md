# AMC12 2022-B Q05

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q05.png]]

## Problem

The point $(-1,-2)$ is rotated $270^\circ$ counterclockwise about the point $(3,1)$. What are the coordinates of its new position?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $(0,5)$**

## Solution

Translate the center of rotation to the origin. The relative vector is
$$
(-1,-2)-(3,1)=(-4,-3).
$$
A $270^\circ$ counterclockwise rotation sends $(u,v)$ to $(v,-u)$, so
$$
(-4,-3)\longmapsto(-3,4).
$$
Translate back by $(3,1)$:
$$
(3,1)+(-3,4)=(0,5).
$$

## Knowledge points

- Rotation rules
- Translation of coordinates

## Key idea

Subtract the rotation center before applying the standard rotation formula.

## Common mistakes

- Rotating the original coordinates around the origin.
- Using the clockwise $90^\circ$ rule with the wrong sign.
