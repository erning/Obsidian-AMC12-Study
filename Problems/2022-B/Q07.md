# AMC12 2022-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Camila writes down five positive integers. The unique mode of these integers is $2$ greater than their median, and the median is $2$ greater than their arithmetic mean. What is the least possible value for the mode?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $11$**

## Solution

Let the median be $m$. The mode is $m+2$. In increasing order, the mode can occur only as the two largest entries; because it is the unique mode, both largest entries must be $m+2$. The total of the five numbers is $5(m-2)$, so the two smallest entries have sum
$$
5(m-2)-m-2(m+2)=2m-14.
$$
They are distinct positive integers, so their sum is at least $1+2=3$. Hence $2m-14\ge3$, giving $m\ge9$. At $m=9$, the list
$$
1,3,9,11,11
$$
works, so the least mode is $m+2=11$.

## Knowledge points

- Mean, median, and mode
- Ordering constraints
- Minimum values

## Key idea

Express the two smallest numbers through the mean condition, then use their minimum possible sum.

## Common mistakes

- Allowing the mode to occur only once.
- Forgetting that the two smaller entries must be distinct for uniqueness of the mode.
