# AMC12 2022-B Q01

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q01.png]]

## Problem

Define $x\diamond y=|x-y|$ for all real numbers $x$ and $y$. What is the value of
$$
(1\diamond(2\diamond3))-((1\diamond2)\diamond3)?
$$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $-2$**

## Solution

Evaluate the two parenthesized expressions separately. Since
$$
2\diamond3=|2-3|=1,
$$
the first expression is $1\diamond1=0$. For the second expression,
$$
1\diamond2=1,
\qquad 1\diamond3=2.
$$
Therefore the requested difference is $0-2=-2$.

## Knowledge points

- Absolute value
- Nonassociative operations

## Key idea

Follow the parentheses carefully; the operation $\diamond$ is not associative.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the two parenthesizations as equivalent.
- Forgetting that the absolute value is applied at each operation.
