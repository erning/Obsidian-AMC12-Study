# AMC12 2022-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

Let
$$
f(n)=\left(\frac{-1+i\sqrt3}{2}\right)^n+\left(\frac{-1-i\sqrt3}{2}\right)^n,
$$
where $i=\sqrt{-1}$. What is $f(2022)$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $2$**

## Solution

The two bases are the primitive cube roots of unity:
$$
\omega=e^{2\pi i/3},\qquad \overline\omega=e^{-2\pi i/3}.
$$
Therefore
$$
f(n)=\omega^n+\overline\omega^n=2\cos\left(\frac{2\pi n}{3}\right).
$$
Since $2022$ is divisible by $3$, the cosine equals $1$, and hence $f(2022)=2$.

## Knowledge points

- Complex numbers in polar form
- Roots of unity
- Periodicity

## Key idea

The expression has period $3$ because both bases are cube roots of unity.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the two terms as unrelated powers.
- Using $2022$ directly instead of reducing it modulo $3$.
