# AMC12 2022-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

The diagram shows a rectangle with side lengths $4$ and $8$ and a square with side length $5$. Three vertices of the square lie on three different sides of the rectangle, as shown. What is the area of the region inside both the square and the rectangle?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $\frac{125}{8}$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q13-draft.svg]]

Place the rectangle at $[0,8]\times[0,4]$. The tilted square has vertices
$$
(4,0),\ (0,3),\ (3,7),\ (7,4).
$$
The square has area $25$. Only the triangle above the top edge of the rectangle lies outside the rectangle. Its vertices are $(3,7)$, $(7,4)$, and the intersection of the line from $(0,3)$ to $(3,7)$ with $y=4$, namely $(3/4,4)$. Its area is
$$
\frac12\left(7-\frac34\right)(7-4)=\frac{75}{8}.
$$
Therefore the common area is
$$
25-\frac{75}{8}=\frac{125}{8}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Coordinate geometry
- Triangle area
- Area subtraction

## Key idea

Compute the square's area and subtract the single triangular part outside the rectangle.

## Common mistakes

- Subtracting a triangle with the wrong base.
- Assuming the entire square is inside the rectangle.
