# AMC12 2022-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

The graph of $y=x^2+2x-15$ intersects the $x$-axis at points $A$ and $C$ and the $y$-axis at point $B$. What is $\tan(\angle ABC)$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $\frac47$**

## Solution

Factor the quadratic:
$$
x^2+2x-15=(x+5)(x-3).
$$
Thus $A=(-5,0)$, $C=(3,0)$, and $B=(0,-15)$. The vectors from $B$ are
$$
\overrightarrow{BA}=(-5,15),\qquad \overrightarrow{BC}=(3,15).
$$
For two vectors, the tangent of the angle between them is the absolute value of the cross product divided by the dot product:
$$
\tan(\angle ABC)=\frac{|(-5)(15)-(15)(3)|}{(-5)(3)+15\cdot15}
=\frac{120}{210}=\frac47.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Factoring quadratics
- Dot and cross products
- Tangent of an angle

## Key idea

Use $|u\times v|/(u\cdot v)$ to avoid finding the angle itself.

## Common mistakes

- Using vectors $AB$ and $BC$, which do not share the vertex $B$ correctly.
- Forgetting the absolute value in the cross product.
