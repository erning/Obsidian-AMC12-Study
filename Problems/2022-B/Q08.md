# AMC12 2022-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

What is the graph of $y^4+1=x^4+2y^2$ in the coordinate plane?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) a circle and a hyperbola**

## Solution

Rearrange and factor:
$$
y^4-2y^2+1=x^4,
\qquad (y^2-1)^2=x^4.
$$
Taking the two square-root branches is equivalent to
$$
y^2-1=x^2\quad\text{or}\quad y^2-1=-x^2.
$$
These become
$$
x^2+y^2=1
\quad\text{and}\quad
y^2-x^2=1,
$$
which are a circle and a hyperbola.

## Knowledge points

- Factoring a fourth-degree equation
- Standard conics

## Key idea

Recognize the left side as $(y^2-1)^2$ and split into two quadratic curves.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the original equation as one quartic curve without factoring.
- Losing one branch when taking square roots.
