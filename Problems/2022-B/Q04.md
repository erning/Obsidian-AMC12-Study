# AMC12 2022-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

For how many values of the constant $k$ will the polynomial $x^2+kx+36$ have two distinct integer roots?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $8$**

## Solution

If the integer roots are $p$ and $q$, then
$$
pq=36,
\qquad k=-(p+q).
$$
The roots must be distinct. The positive factor pairs of $36$ are
$$
(1,36),(2,18),(3,12),(4,9),
$$
and the four corresponding negative pairs are also possible. The pair $(6,6)$ is excluded because the roots are not distinct. Each pair gives a different sum and hence a different $k$, for a total of $8$ values.

## Knowledge points

- Vieta's formulas
- Factor pairs
- Distinct roots

## Key idea

Count distinct integer factor pairs of the constant term.

## Common mistakes

- Counting $(p,q)$ and $(q,p)$ separately.
- Including the repeated-root pair $(6,6)$.
