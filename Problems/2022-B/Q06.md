# AMC12 2022-B Q06

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Consider the following $100$ sets of $10$ elements each:
$$
\{1,2,\ldots,10\},\ \{11,12,\ldots,20\},\ \ldots,\ \{991,992,\ldots,1000\}.
$$
How many of these sets contain exactly two multiples of $7$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $42$**

## Solution

There are
$$
\left\lfloor\frac{1000}{7}\right\rfloor=142
$$
multiples of $7$ among $1$ through $1000$. Every block of $10$ consecutive integers contains either one or two multiples of $7$. If $x$ blocks contain two, the total number of multiples is
$$
2x+(100-x)=100+x=142.
$$
Thus $x=42$.

## Knowledge points

- Floor functions
- Counting by blocks
- Divisibility

## Key idea

Count the total number of multiples and compare it with the baseline of one per block.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming every block has the same number of multiples.
- Forgetting that there are $142$, not $143$, multiples of $7$ up to $1000$.
