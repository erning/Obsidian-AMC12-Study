# AMC12 2022-B Q02

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q02.png]]

## Problem

In rhombus $ABCD$, point $P$ lies on segment $AD$ so that $BP\perp AD$, $AP=3$, and $PD=2$. What is the area of $ABCD$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $20$**

## Solution

Because $AP+PD=5$, the side $AD$ has length $5$. All sides of a rhombus are equal, so $AB=5$. Triangle $APB$ is right with hypotenuse $AB$ and leg $AP=3$, hence
$$
BP=\sqrt{5^2-3^2}=4.
$$
Using $AD$ as the base and $BP$ as the height,
$$
[ABCD]=AD\cdot BP=5\cdot4=20.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Rhombus properties
- Pythagorean theorem
- Area of a parallelogram

## Key idea

The perpendicular segment $BP$ is the height corresponding to base $AD$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $AP=3$ as the rhombus side length.
- Computing the area of triangle $APB$ instead of the parallelogram.
