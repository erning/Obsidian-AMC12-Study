# AMC12 2022-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2022-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

The sequence $a_0,a_1,a_2,\ldots$ is a strictly increasing arithmetic sequence of positive integers such that
$$
2^{a_7}=2^{27}a_7.
$$
What is the minimum possible value of $a_2$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $12$**

## Solution

Dividing by $2^{27}$ gives
$$
2^{a_7-27}=a_7.
$$
The positive integer solution is $a_7=32$, since $2^{32-27}=2^5=32$. Write the common difference as $d\ge1$. Then
$$
a_0=32-7d\ge1,
$$
so $d\le4$. Also $a_2=32-5d$, which is minimized by the largest possible $d$, namely $d=4$. Thus
$$
a_2=32-20=12.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Arithmetic sequences
- Exponential equations

## Key idea

First determine $a_7$, then maximize the common difference subject to $a_0>0$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $a_2=32-2d$ instead of counting backward from $a_7$.
- Allowing $a_0=0$, even though all terms are positive.
