# AMC12 2010-B Q03

![[assets/problems/2010-B/Q03.png]]

## Problem

A ticket to a school play costs $x$ dollars, where $x$ is a whole number. A group of $9$th graders buys tickets costing a total of $\$48$, and a group of $10$th graders buys tickets costing a total of $\$64$. How many values for $x$ are possible?

(A) $1$
(B) $2$
(C) $3$
(D) $4$
(E) $5$

## Answer

**E.** $5$

## Solution

The price $x$ must divide both totals, so $x$ is a common divisor of $48$ and $64.$

Since $\gcd(48,64)=16,$ the common divisors are $1, 2, 4, 8,$ and $16.$ There are $5$ possible values.

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Greatest common divisor
- Factor counting

## Key idea

Translate the ticket counts into a divisibility condition on $x$, then use the total-ticket difference to determine the greatest common divisor.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
