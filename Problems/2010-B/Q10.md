# AMC12 2010-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2010-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

The average of the numbers $1, 2, 3, \ldots, 98, 99,$ and $x$ is $100x.$ What is $x?$

(A) $\frac{49}{101}$
(B) $\frac{50}{101}$
(C) $\frac{1}{2}$
(D) $\frac{51}{101}$
(E) $\frac{50}{99}$

## Answer

**B.** $\frac{50}{101}$

## Solution

The numbers $1$ through $99$ sum to $\frac{99\cdot100}{2}=4950.$

The average condition is $$ \frac{4950+x}{100}=100x, $$ so $4950+x=10000x$ and $9999x=4950.$

Thus $x=\frac{4950}{9999}=\frac{50}{101}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Mean
- Summation
- Linear equation

## Key idea

Sum the first $100$ integers and use the average condition to solve for the missing weighted term.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
