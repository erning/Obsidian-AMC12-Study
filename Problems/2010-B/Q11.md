# AMC12 2010-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2010-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

A palindrome between $1000$ and $10{,}000$ is chosen at random. What is the probability that it is divisible by $7?$

(A) $\frac{1}{10}$
(B) $\frac{1}{9}$
(C) $\frac{1}{7}$
(D) $\frac{1}{6}$
(E) $\frac{1}{5}$

## Answer

**E.** $\frac{1}{5}$

## Solution

A four-digit palindrome has the form $\overline{abba}=1001a+110b$ with $1\le a\le9$ and $0\le b\le9.$

Since $1001=7\cdot11\cdot13$ is divisible by $7$ and $110$ is not, the number is divisible by $7$ exactly when $7\mid b,$ that is $b=0$ or $b=7.$

For each $a,$ that is $2$ of the $10$ choices of $b,$ a probability of $\frac{2}{10}=\frac15.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Palindrome
- Divisibility
- Basic probability

## Key idea

Parameterize a four-digit palindrome by its first two digits and count those divisible by $7$.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
