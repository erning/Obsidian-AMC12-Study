# AMC12 2010-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2010-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

For what value of $x$ does $$ \begin{aligned} &\log_{\sqrt2}\sqrt{x}+\log_2 x \\ &\quad {}+\log_4\left(x^2\right)+\log_8\left(x^3\right) \\ &\quad {}+\log_{16}\left(x^4\right)=40? \end{aligned} $$

(A) $8$
(B) $16$
(C) $32$
(D) $256$
(E) $1024$

## Answer

**D.** $256$

## Solution

Let $L=\log_2 x.$ Converting each term to base $2:$

$\log_{\sqrt2}\sqrt{x}=\frac{L/2}{1/2}=L,$ $\log_2 x=L,$ $\log_4 x^2=\frac{2L}{2}=L,$ $\log_8 x^3=\frac{3L}{3}=L,$ and $\log_{16}x^4=\frac{4L}{4}=L.$

The equation becomes $5L=40,$ so $L=8$ and $x=2^8=256.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm

## Key idea

Combine logarithms into a single equation, exponentiate, and solve for the positive value of $x$.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
