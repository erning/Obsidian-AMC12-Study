# AMC12 2011-A Q04

![[assets/problems/2011-A/Q04.png]]

## Problem

At an elementary school, the students in third grade, fourth grade, and fifth grade run an average of $12,$ $15,$ and $10$ minutes per day, respectively. There are twice as many third graders as fourth graders, and twice as many fourth graders as fifth graders. What is the average number of minutes run per day by these students?

(A) $12$
(B) $\frac{37}{3}$
(C) $\frac{88}{7}$
(D) $13$
(E) $14$

## Answer

**C.** $\frac{88}{7}$

## Solution

Take the grade sizes in the ratio $4 : 2 : 1$ for third, fourth, and fifth grades. The weighted average is $$ \begin{gathered} \frac{4 \cdot 12 + 2 \cdot 15 + 1 \cdot 10}{7} \\ = \frac{48 + 30 + 10}{7} \\ = \frac{88}{7}. \end{gathered} $$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Weighted mean
- Ratio and proportion

## Key idea

Use the grade-size ratio $4:2:1$ to weight the three running-time averages.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
