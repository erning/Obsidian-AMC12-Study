# AMC12 2011-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2011-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

A small bottle of shampoo can hold $35$ milliliters of shampoo, whereas a large bottle can hold $500$ milliliters of shampoo. Jasmine wants to buy the minimum number of small bottles necessary to completely fill a large bottle. How many bottles must she buy?

(A) $11$
(B) $12$
(C) $13$
(D) $14$
(E) $15$

## Answer

**E.** $15$

## Solution

Fourteen bottles hold $14 \cdot 35 = 490$ milliliters, which is not enough. Fifteen bottles hold $15 \cdot 35 = 525$ milliliters, which suffices.

So Jasmine needs $15$ bottles.

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Floor and ceiling functions
- Estimation

## Key idea

Convert the capacity requirement into a number of whole bottles by using the ceiling function.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
