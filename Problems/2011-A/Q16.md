# AMC12 2011-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2011-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

Each vertex of convex pentagon $ABCDE$ is to be assigned a color. There are $6$ colors to choose from, and the ends of each diagonal must have different colors. How many different colorings are possible?

(A) $2520$
(B) $2880$
(C) $3120$
(D) $3250$
(E) $3750$

## Answer

**C.** $3120$

## Solution

The diagonals connect the vertices in the order $A - C - E - B - D - A,$ which is a $5$-cycle. The condition is exactly that this cycle is properly colored.

The number of proper $k$-colorings of a cycle of length $n$ is $(k-1)^n + (-1)^n (k-1).$ With $n = 5$ and $k = 6,$ $$ \begin{gathered} 5^5 + (-1)^5 \cdot 5 \\ = 3125 - 5 = 3120. \end{gathered} $$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Graph theory
- Inclusion-exclusion

## Key idea

The diagonals form a $5$-cycle $A\!-\!C\!-\!E\!-\!B\!-\!D\!-\!A;$ count its proper colorings with the cycle chromatic polynomial $(k-1)^n + (-1)^n(k-1).$

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
