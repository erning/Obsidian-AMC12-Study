# AMC12 2011-A Q13

![[assets/problems/2011-A/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Triangle $ABC$ has side-lengths $AB = 12,$ $BC = 24,$ and $AC = 18.$ The line through the incenter of $\triangle ABC$ parallel to $\overline{BC}$ intersects $\overline{AB}$ at $M$ and $\overline{AC}$ at $N.$ What is the perimeter of $\triangle AMN?$

(A) $27$
(B) $30$
(C) $33$
(D) $36$
(E) $42$

## Answer

**B.** $30$

## Solution

Let $I$ be the incenter. Because $\overline{BI}$ bisects $\angle B$ and $MN \parallel BC,$ alternate angles give $\angle MIB = \angle IBC = \angle MBI,$ so $\triangle MBI$ is isosceles with $MB = MI.$ Similarly $NC = NI.$

Therefore the perimeter of $\triangle AMN$ is $$ \begin{gathered} AM + MN + NA \\ = AM + (MI + IN) + NA \\ = AM + MB + NC + NA \\ = AB + AC = 12 + 18 = 30. \end{gathered} $$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Incircle, incenter, and inradius
- Parallel lines
- Isosceles triangle

## Key idea

The angle bisector and the parallel line make $\triangle MBI$ and $\triangle NCI$ isosceles, so $MN = MB + NC$ and the perimeter collapses to $AB + AC.$

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
