# AMC12 2011-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2011-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Last summer $30\%$ of the birds living on Town Lake were geese, $25\%$ were swans, $10\%$ were herons, and $35\%$ were ducks. What percent of the birds that were not swans were geese?

(A) $20$
(B) $30$
(C) $40$
(D) $50$
(E) $60$

## Answer

**C.** $40$

## Solution

The birds that are not swans make up $100\% - 25\% = 75\%$ of the total, and geese are $30\%$ of the total. The requested fraction is $$ \frac{30}{75} = \frac{2}{5} = 40\%. $$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Percentage
- Conditional probability

## Key idea

Use the given percentages to find the geese and non-geese populations, then apply conditional probability to the non-geese group.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
