# AMC12 2011-A Q19

![[assets/problems/2011-A/Q19.png]]

## Problem

At a competition with $N$ players, the number of players given elite status is equal to $$ 2^{1 + \lfloor \log_2 (N - 1) \rfloor} - N. $$ Suppose that $19$ players are given elite status. What is the sum of the two smallest possible values of $N?$

Note: $\lfloor x \rfloor$ is the greatest integer less than or equal to $x.$

(A) $38$
(B) $90$
(C) $154$
(D) $406$
(E) $1024$

## Answer

**C.** $154$

## Solution

Let $m = \lfloor \log_2 (N - 1) \rfloor,$ so the elite count is $2^{m+1} - N = 19,$ giving $N = 2^{m+1} - 19.$

Consistency requires $2^m \le N - 1 = 2^{m+1} - 20,$ i.e. $2^m \ge 20,$ so $m \ge 5.$

The two smallest choices are $m = 5$ giving $N = 64 - 19 = 45,$ and $m = 6$ giving $N = 128 - 19 = 109.$ Their sum is $154.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

The number of elite players is a power of $2$ determined by the floor of a logarithm; classify the possible powers and count the resulting values of $N$.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
