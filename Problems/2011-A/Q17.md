# AMC12 2011-A Q17

![[assets/problems/2011-A/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Circles with radii $1,$ $2,$ and $3$ are mutually externally tangent. What is the area of the triangle determined by the points of tangency?

(A) $\frac{3}{5}$
(B) $\frac{4}{5}$
(C) $1$
(D) $\frac{6}{5}$
(E) $\frac{4}{3}$

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{6}{5}$

## Solution

The centers are separated by the sums of radii: $3,$ $4,$ and $5,$ a right triangle with the right angle at the radius-$1$ center. Place that center at $(0,0),$ the radius-$2$ center at $(3,0),$ and the radius-$3$ center at $(0,4).$

The tangency points lie on the segments at distances equal to the radii: $(1, 0),$ $(0, 1),$ and on the hypotenuse at $(3,0) + 2 \cdot \tfrac{(-3,4)}{5} = \left(\tfrac95, \tfrac85\right).$

By the shoelace formula the area is $$ \begin{gathered} \tfrac12\left| 1\left(1 - \tfrac85\right) + 0 + \tfrac95(0 - 1) \right| \\ = \tfrac12 \cdot \tfrac{12}{5} = \tfrac65. \end{gathered} $$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Tangent circles
- Coordinate geometry
- Shoelace formula

## Key idea

Place the mutually tangent circles by their centers, determine the triangle’s vertices from the radii, and calculate its area with coordinates.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
