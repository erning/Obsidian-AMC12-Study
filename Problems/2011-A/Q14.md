# AMC12 2011-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2011-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Suppose $a$ and $b$ are single-digit positive integers chosen independently and at random. What is the probability that the point $(a, b)$ lies above the parabola $y = ax^2 - bx?$

(A) $\frac{11}{81}$
(B) $\frac{13}{81}$
(C) $\frac{5}{27}$
(D) $\frac{17}{81}$
(E) $\frac{19}{81}$

## Answer

**E.** $\frac{19}{81}$

## Solution

Substituting $x = a,$ $y = b,$ the point is above the parabola when $b \gt a^3 - ab,$ i.e. $b(a + 1) \gt a^3.$

For $a = 1:$ $b \gt \tfrac12,$ all $9$ values work. For $a = 2:$ $b \gt \tfrac83,$ so $b \ge 3,$ giving $7.$ For $a = 3:$ $b \gt \tfrac{27}{4} = 6.75,$ so $b \ge 7,$ giving $3.$ For $a \ge 4,$ no $b \le 9$ works.

The count is $9 + 7 + 3 = 19$ out of $81,$ so the probability is $\frac{19}{81}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Key idea

Enumerate the digit pairs only through their sum and product, then use the quadratic-root condition and probability count.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
