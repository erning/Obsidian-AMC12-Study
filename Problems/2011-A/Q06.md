# AMC12 2011-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2011-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

The players on a basketball team made some three-point shots, some two-point shots, and some one-point free throws. They scored as many points with two-point shots as with three-point shots. Their number of successful free throws was one more than their number of successful two-point shots. The team's total score was $61$ points. How many free throws did they make?

(A) $13$
(B) $14$
(C) $15$
(D) $16$
(E) $17$

## Answer

**A.** $13$

## Solution

Let $a$ be the number of two-point shots. The two-point shots score $2a$ points, and the three-point shots score the same $2a$ points. The free throws number $a + 1$ and score $a + 1$ points.

The total is $$ \begin{gathered} 2a + 2a + (a + 1) \\ = 5a + 1 = 61, \end{gathered} $$ so $a = 12$ and the free throws number $a + 1 = 13.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- System of equations
- Substitution

## Key idea

Let the counts of three-point and two-point shots be variables and solve the two linear equations from the total score and shot count.

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
