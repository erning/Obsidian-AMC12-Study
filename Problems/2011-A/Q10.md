# AMC12 2011-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2011-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

A pair of standard $6$-sided fair dice is rolled once. The sum of the numbers rolled determines the diameter of a circle. What is the probability that the numerical value of the area of the circle is less than the numerical value of the circle's circumference?

(A) $\frac{1}{36}$
(B) $\frac{1}{12}$
(C) $\frac{1}{6}$
(D) $\frac{1}{4}$
(E) $\frac{5}{18}$

## Answer

**B.** $\frac{1}{12}$

## Solution

For diameter $d,$ area $\lt$ circumference means $\frac{\pi d^2}{4} \lt \pi d,$ i.e. $d \lt 4.$ Since $d \ge 2,$ this needs a sum of $2$ or $3.$

A sum of $2$ has probability $\frac{1}{36}$ and a sum of $3$ has probability $\frac{2}{36},$ totaling $\frac{3}{36} = \frac{1}{12}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Dice (probability)
- Circle
- Inequality

## Key idea

Translate area $<$ circumference into $d < 4,$ then count ordered die outcomes whose sum is $2$ or $3.$

## Common mistakes

- Applying a formula before identifying the relevant quantities and constraints.
- Counting invalid cases or overlooking a boundary condition from the statement.
