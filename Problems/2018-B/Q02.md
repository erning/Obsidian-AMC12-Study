# AMC12 2018-B Q02

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q02.png]]

## Problem

Sam drove 96 miles in 90 minutes. His average speeds during the first and second 30-minute intervals were 60 mph and 65 mph. What was his average speed during the final 30 minutes?

## Answer

**D.** $67$

## Solution

The first two intervals cover

$$60\left(\frac12\right)+65\left(\frac12\right)=30+32.5=62.5$$

miles. The final interval therefore covers $96-62.5=33.5$ miles. Since it lasts half an hour, its speed is

$$\frac{33.5}{1/2}=67\text{ mph}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Average speed
- Distance, rate, and time

## Key idea

Compute the remaining distance before dividing by the remaining time.

## Common mistakes

- Averaging 60, 65, and the unknown speed directly.
