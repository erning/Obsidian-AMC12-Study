# AMC12 2018-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

$AB$ is a diameter of a circle with $AB=24$. As $C$ moves around the circle, the centroid of $\triangle ABC$ traces a closed curve missing two points. To the nearest positive integer, what is the area bounded by the curve?

## Answer

**C.** $50$

## Solution

Let $O$ be the center. Since $O$ is the midpoint of $AB$, the centroid is

$$\frac{A+B+C}{3}=\frac{2O+C}{3}=O+\frac13\overrightarrow{OC}.$$

Thus it traces a circle centered at $O$ with radius $12/3=4$. Its area is $16\pi\approx50.27$, which rounds to 50.

## Knowledge points

- Centroids as affine averages
- Circle area

## Key idea

The midpoint of $AB$ is the center of the centroid circle.

## Common mistakes

- Using radius 12 instead of 4.
