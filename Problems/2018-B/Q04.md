# AMC12 2018-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

A circle has a chord of length 10, and the distance from its center to the chord is 5. What is the area of the circle?

## Answer

**B.** $50\pi$

## Solution

The perpendicular from the center bisects the chord. A right triangle has legs $5$ and $5$, with radius as hypotenuse, so

$$r^2=5^2+5^2=50.$$

The circle area is therefore $\pi r^2=50\pi$.

## Knowledge points

- Chords and perpendicular bisectors
- Pythagorean theorem

## Key idea

Use half the chord in the right triangle.

## Common mistakes

- Using chord length 10 as a leg.
