# AMC12 2018-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Square $ABCD$ has side 30. Point $P$ is inside it with $AP=12$ and $BP=26$. The centroids of $\triangle ABP$, $BCP$, $CDP$, and $DAP$ form a convex quadrilateral. Find its area.

## Answer

**C.** $200$

## Solution

Use coordinates $A=(0,30)$, $B=(0,0)$, $C=(30,0)$, $D=(30,30)$ and write $P=(3x,3y)$. The four centroids are

$$ (x,y+10),\ (x+10,y),\ (x+20,y+10),\ (x+10,y+20).$$

They form a square whose horizontal and vertical diagonals both have length 20. Hence its area is

$$\frac12(20)(20)=200,$$

independent of $P$.

## Knowledge points

- Centroids in coordinates
- Area from diagonals

## Key idea

The unknown coordinates translate the centroid square but do not change its diagonals.

## Common mistakes

- Trying to solve for $P$ from the two distances unnecessarily.
