# AMC12 2018-B Q20

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q20.png]]

## Problem

In a regular hexagon of side 1, $X,Y,Z$ are the midpoints of $AB,CD,EF$. Find the area of the convex hexagon formed by the intersection of the interiors of $\triangle ACE$ and $\triangle XYZ$.

## Answer

**C.** $\frac{15\sqrt3}{32}$

## Solution

Both $ACE$ and $XYZ$ are equilateral. The triangle $ACE$ has half the area of the regular hexagon, and the midpoint triangle determined inside it has one quarter of the area of $ACE$, hence one eighth of the hexagon. The intersection hexagon occupies $5/2$ of this midpoint triangle, so it is $5/16$ of the regular hexagon.

The hexagon area is

$$\frac{3\sqrt3}{2}.$$

Therefore the intersection area is

$$\frac5{16}\cdot\frac{3\sqrt3}{2}=\frac{15\sqrt3}{32}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Regular hexagon area
- Similarity and midpoint scaling

## Key idea

Compare the intersection to the midpoint triangle and then to the original hexagon.

## Common mistakes

- Using the area of $ACE$ as the area of the hexagon.
