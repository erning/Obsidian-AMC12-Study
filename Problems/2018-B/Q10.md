# AMC12 2018-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

A list of 2018 positive integers has a unique mode occurring exactly 10 times. What is the least possible number of distinct values in the list?

## Answer

**D.** $225$

## Solution

After the 10 copies of the mode, $2008$ entries remain. Every other value can occur at most 9 times, so at least

$$\left\lceil\frac{2008}{9}\right\rceil=224$$

non-mode values are necessary. Including the mode gives at least $225$ distinct values. This is attainable by using 9 copies of 223 values, 10 copies of one mode, and one copy of another value.

## Knowledge points

- Pigeonhole principle
- Extremal counting

## Key idea

Pack every non-mode value as many times as allowed.

## Common mistakes

- Dividing 2018 rather than the 2008 non-mode entries.
- Forgetting to count the mode itself.
