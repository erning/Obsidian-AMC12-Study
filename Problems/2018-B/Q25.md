# AMC12 2018-B Q25

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q25.png]]

## Problem

Three mutually externally tangent circles each have radius 4. Points $P_1,P_2,P_3$ lie on the respective circles, with equal side lengths and each connecting line tangent to the corresponding circle. The area of $\triangle P_1P_2P_3$ is $\sqrt a+\sqrt b$. Find $a+b$.

## Answer

**D.** $552$

## Solution

Let $K$ be the intersection of the lines from the first two circle centers to $P_1,P_2$. The equal tangency configuration gives a $30$-$60$-$90$ triangle, so if $P_2K=d$, then

$$P_1K=2d,\qquad P_1P_2=\sqrt3d.$$

The two circle centers are 8 apart. Applying the Law of Cosines in the triangle of centers and $K$ gives

$$8^2=(d+4)^2+(2d-4)^2-2(d+4)(2d-4)\cos60^\circ.$$

This simplifies to $3d^2-12d-16=0$, so

$$d=2+\frac{2\sqrt{21}}3.$$

Therefore

$$P_1P_2=\sqrt3d=2\sqrt3+2\sqrt7.$$

The triangle is equilateral, so its area is

$$
\frac{\sqrt3}{4}(2\sqrt3+2\sqrt7)^2
=10\sqrt3+6\sqrt7
=\sqrt{300}+\sqrt{252}.
$$

Hence

$$a+b=300+252=552.$$

## Knowledge points

- Tangent circles
- $30$-$60$-$90$ triangles
- Equilateral-triangle area

## Key idea

Reduce the symmetric tangency picture to one Law of Cosines equation.

## Common mistakes

- Using center distance 4 instead of 8.
- Forgetting that the requested form uses square-root radicands, not coefficients.
