# AMC12 2018-B Q21

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q21.png]]

## Problem

In a $5$-$12$-$13$ triangle, $O$ and $I$ are the circumcenter and incenter. A circle centered at $M$ is tangent to legs $AC$ and $BC$ and internally tangent to the circumcircle. Find the area of $\triangle MOI$.

## Answer

**E.** $\frac72$

## Solution

Set $C=(0,0)$, $A=(12,0)$, and $B=(0,5)$. The circumcenter is the midpoint of $AB$:

$$O=\left(6,\frac52\right),$$

with circumradius $13/2$. The inradius is $[ABC]/s=30/15=2$, so $I=(2,2)$. A circle tangent to both coordinate legs has center $M=(r,r)$. Internal tangency gives $MO=13/2-r$, which yields $r=4$, so $M=(4,4)$. The shoelace formula for $M,O,I$ gives area $7/2$.

## Knowledge points

- Right triangles and centers
- Internal tangency
- Coordinate area

## Key idea

Use the coordinate axes to make both the incenter and tangent-circle center simple.

## Common mistakes

- Using circumradius 13 instead of $13/2$.
