# AMC12 2018-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Joey, Chloe, and their daughter Zoe share a birthday. Joey is one year older than Chloe, and Zoe is exactly one today. Today is the first of the nine birthdays on which Chloe’s age is an integral multiple of Zoe’s age. What is the digit sum of Joey’s age the next time his age is a multiple of Zoe’s age?

## Answer

**E.** $11$

## Solution

Let Chloe’s current age be $n$. In $y$ years, Chloe’s age is $n+y$ and Zoe’s is $1+y$. The divisibility condition is equivalent to

$$1+y\mid n-1.$$

Thus $n-1$ has exactly nine divisors. A number with nine divisors is $p^8$ or $p^2q^2$; the only suitable two-digit value is $36$. Hence $n=37$ and Joey is 38. Joey’s condition is $1+y\mid37$, so the next positive time is $y=36$, giving age $74$ and digit sum $11$.

## Knowledge points

- Divisor-count function
- Age equations

## Key idea

Translate “multiple of Zoe’s age” into divisors of a fixed age difference.

## Common mistakes

- Counting the current birthday incorrectly.
- Choosing $y=0$ as the next time.
