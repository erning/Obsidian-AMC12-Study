# AMC12 2018-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

A square-base box has base side $w$ and height $h$. It is centered on a square sheet so that the base vertices lie on the sheet’s midlines; folding each corner to the center of the top gives the required wrapping. What is the sheet area?

## Answer

**A.** $2(w+h)^2$

## Solution

From a sheet corner to the center of the top, the horizontal distance is $w/2$ and the vertical distance is $h$. The same horizontal distance $w/2$ occurs on the other side, so the relevant $45$-degree triangle has leg $w+h$. Its hypotenuse is the side of the square sheet, hence the sheet side is

$$\sqrt2(w+h).$$

The area is

$$\bigl(\sqrt2(w+h)\bigr)^2=2(w+h)^2.$$

## Knowledge points

- Net geometry
- $45^\circ$ right triangles

## Key idea

Find the sheet side from the diagonal fold triangle.

## Common mistakes

- Using $w+2h$ as the triangle leg.
