# AMC12 2018-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

In $\triangle ABC$, $AB=10$. The angle bisector from $A$ meets $BC$ at $D$, and $CD=3$. The possible values of $AC$ form an open interval $(m,n)$. Find $m+n$.

## Answer

**C.** $18$

## Solution

Let $AC=q$ and $BD=r$. The Angle Bisector Theorem gives

$$\frac q3=\frac{10}{r},\qquad r=\frac{30}{q}.$$

Apply the triangle inequalities to sides $q,10,3+r$. The inequalities reduce to

$$ (q-15)(q+2)<0,\qquad(q-3)(q+10)>0.$$

Together they give $3<q<15$. Therefore $(m,n)=(3,15)$ and $m+n=18$.

## Knowledge points

- Angle Bisector Theorem
- Triangle inequalities

## Key idea

Express the unknown segment using the angle-bisector ratio before applying inequalities.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $CD=3$ as the whole side $BC$.
- Including degenerate triangles at the endpoints.
