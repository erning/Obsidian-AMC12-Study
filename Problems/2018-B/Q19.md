# AMC12 2018-B Q19

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q19.png]]

## Problem

Mary chose an even 4-digit number $n$ and listed its divisors. At some point she wrote 323. What is the smallest possible next divisor?

## Answer

**C.** $340$

## Solution

Let $d$ be the next divisor. If $\gcd(d,323)=1$, then $323d\mid n$, forcing $n\ge323d>323^2>9999$, impossible for a 4-digit number. Therefore $d$ shares a prime factor with $323=17\cdot19$.

Since $d>323$, the difference $d-323$ is a positive multiple of at least 17, so $d\ge340$. This value is attainable: $n=17\cdot19\cdot20=6460$ is even and 4-digit, and 340 is the next divisor after 323. Hence the minimum is 340.

## Knowledge points

- Divisibility
- GCD and factor bounds

## Key idea

Use the 4-digit upper bound to force a common prime factor.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the next divisor must be prime.
- Forgetting to prove that the lower bound is attainable.
