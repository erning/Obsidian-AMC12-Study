# AMC12 2018-B Q03

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q03.png]]

## Problem

A line of slope 2 and a line of slope 6 intersect at $(40,30)$. What is the distance between their $x$-intercepts?

## Answer

**B.** $10$

## Solution

For slope 2,

$$y-30=2(x-40).$$

Setting $y=0$ gives $x=25$. For slope 6,

$$y-30=6(x-40),$$

which gives $x=35$ at the $x$-axis. The distance is $|35-25|=10$.

## Knowledge points

- Point-slope form
- Intercepts

## Key idea

Use the common intersection point to write both line equations.

## Common mistakes

- Using the $y$-intercepts instead of the $x$-intercepts.
