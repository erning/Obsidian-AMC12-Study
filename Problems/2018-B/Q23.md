# AMC12 2018-B Q23

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Ajay is at $A$ on the equator at $110^\circ$ E longitude. Billy is at $B$ at $45^\circ$ N and $115^\circ$ W. Assuming Earth is a sphere, what is $\angle ACB$ at the center $C$?

## Answer

**C.** $120^\circ$

## Solution

The longitude difference is

$$360^\circ-(110^\circ+115^\circ)=135^\circ.$$

Take the unit-sphere coordinates $A=(1,0,0)$. Billy’s coordinates are

$$B=\left(\cos45^\circ\cos135^\circ,\cos45^\circ\sin135^\circ,\sin45^\circ\right)
=\left(-\frac12,\frac12,\frac{\sqrt2}{2}\right).$$

Thus $A\cdot B=-1/2$, so $\cos\angle ACB=-1/2$ and $\angle ACB=120^\circ$.

## Knowledge points

- Spherical coordinates
- Dot products

## Key idea

The central angle is determined by the dot product of unit position vectors.

## Common mistakes

- Using the longitude difference alone.
- Treating latitude as a planar vertical distance.
