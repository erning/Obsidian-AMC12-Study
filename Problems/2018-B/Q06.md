# AMC12 2018-B Q06

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q06.png]]

## Problem

If $S$ cans cost $Q$ quarters, which expression gives the number of cans purchasable for $D$ dollars, where one dollar is four quarters?

## Answer

**B.** $\frac{4DS}{Q}$

## Solution

The price of one can is $Q/S$ quarters, or $Q/(4S)$ dollars. Therefore $D$ dollars buy

$$\frac{D}{Q/(4S)}=\frac{4DS}{Q}$$

cans.

## Knowledge points

- Unit conversion
- Rates and proportions

## Key idea

Convert the total price from quarters to dollars before dividing.

## Common mistakes

- Multiplying by 4 in the wrong direction.
