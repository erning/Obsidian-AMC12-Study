# AMC12 2018-B Q16

![[assets/problems/2018-B/Q16.png]]

## Problem

The solutions of $(z+6)^8=81$ form a regular octagon. What is the least possible area of a triangle formed by three vertices?

## Answer

**B.** $\frac32\sqrt2-\frac32$

## Solution

Translation by $-6$ does not change area. The roots of $z^8=81$ lie on a circle of radius $\sqrt3$ and form a regular octagon. The minimum triangle uses three consecutive vertices. For three consecutive vertices, the shoelace formula gives

$$[ABC]=\frac{(\sqrt3)^2}{2}\bigl(\sin45^\circ+\sin45^\circ-\sin90^\circ\bigr)=\frac32(\sqrt2-1).$$

## Knowledge points

- Roots of unity
- Regular polygon geometry

## Key idea

Choose consecutive vertices for the smallest triangle and use the octagon’s symmetry.

## Common mistakes

- Using the full diameter as a side.
- Forgetting that translation by 6 is irrelevant to area.
