# AMC12 2006-A Q19

![[assets/problems/2006-A/Q19.png]]

## Problem

Circles with centers $(2, 4)$ and $(14, 9)$ have radii $4$ and $9,$ respectively. The equation of a common external tangent to the circles can be written in the form $y = mx + b$ with $m \gt 0.$ What is $b?$

(A) $\dfrac{908}{119}$
(B) $\dfrac{909}{119}$
(C) $\dfrac{130}{17}$
(D) $\dfrac{911}{119}$
(E) $\dfrac{912}{119}$

## Answer

**E.** $\dfrac{912}{119}$

## Solution

Each circle's radius equals its center's $y$-coordinate, so both are tangent to the $x$-axis, which is a common external tangent. The two external tangents meet at the $x$-intercept of the line through the centers.

That line has slope $\tfrac{9 - 4}{14 - 2} = \tfrac{5}{12} = \tan\theta$ and passes through $(2, 4),$ meeting the $x$-axis at $\left(-\tfrac{38}{5}, 0\right).$

The other tangent makes angle $2\theta$ with the $x$-axis, so its slope is
$$
 \tan 2\theta = \frac{2 \cdot \tfrac{5}{12}}{1 - \left(\tfrac{5}{12}\right)^2} = \frac{120}{119}.
$$
 Then $b = \tfrac{120}{119} \cdot \tfrac{38}{5} = \tfrac{912}{119}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

- Tangent line
- Trigonometric identity
- Coordinate geometry

## Key idea

Choose coordinates that make the line and circle equations simple.
