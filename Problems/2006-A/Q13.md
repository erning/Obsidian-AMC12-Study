# AMC12 2006-A Q13

![[assets/problems/2006-A/Q13.png]]

## Problem

The vertices of a $3$–$4$–$5$ right triangle are the centers of three mutually externally tangent circles, as shown. What is the sum of the areas of these circles?

(A) $12\pi$
(B) $\dfrac{25\pi}{2}$
(C) $13\pi$
(D) $\dfrac{27\pi}{2}$
(E) $14\pi$

## Answer

**E.** $14\pi$

## Solution

If $r, s, t$ are the radii at the vertices, then $r + s = 3,\ r + t = 4,\ s + t = 5.$ Adding all three gives $r + s + t = 6,$ so $r = 1,\ s = 2,\ t = 3.$

The sum of the areas is $\pi(1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2) = 14\pi.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Tangent circles
- System of equations
- Circle area

## Key idea

Use the common tangent and the distance between centers to obtain the radius relation.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
