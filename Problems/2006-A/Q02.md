# AMC12 2006-A Q02

![[assets/problems/2006-A/Q02.png]]

## Problem

Define $x \otimes y = x^3 - y.$ What is $h \otimes (h \otimes h)?$

(A) $-h$
(B) $0$
(C) $h$
(D) $2h$
(E) $h^3$

## Answer

**C.** $h$

## Solution

By the definition, $h \otimes h = h^3 - h.$ Then
$$
 \begin{gathered} h \otimes (h^3 - h) \\ = h^3 - (h^3 - h) \\ = h. \end{gathered}
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.
