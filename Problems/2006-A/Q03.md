# AMC12 2006-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2006-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

The ratio of Mary's age to Alice's age is $3 : 5.$ Alice is $30$ years old. How old is Mary?

(A) $15$
(B) $18$
(C) $20$
(D) $24$
(E) $50$

## Answer

**B.** $18$

## Solution

Mary's age is $\tfrac{3}{5}$ of Alice's, so Mary is $\tfrac{3}{5}\cdot 30 = 18$ years old.

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Key idea

Introduce one scale factor for all corresponding ages or lengths.
