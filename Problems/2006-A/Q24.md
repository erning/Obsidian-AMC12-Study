# AMC12 2006-A Q24

![[assets/problems/2006-A/Q24.png]]

## Problem

The expression

$$
 (x + y + z)^{2006} + (x - y - z)^{2006}
$$

is simplified by expanding it and combining like terms. How many terms are in the simplified expression?

(A) $6018$
(B) $671{,}676$
(C) $1{,}007{,}514$
(D) $1{,}008{,}016$
(E) $2{,}015{,}028$

## Answer

**D.** $1{,}008{,}016$

## Solution

A term $x^a y^b z^c$ survives only when $a$ is even, since terms with odd $a$ cancel between the two expansions.

For each even $a$ with $0 \le a \le 2006,$ the exponent $b$ ranges over $2007 - a$ values and $c = 2006 - a - b$ is then determined. Summing over even $a:$
$$
 \begin{gathered} (2007 - 0) + (2007 - 2) \\ {}+ \cdots + (2007 - 2006) \\ = 2007 + 2005 + \cdots + 1, \end{gathered}
$$
 the sum of the first $1004$ odd positive integers, which is $1004^2 = 1{,}008{,}016.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.
