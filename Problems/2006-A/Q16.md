# AMC12 2006-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2006-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

Circles with centers $A$ and $B$ have radii $3$ and $8,$ respectively. A common internal tangent intersects the circles at $C$ and $D,$ respectively. Lines $AB$ and $CD$ intersect at $E,$ and $AE = 5.$ What is $CD?$

(A) $13$
(B) $\dfrac{44}{3}$
(C) $\sqrt{221}$
(D) $\sqrt{255}$
(E) $\dfrac{55}{3}$

## Answer

**B.** $\dfrac{44}{3}$

## Solution

The radii satisfy $AC \perp CD$ and $BD \perp CD.$ By the Pythagorean theorem, $CE = \sqrt{5^2 - 3^2} = 4.$

Since $\triangle ACE \sim \triangle BDE,$ we get $\tfrac{DE}{CE} = \tfrac{BD}{AC} = \tfrac{8}{3},$ so $DE = 4 \cdot \tfrac{8}{3} = \tfrac{32}{3}.$ Then
$$
 \begin{gathered} CD = CE + DE \\ = 4 + \frac{32}{3} \\ = \frac{44}{3}. \end{gathered}
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Similarity
- Tangent line
- Pythagorean theorem

## Key idea

Use corresponding side ratios and convert the length ratio to the desired quantity.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
