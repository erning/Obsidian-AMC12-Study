# AMC12 2006-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2006-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Let

$$
 \tiny S_1 = \{(x, y) \mid \log_{10}(1 + x^2 + y^2) \le 1 + \log_{10}(x + y)\}
$$

and

$$
 \tiny S_2 = \{(x, y) \mid \log_{10}(2 + x^2 + y^2) \le 2 + \log_{10}(x + y)\}.
$$

What is the ratio of the area of $S_2$ to the area of $S_1?$

(A) $98$
(B) $99$
(C) $100$
(D) $101$
(E) $102$

## Answer

**E.** $102$

## Solution

For $j = 1, 2,$ the condition becomes $j + x^2 + y^2 \le 10^j(x + y),$ i.e.
$$
 \begin{gathered} \left(x - \frac{10^j}{2}\right)^2 \\ {}+ \left(y - \frac{10^j}{2}\right)^2 \\ \le \frac{10^{2j}}{2} - j. \end{gathered}
$$

These are disks with squared radii $\tfrac{100}{2} - 1 = 49$ for $S_1$ and $\tfrac{10000}{2} - 2 = 4998$ for $S_2.$ The area ratio is $\dfrac{4998}{49} = 102.$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm
- Completing the square
- Circle area

## Key idea

Apply the area formula after finding the radius.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
