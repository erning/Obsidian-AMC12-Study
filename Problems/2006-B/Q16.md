## Problem

Regular hexagon $ABCDEF$ has vertices $A$ and $C$ at $(0, 0)$ and $(7, 1),$ respectively. What is its area?

(A) $20\sqrt{3}$
(B) $22\sqrt{3}$
(C) $25\sqrt{3}$
(D) $27\sqrt{3}$
(E) $50$

## Answer

**C.** $25\sqrt{3}$

## Solution

The distance is $AC = \sqrt{7^2 + 1^2} = \sqrt{50}.$ In a regular hexagon with side $s,$ the distance between vertices two apart is $s\sqrt3,$ so $s^2 \cdot 3 = 50,$ giving $s^2 = \dfrac{50}{3}.$

The hexagon's area is
$$
\frac{3\sqrt3}{2}s^2 = \frac{3\sqrt3}{2} \cdot \frac{50}{3} = 25\sqrt3.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

Use central angles and symmetry of the regular polygon.
