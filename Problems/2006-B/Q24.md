# AMC12 2006-B Q24

![[assets/problems/2006-B/Q24.png]]

## Problem

Let $S$ be the set of all points $(x, y)$ in the coordinate plane such that $0 \le x \le \dfrac{\pi}{2}$ and $0 \le y \le \dfrac{\pi}{2}.$ What is the area of the subset of $S$ for which
$$
\sin^2 x - \sin x \sin y + \sin^2 y \le \frac{3}{4}?
$$

(A) $\dfrac{\pi^2}{9}$
(B) $\dfrac{\pi^2}{8}$
(C) $\dfrac{\pi^2}{6}$
(D) $\dfrac{3\pi^2}{16}$
(E) $\dfrac{2\pi^2}{9}$

## Answer

**C.** $\dfrac{\pi^2}{6}$

## Solution

Fixing $y,$ solve $\sin^2 x - \sin x \sin y + \sin^2 y = \dfrac34$ as a quadratic in $\sin x:$
$$
 \begin{aligned} &\sin x = \frac{1}{2}\sin y \\ &\quad {}\pm \frac{\sqrt3}{2}\cos y \\ &\quad = \sin\!\left(y \pm \frac{\pi}{3}\right). \end{aligned}
$$

Within $S,$ $\sin x = \sin\!\left(y - \tfrac{\pi}{3}\right)$ gives the line $x = y - \tfrac{\pi}{3},$ while $\sin x = \sin\!\left(y + \tfrac{\pi}{3}\right)$ gives $x = y + \tfrac{\pi}{3}$ for $y \le \tfrac{\pi}{6}$ and $x = -y + \tfrac{2\pi}{3}$ for $y \ge \tfrac{\pi}{6}.$

These lines split $S$ into regions; testing the corners shows the inequality holds only in the middle band. Its area is
$$
 \begin{aligned} &\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right)^2 - \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{\pi}{3}\right)^2 \\ &\quad {}- 2 \cdot \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\right)^2 \\ &\quad = \frac{\pi^2}{6}. \end{aligned}
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.
