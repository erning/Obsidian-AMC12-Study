# AMC12 2006-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2006-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

In a triangle with integer side lengths, one side is three times as long as a second side, and the length of the third side is $15.$ What is the greatest possible perimeter of the triangle?

(A) $43$
(B) $44$
(C) $45$
(D) $46$
(E) $47$

## Answer

**A.** $43$

## Solution

Let the sides be $x,$ $3x,$ and $15.$ The triangle inequality requires $x + 3x \gt 15,$ so $x \geq 4,$ and $x + 15 \gt 3x,$ so $x \leq 7.$

The perimeter $4x + 15$ is largest when $x = 7,$ giving $7 + 21 + 15 = 43.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Triangle inequality
- Optimization

## Key idea

Use the triangle inequality to obtain the sharp bound.
