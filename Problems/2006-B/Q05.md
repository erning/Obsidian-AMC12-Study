# AMC12 2006-B Q05

![[assets/problems/2006-B/Q05.png]]

## Problem

John is walking east at a speed of $3$ miles per hour, while Bob is also walking east, but at a speed of $5$ miles per hour. If Bob is now $1$ mile west of John, how many minutes will it take for Bob to catch up to John?

(A) $30$
(B) $50$
(C) $60$
(D) $90$
(E) $120$

## Answer

**A.** $30$

## Solution

Bob closes the gap at a relative speed of $5 - 3 = 2$ miles per hour. To cover the $1$-mile gap takes
$$
\frac{1}{2}
$$
 hour, or $30$ minutes.

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Relative speed
- Distance rate and time

## Key idea

Compare the two motions in one reference frame.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
