# AMC12 2006-B Q01

![[assets/problems/2006-B/Q01.png]]

## Problem

What is
$$
(-1)^1 + (-1)^2 + \cdots + (-1)^{2006}?
$$

(A) $-2006$
(B) $-1$
(C) $0$
(D) $1$
(E) $2006$

## Answer

**C.** $0$

## Solution

Since $(-1)^k = -1$ for odd $k$ and $(-1)^k = 1$ for even $k,$ the terms alternate $-1, 1, -1, 1, \ldots$

There are $2006$ terms, forming $1003$ pairs, each equal to $(-1) + 1 = 0.$ The total is $0.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Exponent
- Pairing and grouping

## Key idea

Pair terms with opposite exponents so the powers simplify or cancel.
