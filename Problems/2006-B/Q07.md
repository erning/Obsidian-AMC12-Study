# AMC12 2006-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2006-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Mr. and Mrs. Lopez have two children. When they get into their family car, two people sit in the front, and the other two sit in the back. Either Mr. Lopez or Mrs. Lopez must sit in the driver's seat. How many seating arrangements are possible?

(A) $4$
(B) $12$
(C) $16$
(D) $24$
(E) $48$

## Answer

**B.** $12$

## Solution

The driver is one of the two parents: $2$ choices.

Any of the remaining $3$ people can sit in the front passenger seat, and the last $2$ people fill the back in $2$ orders.

The total is $2 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 = 12.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Multiplication principle
- Arrangements with restrictions

## Key idea

Count each independent choice and multiply the numbers of options.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
