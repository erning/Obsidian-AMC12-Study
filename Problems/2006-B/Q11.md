# AMC12 2006-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2006-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

Joe and JoAnn each bought $12$ ounces of coffee in a $16$-ounce cup. Joe drank $2$ ounces of his coffee and then added $2$ ounces of cream. JoAnn added $2$ ounces of cream, stirred the coffee well, and then drank $2$ ounces. What is the resulting ratio of the amount of cream in Joe's coffee to that in JoAnn's coffee?

(A) $\dfrac{6}{7}$
(B) $\dfrac{13}{14}$
(C) $1$
(D) $\dfrac{14}{13}$
(E) $\dfrac{7}{6}$

## Answer

**E.** $\dfrac{7}{6}$

## Solution

Joe adds the cream last, so his cup holds all $2$ ounces of cream.

JoAnn's cup has $14$ ounces of mixture containing $2$ ounces of cream. Drinking $2$ ounces removes a fraction $\tfrac{2}{14}$ of everything, leaving
$$
2 \cdot \frac{12}{14} = \frac{12}{7}
$$
 ounces of cream.

The ratio is
$$
\frac{2}{\,12/7\,} = \frac{14}{12} = \frac{7}{6}.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- Mixture
- Ratio and proportion

## Key idea

Track the amount of each ingredient or component separately.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
