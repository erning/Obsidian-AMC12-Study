# AMC12 2006-B Q03

![[assets/problems/2006-B/Q03.png]]

## Problem

A football game was played between two teams, the Cougars and the Panthers. The two teams scored a total of $34$ points, and the Cougars won by a margin of $14$ points. How many points did the Panthers score?

(A) $10$
(B) $14$
(C) $17$
(D) $20$
(E) $24$

## Answer

**A.** $10$

## Solution

Let $c$ and $p$ be the Cougars' and Panthers' scores. Then $c + p = 34$ and $c - p = 14.$

Subtracting gives $2p = 20,$ so $p = 10.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- System of equations

## Key idea

Translate each condition into an equation and solve them simultaneously.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
