# AMC12 2006-B Q20

![[assets/problems/2006-B/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Let $x$ be chosen at random from the interval $(0, 1).$ What is the probability that
$$
\lfloor \log_{10} 4x \rfloor - \lfloor \log_{10} x \rfloor = 0?
$$
 Here $\lfloor x \rfloor$ denotes the greatest integer that is less than or equal to $x.$

(A) $\dfrac{1}{8}$
(B) $\dfrac{3}{20}$
(C) $\dfrac{1}{6}$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{5}$
(E) $\dfrac{1}{4}$

## Answer

**C.** $\dfrac{1}{6}$

## Solution

The equation says $\lfloor \log_{10} x \rfloor = \lfloor \log_{10} 4x \rfloor,$ i.e. $x$ and $4x$ lie in the same interval $[10^n, 10^{n+1}).$

This holds exactly when $10^n \le x$ and $4x \lt 10^{n+1},$ that is $10^n \le x \lt \dfrac{10^{n+1}}{4}.$

Within $[10^n, 10^{n+1}),$ the favorable fraction is
$$
 \begin{aligned} &\frac{10^{n+1}/4 - 10^n}{10^{n+1} - 10^n} \\ &= \frac{10/4 - 1}{10 - 1} = \frac{1}{6}. \end{aligned}
$$

Since this fraction is the same on every such interval, the overall probability is $\dfrac{1}{6}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

- Logarithm
- Geometric probability

## Key idea

Translate the floor/ceiling conditions into intervals.
