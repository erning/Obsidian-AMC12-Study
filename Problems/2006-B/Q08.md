# AMC12 2006-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2006-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

The lines
$$
x = \tfrac14 y + a, \qquad y = \tfrac14 x + b
$$
 intersect at the point $(1, 2).$ What is $a + b?$

(A) $0$
(B) $\dfrac{3}{4}$
(C) $1$
(D) $2$
(E) $\dfrac{9}{4}$

## Answer

**E.** $\dfrac{9}{4}$

## Solution

Substituting $(1, 2)$ gives
$$
1 = \frac{2}{4} + a \quad\Rightarrow\quad a = \frac{1}{2},
$$
 and
$$
2 = \frac{1}{4} + b \quad\Rightarrow\quad b = \frac{7}{4}.
$$

Therefore
$$
a + b = \frac{1}{2} + \frac{7}{4} = \frac{9}{4}.
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Knowledge points

- System of equations
- Substitution

## Key idea

Translate each condition into an equation and solve them simultaneously.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
