# AMC12 2006-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2006-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Elmo makes $N$ sandwiches for a fundraiser. For each sandwich he uses $B$ globs of peanut butter at $4$¢ per glob and $J$ blobs of jam at $5$¢ per blob. The cost of the peanut butter and jam to make all the sandwiches is $\$2.53.$ Assume that $B,$ $J,$ and $N$ are positive integers with $N \gt 1.$ What is the cost of the jam Elmo uses to make the sandwiches?

(A) $\$1.05$
(B) $\$1.25$
(C) $\$1.45$
(D) $\$1.65$
(E) $\$1.85$

## Answer

**D.** $\$1.65$

## Solution

The total cost in cents is $N(4B + 5J) = 253 = 11 \cdot 23.$ Since $N \gt 1,$ the value of $N$ is $11,$ $23,$ or $253.$

If $N = 253$ then $4B + 5J = 1,$ and if $N = 23$ then $4B + 5J = 11;$ neither has a positive integer solution.

So $N = 11$ and $4B + 5J = 23,$ whose only positive solution is $B = 2,$ $J = 3.$

The jam costs $N \cdot J \cdot 5$¢ $= 11 \cdot 3 \cdot 5 = 165$ cents, or $\$1.65.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Key idea

Split the possibilities into disjoint, exhaustive cases.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
