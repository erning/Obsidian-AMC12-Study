# AMC12 2006-B Q17

![[assets/problems/2006-B/Q17.png]]

## Problem

For a particular peculiar pair of dice, the probabilities of rolling $1, 2, 3, 4, 5,$ and $6$ on each die are in the ratio $1 : 2 : 3 : 4 : 5 : 6.$ What is the probability of rolling a total of $7$ on the two dice?

(A) $\dfrac{4}{63}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{8}$
(C) $\dfrac{8}{63}$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{6}$
(E) $\dfrac{2}{7}$

## Answer

**C.** $\dfrac{8}{63}$

## Solution

Since the weights sum to $21,$ the probability of rolling $k$ is $\dfrac{k}{21}.$

A total of $7$ comes from $(1,6), (2,5), \ldots, (6,1),$ so the probability is
$$
 \begin{aligned} &\scriptsize \frac{1 \cdot 6 + 2 \cdot 5 + 3 \cdot 4 + 4 \cdot 3 + 5 \cdot 2 + 6 \cdot 1}{21^2} \\ &= \frac{56}{441} = \frac{8}{63}. \end{aligned}
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

Count equally likely dice outcomes and divide favorable outcomes by the total.
