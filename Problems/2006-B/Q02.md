# AMC12 2006-B Q02

![[assets/problems/2006-B/Q02.png]]

## Problem

For real numbers $x$ and $y,$ define
$$
x \spadesuit y = (x + y)(x - y).
$$
 What is $3 \spadesuit (4 \spadesuit 5)?$

(A) $-72$
(B) $-27$
(C) $-24$
(D) $24$
(E) $72$

## Answer

**A.** $-72$

## Solution

Since $x \spadesuit y = x^2 - y^2,$ the inner value is $4 \spadesuit 5 = 16 - 25 = -9.$

Then $3 \spadesuit (-9) = 3^2 - (-9)^2$ $= 9 - 81 = -72.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.
