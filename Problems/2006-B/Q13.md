# AMC12 2006-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2006-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Rhombus $ABCD$ is similar to rhombus $BFDE.$ The area of rhombus $ABCD$ is $24,$ and $\angle BAD = 60^\circ.$ What is the area of rhombus $BFDE?$

(A) $6$
(B) $4\sqrt{3}$
(C) $8$
(D) $9$
(E) $6\sqrt{3}$

## Answer

**C.** $8$

## Solution

Because $\angle BAD = 60^\circ$ and $AB = AD,$ triangle $ABD$ is equilateral. The diagonals $AC$ and $BD$ together with segments $BE,$ $DF$ split $ABCD$ into six congruent triangles.

Each of these triangles has area $\dfrac{24}{6} = 4.$

Rhombus $BFDE$ is the union of $\triangle BED$ and $\triangle BFD,$ two of them, so its area is $8.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Rhombus
- Equilateral triangle
- Area decomposition

## Key idea

Exploit equal sides and diagonal symmetry of the rhombus.
