# AMC12 2006-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2006-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

Mary is about to pay for five items at the grocery store. The prices of the items are $\$7.99,$ $\$4.99,$ $\$2.99,$ $\$1.99,$ and $\$0.99.$ Mary will pay with a twenty-dollar bill. Which of the following is closest to the percentage of the $\$20.00$ that she will receive in change?

(A) $5$
(B) $10$
(C) $15$
(D) $20$
(E) $25$

## Answer

**A.** $5$

## Solution

The five prices total about $8 + 5 + 3 + 2 + 1 = 19$ dollars, so the change is about $\$1.00.$

This is
$$
\frac{1}{20} = 5\%
$$
 of the twenty-dollar bill.

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Knowledge points

- Estimation
- Percentage

## Key idea

Bound the quantity tightly enough to identify the only answer choice.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
