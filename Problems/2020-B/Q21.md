# AMC12 2020-B Q21

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q21.png]]

## Problem

How many positive integers $n$ satisfy

$$\frac{n+1000}{70}=\lfloor\sqrt n\rfloor?$$

## Answer

**C.** $6$

## Solution

Let $k=\lfloor\sqrt n\rfloor$. Then $n=70k-1000$, and the floor condition is equivalent to

$$k^2\le 70k-1000<(k+1)^2.$$

The first inequality becomes

$$k^2-70k+1000\le0
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
(k-20)(k-50)\le0,$$

so $20\le k\le50$. The second inequality becomes

$$70k-1000<k^2+2k+1
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
k^2-68k+1001>0.
$$

The roots are $21$ and $47$, so the integer solutions are $k\le21$ or $k\ge47$. Intersecting the two ranges gives

$$k\in\{20,21,47,48,49,50\},$$

which produces $6$ values of $n$.

## Knowledge points

- Floor functions
- Quadratic inequalities
- Bounding by consecutive squares

## Key idea

Replace the floor by $k^2\le n<(k+1)^2$ and solve for the possible integer values of $k$.

## Common mistakes

- Replacing $\lfloor\sqrt n\rfloor$ with $\sqrt n$.
- Using a non-strict inequality in the upper bound.
