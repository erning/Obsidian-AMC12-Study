# AMC12 2020-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

The acute angles of a right triangle are $a^\circ$ and $b^\circ$, where $a>b$ and both $a$ and $b$ are prime numbers. What is the least possible value of $b$?

## Answer

**D.** $7$

## Solution

The acute angles of a right triangle sum to $90^\circ$, so $a=90-b$. Test prime values of $b$ in increasing order:

$$b=2\Rightarrow a=88,
\quad b=3\Rightarrow a=87,
\quad b=5\Rightarrow a=85.$$

None of these values of $a$ is prime. For $b=7$, we get $a=83$, which is prime and larger than $7$. Thus the least possible value is $7$.

## Knowledge points

- Triangle angle sum
- Prime-number testing

## Key idea

Use the complementary-angle condition to reduce the search to small primes $b$.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting the condition $a>b$.
- Treating $1$ as a prime number.
