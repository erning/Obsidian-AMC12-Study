# AMC12 2020-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Two players, Bela and Jenn, play a game on the interval $[0,n]$, where $n$ is an integer greater than $4$. Bela goes first and chooses a real number in the interval. Each subsequent move must be more than $1$ unit away from every number previously chosen. The first player unable to move loses. Who has a winning strategy?

## Answer

**A.** Bela always wins

## Solution

Bela first chooses the midpoint $n/2$. Whenever Jenn chooses $x$, Bela chooses its reflection $n-x$ across the midpoint.

If Jenn's move is more than $1$ away from every previous choice, then the reflected move is also more than $1$ away from every previous choice: reflection preserves distances. The reflected point cannot equal a previous point, because that would imply that Jenn's point was already present as its reflection. Thus Bela can always answer Jenn's move whenever Jenn can move.

Eventually Jenn is the first player with no legal move, so Bela wins.

## Knowledge points

- Symmetry and reflection
- Invariant strategy
- Game theory

## Key idea

Choose the midpoint and mirror every later move. The symmetry makes Bela's response legal whenever Jenn's move is legal.

## Common mistakes

- Choosing an arbitrary first point instead of the midpoint.
- Checking only distance from the latest move rather than from all previous moves.
