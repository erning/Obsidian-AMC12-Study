# AMC12 2020-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

How many ordered pairs of integers $(x,y)$ satisfy

$$x^{2020}+y^2=2y?$$

## Answer

**D.** $4$

## Solution

Complete the square in $y$:

$$x^{2020}+(y-1)^2=1.$$

Both terms are nonnegative, so $x^{2020}\le1$. For integer $x$, this gives $x\in\{-1,0,1\}$.

If $x=0$, then $(y-1)^2=1$, giving $y=0$ or $2$. If $x=\pm1$, then $(y-1)^2=0$, giving $y=1$. Thus the four solutions are

$$ (0,0),\ (0,2),\ (1,1),\ (-1,1).$$

## Knowledge points

- Completing the square
- Integer constraints

## Key idea

Turn the equation into a sum of nonnegative integer terms equal to $1$.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming $x^{2020}=1$ forces $x=1$ and forgetting $x=-1$.
- Counting $(0,1)$, which does not satisfy the equation.
