# AMC12 2020-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

A three-quarter sector of a circle of radius $4$ inches, together with its interior, is rolled up to form the lateral surface of a right circular cone by taping together along the two radii. What is the volume of the cone?

## Answer

**C.** $3\pi\sqrt7$

## Solution

The sector's arc length becomes the cone's base circumference:

$$\frac34(2\pi\cdot4)=6\pi=2\pi r,$$

so the cone radius is $r=3$. The sector radius becomes the slant height $4$, so the cone height is

$$h=\sqrt{4^2-3^2}=\sqrt7.$$

Therefore

$$V=\frac13\pi r^2h=\frac13\pi(3^2)\sqrt7=3\pi\sqrt7.$$

## Knowledge points

- Sector arc length
- Cone volume and Pythagorean theorem

## Key idea

Match the sector's arc to the cone circumference and its radius to the cone slant height.

## Common mistakes

- Using $4$ as the cone height rather than the slant height.
- Using the full circle circumference instead of three quarters of it.
