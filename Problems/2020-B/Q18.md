# AMC12 2020-B Q18

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q18.png]]

## Problem

In square $ABCD$, points $E$ and $H$ lie on $AB$ and $DA$, respectively, with $AE=AH$. Points $F$ and $G$ lie on $BC$ and $CD$. Points $I$ and $J$ lie on $EH$, with $FI\perp EH$ and $GJ\perp EH$. The four regions with vertices $AEH$, $BFIE$, $DHJG$, and $FCGJI$ each have area $1$. Find $FI^2$.

## Answer

**B.** $8-4\sqrt2$

## Solution

The four regions partition the square, so the square has area $4$ and side length $2$. Since triangle $AEH$ is right isosceles and has area $1$,

$$\frac12 AE\cdot AH=1\quad\Longrightarrow\quad AE=AH=\sqrt2.$$

Set $A=(0,0)$, $B=(2,0)$, $C=(2,2)$, and $D=(0,2)$. Then

$$E=(\sqrt2,0),\qquad H=(0,\sqrt2),$$

so $EH$ has equation $x+y=\sqrt2$. Let $F=(2,t)$. The perpendicular distance from $F$ to $EH$ is

$$FI=\frac{2+t-\sqrt2}{\sqrt2}.$$

Using the shoelace formula on quadrilateral $BFIE$, with $I$ the perpendicular projection of $F$ onto $EH$, and setting its area equal to $1$, gives

$$\frac{t^2}{4}+\left(1-\frac{\sqrt2}{2}\right)t-\frac32+\sqrt2=1.$$

The root satisfying $0\le t\le2$ is the position shown in the diagram. Substituting it into the distance formula, or simplifying with the equivalent variable $s=FI/\sqrt2$, yields

$$s^2=4-2\sqrt2,$$

and therefore

$$FI^2=2s^2=8-4\sqrt2.$$

## Knowledge points

- Area decomposition
- Coordinate geometry
- Distance from a point to a line
- Shoelace formula

## Key idea

Use the equal-area condition to determine the square and triangle sizes, then place the figure in coordinates.

## Common mistakes

- Using $AE=AH=1$ instead of deriving the length from the triangle's area.
- Applying the distance formula to the wrong line.
- Keeping the algebraic root that places $F$ outside segment $BC$.
