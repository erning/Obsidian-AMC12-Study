# AMC12 2020-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Which of the following is the value of

$$\sqrt{\log_2 6+\log_3 6}?$$

## Answer

**D.** $\sqrt{\log_2 3}+\sqrt{\log_3 2}$

## Solution

Let $a=\log_2 3$. Then $\log_2 6=1+a$ and

$$\log_3 6=1+\log_3 2=1+\frac1a.$$

Thus the expression under the radical is

$$1+a+1+\frac1a=a+2+\frac1a
=\left(\sqrt a+\frac1{\sqrt a}\right)^2.$$

Since all terms are positive,

$$\sqrt{\log_2 6+\log_3 6}
=\sqrt{\log_2 3}+\sqrt{\log_3 2}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Change of base for logarithms
- Perfect-square recognition

## Key idea

Use $\log_3 2=1/\log_2 3$ to turn the radicand into a square.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming $\log_2 6+\log_3 6=2$.
- Forgetting that $\sqrt{a+2+1/a}=\sqrt a+1/\sqrt a$ here because the expression is a perfect square.
