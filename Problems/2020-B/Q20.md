# AMC12 2020-B Q20

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Two cubes of the same size are independently painted so that each face is either black or white, with all $2^6$ colorings equally likely. What is the probability that the two painted cubes can be rotated so that their color patterns are identical?

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{147}{1024}$

## Solution

Fix the first cube. For a coloring $C$, the number of colorings of the second cube that can be rotated to match $C$ is the size of the rotation orbit of $C$. Thus the desired probability is

$$\frac{\sum_C |\operatorname{Orb}(C)|}{2^{12}}.$$

Equivalently, group the colorings into rotation orbits and sum the squares of their sizes. Classifying by the number of black faces gives the following orbit-size contributions:

$$1+36+(9+144)+(64+144)+(9+144)+36+1=588.$$

The numerator is therefore $588$, while there are $2^6\cdot2^6=4096$ ordered pairs of colorings. Hence

$$\frac{588}{4096}=\frac{147}{1024}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Symmetry and group actions
- Orbits under rotations
- Counting by cases

## Key idea

Count rotation-equivalence classes of cube colorings and weight each class by the square of its orbit size.

## Common mistakes

- Dividing by $24$ for every coloring, even when a coloring has rotational symmetry.
- Counting colorings with black and white interchanged as identical.
- Forgetting that the two cubes are ordered in the probability calculation.
