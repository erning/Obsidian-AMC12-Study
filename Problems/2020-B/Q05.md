# AMC12 2020-B Q05

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Teams $A$ and $B$ are playing in a basketball league. Team $A$ has won $\frac23$ of its games and team $B$ has won $\frac58$ of its games. Team $B$ has won $7$ more games and lost $7$ more games than team $A$. How many games has team $A$ played?

## Answer

**C.** $42$

## Solution

Let $a$ and $b$ be the numbers of games played by teams $A$ and $B$. The win and loss counts give

$$\frac58b=\frac23a+7,
\qquad \frac38b=\frac13a+7.$$

Subtracting the equations after clearing denominators yields $b=\frac43a$. Substitute into the loss equation:

$$\frac38\cdot\frac43a=\frac13a+7.$$

Thus $\frac12a=\frac13a+7$, so $a=42$.

## Knowledge points

- Fractions of totals
- Systems of linear equations

## Key idea

Translate the differences in wins and losses into two separate equations before eliminating the unknown number of games.

## Common mistakes

- Using $\frac38$ as team $A$'s loss fraction and $\frac13$ as team $B$'s loss fraction.
- Equating the total games of the two teams, even though they need not have played the same number.
