# AMC12 2020-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

In unit square $ABCD$, the inscribed circle $\omega$ intersects $\overline{CD}$ at $M$, and $\overline{AM}$ intersects $\omega$ at a point $P$ different from $M$. What is $AP$?

## Answer

**B.** $\frac{\sqrt5}{10}$

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q10-draft.svg]]

Set $A=(0,0)$, $B=(1,0)$, $C=(1,1)$, and $D=(0,1)$. The circle has center $(\frac12,\frac12)$ and radius $\frac12$. Its tangency point on $CD$ is $M=(\frac12,1)$.

Line $AM$ is $y=2x$. Substitute into the circle equation:

$$\left(x-\frac12\right)^2+\left(2x-\frac12\right)^2=\frac14.$$

This gives

$$20x^2-12x+1=0.$$

The two roots are $x=\frac12$ for $M$ and $x=\frac1{10}$ for $P$. Hence

$$AP=\sqrt{\left(\frac1{10}\right)^2+\left(\frac15\right)^2}=\frac{\sqrt5}{10}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Coordinate geometry
- Circle equations
- Line-circle intersections

## Key idea

Place the square in coordinates so the line and the inscribed circle have simple equations.

## Common mistakes

- Choosing $M$ rather than the second intersection $P$.
- Using radius $1$ for the unit square's incircle.
