# AMC12 2020-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

Let $\overline{AB}$ be a diameter in a circle of radius $5\sqrt2$. Let $\overline{CD}$ be a chord that intersects $\overline{AB}$ at $E$ such that $BE=2\sqrt5$ and $\angle AEC=45^\circ$. What is $CE^2+DE^2$?

## Answer

**E.** $100$

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q12-draft.svg]]

Place the circle's center at the origin and $AB$ on the $x$-axis. Its equation is $x^2+y^2=50$. Let $E=(e,0)$; the given distance from the right endpoint $B=(5\sqrt2,0)$ gives $e=5\sqrt2-2\sqrt5$.

Because $\angle AEC=45^\circ$, parametrize the chord line through $E$ as

$$ (x,y)=E+t\left(\frac1{\sqrt2},\frac1{\sqrt2}\right).$$

Substitution into the circle equation gives a quadratic in $t$ whose two roots correspond to the signed distances to $C$ and $D$. Vieta's formulas give

$$t_1+t_2=\sqrt2(5\sqrt2-e),\qquad t_1t_2=e^2-50.$$

Therefore

$$CE^2+DE^2=t_1^2+t_2^2=(t_1+t_2)^2-2t_1t_2=100.$$

## Knowledge points

- Coordinate geometry of a circle
- Parametrizing a chord
- Vieta's formulas

## Key idea

The individual chord lengths are unnecessary; use the sum and product of the two intersection parameters.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $CE$ and $DE$ as ordinary horizontal distances.
- Using the circle radius as $5$ instead of $5\sqrt2$.
