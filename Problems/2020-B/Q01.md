# AMC12 2020-B Q01

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q01.png]]

## Problem

What is the value in simplest form of

$$\sqrt1+\sqrt{1+3}+\sqrt{1+3+5}+\sqrt{1+3+5+7}?$$

## Answer

**C.** $10$

## Solution

The sum of the first $k$ odd positive integers is $k^2$. Therefore the four radicals are

$$\sqrt1=1,\quad\sqrt4=2,\quad\sqrt9=3,\quad\sqrt{16}=4.$$

Their sum is $1+2+3+4=10$.

## Knowledge points

- Sum of consecutive odd integers
- Simplifying radicals

## Key idea

Recognize the identity $1+3+\cdots+(2k-1)=k^2$.

## Common mistakes

- Combining the radicals before evaluating their radicands.
- Treating $1+3+5+7$ as $4^2$ without checking the earlier terms.
