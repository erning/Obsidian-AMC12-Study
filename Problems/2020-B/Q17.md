# AMC12 2020-B Q17

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q17.png]]

## Problem

Let $\omega=\frac{-1+i\sqrt3}{2}$. How many polynomials of the form

$$x^5+ax^4+bx^3+cx^2+dx+2020,$$

where $a,b,c,d$ are real numbers, have the property that whenever $r$ is a root, $\omega r$ is also a root?

## Answer

**C.** $2$

## Solution

Because the constant term is nonzero, $0$ is not a root. The roots therefore occur in orbits

$$r,\quad \omega r,\quad \omega^2r.$$

The degree is $5$, so the multiset of roots consists of one orbit of multiplicity $2$ and one orbit of multiplicity $1$. Since the coefficients are real, the root multiset must also be closed under complex conjugation.

There are two possible orbit directions compatible with this conjugation condition: one orbit lies at angles $0^\circ,120^\circ,240^\circ$, and the other lies at angles $60^\circ,180^\circ,300^\circ$. The first has positive real product, which cannot produce the required product of roots $-2020$. The second has negative real product and allows two conjugation-compatible multiplicity patterns: multiplicities $(1,3,1)$ or $(2,1,2)$ around the orbit.

Each pattern determines the roots and hence the polynomial uniquely. Therefore there are $2$ polynomials.

## Knowledge points

- Roots of polynomials
- Complex numbers and cube roots of unity
- Conjugate roots
- Viète's formulas

## Key idea

Organize the roots into three-element $\omega$-orbits, then impose degree, real-coefficient, and product constraints.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming every root must be real.
- Forgetting that roots are counted with multiplicity.
- Using conjugation without checking the product of all roots.
