# AMC12 2020-B Q03

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q03.png]]

## Problem

The ratio of $w$ to $x$ is $4:3$, the ratio of $y$ to $z$ is $3:2$, and the ratio of $z$ to $x$ is $1:6$. What is the ratio of $w$ to $y$?

## Answer

**E.** $16:3$

## Solution

Choose $x=6$. From $z:x=1:6$, we get $z=1$. Then $y:z=3:2$ gives

$$y=\frac32.$$

Also $w:x=4:3$ gives $w=8$. Therefore

$$w:y=8:\frac32=16:3.$$

## Knowledge points

- Chained ratios
- Scaling variables

## Key idea

Choose a convenient value for the variable that appears in two ratios, then propagate the ratios.

## Common mistakes

- Using $z=6$ instead of $z=1$ after choosing $x=6$.
- Simplifying $8:\frac32$ incorrectly.
