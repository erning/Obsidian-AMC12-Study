# AMC12 2020-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Two nonhorizontal, nonvertical lines in the $xy$-plane intersect to form a $45^\circ$ angle. One line has slope equal to $6$ times the slope of the other line. What is the greatest possible value of the product of the slopes?

## Answer

**C.** $\frac32$

## Solution

Let the slopes be $m$ and $6m$. The tangent of the angle between two lines is

$$\left|\frac{6m-m}{1+6m^2}\right|=\tan45^\circ=1.$$

Hence

$$5m=\pm(1+6m^2).$$

For the positive-sign equation, $6m^2-5m+1=0$, whose roots are $m=\frac13$ and $m=\frac12$. The products $6m^2$ are $\frac23$ and $\frac32$; the negative-sign equation gives the same products. Therefore the greatest is $\frac32$.

## Knowledge points

- Angle between lines
- Slopes and quadratic equations

## Key idea

Use the tangent formula for the angle between two lines and compare all resulting slope products.

## Common mistakes

- Omitting the absolute value in the angle formula.
- Assuming only positive slopes are possible.
