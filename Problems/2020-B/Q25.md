# AMC12 2020-B Q25

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q25.png]]

## Problem

Let $a$ be selected uniformly at random from $[0,1]$. Independently, let $x$ be selected uniformly from $[0,a]$ and $y$ be selected uniformly from $[0,1]$. For which value of $a$ is the probability that

$$\sin^2(\pi x)+\sin^2(\pi y)>1$$

maximized?

## Answer

**B.** $2-\sqrt2$

## Solution

For a fixed $x$, use $\sin^2\theta=1-\cos^2\theta$. The inequality is equivalent to

$$|\cos(\pi y)|<\sin(\pi x).$$

As $y$ runs from $0$ to $1$, the measure of the set satisfying this inequality is

$$g(x)=
\begin{cases}
2x,&0\le x\le\frac12,\\
2-2x,&\frac12\le x\le1.
\end{cases}$$

Thus the desired probability is the average of $g(x)$ over $[0,a]$.

For $a\ge1/2$,

$$P(a)=\frac1a\left(\int_0^{1/2}2x\,dx+\int_{1/2}^a(2-2x)\,dx\right)
=2-a-\frac1{2a}.$$

Differentiate:

$$P'(a)=-1+\frac1{2a^2}.$$ 

The critical point in $[1/2,1]$ is $a=1/\sqrt2$. Since $P'(a)$ changes from positive to negative there, it gives the maximum. Therefore

$$P\left(\frac1{\sqrt2}\right)=2-\frac1{\sqrt2}-\frac1{2/(\sqrt2)}=2-\sqrt2.$$

## Knowledge points

- Probability as area or interval length
- Trigonometric identities
- Piecewise functions
- Calculus optimization

## Key idea

First find the allowed proportion of $y$ for each fixed $x$, then optimize the average over $[0,a]$.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $x$ as uniform on $[0,1]$ instead of $[0,a]$.
- Forgetting the piecewise behavior at $x=1/2$.
- Maximizing the integral without dividing by the interval length $a$.
