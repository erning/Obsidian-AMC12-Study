# AMC12 2020-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

Six semicircles lie inside a regular hexagon with side length $2$, with the diameters of the semicircles coinciding with the sides of the hexagon. What is the area inside the hexagon but outside all the semicircles?

## Answer

**D.** $3\sqrt3-\pi$

## Solution

The regular hexagon has area

$$\frac{3\sqrt3}{2}(2^2)=6\sqrt3.$$

Each semicircle has radius $1$, so the sum of their areas is $3\pi$. Adjacent semicircles overlap in six congruent lenses. Their centers are distance $\sqrt3$ apart, so one lens has area

$$2\cos^{-1}\left(\frac{\sqrt3}{2}\right)-\frac{\sqrt3}{2}\sqrt{4-3}
=\frac\pi3-\frac{\sqrt3}{2}.$$

Thus the union of the semicircles has area

$$3\pi-6\left(\frac\pi3-\frac{\sqrt3}{2}\right)=\pi+3\sqrt3.$$

The shaded area is

$$6\sqrt3-(\pi+3\sqrt3)=3\sqrt3-\pi.$$

## Knowledge points

- Regular hexagon area
- Circular-segment and lens area
- Inclusion-exclusion

## Key idea

Subtract the union of the semicircles, correcting the six pairwise overlaps.

## Common mistakes

- Subtracting $3\pi$ without correcting overlaps.
- Using the hexagon's apothem formula with the wrong side length.
