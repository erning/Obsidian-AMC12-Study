# AMC12 2020-B Q02

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q02.png]]

## Problem

What is the value of

$$\frac{100^2-7^2}{70^2-11^2}\cdot\frac{(70-11)(70+11)}{(100-7)(100+7)}?$$

## Answer

**A.** $1$

## Solution

Apply the difference-of-squares identity to both quadratic differences:

$$100^2-7^2=(100-7)(100+7),$$

$$70^2-11^2=(70-11)(70+11).$$

The two factors cancel exactly, leaving $1$.

## Knowledge points

- Difference of squares
- Algebraic cancellation

## Key idea

Factor before multiplying; the second fraction is precisely the reciprocal of the first.

## Common mistakes

- Expanding all four quadratic differences unnecessarily.
- Reversing one of the factors in the reciprocal.
