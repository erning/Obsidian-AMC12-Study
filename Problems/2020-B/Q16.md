# AMC12 2020-B Q16

![[assets/problems/2020-B/Q16.png]]

## Problem

An urn initially contains one red ball and one blue ball. Four times, a ball is selected at random, its color is recorded, and a new ball of the same color is added to the urn. What is the probability that exactly two red balls and two blue balls are added?

## Answer

**B.** $\frac15$

## Solution

Consider any fixed sequence containing two red selections and two blue selections. At each step, the numerator is the number of balls of the selected color, while the denominator is the total number of balls.

Regardless of the order, the numerators multiply to $1\cdot2\cdot1\cdot2=4$, and the denominators multiply to $2\cdot3\cdot4\cdot5=120$. Hence each such sequence has probability

$$\frac4{120}=\frac1{30}.$$

There are $\binom42=6$ sequences with two red and two blue selections. Thus the desired probability is

$$6\cdot\frac1{30}=\frac15.$$

## Knowledge points

- Conditional probability
- Counting sequences
- Pólya urn process

## Key idea

Every sequence with two selections of each color has the same probability, so count the six possible orders.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the four colors recorded as independent trials.
- Forgetting that drawing a color increases the chance of drawing that color again.
