# AMC12 2005-A Q13

![[assets/problems/2005-A/Q13.png]]

## Problem

In the five-sided star shown, the letters $A,$ $B,$ $C,$ $D,$ and $E$ are replaced by the numbers $3, 5, 6, 7,$ and $9,$ although not necessarily in that order. The sums of the numbers at the ends of the line segments $AB,$ $BC,$ $CD,$ $DE,$ and $EA$ form an arithmetic sequence, although not necessarily in that order. What is the middle term of the arithmetic sequence?

(A) $9$
(B) $10$
(C) $11$
(D) $12$
(E) $13$

## Answer

**D.** $12$

## Solution

Every number appears as an endpoint of exactly two of the five segments, so the total of the five sums is
$$
 2(3 + 5 + 6 + 7 + 9) = 60.
$$

The middle term of a five-term arithmetic sequence is its mean, namely $60 \div 5 = 12.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Arithmetic sequence
- Double counting

## Key idea

Use the constant difference and sum or count the required terms.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
