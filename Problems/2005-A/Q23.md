# AMC12 2005-A Q23

![[assets/problems/2005-A/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Two distinct numbers $a$ and $b$ are chosen randomly from the set $\{2, 2^2, 2^3, \ldots, 2^{25}\}.$ What is the probability that $\log_a b$ is an integer?

(A) $\dfrac{2}{25}$
(B) $\dfrac{31}{300}$
(C) $\dfrac{13}{100}$
(D) $\dfrac{7}{50}$
(E) $\dfrac{1}{2}$

## Answer

**B.** $\dfrac{31}{300}$

## Solution

Let $a = 2^j$ and $b = 2^k.$ Then $\log_a b = \dfrac{k}{j},$ which is an integer exactly when $j \mid k.$

For each $j,$ the number of valid $k \ne j$ in $\{1, \ldots, 25\}$ is $\left\lfloor \tfrac{25}{j} \right\rfloor - 1.$ Summing over $j$ gives
$$
 \begin{aligned} &24 + 11 + 7 + 5 + 4 + 3 + 2 \\ &\quad {}+ 2 + 4 \cdot 1 = 62 \end{aligned}
$$
 ordered pairs $(a, b).$

Since there are $25 \cdot 24 = 600$ ordered pairs of distinct elements, the probability is $\dfrac{62}{600} = \dfrac{31}{300}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.
