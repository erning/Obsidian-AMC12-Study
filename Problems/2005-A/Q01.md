# AMC12 2005-A Q01

![[assets/problems/2005-A/Q01.png]]

## Problem

Two is $10\%$ of $x$ and $20\%$ of $y.$ What is $x - y?$

(A) $1$
(B) $2$
(C) $5$
(D) $10$
(E) $20$

## Answer

**D.** $10$

## Solution

From $0.1x = 2$ we get $x = 20,$ and from $0.2y = 2$ we get $y = 10.$

Therefore $x - y = 20 - 10 = 10.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Percentage
- Linear equation

## Key idea

Translate the percentage into an equation for the same unknown total.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
