# AMC12 2005-A Q09

![[assets/problems/2005-A/Q09.png]]

## Problem

There are two values of $a$ for which the equation $4x^2 + ax + 8x + 9 = 0$ has only one solution for $x.$ What is the sum of those values of $a?$

(A) $-16$
(B) $-8$
(C) $0$
(D) $8$
(E) $20$

## Answer

**A.** $-16$

## Solution

The equation is $4x^2 + (a+8)x + 9 = 0.$ It has one solution when the discriminant vanishes:
$$
 (a+8)^2 - 4 \cdot 4 \cdot 9 = 0,
$$
 so $(a+8)^2 = 144$ and $a + 8 = \pm 12.$

Thus $a = 4$ or $a = -20,$ and their sum is $-16.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.
