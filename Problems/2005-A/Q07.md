# AMC12 2005-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2005-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Square $EFGH$ is inside square $ABCD$ so that each side of $EFGH$ can be extended to pass through a vertex of $ABCD.$ Square $ABCD$ has side length $\sqrt{50},$ $E$ is between $B$ and $H,$ and $BE = 1.$ What is the area of the inner square $EFGH?$

(A) $25$
(B) $32$
(C) $36$
(D) $40$
(E) $42$

## Answer

**C.** $36$

## Solution

By the symmetry of the figure, triangles $ABH,$ $BCE,$ $CDF,$ and $DAG$ are congruent right triangles. Hence
$$
 \begin{aligned} &BH = CE = \sqrt{BC^2 - BE^2} \\ &= \sqrt{50 - 1} = 7. \end{aligned}
$$

Since $E$ lies between $B$ and $H,$ the side of the inner square is $EH = BH - BE = 7 - 1 = 6.$

Therefore the area of $EFGH$ is $6^2 = 36.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Knowledge points

- Square geometry
- Pythagorean theorem
- Congruence geometry

## Key idea

Use symmetry and the diagonal relationship of the square.
