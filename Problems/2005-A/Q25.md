# AMC12 2005-A Q25

![[assets/problems/2005-A/Q25.png]]

## Problem

Let $S$ be the set of all points with coordinates $(x, y, z),$ where $x, y,$ and $z$ are each chosen from the set $\{0, 1, 2\}.$ How many equilateral triangles have all their vertices in $S?$

(A) $72$
(B) $76$
(C) $80$
(D) $84$
(E) $88$

## Answer

**C.** $80$

## Solution

The three equal sides of such a triangle must all have the same length. Checking the possible squared lengths in the $3 \times 3 \times 3$ grid, only three families of side occur.

**Face diagonals of a unit cube** (length $\sqrt2$): each of the $8$ unit cubes contributes $8$ triangles, one at each corner, for $8 \cdot 8 = 64.$

**Face diagonals of the $2 \times 2 \times 2$ cube** (length $2\sqrt2$): the three faces meeting at a vertex form one triangle, giving $8$ triangles.

**Edge-midpoint segments** (length $\sqrt6,$ joining midpoints of two edges): each of the $12$ edge midpoints is a vertex of two such triangles, for $\dfrac{12 \cdot 2}{3} = 8.$

The total is $64 + 8 + 8 = 80.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.
