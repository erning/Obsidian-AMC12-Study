# AMC12 2005-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2005-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Josh and Mike live $13$ miles apart. Yesterday Josh started to ride his bicycle toward Mike's house. A little later Mike started to ride his bicycle toward Josh's house. When they met, Josh had ridden for twice the length of time as Mike and at four-fifths of Mike's rate. How many miles had Mike ridden when they met?

(A) $4$
(B) $5$
(C) $6$
(D) $7$
(E) $8$

## Answer

**B.** $5$

## Solution

Since distance is rate times time, Josh rode $\dfrac{4}{5} \cdot 2 = \dfrac{8}{5}$ as far as Mike.

Let $m$ be the miles Mike rode. Then
$$
 13 = m + \dfrac{8}{5}m = \dfrac{13}{5}m,
$$
 so $m = 5.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Distance rate and time
- Ratio and proportion

## Key idea

Use distance $=$ rate $\imes$ time with consistent units.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
