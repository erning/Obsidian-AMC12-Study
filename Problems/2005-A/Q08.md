# AMC12 2005-A Q08

![[assets/problems/2005-A/Q08.png]]

## Problem

Let $A,$ $M,$ and $C$ be digits with
$$
 \begin{aligned} &(100A + 10M + C) \\ &\quad {}\cdot (A + M + C) = 2005. \end{aligned}
$$
 What is $A?$

(A) $1$
(B) $2$
(C) $3$
(D) $4$
(E) $5$

## Answer

**D.** $4$

## Solution

Since $A + M + C \le 9 + 9 + 9 = 27,$ and $2005 = 5 \cdot 401,$ the digit sum must be the smaller factor:
$$
 \begin{aligned} &100A + 10M + C = 401, \\ &\quad A + M + C = 5. \end{aligned}
$$

Reading off the digits, $A = 4,$ $M = 0,$ and $C = 1.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

- Digits
- Bounding to limit cases

## Key idea

Factor the number and use the prime exponents.
