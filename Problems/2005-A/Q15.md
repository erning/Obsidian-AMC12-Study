# AMC12 2005-A Q15

![[assets/problems/2005-A/Q15.png]]

## Problem

Let $AB$ be a diameter of a circle and $C$ be a point on $AB$ with $2 \cdot AC = BC.$ Let $D$ and $E$ be points on the circle such that $DC \perp AB$ and $DE$ is a second diameter. What is the ratio of the area of $\triangle DCE$ to the area of $\triangle ABD?$

(A) $\dfrac{1}{6}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{4}$
(C) $\dfrac{1}{3}$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{2}$
(E) $\dfrac{2}{3}$

## Answer

**C.** $\dfrac{1}{3}$

## Solution

Let $O$ be the center. Since $2 \cdot AC = BC,$ we have $AC = \dfrac{AB}{3},$ and $AO = \dfrac{AB}{2},$ so
$$
 \begin{aligned} &CO = AO - AC \\ &= \dfrac{AB}{2} - \dfrac{AB}{3} \\ &= \dfrac{AB}{6}. \end{aligned}
$$

Triangles $DCO$ and $DAB$ share the same altitude from $D$ to line $AB,$ so
$$
 \dfrac{[DCO]}{[DAB]} = \dfrac{CO}{AB} = \dfrac{1}{6}.
$$

Because $O$ is the midpoint of $DE,$ triangles $DCO$ and $ECO$ have equal areas, so
$$
 \begin{aligned} &[DCE] = 2\,[DCO] \\ &= \dfrac{2}{6}[DAB] = \dfrac{1}{3}[DAB]. \end{aligned}
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.
