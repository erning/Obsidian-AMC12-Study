# AMC12 2005-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2005-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

A rectangle with a diagonal of length $x$ is twice as long as it is wide. What is the area of the rectangle?

(A) $\dfrac{1}{4}x^2$
(B) $\dfrac{2}{5}x^2$
(C) $\dfrac{1}{2}x^2$
(D) $x^2$
(E) $\dfrac{3}{2}x^2$

## Answer

**B.** $\dfrac{2}{5}x^2$

## Solution

Let the width be $w.$ Then the length is $2w,$ and the diagonal gives
$$
 x^2 = w^2 + (2w)^2 = 5w^2.
$$

The area is $w \cdot 2w = 2w^2 = \dfrac{2}{5}x^2.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Rectangle
- Pythagorean theorem
- Area

## Key idea

Use the rectangle’s perpendicular sides and equal opposite sides.

## Common mistakes

- Do not use a formula before identifying exactly which quantities the statement fixes.
- Keep track of units, signs, and endpoint conditions throughout the calculation.
