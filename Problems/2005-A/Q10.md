# AMC12 2005-A Q10

![[assets/problems/2005-A/Q10.png]]

## Problem

A wooden cube $n$ units on a side is painted red on all six faces and then cut into $n^3$ unit cubes. Exactly one-fourth of the total number of faces of the unit cubes are red. What is $n?$

(A) $3$
(B) $4$
(C) $5$
(D) $6$
(E) $7$

## Answer

**B.** $4$

## Solution

The $n^3$ unit cubes have $6n^3$ faces total. The red faces are exactly the surface of the original cube, $6n^2$ of them.

Setting the red fraction to one-fourth,
$$
 \dfrac{6n^2}{6n^3} = \dfrac{1}{n} = \dfrac{1}{4},
$$
 so $n = 4.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.
