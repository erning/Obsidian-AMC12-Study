# AMC12 2005-A Q02

![[assets/problems/2005-A/Q02.png]]

## Problem

The equations $2x + 7 = 3$ and $bx - 10 = -2$ have the same solution for $x.$ What is the value of $b?$

(A) $-8$
(B) $-4$
(C) $-2$
(D) $4$
(E) $8$

## Answer

**B.** $-4$

## Solution

Solving $2x + 7 = 3$ gives $x = -2.$

Substituting into the second equation, $-2b - 10 = -2,$ so $-2b = 8$ and $b = -4.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Knowledge points

- Linear equation
- Substitution

## Key idea

Define the unknown and solve the resulting linear equation.
