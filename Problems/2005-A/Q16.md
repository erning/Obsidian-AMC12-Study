# AMC12 2005-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2005-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

Three circles of radius $s$ are drawn in the first quadrant of the $xy$-plane. The first circle is tangent to both axes, the second is tangent to the first circle and the $x$-axis, and the third is tangent to the first circle and the $y$-axis. A circle of radius $r \gt s$ is tangent to both axes and to the second and third circles. What is $r/s?$

(A) $5$
(B) $6$
(C) $8$
(D) $9$
(E) $10$

## Answer

**D.** $9$

## Solution

Put the big circle's center at $(r, r)$ and the second small circle's center at $(3s, s).$ They are externally tangent, so the distance between centers is $r + s.$

The horizontal and vertical gaps are $r - 3s$ and $r - s,$ so
$$
 (r + s)^2 = (r - 3s)^2 + (r - s)^2.
$$

Expanding gives $0 = r^2 - 10rs + 9s^2$ $= (r - 9s)(r - s).$ Since $r \ne s,$ we get $r = 9s,$ so $r/s = 9.$

Thus, the correct answer is **D**.

## Knowledge points

- Tangent circles
- Coordinate geometry
- Pythagorean theorem

## Key idea

Use tangent lengths and distances between centers.
