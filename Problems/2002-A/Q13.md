# AMC12 2002-A Q13

![[assets/problems/2002-A/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Two different positive numbers $a$ and $b$ each differ from their reciprocals by $1.$ What is $a + b?$

(A) $1$
(B) $2$
(C) $\sqrt{5}$
(D) $\sqrt{6}$
(E) $3$

## Answer

**C.** $\sqrt{5}$

## Solution

A positive number $x$ differs from its reciprocal by $1$ when $x - \dfrac1x = 1$ or $x - \dfrac1x = -1,$ i.e. $x^2 - x - 1 = 0$ or $x^2 + x - 1 = 0.$

The positive roots are $\dfrac{1+\sqrt5}{2}$ and $\dfrac{-1+\sqrt5}{2},$ which are reciprocals of each other. Their sum is $a + b = \sqrt5.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- A number $x$ with $\left|x - \dfrac1x\right| = 1$ satisfies $x^2 - x - 1 = 0$ or $x^2 + x - 1 = 0$
- The two positive solutions are reciprocals of each other; add them using the quadratic formula
