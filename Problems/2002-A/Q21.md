# AMC12 2002-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2002-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Consider the sequence of numbers $4, 7, 1, 8, 9, 7, 6, \ldots$ For $n \gt 2,$ the $n$th term of the sequence is the units digit of the sum of the two previous terms. Let $S_n$ denote the sum of the first $n$ terms of this sequence. The smallest value of $n$ for which $S_n \gt 10{,}000$ is

(A) $1992$
(B) $1999$
(C) $2001$
(D) $2002$
(E) $2004$

## Answer

**B.** $1999$

## Solution

Continuing the sequence gives $4, 7, 1, 8, 9, 7, 6,$ $3, 9, 2, 1, 3, 4, 7, 1, \ldots,$ which repeats with period $12.$ Each block of $12$ terms sums to $60.$

The largest $k$ with $60k \le 10{,}000$ is $k = 166,$ giving $S_{12\cdot 166} = 9960.$ Adding the next terms $4, 7, 1, 8, 9, 7, 6$ contributes $42,$ pushing the total past $10{,}000.$ So $n = 12\cdot 166 + 7 = 1999.$

Thus, the correct answer is **B**.

## Key idea

- Write out terms until the pattern repeats
- The first $12$ terms sum to $60$ and then repeat; find how many full blocks stay under $10{,}000$
