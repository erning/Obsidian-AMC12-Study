# AMC12 2002-A Q05

![[assets/problems/2002-A/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Each of the small circles in the figure has radius one. The innermost circle is tangent to the six circles that surround it, and each of those circles is tangent to the large circle and to its small-circle neighbors. Find the area of the shaded region.

(A) $\pi$
(B) $1.5\pi$
(C) $2\pi$
(D) $3\pi$
(E) $3.5\pi$

## Answer

**C.** $2\pi$

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2002-A/Q05-draft.svg]]

Each of the six outer unit circles is tangent to the central unit circle, so its center is $2$ units from the center. Adding one more radius, the large circle has radius $3$ and area $9\pi.$

The seven unit circles have total area $7\pi,$ so the shaded region has area $9\pi - 7\pi = 2\pi.$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- Each surrounding circle has its center $2$ units from the center, so the large circle has radius $3$
- Subtract the areas of the seven unit circles from the area of the large circle
