# AMC12 2002-A Q01

![[assets/problems/2002-A/Q01.png]]

## Problem

Compute the sum of all the roots of
$$
 \begin{aligned} &(2x+3)(x-4) \\ &\quad {}+(2x+3)(x-6)=0. \end{aligned}
$$

(A) $\dfrac{7}{2}$
(B) $4$
(C) $5$
(D) $7$
(E) $13$

## Answer

**A.** $\dfrac{7}{2}$

## Solution

Factoring out $2x+3$ gives $(2x+3)\big[(x-4)+(x-6)\big]$ $= (2x+3)(2x-10)$ $= 0.$

The roots are $-\dfrac{3}{2}$ and $5,$ whose sum is $-\dfrac{3}{2} + 5 = \dfrac{7}{2}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Key idea

- Both terms share the factor $2x+3$
- The equation becomes $(2x+3)(2x-10)=0$
