# AMC12 2002-A Q07

![[assets/problems/2002-A/Q07.png]]

## Problem

If an arc of $45^\circ$ on circle $A$ has the same length as an arc of $30^\circ$ on circle $B,$ then the ratio of the area of circle $A$ to the area of circle $B$ is

(A) $\dfrac{4}{9}$
(B) $\dfrac{2}{3}$
(C) $\dfrac{5}{6}$
(D) $\dfrac{3}{2}$
(E) $\dfrac{9}{4}$

## Answer

**A.** $\dfrac{4}{9}$

## Solution

Equal arc lengths give $\dfrac{45}{360}\cdot 2\pi R_A = \dfrac{30}{360}\cdot 2\pi R_B,$ so $\dfrac{R_A}{R_B} = \dfrac{30}{45} = \dfrac{2}{3}.$

The ratio of areas is $\left(\dfrac{R_A}{R_B}\right)^2 = \dfrac{4}{9}.$

Thus, the correct answer is **A**.

## Key idea

- Equal arc lengths: $\dfrac{45}{360}\cdot 2\pi R_A = \dfrac{30}{360}\cdot 2\pi R_B$
- Find $\dfrac{R_A}{R_B};$ the area ratio is its square
