# AMC12 2002-A Q06

![[assets/problems/2002-A/Q06.png]]

## Problem

For how many positive integers $m$ does there exist at least one positive integer $n$ such that $m \cdot n \le m + n?$

(A) $4$
(B) $6$
(C) $9$
(D) $12$
(E) infinitely many

## Answer

**E.** infinitely many

## Solution

Taking $n = 1,$ the inequality becomes $m \le m + 1,$ which holds for every positive integer $m.$

So every positive integer $m$ works, and there are infinitely many.

Thus, the correct answer is **E**.

## Key idea

- Consider what happens when $n = 1$
- For $n = 1,$ the inequality reduces to $m \le m + 1$
