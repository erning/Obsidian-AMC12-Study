# AMC12 2002-A Q22

![[assets/problems/2002-A/Q22.png]]

## Problem

Triangle $ABC$ is a right triangle with $\angle ACB$ as its right angle, $m\angle ABC = 60^\circ,$ and $AB = 10.$ Let $P$ be randomly chosen inside $\triangle ABC,$ and extend $\overline{BP}$ to meet $\overline{AC}$ at $D.$ What is the probability that $BD \gt 5\sqrt{2}?$

(A) $\dfrac{2 - \sqrt{2}}{2}$
(B) $\dfrac{1}{3}$
(C) $\dfrac{3 - \sqrt{3}}{3}$
(D) $\dfrac{1}{2}$
(E) $\dfrac{5 - \sqrt{5}}{5}$

## Answer

**C.** $\dfrac{3 - \sqrt{3}}{3}$

## Solution

Since $AB = 10$ and $\angle ABC = 60^\circ,$ the $30$-$60$-$90$ triangle has $BC = 5$ and $AC = 5\sqrt3.$

Place $E$ on $\overline{AC}$ with $CE = 5;$ then $BE = \sqrt{5^2 + 5^2} = 5\sqrt2.$ As $D$ moves along $\overline{AC},$ $BD = \sqrt{25 + CD^2}$ exceeds $5\sqrt2$ exactly when $CD \gt 5,$ i.e. when $D$ lies beyond $E,$ which happens iff $P$ is inside $\triangle ABE.$

The probability is
$$
 \begin{aligned} \dfrac{[ABE]}{[ABC]} &= \dfrac{EA}{CA} \\ &= \dfrac{5\sqrt3 - 5}{5\sqrt3} \\ &= \dfrac{3 - \sqrt3}{3}. \end{aligned}
$$

Thus, the correct answer is **C**.

## Key idea

- $\triangle ABC$ is $30$-$60$-$90$ with $BC = 5$ and $AC = 5\sqrt{3}$
- Choose $E$ on $\overline{AC}$ with $CE = 5,$ so $BE = 5\sqrt{2};$ then $BD \gt 5\sqrt{2}$ iff $P$ lies inside $\triangle ABE$
