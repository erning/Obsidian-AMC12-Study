# AMC12 2018-A Q09

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q09.png]]

## Problem

Which largest subset of $y\in[0,\pi]$ makes

$$\sin(x+y)\le\sin x+\sin y$$

for every $x\in[0,\pi]$?

## Answer

**E.** $0\le y\le\pi$

## Solution

For $x,y\in[0,\pi]$, we have $\sin x,\sin y\ge0$ and $\cos x,\cos y\le1$. Therefore

$$\sin(x+y)=\sin x\cos y+\cos x\sin y\le\sin x+\sin y.$$

The inequality holds for every permitted $y$, so the largest subset is the whole interval.

## Knowledge points

- Angle addition identities
- Sign and range analysis

## Key idea

Bound each term in the angle-addition formula separately.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming $x+y$ must lie in $[0,\pi]$.
- Treating $\cos x$ as nonnegative throughout the interval.
