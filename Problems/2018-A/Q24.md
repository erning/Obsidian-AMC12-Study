# AMC12 2018-A Q24

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q24.png]]

## Problem

Alice chooses uniformly from $[0,1]$, Bob chooses uniformly from $[1/2,2/3]$, and Carol chooses a fixed number in $[0,1]$. The winner is the player whose number lies between the other two. What should Carol choose to maximize her chance of winning?

## Answer

**B.** $\frac{13}{24}$

## Solution

For $c\le1/2$, Carol wins only when Alice is below $c$, so the probability is at most $1/2$. For $c\ge2/3$, she wins only when Alice is above $c$, giving probability at most $1/3$.

For $1/2<c<2/3$, the probability that Bob is above $c$ is

$$\frac{2/3-c}{1/6}=4-6c,$$

and the probability that Bob is below $c$ is $6c-3$. Thus

$$P(c)=c(4-6c)+(1-c)(6c-3)=-12c^2+13c-3.$$

This downward-opening quadratic is maximized at

$$c=\frac{-13}{2(-12)}=\frac{13}{24},$$

which lies in the required interval.

## Knowledge points

- Continuous probability
- Quadratic optimization

## Key idea

Split the event according to whether Bob is above or below Carol.

## Common mistakes

- Treating Bob as uniform on $[0,1]$.
- Optimizing only one of the two possible orderings.
