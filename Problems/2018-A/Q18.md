# AMC12 2018-A Q18

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Triangle $ABC$ has $AB=50$, $AC=10$, and area 120. $D$ and $E$ are the midpoints of $AB$ and $AC$. The angle bisector of $\angle BAC$ meets $DE$ at $F$ and $BC$ at $G$. Find the area of quadrilateral $FDBG$.

## Answer

**D.** $75$

## Solution

Triangle $ADE$ is similar to $ABC$ with scale factor $1/2$, so its area is $120/4=30$. Therefore trapezoid $EDBC$ has area $90$.

By the Angle Bisector Theorem, $G$ divides $BC$ in the ratio $AB:AC=5:1$, so $BG/BC=5/6$. The same ratio holds on $DE$, giving $DF/DE=5/6$. Quadrilaterals $FDBG$ and $EDBC$ have the same altitude, so their areas have ratio $5/6$. Hence

$$[FDBG]=\frac56(90)=75.$$

## Knowledge points

- Similarity and midpoints
- Angle Bisector Theorem

## Key idea

Convert the angle-bisector ratio into an area ratio between equal-height regions.

## Common mistakes

- Using $1/6$ instead of $5/6$.
- Forgetting the area scaling factor $1/4$ for $ADE$.
