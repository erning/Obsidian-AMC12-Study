# AMC12 2018-A Q02

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q02.png]]

## Problem

There are 5-pound rocks worth $14 each, 4-pound rocks worth $11 each, and 1-pound rocks worth $2 each, with at least 20 of each. Carl can carry at most 18 pounds. What is the maximum value he can carry?

## Answer

**C.** $50$

## Solution

The values per pound are $14/5$, $11/4$, and $2$. The 5-pound rocks are slightly more valuable per pound than the 4-pound rocks, while 1-pound rocks are least valuable. Check the possible number of 5-pound rocks:

- Three 5-pound rocks leave 3 pounds, worth at most $42+6=48$.
- Two 5-pound rocks leave 8 pounds, so two 4-pound rocks give $28+22=50$.
- One 5-pound rock allows three 4-pound rocks and one 1-pound rock, worth $14+33+2=49$.

Thus the maximum is $50$.

## Knowledge points

- Integer optimization
- Value density

## Key idea

Use the limited capacity and enumerate the few possible counts of the heaviest rocks.

## Common mistakes

- Choosing only by total price rather than price per pound.
- Assuming filling all 18 pounds automatically gives the best value.
