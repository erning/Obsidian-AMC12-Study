# AMC12 2018-A Q22

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q22.png]]

## Problem

The solutions of

$$z^2=4+4\sqrt{15}i,\qquad z^2=2+2\sqrt3i$$

form the vertices of a parallelogram. Its area is $p\sqrt q-r\sqrt s$ in the required reduced form. Find $p+q+r+s$.

## Answer

**A.** $20$

## Solution

For $z=a+bi$ in the first equation,

$$a^2-b^2=4,\qquad 2ab=4\sqrt{15}.$$

The modulus gives $a^2+b^2=16$, so $a^2=10$ and $b^2=6$. The roots are $\pm(\sqrt{10}+\sqrt6i)$. Similarly, the second equation gives roots $\pm(\sqrt3+i)$.

The parallelogram has area twice the absolute cross product of these two representative vectors:

$$2\left|\sqrt{10}-\sqrt{18}\right|=6\sqrt2-2\sqrt{10}.$$

Thus $(p,q,r,s)=(6,2,2,10)$ and the sum is 20.

## Knowledge points

- Complex square roots
- Vector cross products

## Key idea

Use modulus and real/imaginary parts to find the roots, then view them as vectors.

## Common mistakes

- Using the area of one triangle instead of the parallelogram.
- Forgetting the factor 2 from the opposite vertices.
