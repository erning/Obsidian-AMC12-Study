# AMC12 2018-A Q19

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q19.png]]

## Problem

Let $A$ be the positive integers with no prime factors other than 2, 3, or 5. The sum of reciprocals of all elements of $A$ is $m/n$ in lowest terms. Find $m+n$.

## Answer

**C.** $19$

## Solution

Every element has a unique form $2^i3^j5^k$ with $i,j,k\ge0$. Therefore the sum factors into geometric series:

$$
\sum_{i,j,k\ge0}\frac1{2^i3^j5^k}
=\frac1{1-1/2}\cdot\frac1{1-1/3}\cdot\frac1{1-1/5}
=2\cdot\frac32\cdot\frac54=\frac{15}{4}.
$$

Thus $m+n=15+4=19$.

## Knowledge points

- Unique prime factorization
- Infinite geometric series

## Key idea

Separate the independent exponents of 2, 3, and 5.

## Common mistakes

- Including numbers with another prime factor.
- Treating the sum as a finite divisor sum.
