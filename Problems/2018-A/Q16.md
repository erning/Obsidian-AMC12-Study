# AMC12 2018-A Q16

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q16.png]]

## Problem

For which values of $a$ do the curves

$$x^2+y^2=a^2,\qquad y=x^2-a$$

intersect at exactly three real points?

## Answer

**E.** $a>\frac12$

## Solution

From the parabola, $x^2=y+a$. Substitute into the circle:

$$y^2+y+a-a^2=0=(y+a)(y+1-a).$$

Thus $y=-a$ or $y=a-1$. The first gives $x^2=0$, always one point. The second gives

$$x^2=2a-1,$$

which gives two additional points exactly when $2a-1>0$. Hence $a>1/2$.

## Knowledge points

- Substitution of curves
- Counting real roots

## Key idea

Separate the intersection points by the two possible $y$-values.

## Common mistakes

- Counting $x=0$ as two points.
- Including the tangent case $a=1/2$, where the two points merge.
