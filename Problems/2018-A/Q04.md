# AMC12 2018-A Q04

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q04.png]]

## Problem

Alice says the nearest town is at least 6 miles away, Bob says it is at most 5 miles away, and Charlie says it is at most 4 miles away. None of the statements is true. If the distance is $d$, what is the set of possible values?

## Answer

**D.** $(5,6)$

## Solution

Negating “$d\ge6$” gives $d<6$. Negating “$d\le5$” gives $d>5$, and negating “$d\le4$” gives $d>4$. Their intersection is simply

$$5<d<6.$$

## Knowledge points

- Negation of inequalities
- Intersections of intervals

## Key idea

Translate each false statement carefully before intersecting the conditions.

## Common mistakes

- Replacing “at least” with a strict inequality before negating.
- Including either endpoint.
