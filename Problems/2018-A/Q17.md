# AMC12 2018-A Q17

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q17.png]]

## Problem

A right-triangular field has legs 3 and 4. A square in the right-angle corner is left unplanted. Its shortest distance to the hypotenuse is 2. What fraction of the field is planted?

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{145}{147}$

## Solution

Place the right angle at the origin, with the hypotenuse on

$$3x+4y-12=0.$$

If the square has side $s$, its nearest corner is $(s,s)$. The distance to the hypotenuse is

$$\frac{|7s-12|}{5}=2.$$

The solution compatible with the square lying in the triangle is $s=2/7$. The field area is $\frac12(3)(4)=6$, while the square area is $4/49$. Thus the planted fraction is

$$1-\frac{4/49}{6}=1-\frac2{147}=\frac{145}{147}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Distance from a point to a line
- Area ratios

## Key idea

Use the nearest square corner and reject the algebraic root that does not fit inside the triangle.

## Common mistakes

- Using distance denominator 4 or 3 instead of 5.
- Choosing $s=22/7$, which is geometrically impossible.
