# AMC12 2018-A Q03

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q03.png]]

## Problem

How many ways can algebra, geometry, and number theory be scheduled in a 6-period day if no two mathematics courses are in consecutive periods?

## Answer

**E.** $24$

## Solution

The three nonconsecutive periods can be chosen in four ways:

$$\{1,3,5\},\ \{1,3,6\},\ \{1,4,6\},\ \{2,4,6\}.$$

For each choice, the three distinct courses can be assigned in $3!=6$ orders. Hence the total is $4\cdot6=24$.

## Knowledge points

- Combinations
- Permutations

## Key idea

Separate choosing the periods from assigning the courses.

## Common mistakes

- Counting only the period sets and forgetting course order.
- Allowing adjacent periods.
