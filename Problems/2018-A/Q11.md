# AMC12 2018-A Q11

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q11.png]]

## Problem

A paper triangle has sides 3, 4, and 5 inches. It is folded so that point $A$ falls on point $B$. What is the length of the crease?

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{15}{8}$

## Solution

The crease is the perpendicular bisector of $AB$, meeting $AC$ at $E$. Let $D$ be the midpoint of $AB$, so $AD=5/2$. Since $\triangle ADE$ and $\triangle ACB$ are similar right triangles,

$$\frac{DE}{AD}=\frac{CB}{CA}=\frac34.$$

Thus

$$DE=\frac34\cdot\frac52=\frac{15}{8}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Perpendicular bisectors
- Similar right triangles

## Key idea

Folding $A$ onto $B$ makes every crease point equidistant from $A$ and $B$.

## Common mistakes

- Using the full side $AB=5$ instead of its midpoint distance.
- Confusing the crease with the altitude of the original triangle.
