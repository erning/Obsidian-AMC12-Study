# AMC12 2018-A Q08

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q08.png]]

## Problem

All triangles in the diagram are similar to isosceles triangle $ABC$, with $AB=AC$. Each of the 7 smallest triangles has area 1, and $\triangle ABC$ has area 40. Find the area of trapezoid $DBCE$.

## Answer

**E.** $24$

## Solution

The base $DE$ of the large inner triangle $ADE$ is four times the base of a smallest triangle. Similar figures have areas in the square ratio, so

$$[ADE]=4^2\cdot1=16.$$

The trapezoid is the part of $ABC$ outside $ADE$, hence

$$[DBCE]=40-16=24.$$

## Knowledge points

- Similar triangles
- Area scaling

## Key idea

Square the linear scale factor when comparing areas.

## Common mistakes

- Subtracting 4 instead of $4^2$.
- Using the seven smallest triangles as the whole inner triangle without the scale factor.
