# AMC12 2018-A Q21

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Which polynomial has the greatest real root?

## Answer

**B.** $x^{17}+2018x^{11}+1$

## Solution

Each of the first four polynomials has one negative root in $(-1,0)$ and no positive root. On this interval, the lower odd powers are more negative: $x^{17}<x^{19}$ and $x^{11}<x^{13}$. Since these polynomials are increasing on $(-1,0)$, the smaller values in choice B shift its zero to the right, so B has the greatest root among A–D.

Choice E has root $-2018/2019$, very close to $-1$. Evaluating choice B at this point gives a negative value, so B’s root lies to the right of E’s root. Therefore B is greatest.

## Knowledge points

- Monotonicity of polynomials
- Comparing roots without solving them

## Key idea

Compare polynomial values on the common interval containing their roots.

## Common mistakes

- Comparing coefficients only.
- Forgetting that powers behave differently for negative $x$.
