# AMC12 2018-A Q15

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q15.png]]

## Problem

A 7 by 7 grid has black and white squares, with at least one square of each color. A code is symmetric under all rotations by multiples of $90^\circ$ and reflections across the four symmetry axes of the square. How many symmetric codes are possible?

## Answer

**B.** $1022$

## Solution

Under all these symmetries, the 49 cells split into 10 orbits. Every cell in one orbit must have the same color, so each orbit can be chosen independently in $2^{10}$ ways. Exclude the two constant-color grids:

$$2^{10}-2=1022.$$

## Knowledge points

- Group actions and orbits
- Symmetry counting

## Key idea

Color orbits, not individual cells.

## Common mistakes

- Using $2^{49}$ colorings.
- Forgetting to exclude all-black and all-white codes.
