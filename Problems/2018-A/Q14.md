# AMC12 2018-A Q14

![[assets/problems/2018-A/Q14.png]]

## Problem

The solution $x\ne1,1/3$ to

$$\log_{3x}4=\log_{2x}8$$

can be written as $p/q$ in lowest terms. Find $p+q$.

## Answer

**D.** $31$

## Solution

Use natural logarithms:

$$\frac{\ln4}{\ln(3x)}=\frac{\ln8}{\ln(2x)}.$$

Since $\ln4=2\ln2$ and $\ln8=3\ln2$, this becomes

$$2\ln(2x)=3\ln(3x).$$

Exponentiating gives

$$ (2x)^2=(3x)^3,$$

so $4x^2=27x^3$ and, after excluding $x=0$, $x=4/27$. Therefore $p+q=4+27=31$.

## Knowledge points

- Change of base for logarithms
- Exponential equations

## Key idea

Cross-multiply logarithms before exponentiating.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $\log_{3x}4$ as $\log_3(4x)$.
- Keeping excluded or invalid base values.
