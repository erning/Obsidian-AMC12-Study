# AMC12 2019-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

In the displayed road map, how many routes use exactly 13 roads, start at $A$, end at $L$, and never traverse any portion of a road more than once?

## Answer

**E.** $4$

## Solution

Such a route is an open Euler trail. Its used graph must have exactly two odd-degree vertices, $A$ and $L$. The full graph has 17 edges; deleting four edges must change the parity of $A,L$ and the six degree-3 edge cities. The four deleted edges are forced by this parity condition, so the 13-edge subgraph is unique. Directly tracing its Euler trails from $A$ to $L$ gives four possible routes.

## Knowledge points

- Graph degree parity
- Euler trails

## Key idea

Use parity to determine the used edge set before counting trails.

## Common mistakes

- Counting paths that repeat a road.
- Applying the Euler condition to the full 17-edge map rather than the 13 used edges.
