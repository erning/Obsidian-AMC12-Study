# AMC12 2019-B Q02

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q02.png]]

## Problem

Consider the statement: “If $n$ is not prime, then $n-2$ is prime.” Which value of $n$ is a counterexample?

## Answer

**E.** $27$

## Solution

A counterexample must satisfy both the hypothesis and the failure of the conclusion: $n$ is not prime and $n-2$ is not prime. For $n=27$, we have $27$ composite and $27-2=25$ composite. The other composite choice is $15$, but $15-2=13$ is prime; $21-2=19$ is also prime. Hence $27$ is the counterexample.

## Knowledge points

- Prime and composite numbers
- Counterexamples to conditional statements

## Key idea

Check the hypothesis and conclusion separately.

## Common mistakes

- Treating a prime $n$ as a counterexample.
- Checking only that $n-2$ is composite.
