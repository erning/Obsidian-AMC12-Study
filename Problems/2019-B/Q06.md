# AMC12 2019-B Q06

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Points $A$ and $B$ are 10 units apart. How many points $C$ make a triangle with perimeter 50 and area 100?

## Answer

**A.** $0$

## Solution

The perimeter condition gives $CA+CB=40$. Thus $C$ lies on an ellipse with foci $A,B$, semimajor axis $a=20$, and focal distance $c=5$. Its semiminor axis is

$$b=\sqrt{a^2-c^2}=\sqrt{375}=5\sqrt{15}<20.$$

With base $AB=10$, area 100 would require height $2(100)/10=20$. But the greatest possible height on the ellipse is $b<20$, so no point works.

## Knowledge points

- Ellipses
- Triangle area

## Key idea

Compare the required height with the ellipse’s maximum height.

## Common mistakes

- Using $2a=20$ instead of $2a=40$.
- Counting intersections without checking whether the area is achievable.
