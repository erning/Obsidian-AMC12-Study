# AMC12 2019-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

For how many integral $x$ can a positive-area triangle have side lengths $\log_2x$, $\log_4x$, and 3?

## Answer

**B.** $59$

## Solution

Let $t=\log_2x$. Then $\log_4x=t/2$, so the side lengths are $t,t/2,3$. The nontrivial triangle inequalities are

$$t+\frac t2>3\quad\text{and}\quad \frac t2+3>t,$$

which give $t>2$ and $t<6$. Hence $4<x<64$. The integers are $5,6,\ldots,63$, numbering $63-5+1=59$.

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm change of base
- Triangle inequalities

## Key idea

Convert both logarithms to base 2 before applying the inequalities.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting the strict inequalities for positive area.
- Counting 4 or 64.
