# AMC12 2019-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

A red ball and a green ball are independently tossed into positive-integer bins, with probability $2^{-k}$ for bin $k$. What is the probability that the red ball lands in a higher-numbered bin than the green ball?

## Answer

**C.** $\frac13$

## Solution

The probability that both balls land in the same bin is

$$\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}2^{-2k}=\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}4^{-k}=\frac{1/4}{1-1/4}=\frac13.$$

By symmetry, the two strict orderings have equal probability. Their combined probability is $1-1/3=2/3$, so the desired probability is $1/3$.

## Knowledge points

- Infinite geometric series
- Symmetry and complementary events

## Key idea

Compute equality first, then split the remaining probability equally.

## Common mistakes

- Starting the geometric series at $k=0$.
- Assuming the strict ordering probabilities sum to 1.
