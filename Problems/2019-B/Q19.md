# AMC12 2019-B Q19

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q19.png]]

## Problem

Three players each start with $1. Every 15 seconds, each player who has money independently gives $1 to one of the other two players, chosen uniformly. What is the probability that after 2019 rounds each player again has $1?

## Answer

**B.** $\frac14$

## Solution

From state $(1,1,1)$ there are $2^3=8$ equally likely choices. Exactly two cyclic choices return to $(1,1,1)$, giving probability $1/4$. Every other outcome has state type $(2,1,0)$ up to permutation. From such a state, the player with 2 dollars gives one dollar, and the player with 1 dollar gives one dollar; among the four choices, exactly one returns to $(1,1,1)$. Thus regardless of the current nonterminal state, the probability of being at $(1,1,1)$ after the next round is $1/4$. In particular it is $1/4$ after 2019 rounds.

## Knowledge points

- Finite-state probability
- Symmetry and state classification

## Key idea

Collapse all holdings into the two possible state types.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the state stays $(1,1,1)$ at every round.
- Treating a broke player as making a random choice.
