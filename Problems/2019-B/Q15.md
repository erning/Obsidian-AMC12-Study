# AMC12 2019-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

In the figure, $AD$ is trisected by $B,C$ with $AB=BC=CD=2$. Three radius-1 semicircles have diameters on $AD$ and are tangent to $EG$. A radius-2 circle is centered at $F$. If the shaded area is $\frac ab\pi-\sqrt c+d$, find $a+b+c+d$.

## Answer

**E.** $17$

## Solution

Place $A,B,C,D$ at $0,2,4,6$ on the $x$-axis. Then $F=(3,1)$ and the large circle has area $4\pi$. The middle semicircle lies inside it and removes $\pi/2$. For either outer semicircle, the overlap with the large circle is

$$R=\frac{7\pi}{12}-2+\frac12\sqrt3.$$

Therefore the shaded area is

$$4\pi-\frac\pi2-2R=\frac73\pi-\sqrt3+4.$$

Thus $(a,b,c,d)=(7,3,3,4)$ and the sum is 17.

## Knowledge points

- Circle areas and overlaps
- Symmetry

## Key idea

Subtract the middle semicircle and twice the identical outer overlap.

## Common mistakes

- Subtracting the whole outer semicircles instead of their overlap.
- Forgetting the two symmetric outer regions.
