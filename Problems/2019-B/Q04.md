# AMC12 2019-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

A positive integer $n$ satisfies $(n+1)!+(n+2)!=440n!$. What is the sum of the digits of $n$?

## Answer

**C.** $10$

## Solution

Factor the left side and divide by $n!$:

$$
(n+1)!+(n+2)!=(n+1)!(n+3),
$$

so

$$
(n+1)(n+3)=440.
$$

Thus $n^2+4n-437=0=(n-19)(n+23)$, and positivity gives $n=19$. Its digit sum is $1+9=10$.

## Knowledge points

- Factorials
- Quadratic equations

## Key idea

Cancel $n!$ before expanding.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $(n+2)!$ as $(n+2)n!$.
- Keeping the negative root.
