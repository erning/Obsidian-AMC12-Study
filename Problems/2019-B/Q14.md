# AMC12 2019-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

Let $S$ be the positive divisors of $100{,}000$. How many numbers are products of two distinct elements of $S$?

## Answer

**C.** $117$

## Solution

Since $100{,}000=2^5\cdot5^5$, every divisor is $2^a5^b$ with $0\le a,b\le5$. A product has the form $2^x5^y$ with $0\le x,y\le10$, so there are $11^2=121$ possible values. A value can only be obtained as $d^2$ in the four corner cases $(x,y)\in\{0,10\}^2$; those four cannot be represented by two distinct divisors. Thus the answer is $121-4=117$.

## Knowledge points

- Prime factorization
- Divisor exponents

## Key idea

Count exponent pairs, then remove values forced to use equal factors.

## Common mistakes

- Counting divisors of $100{,}000$ instead of products.
- Removing all square products rather than only the four forced cases.
