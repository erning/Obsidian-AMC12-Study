# AMC12 2019-B Q18

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Square pyramid $ABCDE$ has base side 3 and altitude $AE=6$. Points $P,Q,R$ lie one third, one third, and two thirds of the way from $B,D,C$ toward $E$, respectively. Find the area of $\triangle PQR$.

## Answer

**C.** $2\sqrt2$

## Solution

Use coordinates

$$A=(0,0,0),\ B=(3,0,0),\ C=(3,3,0),\ D=(0,3,0),\ E=(0,0,6).$$

The points are $P=(2,0,2)$, $Q=(0,2,2)$, and $R=(1,1,4)$. Hence

$$\overrightarrow{PQ}=(-2,2,0),\qquad \overrightarrow{PR}=(-1,1,2).$$

Their cross product is $(4,4,0)$, whose magnitude is $4\sqrt2$. The triangle area is half of that, $2\sqrt2$.

## Knowledge points

- Coordinates in three dimensions
- Cross products and triangle area

## Key idea

Use two vectors from one vertex and take half the parallelogram area.

## Common mistakes

- Using $R$ one third of the way instead of two thirds.
- Forgetting the factor $1/2$.
