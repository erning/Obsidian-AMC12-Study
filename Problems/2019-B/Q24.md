# AMC12 2019-B Q24

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q24.png]]

## Problem

Let $\omega=-\frac12+\frac{\sqrt3}{2}i$. Let $S$ be the set of all points $a+b\omega+c\omega^2$ where $0\le a,b,c\le1$. What is the area of $S$?

## Answer

**C.** $\frac{3\sqrt3}{2}$

## Solution

The set is the Minkowski sum of three unit segments in directions $1,\omega,\omega^2$. Their vectors are

$$v_1=(1,0),\quad v_2=\left(-\frac12,\frac{\sqrt3}{2}\right),\quad v_3=\left(-\frac12,-\frac{\sqrt3}{2}\right).$$

The resulting zonogon has area equal to the sum of the magnitudes of the pairwise cross products. Each pair contributes $\sqrt3/2$, so

$$[S]=3\cdot\frac{\sqrt3}{2}=\frac{3\sqrt3}{2}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Complex numbers as vectors
- Minkowski sums and cross products

## Key idea

Translate the three coefficient intervals into three vector segments.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $S$ as a triangle.
- Using the signed cross products instead of their magnitudes.
