# AMC12 2019-B Q01

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q01.png]]

## Problem

Alicia had two containers. The first was $\frac{5}{6}$ full of water and the second was empty. After she poured the water into the second container, it was $\frac{3}{4}$ full. What is the ratio of the volume of the first container to the volume of the second container?

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{9}{10}$

## Solution

Let the container volumes be $V_1$ and $V_2$. The amount of water is unchanged, so

$$\frac{5}{6}V_1=\frac{3}{4}V_2.$$

Therefore

$$\frac{V_1}{V_2}=\frac{3/4}{5/6}=\frac{9}{10}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Ratios and proportions
- Conservation of quantity

## Key idea

Represent the common amount of water in both containers.

## Common mistakes

- Reversing the requested ratio.
- Comparing the fill percentages without accounting for different container volumes.
