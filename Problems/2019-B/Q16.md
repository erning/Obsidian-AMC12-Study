# AMC12 2019-B Q16

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q16.png]]

## Problem

Fiona starts on lily pad 0. From each pad she has equal chances to hop 1 or 2 pads. Pads 3 and 6 contain predators, and food is on pad 10. What is the probability she reaches 10 without landing on a predator?

## Answer

**A.** $\frac{15}{256}$

## Solution

Let $p(n)$ be the probability of reaching pad $n$ without previously landing on 3 or 6. Each safe pad sends half its probability one step and half two steps, while $p(3)=p(6)=0$. The values are

$$p(8)=\frac3{64},\qquad p(9)=\frac9{128}.$$

Thus

$$p(10)=\frac12p(8)+\frac12p(9)=\frac3{128}+\frac9{256}=\frac{15}{256}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Recurrence relations
- Conditional path counting

## Key idea

Propagate only probability arriving safely at each pad.

## Common mistakes

- Removing paths that jump over a predator; jumping over is allowed.
- Continuing to propagate probability from pads 3 or 6.
