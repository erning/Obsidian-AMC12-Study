# AMC12 2019-B Q05

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Candy prices are whole numbers of cents. Casper has exactly enough money to buy 12 red, 14 green, or 15 blue candies. Purple candies cost 20 cents each. What is the smallest possible number $n$ of purple candies he can buy?

## Answer

**B.** $21$

## Solution

If Casper has $M$ cents, then $M$ is divisible by $12$, $14$, and $15$. Hence

$$M\ge\operatorname{lcm}(12,14,15)=420.$$

The purple count is $n=M/20$, so the smallest possible value comes from $M=420$:

$$n=\frac{420}{20}=21.$$

## Knowledge points

- Least common multiples
- Divisibility

## Key idea

The common budget must be a common multiple of all three quantities.

## Common mistakes

- Using the product instead of the least common multiple.
- Forgetting that the number of purple candies must be integral.
