# AMC12 2019-B Q17

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q17.png]]

## Problem

How many nonzero complex numbers $z$ make $0,z,z^3$ the vertices of an equilateral triangle?

## Answer

**D.** $4$

## Solution

Equal side lengths require $|z|=|z^3|=|z^3-z|$. The first equality gives $|z|=1$. Then

$$|z^3-z|=|z||z^2-1|=|z^2-1|.$$

Writing $z=e^{i\theta}$, we have $|z^2-1|=2|\sin\theta|$. Thus $|\sin\theta|=1/2$, giving four angles modulo $2\pi$: $30^\circ,150^\circ,210^\circ,330^\circ$.

## Knowledge points

- Complex numbers in polar form
- Modulus and equilateral triangles

## Key idea

Normalize $|z|$ first, then reduce the third side to a trigonometric condition.

## Common mistakes

- Counting infinitely many points on the unit circle.
- Forgetting that $z$ must be nonzero and the vertices distinct.
