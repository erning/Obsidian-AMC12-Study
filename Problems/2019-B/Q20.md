# AMC12 2019-B Q20

![[assets/problems/2019-B/Q20.png]]

## Problem

Points $A(6,13)$ and $B(12,11)$ lie on a circle. The tangent lines at $A$ and $B$ meet on the $x$-axis. What is the area of the circle?

## Answer

**C.** $\frac{85\pi}{8}$

## Solution

Let the tangent intersection be $P=(x,0)$. Equal tangent lengths give $PA=PB$:

$$ (x-6)^2+13^2=(x-12)^2+11^2,$$

so $x=5$. Since the radius is perpendicular to the tangent, if the center is $O=(h,k)$ then

$$h+13k=175,\qquad 7h+11k=205.$$

Solving gives $O=(37/4,51/4)$. Therefore

$$r^2=\left(\frac{37}{4}-6\right)^2+\left(\frac{51}{4}-13\right)^2=\frac{85}{8},$$

and the area is $85\pi/8$.

## Knowledge points

- Tangent-radius perpendicularity
- Coordinate geometry

## Key idea

Find the tangent intersection first using equal tangent lengths.

## Common mistakes

- Using the slope of a tangent as the radius slope.
- Computing the area from the distance $AB$.
