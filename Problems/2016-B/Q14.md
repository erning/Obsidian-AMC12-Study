# AMC12 2016-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

An infinite geometric series has positive sum $S$ and second term $1$. Find the smallest possible $S$.

## Answer

**E.** $4$

## Solution

Let the ratio be $r$. The first term is $1/r$, so
$$S=\frac{1/r}{1-r}=\frac1{r-r^2}.$$
For $0<r<1$, the denominator is maximized at $r=1/2$, where it equals $1/4$. Hence $S\ge4$, with equality.

## Knowledge points

- Infinite geometric series
- Quadratic optimization

## Key idea

Minimize $1/(r-r^2)$ by maximizing the denominator.

## Common mistakes

- Allowing $r\ge1$
- Forgetting the series must converge
