# AMC12 2016-B Q24

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q24.png]]

## Problem

There are $77,000$ ordered quadruples with gcd $77$ and lcm $n$. Find the smallest possible $n$.

## Answer

**D.** $27,720$

## Solution

Write each entry as $77$ times a quadruple with gcd $1$ and lcm $m=n/77$. For a prime whose maximum exponent in $m$ is $M$, the number of exponent quadruples is
$$2(6M^2+1).$$
The factorization $77,000=14\cdot50\cdot110$ gives maximum exponents $1,2,3$. To minimize $m$, assign exponent $3$ to $2$, exponent $2$ to $3$, and exponent $1$ to $5$:
$$m=2^3\cdot3^2\cdot5=360.$$
Thus $n=77\cdot360=27,720$.

## Knowledge points

- gcd/lcm prime exponents
- Multiplicative counting

## Key idea

Count prime-exponent choices independently, then minimize by assigning larger exponents to smaller primes.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting to restore the gcd factor $77$
- Assigning exponents to primes in the wrong order
