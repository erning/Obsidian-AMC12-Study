# AMC12 2016-B Q18

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q18.png]]

## Problem

Find the area enclosed by $x^2+y^2=|x|+|y|$.

## Answer

**B.** $\pi+2$

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q18-draft.svg]]

In the first quadrant,
$$x^2+y^2=x+y,$$
which is the circle centered at $(1/2,1/2)$ with radius $1/\sqrt2$. The quadrant region is a right triangle of area $1/2$ plus a semicircle of area $\pi/4$. Multiplying by four gives
$$4\left(\frac12+\frac\pi4\right)=\pi+2.$$

## Knowledge points

- Absolute-value symmetry
- Completing the square

## Key idea

Analyze one quadrant and multiply by four.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the entire equation as one circle
- Using the full circle instead of the quadrant sector
