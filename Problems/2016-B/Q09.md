# AMC12 2016-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

A rectangular garden uses $20$ fence posts, with $4$ yards between neighboring posts. The longer side has twice as many posts as the shorter side. Find the area.

## Answer

**B.** $336$

## Solution

Let the shorter side have $x$ posts; the longer has $2x$. Counting corner posts once gives
$$x+2x+x+2x-4=20,$$
so $x=4$. The side lengths are $(4-1)4=12$ and $(8-1)4=28$, giving area $336$.

## Knowledge points

- Counting posts on a rectangle
- Translating spacing into length

## Key idea

Subtract the four duplicated corners before solving for posts.

## Common mistakes

- Counting each corner twice
- Multiplying the number of posts by $4$ directly
