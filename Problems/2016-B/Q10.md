# AMC12 2016-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

The vertices $P(a,b),Q(b,a),R(-a,-b),S(-b,-a)$ form a quadrilateral of area $16$, where $a>b>0$. Find $a+b$.

## Answer

**A.** $4$

## Solution

The side lengths are $(a-b)\sqrt2$ and $(a+b)\sqrt2$. Thus
$$2(a-b)(a+b)=16,$$
so $a^2-b^2=8$. The only positive squares differing by $8$ are $9$ and $1$, giving $(a,b)=(3,1)$ and $a+b=4$.

## Knowledge points

- Coordinate geometry
- Difference of squares

## Key idea

Recognize the quadrilateral as a rectangle.

## Common mistakes

- Using diagonal lengths as side lengths
- Allowing $a<b$
