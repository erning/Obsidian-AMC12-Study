# AMC12 2016-B Q06

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Three vertices of $\triangle ABC$ lie on $y=x^2$, $A$ is the origin, and $BC$ is horizontal. The area is $64$. Find $BC$.

## Answer

**C.** $8$

## Solution

The other vertices are $(x,x^2)$ and $(-x,x^2)$. Thus $BC=2x$ and the height is $x^2$, giving
$$\frac12(2x)(x^2)=x^3=64.$$
Hence $x=4$ and $BC=8$.

## Knowledge points

- Parabola symmetry
- Triangle area

## Key idea

Use symmetry to place the endpoints of the horizontal chord.

## Common mistakes

- Taking the height to be $x$
- Forgetting the factor $1/2$
