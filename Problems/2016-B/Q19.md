# AMC12 2016-B Q19

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q19.png]]

## Problem

Three players flip fair coins until their first heads. What is the probability all three stop on the same flip?

## Answer

**B.** $\frac17$

## Solution

For a fixed $n$, one player first gets heads on flip $n$ with probability $2^{-n}$. All three do so with probability $2^{-3n}=8^{-n}$. Thus
$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}8^{-n}=\frac{1/8}{1-1/8}=\frac17.$$

## Knowledge points

- Geometric series
- Independent events

## Key idea

Sum over the common stopping time.

## Common mistakes

- Using $2^{-n}$ instead of its cube
- Including $n=0$
