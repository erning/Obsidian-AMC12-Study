# AMC12 2016-B Q21

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q21.png]]

## Problem

A recursive construction in a unit square creates triangles $\triangle DQ_iP_i$. Find the infinite sum of their areas.

## Answer

**B.** $\frac14$

## Solution

With suitable coordinates, let $d_i=DQ_i$. The intersection and perpendicular construction gives
$$d_i=\frac1{i+1}.$$
The triangle has base $d_i$ and height $1/(i+2)$, so
$$[DQ_iP_i]=\frac12\left(\frac1{i+1}-\frac1{i+2}\right).$$
The sum telescopes to
$$\frac12\left(1-\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac1{n+2}\right)=\frac12.$$

## Knowledge points

- Coordinate geometry
- Telescoping series

## Key idea

Express each area as a difference of consecutive reciprocals.

## Common mistakes

- Summing the bases without the heights
- Losing the factor $1/2$
