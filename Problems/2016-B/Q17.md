# AMC12 2016-B Q17

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q17.png]]

## Problem

In the given triangle $AB=7$, $BC=8$, $CA=9$. Angle bisectors intersect altitude $AH$ at $P,Q$. Find $PQ$.

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{8\sqrt5}{15}$

## Solution

Let $BH=x$. From the two right triangles,
$$7^2-x^2=9^2-(8-x)^2,$$
so $x=2$ and $AH=3\sqrt5$. Applying the Angle Bisector Theorem in the two subtriangles gives
$$AP=\frac35AH,\qquad AQ=\frac79AH.$$
Therefore
$$PQ=\left(\frac79-\frac35\right)3\sqrt5=\frac{8\sqrt5}{15}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Altitude in a triangle
- Angle Bisector Theorem

## Key idea

Find the altitude and then locate both points as fractions of it.

## Common mistakes

- Using the wrong side ratio in the bisector theorem
- Subtracting lengths on different lines
