# AMC12 2016-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

Two acute angles have ratio $5:4$. The complement of one is twice the complement of the other. Find their sum.

## Answer

**C.** $135^\circ$

## Solution

Let the angles be $\alpha$ and $5\alpha/4$, with the larger angle $\beta$. The smaller complement is paired with $\beta$:
$$90^\circ-\alpha=2(90^\circ-\beta).$$
Using $\beta=5\alpha/4$ gives $\alpha=60^\circ$, $\beta=75^\circ$, so the sum is $135^\circ$.

## Knowledge points

- Complementary angles
- Ratios

## Key idea

Match the larger complement with the smaller angle.

## Common mistakes

- Pairing the complements in the wrong order
- Treating the ratio as $5\alpha:4\alpha$ without normalization
