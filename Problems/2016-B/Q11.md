# AMC12 2016-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

Count axis-parallel integer-coordinate squares contained in the region bounded by $y=\pi x$, $y=-0.1$, and $x=5.1$.

## Answer

**D.** $50$

## Solution

For side length $1$, the four vertical strips contribute $3+6+9+12=30$ squares. For side length $2$, the three possible positions contribute $2+5+8=15$. For side length $3$, they contribute $1+4=5$. No larger squares fit, so the total is
$$30+15+5=50.$$

## Knowledge points

- Integer-coordinate counting
- Geometric inequalities

## Key idea

Count separately by the integer side length.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $\pi$ as $3$
- Counting squares that cross $x=5.1$
