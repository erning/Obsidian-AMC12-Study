# AMC12 2016-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

A uniform equilateral triangle of side $3$ weighs $12$ ounces. What does a similar triangle of side $5$ weigh?

## Answer

**D.** $33.3$

## Solution

With the same density and thickness, weight is proportional to area, hence to the square of side length:
$$12\left(\frac53\right)^2=\frac{100}{3}\approx33.3.$$

## Knowledge points

- Similar figures
- Area scaling

## Key idea

Scale the weight by the square of the side ratio.

## Common mistakes

- Scaling linearly by $5/3$
- Using perimeter instead of area
