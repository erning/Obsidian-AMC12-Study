# AMC12 2016-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

The numbers $2,3,4,5,6,7$ label the faces of a cube. At each vertex multiply the three incident face labels. Maximize the sum of the eight products.

## Answer

**D.** $729$

## Solution

Pair opposite face labels as $(a,b),(c,d),(e,f)$. The vertex sum factors as
$$(a+b)(c+d)(e+f).$$
The three factors sum to $27$, so their product is at most $9^3=729$. This is attained by pairs $(2,7),(3,6),(4,5)$.

## Knowledge points

- Opposite-face pairing
- AM-GM

## Key idea

Factor the eight-term sum into three pair sums.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the eight products as independent
- Maximizing one pair sum without balancing all three
