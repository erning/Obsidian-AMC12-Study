# AMC12 2016-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2016-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Alice and Bob are $10$ miles apart and see an airplane due north and due west, respectively. Their elevation angles are $30^\circ$ and $60^\circ$. Estimate its altitude.

## Answer

**E.** $5.5$

## Solution

If the altitude is $h$, the horizontal distances are $\sqrt3h$ from Alice and $h/\sqrt3$ from Bob. These ground segments are perpendicular, so
$$3h^2+\frac{h^2}{3}=100.$$
Thus $h=\sqrt{30}\approx5.48$, closest to $5.5$.

## Knowledge points

- Right-triangle trigonometry
- Pythagorean theorem

## Key idea

Use the two elevation angles to express the two horizontal distances in terms of $h$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $\tan60^\circ$ for the wrong leg
- Treating the two ground distances as collinear
