# AMC12 2025-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

Find

$$
\sum_{n=2}^{255}
\frac{\log_2\left(1+\frac1n\right)}
{(\log_2 n)(\log_2(n+1))}.
$$

**Choices:** (A) $\frac34$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) $1-\frac1{\log_2 255}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) $\frac78$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) $\frac{15}{16}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) $1$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $\frac78$**

## Solution

Let

$$
a_n=\log_2 n.
$$

Then

$$
\log_2\left(1+\frac1n\right)
=\log_2\left(\frac{n+1}{n}\right)
=a_{n+1}-a_n.
$$

Each summand becomes

$$
\frac{a_{n+1}-a_n}{a_na_{n+1}}
=\frac1{a_n}-\frac1{a_{n+1}}.
$$

The sum telescopes:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\sum_{n=2}^{255}
\left(\frac1{a_n}-\frac1{a_{n+1}}\right)
&=\frac1{a_2}-\frac1{a_{256}}\\
&=1-\frac18\\
&=\boxed{\frac78}.
\end{aligned}
$$

## Knowledge points

- Logarithm quotient rule
- Telescoping sums
- Change of variables

## Key idea

Rewrite $\log_2(1+1/n)$ as $\log_2(n+1)-\log_2 n$.

## Common mistakes

- Replacing $\log_2(n+1)$ with $\log_2 n+1$.
- Reversing the telescoping signs.
- Forgetting that $\log_2 2=1$ and $\log_2 256=8$.
