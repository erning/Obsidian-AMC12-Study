# AMC12 2025-B Q03

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q03.png]]

## Problem

What is the value of $i(i-1)(i-2)(i-3)$, where $i=\sqrt{-1}$?

**Choices:** (A) $6-5i$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) $-10i$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) $10i$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) $-10$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) $10$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $-10$**

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q03-draft.svg]]

Group the factors into two pairs:

$$
i(i-1)=i^2-i=-1-i
$$

and

$$
(i-2)(i-3)=i^2-5i+6=5-5i.
$$

Therefore

$$
\begin{aligned}
i(i-1)(i-2)(i-3)
&=(-1-i)(5-5i)\\
&=-5+5i-5i+5i^2\\
&=-5-5\\
&=\boxed{-10}.
\end{aligned}
$$

## Knowledge points

- Complex number arithmetic
- Using $i^2=-1$
- Factoring a product for simpler expansion

## Key idea

Pair the factors so that each product is easy to simplify before multiplying the two results.

## Common mistakes

- Replacing $i^2$ with $1$.
- Losing the sign in the term $5i^2$.
- Treating $i$ as an ordinary real variable.
