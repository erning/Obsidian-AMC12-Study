# AMC12 2025-B Q05

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q05.png]]

## Problem

Positive integers $x$ and $y$ satisfy

$$
57x+22y=400.
$$

What is the least possible value of $x+y$?

**Choices:** (A) 10 &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) 11 &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) 13 &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) 14 &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) 15

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) 15**

## Solution

Reduce the equation modulo 22:

$$
57x\equiv400\pmod{22}.
$$

Since $57\equiv13$ and $400\equiv4\pmod{22}$,

$$
13x\equiv4\pmod{22}.
$$

The inverse of 13 modulo 22 is 17, because $13\cdot17\equiv1\pmod{22}$. Hence

$$
x\equiv4\cdot17\equiv2\pmod{22}.
$$

Since $x$ is positive and $57x<400$, we have $x\le6$. Therefore the only possible value is $x=2$.

Substitute:

$$
57(2)+22y=400
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
22y=286
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
y=13.
$$

Thus

$$
x+y=2+13=\boxed{15}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Linear Diophantine equations
- Modular inverses
- Bounding positive integer solutions

## Key idea

Use a congruence to determine $x$ modulo 22, then use the size bound to identify the only possible positive value.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting that $x$ and $y$ are positive, not merely nonnegative.
- Stopping at $x\equiv2\pmod{22}$ without applying $x\le6$.
- Dividing a congruence without checking invertibility.
