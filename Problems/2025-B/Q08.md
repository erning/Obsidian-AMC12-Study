# AMC12 2025-B Q08

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q08.png]]

## Problem

There are integers $a$ and $b$ such that

$$
x^3-5x^2+ax+b
$$

has $4+\sqrt5$ as a root. What is $a+b$?

**Choices:** (A) 13 &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) 17 &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) 20 &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) 30 &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) 68

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) 20**

## Solution

Because the polynomial has integer coefficients, the conjugate $4-\sqrt5$ is also a root. Their quadratic factor is

$$
\bigl(x-(4+\sqrt5)\bigr)\bigl(x-(4-\sqrt5)\bigr)
=x^2-8x+11.
$$

Let the third root be $r$. By Vieta, the sum of all three roots is 5, so

$$
(4+\sqrt5)+(4-\sqrt5)+r=5
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
8+r=5
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
r=-3.
$$

Therefore

$$
\begin{aligned}
x^3-5x^2+ax+b
&=(x^2-8x+11)(x+3)\\
&=x^3-5x^2-13x+33.
\end{aligned}
$$

Thus $a=-13$, $b=33$, and

$$
a+b=\boxed{20}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Conjugate roots
- Vieta's formulas
- Factoring polynomials

## Key idea

An integer-coefficient polynomial containing one quadratic irrational root must also contain its conjugate.

## Common mistakes

- Using only $4+\sqrt5$ and ignoring the conjugate.
- Getting the sign of the constant term wrong when multiplying by $(x+3)$.
- Using the product of roots before identifying the third root.
