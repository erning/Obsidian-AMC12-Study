# AMC12 2025-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

The windshield wiper on the driver's side of a large bus is depicted above. Arm $\overline{AB}$ pivots around $A$, sweeping out an arc of $60^\circ$ symmetric about the vertical line through $A$. The wiper blade $\overline{CD}$ is attached to $B$ at its midpoint and stays vertical as the arm moves. The arm is $3$ feet long, and the blade is $3.5$ feet tall. What is the area of the windshield cleaned by the wiper, in square feet, to the nearest hundredth?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $10.50$**

## Solution

Put $A$ at the origin. As the arm rotates through angles from $-30^\circ$ to $30^\circ$ from the vertical, the endpoint $B$ has horizontal coordinate

$$
x=3\sin\theta.
$$

Therefore the blade's midpoint moves horizontally from

$$
3\sin(-30^\circ)=-1.5
$$

to

$$
3\sin(30^\circ)=1.5.
$$

The total horizontal width swept by the blade is $3$ feet. Since the blade remains vertical and has height $3.5$ feet, every vertical slice of the cleaned region has width $3$ feet, or equivalently Cavalieri's principle gives

$$
\text{area}=3\cdot 3.5=\boxed{10.50}.
$$

The curved path of $B$ does not change this area calculation because the blade stays vertical; its horizontal position covers the whole interval from $-1.5$ to $1.5$.

## Knowledge points

- Area of a swept region
- Trigonometric coordinates
- Cavalieri's principle

## Key idea

Use the horizontal range of the blade's midpoint, not the arc length of the arm.

## Common mistakes

- Multiplying blade length by the arc length of $B$.
- Using $3\sin 60^\circ$ instead of the endpoint angles $\pm30^\circ$.
- Treating the blade as if it rotated with the arm.
