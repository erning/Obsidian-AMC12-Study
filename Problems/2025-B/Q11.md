# AMC12 2025-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

Nine athletes, no two of whom are the same height, draw wristbands one at a time without replacement from a bag containing 3 blue, 3 red, and 3 green bands. The athletes are divided into the three corresponding groups. The tallest member of each group is named captain. What is the probability that the captains are the three tallest athletes?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $\frac{9}{28}$**

## Solution

The three tallest athletes are exactly the captains if and only if they receive three different colors. Once the tallest athlete has received a color, there are $8$ wristbands left, of which $6$ have colors different from the first athlete. Thus the second-tallest athlete avoids the first athlete's group with probability

$$
\frac{6}{8}.
$$

After two different colors have been used, there are $7$ wristbands left, and exactly $3$ have the third color. Therefore the third-tallest athlete receives the remaining color with conditional probability

$$
\frac{3}{7}.
$$

Hence

$$
\frac{6}{8}\cdot\frac{3}{7}
=\frac{18}{56}
=\boxed{\frac{9}{28}}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Sequential probability
- Symmetry
- Conditional probability

## Key idea

The tallest athlete is automatically captain; the other two must receive the two colors not used by the tallest athlete.

## Common mistakes

- Requiring the first athlete to have a particular color.
- Using $\frac{2}{3}$ for the second athlete without accounting for the finite number of wristbands.
- Forgetting that the third athlete must receive the one remaining color.
