# AMC12 2025-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

A container has a $1\times1$ square bottom, a $3\times3$ open square top, and four congruent trapezoidal sides, as shown. Starting when the container is empty, a hose running at a constant rate takes $35$ minutes to fill the container up to the midline of the trapezoids. How many more minutes will it take to fill the remainder?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(D) $95$**

## Solution

Let $t$ denote the fraction of the height reached, so $0\le t\le1$. By similarity, the side length of a horizontal square cross-section is

$$
s(t)=1+2t.
$$

Its area is

$$
A(t)=(1+2t)^2.
$$

The volume up to level $t$ is proportional to

$$
\int_0^t(1+2u)^2\,du.
$$

At the midline, $t=\frac12$:

$$
V_{\text{first}}
=\int_0^{1/2}(1+2u)^2\,du
=\frac{7}{6}
$$

after using the common height factor. The full volume is

$$
V_{\text{total}}=\int_0^1(1+2u)^2\,du
=\frac{13}{3}
$$

in the same normalized units. Hence

$$
V_{\text{remaining}}
=V_{\text{total}}-V_{\text{first}}
=\frac{19}{6}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\frac{V_{\text{remaining}}}{V_{\text{first}}}
=\frac{19/6}{7/6}
=\frac{19}{7}.
$$

The remaining time is

$$
35\cdot\frac{19}{7}=\boxed{95}
$$

minutes.

## Knowledge points

- Volume by cross-sections
- Similarity
- Constant-rate proportionality

## Key idea

The side length of each square cross-section changes linearly with height, so the volume ratio is not the same as the height ratio.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming the first and second halves have equal volume.
- Using only the top and bottom areas instead of integrating cross-sections.
- Forgetting that time is proportional to volume because the filling rate is constant.
