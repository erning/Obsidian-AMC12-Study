# AMC12 2025-B Q23

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q23.png]]

## Problem

Let $S$ be the set of integers $z>1$ such that for all nonnegative integers $x<y<z$, the remainder when $2025^x$ is divided by $z$ is less than the remainder when $2025^y$ is divided by $z$. What is the sum of the elements of $S$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $4319$**

## Solution

Consider the residues

$$
2025^0\bmod z,\ 2025^1\bmod z,\ \ldots,\ 2025^{z-1}\bmod z.
$$

They are strictly increasing and each lies in $\{0,1,\ldots,z-1\}$. The only strictly increasing list of $z$ integers chosen from this set is

$$
0,1,2,\ldots,z-1.
$$

In particular, the first two residues are $0$ and $1$, so

$$
2025\equiv1\pmod z.
$$

Thus $z$ must divide

$$
2025-1=2024.
$$

Conversely, if $z\mid2024$, then $2025\equiv1\pmod z$, so $2025^x\equiv x\pmod z$ for all $x$. For $0\le x<y<z$, the residues are exactly $x$ and $y$, so the condition holds.

Therefore $S$ consists of all divisors of $2024$ except $1$. Since

$$
2024=2^3\cdot11\cdot23,
$$

the sum of all positive divisors is

$$
(1+2+4+8)(1+11)(1+23)=15\cdot12\cdot24=4320.
$$

Excluding $1$ gives

$$
\boxed{4319}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Modular powers
- Divisibility
- Sum of divisors

## Key idea

The increasing residue list must contain every residue exactly once, forcing the second residue to be $1$.

## Common mistakes

- Checking only periodicity instead of strict increase.
- Forgetting to exclude $z=1$.
- Assuming $2025^x\equiv x$ without first proving $2025\equiv1\pmod z$.
