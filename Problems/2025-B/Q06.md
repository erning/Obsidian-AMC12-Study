# AMC12 2025-B Q06

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q06.png]]

## Problem

The total cost of 36 sweatshirts is the decimal amount $\$ABB.BA$, where $A$ and $B$ are digits and $A\ne0$. What is $A+B$?

**Choices:** (A) 7 &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) 8 &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) 11 &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) 14 &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) 15

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) 11**

## Solution

Convert the total cost to cents. The amount $\$ABB.BA$ represents

$$
10000A+1110B+A=10001A+1110B
$$

cents. Since it is the total cost of 36 identical shirts, this number must be divisible by 36.

Modulo 36,

$$
10001\equiv29,\qquad1110\equiv30,
$$

so

$$
29A+30B\equiv0\pmod{36}.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
7A+6B\equiv0\pmod{36}.
$$

Testing the digit possibilities with $A\ne0$ gives

$$
A=6,\qquad B=5.
$$

Indeed,

$$
7A+6B=42+30=72,
$$

which is divisible by 36. The total is $\$655.56$, and

$$
655.56\div36=18.21,
$$

so it is a valid total cost.

Therefore

$$
A+B=\boxed{11}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Place value
- Divisibility by 36
- Modular arithmetic

## Key idea

Represent the decimal amount in cents and impose the divisibility condition from the 36 equal prices.

## Common mistakes

- Treating $ABB.BA$ as $1000A+110B$.
- Checking divisibility by 6 but not by 36.
- Allowing $A=0$, even though the problem excludes it.
