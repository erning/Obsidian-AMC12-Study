# AMC12 2025-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

The two-digit number $\overline{ab}$ in base 7 equals the two-digit number $\overline{ba}$ in base 9. What is $a+b$?

**Choices:** (A) 7 &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) 9 &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) 10 &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) 11 &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) 14

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) 7**

## Solution

The base-7 number $\overline{ab}_7$ has value $7a+b$, while $\overline{ba}_9$ has value $9b+a$. Therefore

$$
7a+b=9b+a.
$$

Rearranging,

$$
6a=8b
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
3a=4b.
$$

Because $a$ and $b$ are digits, $a<7$ and $b<9$. The smallest positive solution to $3a=4b$ is

$$
a=4,\qquad b=3.
$$

It checks:

$$
\overline{43}_7=4\cdot7+3=31
$$

and

$$
\overline{34}_9=3\cdot9+4=31.
$$

Thus

$$
a+b=\boxed7.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Positional notation
- Converting numbers between bases
- Linear equations with digit constraints

## Key idea

Translate each two-digit numeral into its base-10 value before solving.

## Common mistakes

- Writing $\overline{ab}_7=10a+b$.
- Forgetting that $a$ and $b$ must be valid digits in both bases.
- Accepting a multiple of $(a,b)=(4,3)$ that exceeds the digit limits.
