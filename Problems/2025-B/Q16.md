# AMC12 2025-B Q16

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q16.png]]

## Problem

An analog clock starts at midnight and runs for $2025$ minutes before stopping. What is the tangent of the acute angle between the hour hand and the minute hand when the clock stops?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(B) $\sqrt{2}-1$**

## Solution

Since $2025=33\cdot60+45$, the clock stops at $9{:}45$ modulo a $12$-hour cycle.

At $9{:}45$, the minute hand is at $270^\circ$. The hour hand has moved $9+\frac34$ hours from $12$, so it is at

$$
30\left(9+\frac34\right)=292.5^\circ.
$$

The acute angle is $22.5^\circ$. Therefore

$$
\tan 22.5^\circ=\sqrt2-1.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Clock angles
- Continuous motion of the hour hand
- Half-angle trigonometric identities

## Key idea

The hour hand moves continuously; at $9{:}45$ it is three-quarters of the way from $9$ to $10$.

## Common mistakes

- Reading the time as $9{:}00$.
- Using $45^\circ$ as the angle between the hands.
- Forgetting to reduce $2025$ minutes modulo $12$ hours.
