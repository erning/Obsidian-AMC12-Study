# AMC12 2025-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

The altitude to the hypotenuse of a $30^\circ$-$60^\circ$-$90^\circ$ right triangle is divided into segments of lengths $x<y$ by the median to the shortest side. What is $\frac{x}{x+y}$?

**Choices:** (A) $\frac37$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) $\frac{\sqrt3}{4}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) $\frac49$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) $\frac5{11}$ &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) $\frac{4\sqrt3}{15}$

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(A) $\frac37$**

## Solution

Scale the triangle so that the legs are 1 and $\sqrt3$. Let the right-angle vertex be $C=(0,0)$, the longer leg endpoint be $A=(\sqrt3,0)$, and the shorter leg endpoint be $B=(0,1)$.

The hypotenuse $AB$ has equation

$$
y=1-\frac{x}{\sqrt3}.
$$

The altitude from $C$ is perpendicular to $AB$, so it has equation $y=\sqrt3x$. Its intersection with $AB$ is

$$
H=\left(\frac{\sqrt3}{4},\frac34\right).
$$

The shortest side is $BC$, whose midpoint is $M=(0,\frac12)$. The median from $A$ to $M$ intersects the altitude at

$$
P=\left(\frac{\sqrt3}{7},\frac37\right).
$$

Since $C$, $P$, and $H$ lie on the same altitude, distances along the altitude are proportional to the parameter in the direction vector $(1,\sqrt3)$. Comparing the $x$-coordinates,

$$
\frac{CP}{CH}
=\frac{\sqrt3/7}{\sqrt3/4}
=\frac47.
$$

Thus $CP=\frac47CH$ and $PH=\frac37CH$. Since $PH$ is the shorter segment $x$,

$$
\frac{x}{x+y}=\frac{PH}{CH}=\boxed{\frac37}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- $30^\circ$-$60^\circ$-$90^\circ$ triangles
- Coordinate geometry
- Medians and altitudes
- Ratios along a line

## Key idea

Choose convenient coordinates so the altitude and median become linear equations; only the relative position of their intersection matters.

## Common mistakes

- Using the median to the hypotenuse instead of the median to the shortest side.
- Assigning the shorter segment to $CP$ instead of $PH$.
- Using the wrong slope for the altitude.
