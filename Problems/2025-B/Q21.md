# AMC12 2025-B Q21

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Two non-congruent triangles have the same area. Each triangle has sides of lengths $8$ and $9$, and the third side of each triangle has integer length. What is the sum of the two third-side lengths?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) $24$**

## Solution

Let $\theta$ be the included angle between the sides $8$ and $9$ in the first triangle. Its area is

$$
\frac12\cdot8\cdot9\sin\theta=36\sin\theta.
$$

The non-congruent triangle must use the supplementary angle $180^\circ-\theta$, because the two triangles have equal area:

$$
\sin(180^\circ-\theta)=\sin\theta.
$$

Let the third sides be $t_1$ and $t_2$. By the Law of Cosines,

$$
t_1^2=8^2+9^2-2(8)(9)\cos\theta=145-144\cos\theta,
$$

and

$$
t_2^2=145+144\cos\theta.
$$

Therefore

$$
t_1^2+t_2^2=290.
$$

The triangle inequality gives $1<t_i<17$. The only pair of positive integer squares in this range summing to $290$ is

$$
11^2+13^2=121+169=290.
$$

Hence

$$
t_1+t_2=\boxed{24}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Area formula with two sides and an included angle
- Law of Cosines
- Triangle inequality

## Key idea

Equal areas force supplementary included angles, causing the cosine terms to cancel when the two third-side squares are added.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming equal area means congruent triangles.
- Using the same cosine sign for both triangles.
- Forgetting the triangle inequality when checking integer pairs.
