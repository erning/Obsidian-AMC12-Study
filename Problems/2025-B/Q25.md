# AMC12 2025-B Q25

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q25.png]]

## Problem

Three concentric circles have radii $1$, $2$, and $3$. An equilateral triangle with side length $s$ has one vertex on each circle. What is $s^2$?

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(E) $7$**

## Solution

### Method 1: Geometric 60ﾂｰ Rotation (Draft Construction)

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q25-draft.svg]]

Let $O$ be the common center of the concentric circles, and let vertices $P, Q, R$ lie on the circles of radii $1, 2, 3$ respectively, so:

$$
OP = 1, ¥qquad OQ = 2, ¥qquad OR = 3.
$$

1. **Rotate**: Rotate $¥triangle POQ$ counterclockwise about vertex $P$ by $60^¥circ$, mapping side $PQ$ onto $PR$, and mapping $O$ to point $O'$.
2. **Lengths**:
   - By congruence $¥triangle POQ ¥cong ¥triangle PO'R$, $PO' = PO = 1$ and $O'R = OQ = 2$.
   - Since $¥angle OPO' = 60^¥circ$ and $PO = PO' = 1$, $¥triangle POO'$ is an equilateral triangle, so $OO' = 1$.
3. **Collinearity**:
   - Note that $OO' = 1$, $O'R = 2$, and $OR = 3$.
   - Since $OO' + O'R = 1 + 2 = 3 = OR$, by equality in the triangle inequality, **$O'$ lies directly on segment $OR$** ($O, O', R$ are collinear).
4. **Law of Cosines**:
   - In equilateral $¥triangle POO'$, $¥angle PO'O = 60^¥circ$.
   - Since $O, O', R$ are collinear, $¥angle PO'R = 180^¥circ - 60^¥circ = 120^¥circ$.
   - In $¥triangle PO'R$, applying the Law of Cosines gives:

$$
s^2 = PR^2 = PO'^2 + O'R^2 - 2(PO')(O'R)¥cos 120^¥circ
$$

$$
s^2 = 1^2 + 2^2 - 2(1)(2)¥left(-¥frac{1}{2}¥right) = 1 + 4 + 2 = ¥boxed{7}.
$$

### Method 2: Algebraic Gram Determinant

Let the position vectors of $P,Q,R$ be $¥mathbf p,¥mathbf q,¥mathbf r$, and set $u=s^2$. Since the three vectors lie in the plane, their $3¥times3$ Gram determinant is $0$. Using

$$
¥mathbf p¥cdot¥mathbf q=¥frac{r_1^2+r_2^2-u}{2},
$$

and the analogous formulas for the other pairs, expansion of that determinant gives

$$
3¥left(r_1^4+r_2^4+r_3^4+u^2¥right)
=¥left(r_1^2+r_2^2+r_3^2+u¥right)^2.
$$

Substitute $r_1=1$, $r_2=2$, and $r_3=3$:

$$
3(1+16+81+u^2)=(1+4+9+u)^2.
$$

Let $u=s^2$. Then

$$
3(98+u^2)=(14+u)^2 ¥implies 2u^2-28u+98=0 ¥implies (u-7)^2=0.
$$

Therefore

$$
s^2=u=¥boxed7.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Equilateral triangle geometry & 60ﾂｰ rotation
- Triangle inequality equality (collinearity test)
- Law of Cosines
- Gram determinant of planar vectors

## Key idea

When given an equilateral triangle with known distances from its vertices to a single point, rotating by 60ﾂｰ around a vertex is the standard, most elegant geometric construction.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the three circles as non-concentric.
- Assuming $O$ is the centroid/circumcenter of $¥triangle PQR$ (if $O$ were the center, $OP=OQ=OR$, contradicting radii $1, 2, 3$).
- Solving for $s$ when the question asks for $s^2$.
