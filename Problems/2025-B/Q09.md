# AMC12 2025-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2025-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

What is the tens digit of $6^{6^6}$?

**Choices:** (A) 1 &nbsp;&nbsp; (B) 3 &nbsp;&nbsp; (C) 5 &nbsp;&nbsp; (D) 7 &nbsp;&nbsp; (E) 9

## Answer

- Correct choice: **(C) 5**

## Solution

For powers of 6 with exponent at least 2, the last two digits cycle:

$$
36,\ 16,\ 96,\ 76,\ 56,
$$

with period 5. We need the exponent $6^6$ modulo 5:

$$
6^6\equiv1^6\equiv1\pmod5.
$$

Therefore $6^{6^6}$ has the same last two digits as $6^1$ in this cycle, namely 56. Its tens digit is

$$
\boxed5.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Modular exponentiation
- Cycles of last digits
- Congruences

## Key idea

To determine the last two digits, reduce the exponent modulo the cycle length 5.

## Common mistakes

- Reading the exponent as $6\cdot6$ instead of $6^6$.
- Using the ones-digit cycle of length 4 for a two-digit question.
- Concluding the answer from the ones digit alone.
