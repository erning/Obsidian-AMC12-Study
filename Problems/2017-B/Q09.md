# AMC12 2017-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2017-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

Two circles have centers $(-10,-4)$ and $(3,9)$, radii $13$ and $\sqrt{65}$. Their common chord lies on $x+y=c$. Find $c$.

## Answer

**A.** $3$

## Solution

![[assets/problems/2017-B/Q09-draft.svg]]

The circle equations are
$$(x+10)^2+(y+4)^2=169,$$
$$(x-3)^2+(y-9)^2=65.$$
Expand both and subtract. The quadratic terms cancel, leaving
$$26x+26y=78,$$
or $x+y=3$. The common chord is the radical axis, so $c=3$.

## Knowledge points

- Equations of circles
- Radical axis of two circles

## Key idea

Subtracting circle equations eliminates $x^2+y^2$.

## Common mistakes

- Solving both circles separately
- Forgetting that the line through intersection points is obtained by subtraction
