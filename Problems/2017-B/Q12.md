# AMC12 2017-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2017-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

Find the sum of the roots of $z^{12}=64$ having positive real part.

## Answer

**D.** $2\sqrt3+6$

## Solution

The roots have modulus $2$ and arguments that are multiples of $30^\circ$. Positive real part means angles
$$0^\circ,\ \pm30^\circ,\ \pm60^\circ.$$
The conjugate pairs cancel in the imaginary direction, so the sum is
$$2+2(2\cos30^\circ)+2(2\cos60^\circ)=2+2\sqrt3+2=2\sqrt3+6.$$

## Knowledge points

- Complex roots
- Polar form and conjugate pairs

## Key idea

Use symmetry to cancel imaginary parts instead of \summing twelve roots individually.

## Common mistakes

- Using modulus $64$ rather than $2$
- Including roots with zero or negative real part
