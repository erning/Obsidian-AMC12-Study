# AMC12 2017-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2017-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Six disks form a triangular arrangement. Paint three blue, two red, and one green. Count colorings up to rotations and reflections.

## Answer

**D.** $12$

## Solution

There are three corner disks and three non-corner disks. Consider the position of the green disk.

If green is a corner, classify the red pair by how many red disks are adjacent to it. The three cases contribute $1+3+2=6$ colorings.

If green is a non-corner, classify the red pair by how many red disks are corners. This again gives $1+3+2=6$. The remaining disks are blue, so the total is
$$6+6=12.$$

## Knowledge points

- Symmetry and equivalence classes
- Casework

## Key idea

Fix the unique green disk first; this removes most of the symmetry.

## Common mistakes

- Counting labeled positions without identifying reflections
- Treating the three corner and three non-corner positions as interchangeable
