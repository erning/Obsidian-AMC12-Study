# AMC12 2017-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2017-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

Each side of an equilateral triangle is extended beyond its endpoint by three times the original side. Find the area ratio of the resulting outer triangle to the original triangle.

## Answer

**E.** $37:1$

## Solution

Let the original area be $X$. The three corner triangles adjacent to the original triangle each have area $3X$.

The three outer corner triangles have a base and altitude scaled so that each has area $9X$. Therefore
$$[A'B'C']=X+3(3X)+3(9X)=37X.$$

## Knowledge points

- Areas of similar triangles
- Geometric decomposition

## Key idea

Partition the large triangle into the original triangle, three triangles of area $3X$, and three of area $9X$.

## Common mistakes

- Scaling area linearly instead of quadratically
- Counting only the three triangles adjacent to the original
