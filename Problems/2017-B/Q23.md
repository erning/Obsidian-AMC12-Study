# AMC12 2017-B Q23

![[assets/problems/2017-B/Q23.png]]

## Problem

A cubic $f$ passes through $(2,4)$, $(3,9)$, and $(4,16)$. The three secant lines meet the graph again at points whose $x$-coordinates sum to $24$. Find $f(0)$.

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{24}{5}$

## Solution

Set
$$g(x)=f(x)-x^2.$$
Since $f(2)=4$, $f(3)=9$, and $f(4)=16$,
$$g(x)=a(x-2)(x-3)(x-4).$$
The coefficient of $x^2$ in $f$ is $1-9a$. For any secant line, the cubic obtained by subtracting that line has root sum
$$-\frac{1-9a}{a}=9-\frac1a.$$
The three secants account for roots ${2,3,x_D}$, ${2,4,x_E}$, and ${3,4,x_F}$. Hence
$$24=3\left(9-\frac1a\right)-2(2+3+4)=9-\frac3a.$$
Thus $a=-1/5$, and
$$f(0)=a(-2)(-3)(-4)=\frac{24}{5}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Vieta's formulas
- Cubic interpolation and secant lines

## Key idea

Subtracting a line does not change the cubic or quadratic coefficients, so Vieta controls every secant intersection.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the three second intersections as roots of one polynomial
- Forgetting that each of $2,3,4$ appears in two secants
