# AMC12 2017-B Q16

![[assets/problems/2017-B/Q16.png]]

## Problem

A divisor of $21!$ is chosen uniformly at random. Find the probability that it is odd.

## Answer

**B.** $\frac1{19}$

## Solution

The exponent of $2$ in $21!$ is
$$\left\lfloor\frac{21}{2}\right\rfloor+\left\lfloor\frac{21}{4}\right\rfloor+\left\lfloor\frac{21}{8}\right\rfloor+\left\lfloor\frac{21}{16}\right\rfloor=10+5+2+1=18.$$
Every divisor chooses the exponent of $2$ from $0$ through $18$. Only exponent $0$ gives an odd divisor, so the probability is
$$\frac1{18+1}=\frac1{19}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Prime factorization of factorials
- Uniform counting of divisors

## Key idea

The odd part of a divisor can vary freely; only the exponent of $2$ matters.

## Common mistakes

- Using the exponent of $2$ in $21$ instead of in $21!$
- Counting only odd divisors of $21!$ directly
