# AMC12 2017-B Q04

![[assets/problems/2017-B/Q04.png]]

## Problem

Samia bicycles half the distance at $17$ km/h and walks the remaining half at $5$ km/h. The total time is $44$ minutes. How far does she walk, to the nearest tenth of a kilometer?

## Answer

**C.** $2.8$

## Solution

Let the walking distance be $d$ km. The bicycle distance is also $d$, so
$$\frac d{17}+\frac d5=\frac{44}{60}=\frac{11}{15}.$$
Since $\frac1{17}+\frac15=\frac{22}{85}$,
$$d=\frac{11}{15}\cdot\frac{85}{22}=\frac{17}{6}\approx2.833.$$
Therefore she walked $2.8$ km to the nearest tenth.

## Knowledge points

- Distance-rate-time
- Weighted average speed

## Key idea

Equal distances do not correspond to equal times; add the two travel times.

## Common mistakes

- Using $11$ km/h as an arithmetic average of the speeds
- Forgetting to convert minutes to hours
