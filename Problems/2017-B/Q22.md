# AMC12 2017-B Q22

![[assets/problems/2017-B/Q22.png]]

## Problem

Four players transfer one coin from the player receiving the green ball to the player receiving the red ball in each of four rounds. What is the probability that all four players finish with their original four coins?

## Answer

**B.** $\frac5{192}$

## Solution

Each round is an ordered pair of distinct players, so there are $4\cdot3=12$ equally likely transfers. Across four rounds there are $12^4$ sequences.

The net transfer is zero in the favorable patterns:

- a directed $4$-cycle: $144$ sequences;
- two disjoint exchanges: $72$ sequences;
- one pair exchanging twice: $36$ sequences;
- one player gives to and receives from two others: $288$ sequences.

The total is $540$, so
$$P=\frac{540}{12^4}=\frac5{192}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Counting ordered outcomes
- Net flow and casework

## Key idea

The final balances depend only on the directed transfer graph, not on the order of the four rounds.

## Common mistakes

- Treating a round as an unordered pair
- Counting only transfers that immediately cancel
