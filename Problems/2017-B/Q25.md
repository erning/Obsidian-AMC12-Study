# AMC12 2017-B Q25

![[assets/problems/2017-B/Q25.png]]

## Problem

There are $T$ distinct five-player teams among $n$ participants. The average number of complete teams inside a random $9$-person subset equals the reciprocal of the corresponding average for a random $8$-person subset. How many $n$ with $9\le n\le2017$ are possible?

## Answer

**D.** $557$

## Solution

A fixed team is contained in $\binom{n-5}{4}$ of the $\binom n9$ nine-person subsets, and in $\binom{n-5}{3}$ of the $\binom n8$ eight-person subsets. Thus the two averages are
$$A_9=T\frac{\binom{n-5}{4}}{\binom n9},qquad
A_8=T\frac{\binom{n-5}{3}}{\binom n8}.$$
The condition $A_9=1/A_8$ simplifies to
$$T=\frac{n(n-1)(n-2)(n-3)(n-4)}{2^5\cdot3^2\cdot5\cdot7}.$$
Therefore the product of five consecutive integers must be divisible by $2^5\cdot3^2\cdot5\cdot7$.

The resulting congruence conditions repeat modulo $1008$. Checking one period gives $280$ valid residues, so there are $560$ valid values among $1$ through $2016$. Remove $1,2,3,4$ and include $2017\equiv1\pmod{1008}$:
$$560-4+1=557.$$

## Knowledge points

- Double counting
- Binomial coefficients
- Periodic divisibility conditions

## Key idea

Count the same team-subset incidences in two ways, then convert integrality into a congruence-counting problem.

## Common mistakes

- Using $\binom{n-5}{4}$ for both subset sizes
- Forgetting that $T$ must be a positive integer
- Counting only one period without adjusting the endpoints
