# AMC12 2017-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2017-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

Sixty percent of students like dancing. Of those, $80%$ say they like it. Of the $40%$ who dislike dancing, $90%$ say they dislike it. What \fraction of students who say they dislike dancing actually like dancing?

## Answer

**D.** $25%$

## Solution

The students who actually like dancing but say they dislike it are
$$0.60(0.20)=0.12.$$
Those who actually dislike it and say so are
$$0.40(0.90)=0.36.$$
Therefore the requested conditional \fraction is
$$\frac{0.12}{0.12+0.36}=\frac14=25%.$$

## Knowledge points

- Conditional probability
- Two-way classification

## Key idea

Restrict the denominator to everyone who says “dislike.”

## Common mistakes

- Answering $20%$, the error rate among dancers
- Using all students as the denominator
