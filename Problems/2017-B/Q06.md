# AMC12 2017-B Q06

![[assets/problems/2017-B/Q06.png]]

## Problem

The endpoints $(0,0)$ and $(8,6)$ are the endpoints of a diameter of a circle. Find the second intersection of the circle with the $x$-axis.

## Answer

**D.** $8$

## Solution

The center is $(4,3)$ and the radius is
$$\sqrt{4^2+3^2}=5.$$
The circle equation is
$$(x-4)^2+(y-3)^2=25.$$
At $y=0$,
$$(x-4)^2+9=25,$$
so $x=0$ or $x=8$. The second point is $x=8$.

## Knowledge points

- Circle equations
- Midpoint and distance formulas

## Key idea

Use the diameter endpoints to obtain the center and radius.

## Common mistakes

- Using $(8,6)$ as the center
- Reporting the already-known intersection $x=0$
