# AMC12 2017-B Q18

![[assets/problems/2017-B/Q18.png]]

## Problem

A circle of radius $2$ has diameter $AB$ extended to $D$ with $BD=3$. Point $E$ satisfies $ED=5$ and $ED\perp AD$. If $AE$ meets the circle again at $C$, find $[ABC]$.

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{140}{37}$

## Solution

Because $AB$ is a diameter, $\angle ACB=90^\circ$. Also $\angle AED=90^\circ$, and the triangles share angle $A$, so
$$\triangle ABC\sim\triangle AED.$$
Here $AB=4$, $AD=7$, and
$$AE^2=AD^2+ED^2=7^2+5^2=74.$$
The area of $\triangle AED$ is $\frac12(7)(5)=\frac{35}{2}$. Similarity gives the area ratio
$$\frac{[ABC]}{[AED]}=\frac{AB^2}{AE^2}=\frac{16}{74}=\frac8{37}.$$
Therefore
$$[ABC]=\frac8{37}\cdot\frac{35}{2}=\frac{140}{37}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Similar triangles
- Thales' theorem
- Area ratios

## Key idea

Similarity converts the requested area into a squared side ratio.

## Common mistakes

- Using $AB=2$ instead of the diameter $AB=4$
- Using a length ratio rather than its square for areas
