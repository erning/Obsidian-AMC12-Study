# AMC12 2017-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2017-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

An ice-cream cup is a $4$-inch frustum with bottom diameter $2$ and top diameter $4$, topped by a cone of height $4$ with base diameter $4$. Find the total volume.

## Answer

**E.** $\frac{44\pi}{3}$

## Solution

Extend the frustum to a cone. Similarity shows it is the difference between a cone of radius $2$, height $8$, and a cone of radius $1$, height $4$:
$$V_{\text{frustum}}=\frac13\pi(2^2)(8)-\frac13\pi(1^2)(4)=\frac{28\pi}{3}.$$
The upper cone contributes
$$V_{\text{cone}}=\frac13\pi(2^2)(4)=\frac{16\pi}{3}.$$
Thus
$$V=\frac{28\pi}{3}+\frac{16\pi}{3}=\frac{44\pi}{3}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Volumes of cones and frustums
- Similar triangles

## Key idea

Represent a frustum as a large cone minus a smaller similar cone.

## Common mistakes

- Using diameter as radius
- Omitting the separate upper cone
