# AMC12 2015-B Q22

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q22.png]]

## Problem

Six chairs are evenly spaced around a circular table. One person is seated in each chair. Each person moves to a chair that is neither their original chair nor adjacent to it. In how many ways can this be done?

## Answer

**D.** $20$

## Solution

For each person, the allowed destinations are the three chairs at offsets
$2$, $3$, and $4$ modulo $6$. Thus the problem is the permanent of the
$6\times6$ allowed-position matrix.

Use inclusion-exclusion on the forbidden positions (the original chair and
its two adjacent chairs). The rook numbers of this forbidden board are

$$1,\ 18,\ 117,\ 336,\ 420,\ 192,\ 20.$$

Therefore the number of permutations avoiding every forbidden position is

$$
\begin{aligned}
&6!-18\cdot5!+117\cdot4!-336\cdot3!\\
&\qquad+420\cdot2!-192\cdot1!+20\cdot0!\\
&=20.
\end{aligned}
$$

So there are $20$ valid seat assignments.

## Knowledge points

- Permutations with forbidden positions
- Circular arrangements
- Casework

## Key idea

Relabel by the opposite-chair permutation, then count the restricted displacement permutations.

## Common mistakes

- Treating rotations of the people as the same arrangement
- Forgetting that every person is individually labeled
- Counting the opposite-chair relabeling twice
