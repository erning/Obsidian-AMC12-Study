# AMC12 2015-B Q12

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q12.png]]

## Problem

Let $a$, $b$, and $c$ be three distinct one-digit numbers. What is the maximum value of the sum of the roots of

$$ (x-a)(x-b)+(x-b)(x-c)=0?$$

## Answer

**D.** $16.5$

## Solution

Factor the left side:

$$
(x-b)\bigl((x-a)+(x-c)\bigr)
=(x-b)(2x-a-c).
$$

The roots are $x=b$ and $x=\frac{a+c}{2}$, so their sum is

$$b+\frac{a+c}{2}.$$

To maximize this using distinct one-digit numbers, choose $b=9$ and choose the two largest remaining digits $a,c=8,7$. This gives

$$9+\frac{8+7}{2}=16.5.$$

## Knowledge points

- Factoring
- Quadratic roots
- Optimization with distinct digits

## Key idea

The variable $b$ appears as one root, so it should receive the largest digit.

## Common mistakes

- Expanding the quadratic without noticing the common factor
- Choosing $a=9$ or $c=9$, which prevents $b$ from being $9$
