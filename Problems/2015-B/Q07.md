# AMC12 2015-B Q07

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q07.png]]

## Problem

A regular $15$-gon has $L$ lines of symmetry, and the smallest positive angle for which it has rotational symmetry is $R$ degrees. What is $L+R$?

## Answer

**D.** $39$

## Solution

A regular $n$-gon has $n$ lines of symmetry, so

$$L=15.$$

The smallest nonzero rotation that maps a regular $15$-gon to itself is one fifteenth of a full turn:

$$R=\frac{360^\circ}{15}=24^\circ.$$

Therefore,

$$L+R=15+24=39.$$

## Knowledge points

- Symmetry of regular polygons
- Rotational angles

## Key idea

For a regular $n$-gon, the number of reflection axes is $n$ and the basic rotation is $360^\circ/n$.

## Common mistakes

- Using $360^\circ$ as the smallest rotational angle
- Counting only the diagonals as lines of symmetry
