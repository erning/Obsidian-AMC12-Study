# AMC12 2015-B Q24

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q24.png]]

## Problem

Four noncongruent circles have centers $A,B,C,D$, and points $P,Q$ lie on all four circles. The radius of circle $A$ is $\frac85$ times the radius of circle $B$, and the radius of circle $C$ is $\frac85$ times the radius of circle $D$. Also $AB=CD=39$ and $PQ=48$. Let $R$ be the midpoint of $PQ$. What is $AR+BR+CR+DR$?

## Answer

**D.** $192$

## Solution

All four centers lie on the perpendicular bisector of $PQ$, and $R$ is its midpoint. Since $PQ=48$, each center has a right triangle with half-chord $PR=24$.

For circles $A$ and $B$, let $AR=y$ and $BR=x$. Their radii are $\sqrt{y^2+24^2}$ and $\sqrt{x^2+24^2}$. The ratio condition and $AB=39$ lead to

$$x=39-2y,\qquad y^2+50y-399=0.$$

The positive solution is $y=7$, so $AR=7$ and $BR=32$.

The analogous calculation for $C,D$ gives $CR=57$ and $DR=96$. Therefore

$$AR+BR+CR+DR=7+32+57+96=192.$$

## Knowledge points

- Chord geometry
- Right triangles
- Algebraic equations from ratio conditions

## Key idea

Use the midpoint of the common chord to reduce each radius to a right-triangle expression.

## Common mistakes

- Using $PQ$ instead of $PR=PQ/2$
- Choosing the negative root of the quadratic
- Assuming the two center pairs have the same orientation
