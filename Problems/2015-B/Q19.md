# AMC12 2015-B Q19

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q19.png]]

## Problem

In right triangle $ABC$, $\angle C=90^\circ$ and $AB=12$. Squares $ABXY$ and $ACWZ$ are constructed outside the triangle. The points $X,Y,Z,W$ lie on a circle. What is the perimeter of triangle $ABC$?

## Answer

**C.** $12+12\sqrt2$

## Solution

Let $BC=a$ and $CA=b$. Because the squares are erected externally, their perpendicular bisectors agree with the perpendicular bisectors of $AB$ and $AC$. The circle through $X,Y,Z,W$ therefore has center at the circumcenter $O$ of right triangle $ABC$.

Since $\angle C=90^\circ$, $O$ is the midpoint of hypotenuse $AB$, so $OA=OB=OC=6$. Let coordinates be chosen so that $C=(0,0)$, $A=(b,0)$, and $B=(0,a)$. The square construction and the equal distances from $O$ give

$$a^2+2b^2=144+2(a+2b)^2.$$

After simplifying, this yields

$$a=b=6\sqrt2.$$

Hence the perimeter is

$$AB+AC+BC=12+6\sqrt2+6\sqrt2=12+12\sqrt2.$$

## Knowledge points

- Pythagorean theorem
- Circumcenter of a right triangle
- Coordinate geometry

## Key idea

The circumcenter is the midpoint of the hypotenuse, which turns the four-point concyclicity condition into an algebraic relation.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the square vertices as arbitrary points on the circle
- Using $AB=12$ as a leg instead of the hypotenuse
