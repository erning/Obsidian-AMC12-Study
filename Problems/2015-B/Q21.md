# AMC12 2015-B Q21

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q21.png]]

## Problem

Cozy the Cat and Dash the Dog climb a staircase with $s$ steps. Cozy jumps $2$ steps at a time, using a shorter final jump if needed. Dash jumps $5$ steps at a time, also using a shorter final jump when needed. Suppose Dash takes $19$ fewer jumps than Cozy. Let $s$ be the sum of all possible staircase sizes. What is the sum of the digits of $s$?

## Answer

**D.** $13$

## Solution

For a staircase of $t$ steps, the numbers of jumps are

$$C(t)=\left\lceil\frac t2\right\rceil,\qquad D(t)=\left\lceil\frac t5\right\rceil.$$

We need

$$C(t)-D(t)=19.$$

Checking the possible values in the interval where this difference can equal $19$ gives

$$t=63,\ 64,\ 66.$$

Therefore

$$s=63+64+66=193,$$

and the digit sum is

$$1+9+3=13.$$

## Knowledge points

- Ceiling functions
- Integer inequalities
- Finite case checking

## Key idea

Translate “jumps” into ceiling functions and solve the integer difference condition.

## Common mistakes

- Using $t/2$ and $t/5$ without taking ceilings
- Including a staircase size where Dash is fewer by more than $19$
