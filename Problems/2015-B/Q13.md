# AMC12 2015-B Q13

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q13.png]]

## Problem

Quadrilateral $ABCD$ is inscribed in a circle with $\angle BAC=70^\circ$, $\angle ADB=40^\circ$, $AD=4$, and $BC=6$. What is $AC$?

## Answer

**B.** $6$

## Solution

Angles $\angle BAC$ and $\angle BDC$ subtend the same chord $BC$, so

$$\angle BDC=70^\circ.$$

Therefore

$$\angle ADC=\angle ADB+\angle BDC=40^\circ+70^\circ=110^\circ.$$

Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral are supplementary, so

$$\angle ABC=180^\circ-110^\circ=70^\circ.$$

Thus $\angle ABC=\angle BAC$, making triangle $ABC$ isosceles with opposite sides $AC=BC$. Hence $AC=6$.

## Knowledge points

- Inscribed angles
- Cyclic quadrilaterals
- Isosceles triangles

## Key idea

Transfer the known angle through the common chord $BC$, then use supplementary opposite angles.

## Common mistakes

- Assuming $\angle BDC=\angle BAC$ without identifying the common chord
- Using adjacent rather than opposite angles in the cyclic quadrilateral
