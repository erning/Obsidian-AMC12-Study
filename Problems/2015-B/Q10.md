# AMC12 2015-B Q10

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q10.png]]

## Problem

How many noncongruent integer-sided triangles with positive area and perimeter less than $15$ are neither equilateral, isosceles, nor right triangles?

## Answer

**C.** $5$

## Solution

A triangle that is neither equilateral nor isosceles has distinct integer sides $a<b<c$. The triangle inequality gives $a+b>c$, so the perimeter is greater than $2c$. Since the perimeter is less than $15$, we have $c\le6$.

Listing the possible scalene triples gives

$$(6,5,3),(6,5,2),(6,4,3),(5,4,3),(5,4,2),(4,3,2).$$

The triple $(5,4,3)$ is right, while the other five are not. Therefore the answer is $5$.

## Knowledge points

- Triangle inequality
- Systematic enumeration
- Pythagorean triples

## Key idea

Use $2c<a+b+c<15$ to bound the largest side before listing cases.

## Common mistakes

- Including isosceles triples
- Forgetting to remove the $(3,4,5)$ right triangle
- Treating different orderings as different triangles
