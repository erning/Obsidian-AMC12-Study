# AMC12 2015-B Q17

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q17.png]]

## Problem

An unfair coin lands heads with probability $\frac14$. When tossed $n$ times, the probability of exactly two heads equals the probability of exactly three heads. What is $n$?

## Answer

**D.** $11$

## Solution

Set the two binomial probabilities equal:

$$
\binom n2\left(\frac14\right)^2\left(\frac34\right)^{n-2}
=
\binom n3\left(\frac14\right)^3\left(\frac34\right)^{n-3}.
$$

Cancel common factors and use

$$\frac{\binom n2}{\binom n3}=\frac{3}{n-2}.$$

Then

$$\frac{3}{n-2}\cdot\frac{3/4}{1/4}=1,$$

so

$$\frac{9}{n-2}=1,\qquad n-2=9.$$

Thus $n=11$.

## Knowledge points

- Binomial probability
- Combinations
- Algebraic simplification

## Key idea

Compare adjacent binomial probabilities through their ratio instead of expanding them.

## Common mistakes

- Using $\binom n2$ for both cases
- Reversing the success and failure probabilities
