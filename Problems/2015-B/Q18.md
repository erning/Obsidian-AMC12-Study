# AMC12 2015-B Q18

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q18.png]]

## Problem

For every composite positive integer $n$, define $r(n)$ to be the sum of the factors in the prime factorization of $n$. For example, $r(50)=2+5+5=12$. What is the range of $r$ over composite positive integers?

## Answer

**D.** The set of integers greater than $3$

## Solution

A composite integer has at least two prime factors counted with multiplicity. Since the smallest prime is $2$, the least possible value is

$$r(4)=2+2=4.$$

Every integer $m\ge4$ occurs. For even $m=2k$, use $n=2^k$, giving $r(n)=2k=m$. For odd $m\ge5$, write $m=2k+3$ with $k\ge1$ and use $n=2^k\cdot3$, giving

$$r(n)=2k+3=m.$$

Therefore the range is precisely the set of integers greater than $3$.

- Prime factorization
- Range construction
- Parity cases

## Key idea

Find the minimum value, then construct an input for every larger value.

## Common mistakes

- Summing distinct prime factors instead of counting multiplicity
- Claiming that $3$ is attained, even though a composite number needs at least two prime factors
