# AMC12 2015-B Q16

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q16.png]]

## Problem

A regular hexagon with sides of length $6$ has an isosceles triangle attached to each side. Each triangle has two sides of length $8$. The triangles are folded to make a pyramid with the hexagon as its base. What is the volume of the pyramid?

## Answer

**C.** $36\sqrt{21}$

## Solution

The pyramid's apex is connected to each vertex of the hexagonal base by a lateral edge of length $8$. The distance from the center of a regular hexagon of side $6$ to a vertex is $6$. Thus the pyramid height $h$ satisfies

$$h^2+6^2=8^2,$$

so

$$h=\sqrt{28}=2\sqrt7.$$

The area of the regular hexagon is

$$\frac{3\sqrt3}{2}\cdot6^2=54\sqrt3.$$

Therefore

$$V=\frac13(54\sqrt3)(2\sqrt7)=36\sqrt{21}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Regular hexagon area
- Pythagorean theorem
- Pyramid volume

## Key idea

The center-to-vertex distance of a regular hexagon equals its side length.

## Common mistakes

- Using the apothem instead of the circumradius
- Forgetting the factor $\frac13$ in pyramid volume
