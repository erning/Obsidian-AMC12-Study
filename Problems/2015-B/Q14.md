# AMC12 2015-B Q14

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q14.png]]

## Problem

A circle of radius $2$ is centered at $A$. An equilateral triangle with side $4$ has a vertex at $A$. What is the difference between the area inside the circle but outside the triangle and the area inside the triangle but outside the circle?

## Answer

**D.** $4(\pi-\sqrt3)$

## Solution

Let $z$ be the area common to the circle and triangle. The requested difference is

$$
(\text{circle}-z)-(\text{triangle}-z)
=\text{circle}-\text{triangle}.
$$

The circle has area $4\pi$. The equilateral triangle has area

$$\frac{\sqrt3}{4}\cdot4^2=4\sqrt3.$$

Therefore the difference is

$$4\pi-4\sqrt3=4(\pi-\sqrt3).$$

## Knowledge points

- Areas of circles and equilateral triangles
- Inclusion-exclusion cancellation

## Key idea

The overlap cancels when the two “outside” regions are subtracted.

## Common mistakes

- Trying to calculate the overlap region
- Using $s^2$ instead of $\frac{\sqrt3}{4}s^2$ for the triangle area
