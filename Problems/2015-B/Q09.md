# AMC12 2015-B Q09

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q09.png]]

## Problem

Larry and Julius take turns throwing a ball at a bottle. Larry throws first, and the probability that a player knocks the bottle off on any turn is $\frac12$, independently of previous turns. What is the probability that Larry wins?

## Answer

**C.** $\frac23$

## Solution

Let $p$ be the probability that Larry eventually wins. Larry wins immediately with probability $\frac12$. If both players miss, which has probability $\frac12\cdot\frac12=\frac14$, the same situation restarts with probability $p$.

Therefore

$$p=\frac12+\frac14p.$$

Solving,

$$\frac34p=\frac12,\qquad p=\frac23.$$

## Knowledge points

- Infinite geometric processes
- Recurrence equations
- Probability

## Key idea

After two misses, the game returns to its original state.

## Common mistakes

- Forgetting that Larry gets the first throw in every restarted round
- Using $\frac12$ instead of $\frac14$ for two consecutive misses
