# AMC12 2015-B Q25

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q25.png]]

## Problem

A bee starts at $P_0$ and flies $1$ inch due east to $P_1$. For $j\ge1$,
once it reaches $P_j$, it turns $30^\circ$ counterclockwise from its
previous direction and flies $j+1$ inches straight to $P_{j+1}$. When it
reaches $P_{2015}$, it is exactly $a\sqrt b+c\sqrt d$ inches away from
$P_0$, where $a,b,c,d$ are positive integers and $b$ and $d$ are not
divisible by the square of any prime. What is $a+b+c+d$?

## Answer

**B.** $2024$

## Solution

Let

$$z=e^{\pi i/6}.$$

The $k$th segment has length $k$ and direction $z^{k-1}$, so the final position is

$$P_{2015}=\sum_{k=1}^{2015}kz^{k-1}.$$

Using the differentiated geometric-series identity,

$$
\sum_{k=1}^{n}kz^{k-1}
=\frac{1-(n+1)z^n+nz^{n+1}}{(1-z)^2}.
$$

For $n=2015$, use $z^{2016}=1$ and $z^{2015}=z^{-1}$. Then

$$
P_{2015}
=\frac{1-2016z^{-1}+2015}{(1-z)^2}
=\frac{2016}{z(z-1)}.
$$

Since $|z|=1$ and

$$
|z-1|=2\sin\frac{\pi}{12}
=\sqrt{2-\sqrt3},
$$

we obtain

$$
|P_{2015}|
=\frac{2016}{\sqrt{2-\sqrt3}}
=2016\sqrt{2+\sqrt3}
=1008\sqrt6+1008\sqrt2.
$$

Thus $(a,b,c,d)=(1008,6,1008,2)$, and

$$a+b+c+d=2024.$$

## Knowledge points

- Complex numbers as rotations
- Geometric series and differentiation
- Exact radical simplification

## Key idea

Encode the successive $30^\circ$ turns by a sixth root of unity and sum the resulting complex vectors.

## Common mistakes

- Starting the segment index at $0$ instead of $1$
- Using $e^{\pi i/3}$ for a $30^\circ$ turn
- Treating the final distance as the sum of segment lengths
