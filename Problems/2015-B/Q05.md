# AMC12 2015-B Q05

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q05.png]]

## Problem

The Tigers beat the Sharks $2$ out of the first $3$ times they played. They then played $N$ more times, and the Sharks ended up winning at least $95\%$ of all the games played. What is the minimum possible value of $N$?

## Answer

**B.** $37$

## Solution

The Sharks won $1$ of the first $3$ games. To make their final winning fraction reach $95\%$ as quickly as possible, they must win every one of the next $N$ games. Thus

$$\frac{N+1}{N+3}\ge\frac{19}{20}.$$

Cross-multiplying gives

$$20N+20\ge19N+57,$$

so $N\ge37$. Taking $N=37$ works, so the minimum is $37$.

## Knowledge points

- Percentages
- Inequalities
- Extremal reasoning

## Key idea

For a minimum, assume every additional game is a Sharks win.

## Common mistakes

- Using $2$ instead of $1$ as the Sharks' initial number of wins
- Comparing the number of additional wins with $95\%$ instead of comparing the final fraction
