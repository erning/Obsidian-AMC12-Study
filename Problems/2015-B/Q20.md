# AMC12 2015-B Q20

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q20.png]]

## Problem

For every positive integer $n$, let $\operatorname{mod}_5(n)$ be the remainder when $n$ is divided by $5$. Define $f:\{0,1,2,3,\ldots\}\times\{0,1,2,3,4\}\to\{0,1,2,3,4\}$ recursively by

$$
f(i,j)=
\begin{cases}
\operatorname{mod}_5(j+1), & i=0,\\
f(i-1,1), & i\ge1,\ j=0,\\
f(i-1,f(i,j-1)), & i\ge1,\ 1\le j\le4.
\end{cases}
$$

What is $f(2015,2)$?

## Answer

**B.** $1$

## Solution

Compute the values row by row. The column with $j=2$ stabilizes after the first few rows; in particular,

$$f(i,2)=1\qquad\text{for all }i\ge5.$$

Since $2015\ge5$,

$$f(2015,2)=1.$$

## Knowledge points

- Recursive definitions
- Modular arithmetic
- Stabilization

## Key idea

The recursion can be evaluated by rows; only a small initial segment is needed before the target column becomes constant.

## Common mistakes

- Applying the $i=0$ rule when $i=2015$
- Assuming the recursion is ordinary addition or multiplication modulo $5$
