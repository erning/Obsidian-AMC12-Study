# AMC12 2015-B Q15

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q15.png]]

## Problem

At Rachelle's school, an A counts $4$ points, a B $3$ points, a C $2$ points, and a D $1$ point. Her GPA for four classes is the total points divided by $4$. She is certain of As in Mathematics and Science, and at least a C in English and History. In English, the probabilities of A and B are $\frac16$ and $\frac14$. In History, the probabilities of A and B are $\frac14$ and $\frac13$, independently of English. What is the probability that her GPA is at least $3.5$?

## Answer

**D.** $\frac{11}{24}$

## Solution

The two guaranteed As contribute $8$ points. A GPA of at least $3.5$ requires at least $14$ total points, so English and History must contribute at least $6$ points.

The probabilities of C are

$$P(E=C)=1-\frac16-\frac14=\frac7{12},$$

$$P(H=C)=1-\frac14-\frac13=\frac5{12}.$$

The favorable grade pairs are

$$(A,A),(A,B),(A,C),(B,A),(B,B),(C,A),$$

because their combined points are at least $6$. Using independence, the probability is

$$
\frac16\cdot\frac14+
\frac16\cdot\frac13+
\frac16\cdot\frac5{12}+
\frac14\cdot\frac14+
\frac14\cdot\frac13+
\frac7{12}\cdot\frac14
=\frac{11}{24}.
$$

## Knowledge points

- Complementary probability
- Independent events
- Case enumeration

## Key idea

Translate the GPA condition into a minimum point total, then enumerate grade pairs.

## Common mistakes

- Requiring English and History to total at least $7$ instead of $6$
- Forgetting that the English and History outcomes are independent
- Using the wrong C probabilities
