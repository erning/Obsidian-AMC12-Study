# AMC12 2015-B Q06

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q06.png]]

## Problem

Back in 1930, Tillie had to memorize her multiplication facts from $0\times0$ through $12\times12$. What fraction of the numbers in the body of the multiplication table are odd, to the nearest hundredth?

## Answer

**A.** $0.21$

## Solution

There are $13$ row labels and $13$ column labels, so the body contains

$$13\cdot13=169$$

products. A product is odd exactly when both factors are odd. Among $0,1,\ldots,12$, the six odd numbers are $1,3,5,7,9,11$. Hence there are

$$6\cdot6=36$$

odd products. The fraction is

$$\frac{36}{169}\approx0.2130,$$

which rounds to $0.21$.

## Knowledge points

- Parity
- Counting by complementary conditions

## Key idea

A product is odd if and only if both factors are odd.

## Common mistakes

- Counting only the odd numbers from $1$ to $12 and forgetting that $0$ is included
- Using $6\cdot7$ instead of $6\cdot6$
