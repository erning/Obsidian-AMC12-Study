# AMC12 2015-B Q11

![[assets/problems/2015-B/Q11.png]]

## Problem

The line $12x+5y=60$ forms a triangle with the coordinate axes. What is the sum of the lengths of the altitudes of this triangle?

## Answer

**E.** $\frac{281}{13}$

## Solution

The intercepts are $(5,0)$ and $(0,12)$, so the triangle is right with legs $5$ and $12$ and hypotenuse $13$. The area is

$$K=\frac12\cdot5\cdot12=30.$$

The altitude to the hypotenuse is

$$h=\frac{2K}{13}=\frac{60}{13}.$$

The other two altitudes are the legs themselves. Thus their sum is

$$5+12+\frac{60}{13}=\frac{281}{13}.$$

## Knowledge points

- Coordinate intercepts
- Right-triangle geometry
- Area and altitude

## Key idea

The altitude to the hypotenuse can be found from $2K=ch$.

## Common mistakes

- Treating the intercept lengths as $12$ and $5$ but using the wrong hypotenuse
- Adding only the three side lengths
